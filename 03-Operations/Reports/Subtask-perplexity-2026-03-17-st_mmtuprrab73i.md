### Best Practices for Building Influence
- **Lead by example and model company values to persuade senior stakeholders**: CBRE requires engineering directors to "Lead by example and model behaviors that are consistent with CBRE RISE values. Anticipate potential objections and persuades others, often at senior levels and of divergent interest, to adopt a different point of view."[1][2][3]
- **Communicate complex ideas to influence multi-discipline teams**: Directors must "Communicate difficult and complex ideas with the ability to influence" and "Impact the achievement of customer, operational, project, or service objectives across multi-discipline teams."[1][2][3][4]
- **Coach and develop teams for broader expertise**: Apply "advanced knowledge to seek and develop new, better methods" and "Coach others to develop in-depth knowledge and expertise in most or all areas within the function."[1][2][3][5]
- **Negotiate with external partners**: "Negotiate with external partners, vendors, and customers of divergent interests to reach a common goal."[4][5]

### Strategies for New Engineering Directors
- **Drive cross-functional outcomes via broad knowledge**: "Influence across teams and stakeholders by applying broad business and technical knowledge to drive cross-functional outcomes."[5][8]
- **Mentor, hire, and evaluate staff**: "Provide formal supervision to employees. Monitor the training and development of staff. Conduct performance evaluations and coaching. Oversee the recruiting and hiring of new employees."[4][5]
- **Implement preventive programs and risk planning**: "Develop, implement, and communicate preventive maintenance programs" and "Lead risk-based reliability planning by developing risk management plans."[2][5]
- **Solve complex problems organization-wide**: "Identify and solve multi-dimensional, complex, operational and organizational problems leveraging the appropriate resources."[4]
