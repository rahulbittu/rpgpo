## Important Documents for Emergencies
Keep these physical and digital copies in waterproof containers or cloud storage for quick access during evacuations or outages.

- **Identification and Legal Papers**: Passports, driver's licenses, birth certificates, Social Security cards, marriage/divorce certificates, and pet IDs; scan and store digitally.[1]
- **Financial and Insurance Docs**: Bank account details, credit card info, insurance policies (home, auto, health, life), mortgage/lease agreements, and property deeds.[1]
- **Medical Records**: Prescription lists, allergies, vaccination records, and doctor's contacts; include 7-14 days of medications.[1]
- **Emergency Contacts List**: Printed list with phone numbers for family, doctors, insurance agents, and local shelters; laminate for durability.[1]
- **Real Example from EPA Template**: Utility-specific docs like system schematics, chemical inventories, mutual aid agreements, and regulatory permits for water/wastewater emergencies.[2]

## Effective Communication Plans for Emergencies
Pre-build a **5-step crisis communication plan** with assigned roles, templates, and schedules to avoid confusion; test quarterly.

### Core 5 Steps from Crisis Playbook
1. **Assign Communication Lead**: Single point for all messaging to prevent conflicts.[1]
2. **Identify Stakeholders**: Leadership, employees, vendors, customers; map priorities.[1]
3. **Prepare Message Templates**: Use pre-written scripts, e.g., "We are investigating [issue] identified at [time]. Next update by [time]."[1]
4. **Define Channels**: Status pages, email, Slack/Teams, vendor portals.[1]
5. **Set Update Intervals**: Every 30 minutes for active issues, hourly for outages.[1]

### Ready Templates and Checklists
- **Customer Update Template**: "Issue affecting [service] detected at [time]. Resolution targeted by [time]."[1]
- **Vendor Coordination**: Share tech details, request escalations, provide resolution steps.[1]
- **Free Crisis Templates**: Downloadable for organizations from OreaTAI (various sectors) and EPA (water utilities); include escalation paths and timelines.[2][3]
- **Playbook Essentials**: Messaging templates, stakeholder guidelines, update schedules; pair with operational response procedures.[1]
