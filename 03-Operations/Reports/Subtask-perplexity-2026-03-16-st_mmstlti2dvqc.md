## Material Comparisons: Brick vs Prefab vs Ooni Portable

- **Brick Ovens**: Custom-built using firebricks for the dome, concrete blocks for the stand, and reinforced concrete slabs; capable of extreme temperatures with efficient heat storage; heavy weight requires strong foundation to prevent cracking[1].
- **Prefab Ovens (e.g., RÖCKA Wood-Fired Oven)**: Pre-manufactured units like Techo-Bloc's RÖCKA model install on geotextile and 4-6" open-graded ASTM #57 sub-base; designed for wood-fired cooking and smoking; easier assembly than full brick but needs stable excavation[3].
- **Ooni Portable**: No specific details in recent searches (last 30 days); lightweight, gas/wood pellet models like Ooni Koda or Karu reach 950°F in 20 minutes, weigh 20-50 lbs, no foundation required—place on heat-safe surface; priced $299-$999 (2025 models)[no URL available; based on pre-2026 knowledge as live search yielded no 2026 updates].

## Foundation Requirements

- Dig footings deep for soil stability; use rectangular wooden mold for slab (e.g., straight boards secured to avoid bowing)[1][2].
- Layer 1: Plastic moisture barrier sheet over ground[1].
- Layer 2: Steel reinforcement mesh for crack resistance under heavy dome[1].
- Layer 3: Pour concrete, level with straight edge/trowel for perfect flatness—critical for stability[1].
- For prefab like RÖCKA: Geotextile up excavation sides (shingled with water flow), 4”-6” ASTM #57 sub-base[3].
- Concrete block stand: Staggered rows, check alignment per course; top with reinforced slab[1].

**Next Steps**: Measure site (e.g., 4x6 ft mold[1]); source ASTM #57 gravel ($50/ton locally); cure slab 7-28 days before loading.

## Curing Processes

- Foundation slab: Pour concrete, spread/level, cure fully (implied 7+ days) before stacking blocks—ensures no "almost level" errors[1].
