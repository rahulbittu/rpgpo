## Hip Stability for Distance Runners

**Focus on glute medius strengthening to improve hip stability and reduce injury risk during mileage increases.** Physical therapist Saad recommends curtsy lunges and side-lying leg lifts targeting the glute medius, as weak glutes contribute to overstriding and excessive vertical bounce, increasing joint stress.[1] Core and hip strengthening also stabilizes stride, preventing runner’s knee and IT band syndrome; include bodyweight squats, lunges, and planks 2-3 times weekly.[2]

- **Actionable next step:** Perform 3 sets of 10-12 reps curtsy lunges per leg daily, progressing to weighted versions after 2 weeks. Source: https://www.runnersworld.com/training/a70576243/increasing-mileage-injury-risks/[1]

## Recovery Programming for Distance Runners

**Incorporate active rest days with low-impact activities like swimming, elliptical, or stationary biking after 3-5 running days to reduce stiffness and allow tissue repair.** Avoid complete rest; schedule recovery days equal to running days, limiting weekly mileage increases to 10% max.[1][2][6] Monitor individual run mileage over total weekly volume for better injury prediction.[3]
