## Shoe Selection Science
Running shoes should be replaced every **300-500 miles** to maintain cushioning and prevent injuries like blisters or plantar fasciitis[1]. For standing desk users like nurses, select shoes with **at least 30 mm heel stack** and **20 mm forefoot stack** for shock absorption, tested via ASTM F1976 standard; wider toeboxes prevent bunions and accommodate foot swelling during long shifts[3]. Podiatrist-recommended fit: thumb's width toe room, no heel slip, avoid tight lacing; match to foot type (e.g., maximalist trainers with thicker midsoles and lower drop for supinators)[4][5].

## Arch Support Assessment
Assess gait via podiatrist or running specialist for pronation/supination; flat feet/overpronation need firmer cushioning, side stability, and secure heel counters to prevent wobbling[3]. Supinators benefit from maximalist shoes with thicker midsoles for impact reduction and fuller foot contact[5]. No specific quantitative metrics found; consult experts for custom orthotics[4].

## Plantar Fasciitis Prevention
Use **RICE (Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevate)** for treatment; prevent via proper shoes with arch support, short strides/quick cadence, and low-impact form landing on midfoot/ball[1][2][3]. Alternate shoes for drying, wear synthetic moisture-wicking socks, apply anti-chafing powder/balm; nearly **50% of runner injuries recur** without rest[1]. Strengthening/stretching (toe raises, heel drops) essential[1][2].

**Source:** https://marathonhandbook.com/runners-feet/[1]; https://www.drmcquaid.com/blog/running-injuries-that-affect-the-feet-and-how-to-prevent-them[2]; https://runrepeat.com/guides/best-for-nurses-walking-shoes[3]

## Ankle Mobility Drills
Perform **toe raises and heel drops on steps** during warmups/cooldowns to stretch and prep ankles/feet for limberness[1]. Regular foot strengthening exercises with proper footwear recommended; no specific reps/sets in results[2]. Low-impact form with quick cadence aids mobility[2].

**Source:** https://marathonhandbook.com/runners-feet/[1]; https://www.drmcquaid.com/blog/running-injuries-that-affect-the-feet-and-how-to-prevent-them[2]

## Recovery Techniques
**RICE protocol** primary for plantar fasciitis and injuries: rest fully (avoid "walk it off"), ice, compress, elevate[1]. Post-run: change sweaty socks immediately, wash/dry feet thoroughly, moisturize skin, let shoes dry fully (alternate pairs), use foam rollers for aches[1][5]. Maintain training schedule with stretching/strengthening to build resilience[2].
