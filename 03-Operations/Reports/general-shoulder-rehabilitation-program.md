## Detailed Analysis

### Phased Rehabilitation Approach

- **Acute Pain Reduction (1-2 weeks)**: Focus on pendulum swings and passive range of motion (ROM) exercises to minimize pain and inflammation. This phase sets the foundation for further recovery by ensuring the shoulder is not aggravated further.[3][4]

- **Mobility Restoration (2-4 weeks)**: Active-assisted ROM exercises and isometrics are introduced to regain mobility while maintaining stability. This phase is crucial for restoring the shoulder's functional range without compromising joint integrity.[3][4]

- **Strengthening (4-8 weeks)**: Incorporate banded rotations and scapular work to build strength in the rotator cuff and surrounding muscles. Emphasize controlled movements and gradual resistance increases to prevent re-injury.[1][2]

- **Functional Return (8+ weeks)**: Transition to sport-specific drills and activities, focusing on pain-free execution and strength endurance. This phase ensures the shoulder is ready for the demands of regular training and competition.[3][4]

### Rotator Cuff Strengthening Exercises

- **External Rotation with Resistance Band**: Essential for stabilizing the shoulder joint, particularly targeting the infraspinatus and teres minor. This exercise helps in preventing future impingement by strengthening the external rotators.[1][2][5]

- **Internal Rotation with Resistance Band**: Focuses on the subscapularis, enhancing anterior shoulder stability and overall rotator cuff balance.[1][2]

- **Dumbbell Scapular Plane Raises (Scaption)**: Safely engages the supraspinatus and deltoid, crucial for shoulder elevation and reducing risk of impingement during overhead activities.[2]

- **Wall Push-Ups**: Provides a controlled environment to build pushing strength without excessive load, promoting scapular stability.[1]

### Overhead Press Modifications

- Use lighter weights and focus on the form to avoid excessive strain on the shoulder.
- Limit the range of motion initially, avoiding full overhead extension until strength and stability are restored.
- Incorporate overhead press variations, such as seated or single-arm presses, to control movement and focus on stability.

### Return to Training Criteria

- Ensure pain-free movement throughout the shoulder's range of motion.
- Achieve strength levels comparable to the non-injured side.
- Successfully perform sport-specific drills without discomfort.
- Gradually increase training intensity, monitoring for any signs of pain or discomfort.
