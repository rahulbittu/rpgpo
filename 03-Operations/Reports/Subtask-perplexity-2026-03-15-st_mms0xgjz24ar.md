## Finding 1: Outlet Placement and Quantity for Home Offices
Home offices require multiple outlets positioned for convenience, including double or quad power points (GPOs) above desk height to support computers, monitors, chargers, printers, desk lamps, and Wi-Fi routers without crawling under furniture. USB-integrated outlets are recommended for charging devices. A licensed electrician should assess electrical load to add dedicated circuits, preventing flickering lights or power drops during video calls; also consider Ethernet data points for stable internet over Wi-Fi.[1][3]

**Key Recommendations:**
- Place outlets behind/near desks, in corners for lamps/vacuums, and near workstations.
- Minimum: 4-6 outlets per room, but scale up for modern devices (e.g., laptops, smart devices).
- Safety: Install AFCI outlets in offices/living areas to prevent arc-fault fires from damaged wires.[4]

## Finding 2: Lighting Requirements for Home Offices
Layer lighting with 2-3 sources per room: overhead for general illumination, mid-level (e.g., desk lamps at 800 lumens), and accent lighting to avoid flat/harsh effects and support focus/work tasks. Dimmable fixtures help adjust for circadian rhythms; position to eliminate shadows on work surfaces.[2]

**Implementation Steps:**
- Overhead: Flush-mount or low-profile ceiling lights.
- Task: Table/sconce lamps near desks (800 lumens ideal).
- Applies universally but prioritize for offices to balance productivity and comfort.

Source: https://thediyplaybook.com/rookie-mistake-rules-for-lighting/[2]

## Finding 3: Overall Electrical Service and Special Considerations
Modern homes need at least 100-amp service (check main breaker or meter); upgrade to 200-amp panel ($5,000-$10,000 typical cost, varies by location) for home office expansions, EV chargers, or high-load setups to avoid overloads—required by many insurers. Special considerations: GFCI outlets not typically needed in dry offices but AFCIs yes; watch for warm outlets, tripping breakers, or flickering as upgrade signs (affects 1 in 5 older installs).[4][5][7] Hire licensed electrician for compliance (e.g., AS/NZS 3000 in Australia).[3]

**Next Steps:**
1. Inspect panel: Note amp rating on main breaker.
2. Load calculation: Electrician assesses (e.g., add 20-50 amps for office gear).
3. Upgrade if <200 amps and remodeling.
