## Finding 1: Audience Alignment for Conference Themes and Topics
Reventals outlines selecting conference themes (applicable to talk topics) by identifying audience type first—e.g., executives, tech innovators, or educators—and matching to their values/challenges. For tech innovators, bold themes like **“Disrupt Everything”** work; for educators, balanced ones like **“Building What’s Next”** or **“Innovating with Purpose”**. Event format (in-person/hybrid) guides choices: in-person favors mood-setting themes via staging; hybrid prioritizes ideological ones like **“Breaking Barriers”** or **“Innovating with Purpose”**.  
Source: https://www.reventals.com/blog/conference-theme-ideas/[1]

## Finding 2: ACU 2026 Selection Criteria for Abstracts (Directly for Talk Topics)
Clinicians.org's ACU 2026 Call for Abstracts (deadline February 27, 2026) scores submissions on 6 criteria, requiring at least 4: (1) addresses current issue/barrier; (2) enables lasting/sustainable change; (3) evidence-based or innovative research; (4) effective innovative option; (5) implementable by others; (6) aligns with conference theme. Tracks include "Improving Clinical Care" with sample topics like sustainable care models or skills for underserved communities. Sessions: 10-15 min presentations + discussion.  
Source: https://clinicians.org/conferences/acu-2026/call-for-abstracts/[2]
