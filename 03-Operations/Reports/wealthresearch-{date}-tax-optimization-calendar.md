## Detailed Analysis

### W-2 and Tax Filing
- **February 2, 2026**: Employers must provide W-2 forms. This is crucial for employees to start preparing their tax returns.
- **April 15, 2026**: Deadline for filing tax returns using Form 1040. Employees with side income should also consider filing Schedule C or 1099 forms for additional income.
- **October 15, 2026**: Extension deadline for filing taxes, which can be requested via Form 4868.

### HSA Contributions
- The 2025 HSA contribution limits are set, but the 2026 limits will be announced later in 2025. Employees should plan contributions based on the 2025 limits until new information is available.

### 401(k) Contributions

## Recommended Actions

1. **January**:
   - **Task**: Prepare for W-2 receipt.
   - **Why**: To ensure timely tax filing.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Readiness to file taxes as soon as W-2 is received.
   - **First Step**: Verify mailing address with employer.

2. **February**:
   - **Task**: Collect W-2 and any 1099 forms.
   - **Why**: Essential for accurate tax filing.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Complete set of documents for tax preparation.
   - **First Step**: Confirm receipt of all necessary forms by mid-February.

3. **March**:
   - **Task**: Begin tax preparation.
   - **Why**: Avoid last-minute rush and potential penalties.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Early filing or readiness to file by April 15.
   - **First Step**: Gather all financial documents and consider using tax software or consulting a tax professional.

4. **April**:
   - **Task**: File taxes by April 15.
   - **Why**: To meet IRS deadline and avoid penalties.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Compliance with tax obligations.
   - **First Step**: Submit Form 1040 and any additional schedules.

5. **October**:
   - **Task**: File taxes if extension was requested.
   - **Why**: Final deadline for extended tax filings.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Completion of tax filing obligations.
   - **First Step**: Ensure all documents are ready and submit by October 15.

6. **Throughout the Year**:
   - **Task**: Monitor IRS announcements for 401(k) and HSA limits.
   - **Why**: To maximize tax-advantaged savings.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Optimized retirement and health savings contributions.
   - **First Step**: Set reminders for IRS updates in late 2025.

By following this calendar, a W-2 employee with side income can effectively manage their tax obligations and maximize their savings opportunities throughout the year.
