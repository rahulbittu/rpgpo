## Solo 401(k) for Self-Employed (2026 Limits)
Solo 401(k) allows both employee deferrals and employer profit-sharing, with Roth options and loans available; total combined limit under age 50 is $72,000.[2][4] Employee elective deferral is $24,500, catch-up for age 50+ is $8,000, super catch-up for age 60-63 is $11,250, plus employer up to 25% of compensation.[2] Roth options exist for employee contributions (mega backdoor Roth possible via after-tax contributions up to total limit).[2][4]
- **Source**: https://www.irafinancial.com/blog/solo-401k-tax-benefits-2026/[2]; https://www.advantaira.com/blog/self-directed-retirement-plans-for-small-business-owners/[4]

## SEP IRA for Self-Employed (2026 Limits)
Employer-only contributions up to lesser of $72,000 or 25% of compensation (20% of net income if self-employed); no employee deferrals or catch-up provisions.[1][4][5] Roth options available via after-tax contributions.[4] No loans or mega backdoor strategy.
- **Source**: https://districtcapitalmanagement.com/sep-ira-vs-simple-ira/[1]; https://www.advantaira.com/blog/self-directed-retirement-plans-for-small-business-owners/[4]; https://www.tristatewealthadvisors.com/blogs/self-employed-retirement-options-a-complete-2026-guide-for-business-owners[5]

## SIMPLE IRA for Self-Employed (2026 Limits)
Employee contributions up to $17,000 (under 50), plus catch-up $4,000 (age 50+); employer must match up to 3% or contribute 2% nonelective.[1][3] No Roth options or mega backdoor; tax-deferred only, for businesses with ≤100 employees.[1][5]
- **Source**: https://districtcapitalmanagement.com/sep-ira-vs-simple-ira/[1]; https://www.britannica.com/money/small-business-retirement-plans[3]; https://www.tristatewealthadvisors.com/blogs/self-employed-retirement-options-a-complete-2026-guide-for-business-owners[5]

## Mega Backdoor Strategies
Mega backdoor Roth viable only in **Solo 401(k)** via after-tax employee contributions (beyond deferral/profit-share, up to $72,000 total under 50 in 2026), convertible to Roth; not available in SEP or SIMPLE IRAs.[2][4]
- **Source**: https://www.irafinancial.com/blog/solo-401k-tax-benefits-2026/[2]; https://www.advantaira.com/blog/self-directed-retirement-plans-for-small-business-owners/[4]
