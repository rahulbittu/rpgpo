## Herman Miller Aeron

**Ergonomic features:** Full mesh backrest for breathability, PostureFit SL (two-pad adjustable lumbar system targeting sacral and lumbar regions), seat depth adjustment, 4D arms, tilt limiter with forward tilt, three sizes for precise fit (up to 350 lb capacity, Size C), 12-year warranty.[1][2][3][7]

**Lumbar support:** Precision-targeted lower back support via adjustable pads that hold position; ideal for known pain spots but less dynamic than competitors.[1][5]

**Pricing under $800:** New fully loaded models exceed $1,000 (e.g., $1,000+ from Herman Miller), but refurbished widely available and supported (seen as low as $711 on Amazon); check for deals.[1][3][7]

**User reviews and expert opinions:** 4.7/5 rating; best for upright sitters, hot users, long task sessions (coding/design); firm mesh seat comfortable long-term but initially stiff vs. cushioned; "most precise fit for height/weight extremes."[2][3][7] Experts at SeatedLab (2026) and ErgonomicChairHub (2026) rank it #1 overall; Creative Bloq praises reduced adjustments for supreme comfort/support after weeks of testing.[2][3][7]

## Steelcase Leap V2

**Ergonomic features:** LiveBack technology (flexing backrest sections follow spine movements), adjustable lumbar height/firmness, Natural Glide System (seat moves with recline), seat depth + edge flex, 4D arms with wide pivot, 5-position tilt limiter, 400 lb capacity, 12-year warranty.[1][3][4][5]

**Lumbar support:** Dynamic, continuous response without manual dial; superior overall back coverage and for chronic pain vs. Aeron's fixed precision.[1][3][5]

**Pricing under $800:** New $998-$1,299 fully loaded; refurbished $500-$800 (recommended by experts for value).[3][5]

**User reviews and expert opinions:** 4.6/5 rating; best for back pain, dynamic sitting, fidgeters; softer immediate seat comfort, high-density foam resists sagging; TechRadar (2026) calls it exceptional for long hours (6'2" reviewer confirms); SeatedLab (2026) top for back pain.[2][3][5] BTOD and OreataAI note chameleon-like spine mimicry.[4][6]

## Secretlab Titan Evo (2022 Series)

**Ergonomic features:** 4D armrests, 4-way magnetic lumbar system, 165-degree recline, cold-cure foam seat (holds shape better than sub-$700 rivals), three sizes (S/R/XL), 5-year warranty; hybrid gaming/office but lacks seat depth adjustment and true synchro-tilt.[3][6][7]

**Lumbar support:** Adjustable magnetic system; comfortable but not as ergonomically advanced as Aeron/Leap for extended office use.[3][6]

**Pricing under $800:** $519-$649 new (standard upholstery $549); fits budget fully.[3][6]

**User reviews and expert opinions:** 4.5/5 rating; best gaming/office hybrid, refined assembly/comfort (5-star from Creative Bloq 2025 re-review); soft seat uses full inches, strong build; BTOD (recent) names it top gaming chair exception vs. office premium.[3][6][7] SeatedLab (2026) recommends for work/gaming split; avoids typical gaming flaws like poor ergonomics but seat length fixed.[3][6]
