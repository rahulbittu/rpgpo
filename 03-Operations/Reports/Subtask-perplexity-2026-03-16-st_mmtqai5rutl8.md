## Finding 1: Engineering Budget Management Framework & Headcount Planning

**Key Practice: Cost Breakdown Structure (CBS) for Engineering Projects**

The most actionable framework comes from Rocketlane's 2026 guide on project expense tracking. Before project kickoff, structure engineering costs as:

**Phase → Work stream → Cost category → Budget → Owner**

For headcount planning specifically:
- Document assumptions in concrete terms: number of engineers, cost rates per role, estimated hours per phase, and external dependencies
- Separate base budget (defined scope), contingency (known risks), and management reserve (unknown risks) as three distinct layers
- Allocate budget to each phase deliberately before execution begins, not after invoices arrive
- Assign a single accountable owner per phase to prevent cost drift

**Why this matters:** Firms that fail to document headcount assumptions upfront see variance later become "opinion rather than analysis."[1]

Source: https://rocketlane.com/blogs/project-expense-tracking

## Finding 4: Program-Level Budget Management & Resource Optimization

**Multi-Project Budget Allocation:**

When managing multiple engineering projects under a program:

1. **Resource capacity planning** – Ensure sufficient headcount to staff projects and avoid unplanned hiring
2. **Skill-based allocation** – Assign resources based on skills, availability, and capacity to prevent bottlenecks
3. **Performance tracking** – Monitor whether engineers are becoming bottlenecks or underutilized
4. **Risk interdependencies** – Risks in one project can cascade; budget reserves must account for this[7]
