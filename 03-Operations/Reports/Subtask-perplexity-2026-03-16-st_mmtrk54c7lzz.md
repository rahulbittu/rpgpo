## Strength & Size Program by StrengthLog (Intermediate Focus, 6 Weeks)

This 6-week program targets intermediate to advanced lifters (6-12+ months experience) for building max strength and muscle size, using 4 gym days per week with progressive overload via increasing intensity (e.g., Week 1: Squat 100 lb x 10 reps; Week 2: 105 lb x 10 reps) or volume (e.g., Bench 135 lb x 8 reps to 9 reps, or add sets).[1]  
**Sample Workout 1 (Quads/Chest/Shoulders/Triceps/Calves):**  
| Exercise       | Sets | Reps |
|----------------|------|------|
| Squat          | 4    | 6    |
| Deadlift       | 3    | 6    |
| Bench Press    | 4    | 6    |
| Overhead Press | 4    | 6    |
| Bar Dip        | 4    | 6    |
| Seated Calf Raise | 4  | 10   |[1]

**Periodization:** Linear progression with weekly volume increases (higher working sets by week 6) and 1RM-based percentages for compounds like squat/bench/deadlift; track in StrengthLog app.[1]  
**Accessory Work:** Includes isolation like calf raises and dips alongside big lifts; full program has 4 workouts covering all major groups.[1]  
**Next Step:** Download StrengthLog app, test 1RMs, start Week 1 at 80-85% 1RM for 6 reps on compounds.

## Extreme Specialization for Deadlift (8 Weeks, StrongFirst)

For intermediates chasing deadlift PRs (key for 1000 lb total: e.g., 350+ squat, 400+ bench, 300+ deadlift), use 8-week deadlift specialization: train 4x/week (3 lower body deadlift-focused, 1 upper maintenance).[3]  
**Structure:** Cycle weights weekly from moderate to heavy; mix speed work, technique, block pulls; avoid failure; alternate hard/easy days.[3]  
**Periodization:** Cycling approach (e.g., Eddy Coan method: build weights progressively per cycle).[3]  
**Accessory Work:** Minimal on deadlift days (few assistance); upper day maintains bench/OHP.[3]  
**Next Step:** Baseline deadlift 1RM, Week 1: 70% 1RM x 5x5 speed sets 3x/week; log progress in app like Strong app.

## Barbell Medicine Progression Guidelines (Intermediate Transition)

15-week program transitions novices to intermediates with 3 blocks; use 6-8/10 effort (RPE) on 6-8 rep sets for squat/bench/deadlift at 1-5% weekly weight increases when warm-ups feel light.[2]  
**Periodization:** Gradual stimulus increase; buffer zone allows non-micromanaged progression.[2]

## 4-Day Upper/Lower Split (Warrior Addict, Beginner-Intermediate)

4-day split for intermediates: 2 upper, 2 lower with compounds; 3-4 sets 6-12 reps, progressive overload via weight/reps.[4]  
**Sample Lower Day:** Squat 4x6-8, Deadlift variant 3x6, Lunges 3x10/leg.[4]  
**Accessory Work:** Rows, presses, calf work post-compounds.[4]  
**Next Step:** Follow split Mon/Tue/Thu/Fri; increase weight 5 lb when hitting top reps.
