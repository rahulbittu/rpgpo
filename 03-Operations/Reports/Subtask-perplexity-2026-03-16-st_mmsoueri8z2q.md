## Distillery Tours
- **Maker’s Mark Distillery** (Loretto, KY): Offers tours of the 1,100-acre Star Hill Farm, covering bourbon-making from mash to bottle with tastings of signature and limited-edition bourbons. Historic site with rolling hills and red shutters; book early for Bourbon Trail visits.[1] Source: https://maplehillmanor.com/blog/2026/02/makers-mark-distillery-tour/
- **Buffalo Trace Distillery** (Frankfort, KY): Complimentary tours daily (reservations recommended); options include Trace Tour (75 min intro to history/science), Hard Hat Tour (105 min production), Old Taylor Tour (75 min historic landmarks), all with tastings. Walk-in tastings and gift shop available; check March 2026 calendar for highlights.[2] Source: https://www.buffalotracedistillery.com/visit-us/
- **Willett Distillery** (Bardstown, KY): In-depth small-batch tours; features The Bar at Willett, rated best restaurant/bar on Bourbon Trail.[1] Source: https://maplehillmanor.com/blog/2026/02/makers-mark-distillery-tour/
- **Preservation Distillery** (location on 40-acre historic tobacco farm): Intimate tours focus on 100% pot-distilled bourbon, sustainability, and rare vintages.[1] Source: https://maplehillmanor.com/blog/2026/02/makers-mark-distillery-tour/
- **Heaven Hill Springs Distillery** (Bardstown, KY): New $200M facility opened September 2025; offers Heritage Rising Tour (small-group) after first barrels filled April 2025.[3] Source: https://www.kentuckytourism.com/media/docs/default-source/media-documents/road-trip-ideas/what's-new-in-ky-bourbon_march-2026.pdf?sfvrsn=78198b4d_0
- **Heaven Hill Bernheim Distillery** (Louisville, KY): Tours and tastings at largest independent family-owned distillery (since 1935); produces all major American whiskey styles with 5 mashbills.[6] Source: https://heavenhilldistillery.com/hhb-experience.php
