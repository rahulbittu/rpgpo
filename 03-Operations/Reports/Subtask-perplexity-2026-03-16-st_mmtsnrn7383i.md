## Finding 1: Extreme Specialization for Deadlift (8-Week Cycle)
Andy Bolton outlines an **extreme specialization** periodization technique adapted from Bulgarian methods for powerlifters, ideal for recreational athletes focusing on one lift like the deadlift. Key protocol: Train 4 days/week for 8 weeks—3 lower body sessions emphasizing deadlift (with squat one day and assistance), 1 upper body maintenance session; incorporate cycling (build weights weekly like Eddy Coan), speed work, technique practice, block pulls; avoid failure with harder/easier days. This builds strength without overtraining, suitable for non-elite lifters.  
Source: https://www.strongfirst.com/the-right-way-to-get-strong/

## Finding 2: Progressive Overload via Volume and Frequency Increases
For safe periodization in powerlifting, increase volume (more sets/reps) to build work capacity and hypertrophy, or add frequency for better movement practice—directly applicable to recreational athletes avoiding injury. No specific cycle length or numbers provided, but emphasizes gradual progression over linear loading.  
Source: https://fickptandperformance.com/powerlifting-safe-progressive-overload-strategies/

## Finding 3: Westside Barbell Conjugate Method
The **Conjugate Method** uses periodization by rotating maximal effort, dynamic effort, and repetition methods weekly to develop multiple strengths simultaneously, preventing accommodation—works for recreational powerlifters via weekly variation in exercises/intensities. No exact cycle durations or rep schemes detailed in excerpt.  
Source: https://www.westside-barbell.com/a/blog/training-specificity
