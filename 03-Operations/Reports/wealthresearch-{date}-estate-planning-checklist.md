## Key Findings

1. **Wills**: Essential for asset distribution and guardianship decisions. Tech workers should integrate wills with revocable living trusts and review them in light of upcoming legislative changes like the "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" (OBBBA), which affects estate tax exemptions.
   
2. **Trusts**: Revocable living trusts are crucial for avoiding probate and managing assets during incapacity. They are particularly beneficial for tech workers with stock portfolios and RSUs, aligning with increased GST tax exemptions under OBBBA.

3. **Beneficiary Designations**: These take precedence over wills for retirement accounts and life insurance policies. It's important to ensure they are up-to-date and reflect current wishes.

4. **Digital Assets**: Tech workers should inventory digital assets and ensure access instructions are clear. This includes cryptocurrencies, online accounts, and intellectual property.

5. **Power of Attorney**: Establishing durable power of attorney is critical for managing financial and healthcare decisions in case of incapacity.

## Detailed Analysis

### Wills
- **Purpose**: Directs asset distribution and appoints guardians for minors.
- **Integration**: Should be integrated with living trusts to ensure seamless management during incapacity.
- **Legislative Impact**: OBBBA increases estate tax exemptions, necessitating will reviews to optimize tax benefits.

### Trusts
- **Functionality**: Avoids probate, maintains privacy, and manages assets during incapacity.
- **Financial Strategy**: Particularly useful for managing tech equity like RSUs and appreciating stock portfolios.
- **Legislative Impact**: Aligns with increased GST tax exemptions, facilitating larger wealth transfers.

### Beneficiary Designations
- **Priority**: Overrides wills, making it crucial to keep designations current.
- **Accounts**: Includes 401(k), IRA, and life insurance policies.

### Digital Assets
- **Inventory**: Includes cryptocurrencies, online accounts, and intellectual property.
- **Access**: Ensure instructions for accessing these assets are clear and legally documented.

### Power of Attorney
- **Types**: Durable power of attorney for financial and healthcare decisions.
- **Importance**: Ensures decisions can be made if incapacitated.

## Recommended Actions

1. **Draft or Update Wills**: 
   - **What to Do**: Consult an estate attorney to draft or update your will.
   - **Why**: Ensures your assets are distributed according to your wishes and integrates with trusts for incapacity protection.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Legal clarity and protection for your beneficiaries.
   - **First Step**: Schedule a consultation with an estate attorney; costs range from $300-$1,200.

2. **Establish a Revocable Living Trust**:
   - **What to Do**: Create a trust to manage assets and avoid probate.
   - **Why**: Protects privacy and ensures asset management during incapacity.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Streamlined asset management and transfer.
   - **First Step**: Consult a financial advisor to integrate tech equity; setup costs $1,500-$2,500.

3. **Review and Update Beneficiary Designations**:
   - **What to Do**: Ensure all beneficiary designations are current.
   - **Why**: These designations override wills and are critical for retirement accounts and insurance.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Accurate reflection of your current wishes.
   - **First Step**: Contact your financial institutions to verify and update designations.

4. **Inventory and Secure Digital Assets**:
   - **What to Do**: Create a comprehensive list of digital assets with access instructions.
   - **Why**: Ensures these assets are not lost and can be managed or transferred.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Secure and accessible digital asset management.
   - **First Step**: Use a digital asset management tool to document and store access information.

5. **Establish Durable Power of Attorney**:
   - **What to Do**: Appoint a durable power of attorney for financial and healthcare decisions.
   - **Why**: Ensures decisions can be made on your behalf if incapacitated.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Legal empowerment for trusted individuals to manage your affairs.
   - **First Step**: Consult with an attorney to draft the necessary documents.
