## Finding 1: 20-20-20 Rule Definition and Core Implementation for Screen Workers
The **20-20-20 rule** requires looking away from screens every **20 minutes** to an object **20 feet** away for at least **20 seconds** to relax eye focusing muscles and reduce digital eye strain. For screen-heavy workers (e.g., 8+ hours daily), implement via timers or apps during meetings and tasks; University Hospitals recommends this for every 20 minutes of near work, including computers, with conscious blinking to combat reduced blink rates causing dry eyes after 2-5 hours.
- **Actionable steps**: Set phone alarms every 20 minutes; during virtual meetings, gaze at a distant window or object; combine with well-lit workspaces to cut glare.

## Finding 2: Evidence from 2020 Study on Dry Eyes in High-Screen Workers
A **2020 study** links over **8 hours** daily screen exposure to symptomatic dry eyes, with risks starting after **2 hours** uninterrupted and worsening after **5 hours**; adults in screen-heavy roles (10+ hours) should cap total time under **7 hours** or use 20-20-20 breaks. Elevate Black Health advises this for office workers translating complex topics, pairing with outdoor breaks for eye development.
- **Actionable steps**: Track daily screen time via device settings; for 10-hour days, insert 20-20-20 every cycle (adds ~1 hour breaks); limit to <7 hours by batching tasks.
Source: https://www.elevateblackhealth.com/digital-eye-strain-and-screen-use-in-black-families/[1]
