## Finding 1: BCG's Agentic AI Build-vs-Buy Framework (2026)
BCG outlines a build-vs-buy decision for agentic AI platforms, emphasizing assessment of core competencies and long-term goals over technology hype. Key evaluation criteria include: strategic impact (end-to-end transformation vs. modular services), proven ROI (60% of providers still use time/materials or fixed-price contracts despite buyer demand for outcome-linked models), and portfolio differentiation (mix full-stack industry solutions with horizontal agentic tools). TCO analysis focuses on reimagining portfolios for $200B opportunity, prioritizing demonstrable outcomes like validated proofs-of-concept over promises. No explicit decision tree, but recommends anchoring in measurable returns.
Source: https://www.bcg.com/publications/2026/the-200-billion-dollar-ai-opportunity-in-tech-services

## Finding 2: ITONICS 7-Step Technology Strategy Process with Prioritization Framework
ITONICS provides a **seven-step process** for technology decisions (Exhibit 2), including mapping current systems/legacy debt, emerging tech (AI/ML/edge), and future needs; then governance and resource allocation. Evaluation criteria: **strategic impact** (alignment to objectives), **value timing** (quick wins vs. long-term), **resource efficiency** (ROI relative to cost/complexity), **risk profile**, **business impact** (measurable outcomes), **execution feasibility**. TCO implied via technical debt gaps and finite budgets; prioritization uses scoring for 40% faster decisions and 30% better ROI. No decision tree, but explicit frameworks for investment ranking.
Source: https://www.itonics-innovation.com/blog/technology-strategy-guide

## Finding 3: WeWeb Enterprise App TCO Criteria (2026 Guide)
WeWeb details **Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)** analysis for buy decisions in enterprise apps: include initial licensing, long-term maintenance, infrastructure, and development talent costs beyond upfront fees. Evaluation criteria: **flexibility** (customizable to business processes), **development speed** (faster deployment for quicker ROI), scalability/security. Recommends low-code platforms to reduce TCO; no decision tree or full build-vs-buy matrix.
Source: https://www.weweb.io/blog/enterprise-application-development-practical-guide

## Finding 4: Riseup Labs Decision Flowchart for Methodologies (2026)
Riseup Labs offers a **5-step decision flowchart** (text-based) adaptable to build-vs-buy: 1) Clear unchanging requirements? → Waterfall/V-Model/Incremental (favor buy fixed-price). 2) Need fast feedback? → Agile/Scrum/Kanban/Lean (favor build T&M). 3) Enterprise scale? → SAFe/LeSS/RUP. 4) Security/compliance? → DevSecOps. 5) Continuous delivery? → DevOps/Kanban. Criteria matrix by industry (regulated: Waterfall), risk tolerance (high risk: Spiral), team experience. TCO via time-to-market and risk; hybrids for 2026 trends.
Source: https://riseuplabs.com/software-development-methodologies/
