## Yogurt Shop Murders Case (Austin, Texas, 1991)

Four teenage girls—Amy Ayers, Eliza Thomas, Jennifer Harbison, and Sarah Harbison—were murdered and the yogurt shop set ablaze on December 6, 1991. Maurice Pierce, Robert Springsteen IV, Michael Scott, and Forrest Welborn were convicted based on false confessions; Springsteen and Scott's convictions were overturned in 2009 after initial DNA advances identified another male suspect (not fully exonerating them at the time).[3][4]

**Exoneration Details**: On March 2026 (exact court date not specified in sources), a Texas judge declared Springsteen, Scott, and Pierce innocent after 34+ years, clearing their names and enabling potential restitution claims. Welborn had died in prison in 2023.[3][4]

**Next Steps from Case**: Families pursued civil suits for compensation; genetic genealogists recommend re-testing cold cases with similar tech for familial matches.[3][4]

## Broader DNA Exoneration Trends (U.S. Nationwide and Missouri)

DNA testing has overturned 560 wrongful convictions since 2000, including 14 in Missouri (last in 2020); advanced tests now detect trace genetic material missed by older methods.[2]

**Technology Used**: Next-generation sensitive DNA analysis (e.g., detecting prosecution team contamination on weapons, absent defendant DNA on scenes).[2]
