## Cost-Saving Strategies

- **Plan expenses in advance using sinking funds**: Save small amounts monthly for predictable large costs like home repairs or HOA fees; for $500 annual dues over 6 months, allocate $83/month or $21/week to avoid credit card debt. Use apps like YNAB for tracking. Source: https://moneyguy.com/article/6-financial-changes-to-make-in-2026/[1]; https://www.nerdwallet.com/finance/learn/nerdwallet-sinking-fund-savings[3]
- **Pay off high-interest debt first (Avalanche method)**: Prioritize highest-interest debt after covering insurance deductibles and employer retirement matches; track exact balances and budget aggressively. Alternative: Snowball method for smallest debts first for motivation. Source: https://moneyguy.com/article/6-financial-changes-to-make-in-2026/[1]; https://theavocadofoundation.org/2026-financial-blueprint-money-management/[4]
- **Build emergency fund of 3-6 months expenses**: Automate savings as first "expense" on payday; redirect recurring small spends (e.g., coffee) to fund without lifestyle cuts. Source: https://www.allgenfinancial.com/2026-financial-checklist/[2]; https://theavocadofoundation.org/2026-financial-blueprint-money-management/[4]; https://lendedu.com/blog/how-to-save-money/[5]
- **Review and align budget annually**: Compare income streams (wages, side projects) vs. fixed/discretionary expenses; redirect savings to priorities like retirement or charity. Source: https://www.allgenfinancial.com/2026-financial-checklist/[2]
- **Consolidate accounts and automate**: Merge forgotten retirement accounts; use multiple savings buckets in one bank account for organization. Source: https://moneyguy.com/article/6-financial-changes-to-make-in-2026/[1]; https://www.nerdwallet.com/finance/learn/nerdwallet-sinking-fund-savings[3]
