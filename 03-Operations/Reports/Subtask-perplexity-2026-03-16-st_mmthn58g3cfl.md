## Finding 1: Magnesium Supplementation for Migraine Prevention

**Specific Data:** Magnesium supplementation at **400-500 mg daily** has demonstrated efficacy in reducing migraine frequency. A meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials found that magnesium reduced migraine frequency by approximately **20-25%** compared to placebo, with the most robust evidence for **magnesium glycinate** formulations due to superior absorption.[1]

**Mechanism:** Magnesium regulates neurotransmitter release and vascular tone; deficiency is implicated in migraine pathophysiology, particularly in patients with aura.

**Evidence Quality:** Moderate-to-strong evidence from multiple RCTs; the American Headache Society acknowledges magnesium as a reasonable preventive option, though it ranks below first-line pharmaceutical agents.

Source: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6042192/

## Finding 2: Riboflavin (Vitamin B2) for Migraine Prevention

**Mechanism:** B2 supports mitochondrial energy production; migraineurs often exhibit mitochondrial dysfunction.

**Timeline:** Requires 2-3 months of consistent use before efficacy is apparent.

Source: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25540322

## Finding 4: Migraine Trigger Identification Best Practices

**Systematic Approach:** Current clinical guidelines recommend a **structured trigger diary** tracking:
- **Timing:** Date, time of onset, duration
- **Triggers:** Food/drink consumed 24 hours prior, sleep patterns, stress levels, hormonal factors, weather changes, sensory exposures
- **Attack characteristics:** Pain intensity (0-10 scale), associated symptoms, medication used, response

**Duration:** Minimum **4-8 weeks** of consistent tracking to identify patterns; many patients require 12 weeks for reliable pattern recognition.[4]

**Common Identified Triggers (in order of frequency):**
- Stress and stress relief (75-80% of patients)
- Hormonal changes (65% of women)
- Sleep disruption (59%)
- Skipped meals (57%)
- Weather changes (53%)
- Caffeine withdrawal (40%)
- Specific foods (27%, most common: aged cheeses, processed meats, alcohol)

**Technology:** Digital migraine tracking apps (e.g., Migraine Buddy, Headache Coach) improve compliance compared to paper diaries and enable pattern analysis.

Source: https://headaches.org/understanding-migraine/migraine-triggers/
