## Finding 1: Multi-Pronged, Periodized Recovery Framework for Athletes
Jason Pullara, MSCC, Purdue University Director of Strength & Conditioning, outlines a recovery model with three principles: (1) recovery pyramid (sleep and nutrition as base), (2) specificity to fatigue type, and (3) periodization across training phases. During off-season, prioritize sleep consistency and fueling for high-volume training; use modalities sparingly to avoid blunting adaptations. Specificity matches strategies to stress (e.g., not all fatigue needs the same intervention). Published Feb 27, 2026.  
**Source:** https://training-conditioning.com/article/multi-pronged-approach-to-athlete-recovery/

## Finding 2: Plyometric Training Recovery with Staged Progression
2025–2026 systematic reviews show plyometric benefits for sprint speed and power (6–12 weeks, 2–3 sessions/week with progressive overload). Post-sprint recovery uses staged plyo progression: Stage 1 (stabilization, e.g., box jumps focusing on landing stick), Stage 2 (strength, e.g., squat jumps), Stage 3 (power/SSC, e.g., depth jumps minimizing ground contact). For explosive/sprint sessions, include 3 minutes active rest in warm-up/recovery protocols (total ~13 minutes).  
**Source:** https://drdidwal.com/jump-higher-run-faster-recover-smarter-the-2026-science-of-plyometric-training  
**Additional Source:** https://heatrick.com/2026/02/27/warm-ups-for-workouts-one-protocol-for-every-cardio-session/

## Finding 3: Sprint-Specific Mental Recovery via Movement Variability
Flinders University study (published 2026 in *Sports Medicine*) on sprint coordination recommends post-fatigue recovery through variable training environments, not repetitive drills. Coaches adjust constraints (e.g., hurdle spacing, surfaces, rhythm) to foster self-organization and adaptability, reducing mental fatigue from rigid models. Emphasizes individuality: match recovery to athlete's structure, strength, and rhythm for resilient performance. Led by Dr. Dylan Hicks.  
**Source:** https://medicalxpress.com/news/2026-02-sprinting-science-rewrites-rulebook.html
