## Implementation Steps

### 1. Tool Storage

- **Pegboards for Lightweight Tools**
  - **What to Do**: Install pegboards on walls above workbenches or near doors.
  - **Why**: Keeps tools visible and accessible without occupying floor space.
  - **Expected Outcome**: Improved organization and accessibility.
  - **First Step**: Purchase pegboards and customizable hooks from a local hardware store or online.

- **Magnetic Strips for Metal Tools**
  - **What to Do**: Mount magnetic strips above or beside workbenches.
  - **Why**: Eliminates the need to search through drawers for frequently used tools.
  - **Expected Outcome**: Increased efficiency in tool retrieval.
  - **First Step**: Order magnetic strips from Amazon or a local supplier.

- **PVC Pipes for Long-handled Tools**
  - **What to Do**: Cut and mount PVC pipes horizontally on walls.
  - **Why**: Provides a secure, low-cost storage solution for long tools.
  - **Expected Outcome**: Neat storage of brooms, rakes, and similar items.
  - **First Step**: Purchase PVC pipes and necessary mounting hardware.

- **Wall-mounted Power Tool Racks**
  - **What to Do**: Install Anqtovp 2-Pack 66-inch wide steel racks.
  - **Why**: Heavy-duty organization for power tools with high capacity.
  - **Expected Outcome**: Safe and organized storage for power tools.
  - **First Step**: Buy racks from Lowe's or similar retailers.

- **Modular Panel Systems**
  - **What to Do**: Use snap-together panels with hooks and bins.
  - **Why**: Provides flexibility and easy assembly for tool organization.
  - **Expected Outcome**: Customizable storage solutions.
  - **First Step**: Order panels from Home Depot.

- **Toolboxes and Carts**
  - **What to Do**: Invest in portable toolboxes and carts.
  - **Why**: Offers mobility and additional storage.
  - **Expected Outcome**: Enhanced organization and tool mobility.
  - **First Step**: Explore options from Eastwood or similar brands.

- **Wood Pallets for Yard Tools**
  - **What to Do**: Anchor pallets vertically for storage.
  - **Why**: Utilizes vertical space effectively.
  - **Expected Outcome**: Organized storage for yard tools.
  - **First Step**: Acquire pallets from local sources or online.

### 2. Workbench Design

- **Central or Wall Placement**
  - **What to Do**: Position workbenches centrally or against walls.
  - **Why**: Maximizes workspace and accessibility.
  - **Expected Outcome**: Efficient use of space and improved workflow.
  - **First Step**: Measure garage space to determine optimal bench placement.

- **Surrounding Cabinets**
  - **What to Do**: Install cabinets around workbenches.
  - **Why**: Provides additional storage and organization.
  - **Expected Outcome**: Increased storage capacity.
  - **First Step**: Plan cabinet layout and purchase materials.

### 3. Lighting

- **LED Lighting Installation**
  - **What to Do**: Install LED lights for bright, energy-efficient lighting.
  - **Why**: Ensures adequate illumination for detailed work.
  - **Expected Outcome**: Improved visibility and reduced energy costs.
  - **First Step**: Select LED fixtures and plan installation points.

### 4. Electrical Layout

- **Strategic Outlet Placement**
  - **What to Do**: Install outlets near workbenches and tool storage areas.
  - **Why**: Provides convenient power access for tools and equipment.
  - **Expected Outcome**: Reduced need for extension cords and improved safety.
  - **First Step**: Consult an electrician to plan and install outlets.
