## Finding 1: Daylight Saving Time Change (Spring Forward) as Key Trigger for Emergency Kit Checks
Multiple U.S. local and state agencies recommend using the **Daylight Saving Time "spring forward" change** (March 8-9, 2026) to **check smoke alarms, review fire escape plans, and update emergency kits** for items like water, food, batteries, flashlights, and first aid supplies. This aligns with spring hazard prep for floods, thunderstorms, or tsunamis.[2][5]  
**Source:** https://www.westminster-ca.gov/Home/Components/News/News/1001/[2]; https://www.wispolitics.com/2026/readywisconsin-spring-into-action-use-daylight-saving-time-to-refresh-emergency-preparedness-plans/?pdf=407775[5]

## Finding 2: Severe Weather Preparedness Week (First Week of March) for Kit Reviews
North Carolina DPS designates the **first week of March** (e.g., March 1-7, 2026) as **Severe Weather Preparedness Week**, urging households to **gather and review emergency supplies** like flashlights and first aid kits as part of a 4-step plan; practice via statewide tornado drill on Day 3 (March 4, 2026, at 9:30 a.m.).[1]  
**Source:** https://www.ncdps.gov/blog/2026/02/27/severe-weather-preparedness-week[1]

## Finding 3: Tsunami Awareness Month (March) for Kit Updates
City of Westminster, CA, highlights **March as Tsunami Awareness Month** (bulletin dated March 4, 2026), advising residents to **update emergency kits** alongside reviewing plans and signing up for tsunami alerts during the spring forward on March 8, 2026.[2]  
**Source:** https://www.westminster-ca.gov/Home/Components/News/News/1001/[2]

## Finding 4: General Kit Refresh Recommendations (No Specific Intervals Found)
No sources specify exact intervals like "every 6 months" beyond seasonal triggers; Florida Disaster and Ready for Wildfire emphasize assembling/checking **3-day supplies** (1 gallon water/person/day, non-perishables, first aid) but tie reviews to events like hurricanes, not fixed schedules. Yuma County echoes 3-day minimums without timelines.[3][4][7]
