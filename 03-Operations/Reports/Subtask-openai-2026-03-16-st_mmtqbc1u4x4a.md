## Detailed Analysis

### Access Point Placement & Coverage
Proper placement of your router is crucial. Central and elevated positioning avoids physical obstructions like walls and furniture, which can weaken signals. For larger homes, mesh systems like Netgear Orbi 770 and Eero Max 7 are effective in covering extensive areas without dead zones[4]. Powerline networking kits are an alternative for areas where Wi-Fi signals struggle to reach[1].

### Channel & Frequency Selection
The 5 GHz band is recommended for remote work due to its higher speed capabilities and lower interference levels. Although 2.4 GHz can penetrate walls better, it is more prone to interference from other devices. Configuring devices to use 5 GHz can enhance performance significantly[2].

### Quality of Service (QoS) Configuration
QoS settings allow you to prioritize bandwidth for specific applications, ensuring that work-related tasks receive the necessary resources. This is particularly useful for video conferencing and cloud-based applications that require stable connections.

### Mesh vs. Router Comparison
Mesh systems offer superior coverage for larger homes by using multiple nodes to create a seamless network. Traditional routers may suffice for smaller spaces but often require additional access points to cover larger areas effectively[4].

### Troubleshooting Common Problems
Interference can often be resolved by changing the Wi-Fi channel. Connection drops might be due to outdated firmware, which can be resolved through updates. Consistent slow speeds might indicate a need for a higher bandwidth plan or additional network infrastructure.
