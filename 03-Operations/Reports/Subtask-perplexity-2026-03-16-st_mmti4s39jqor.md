## Finding 1: LED Lighting Technology & Energy Savings

**LED fixtures reduce energy consumption by 40-60% compared to traditional fluorescent systems** while delivering superior light quality.[2] Key advantages include:

- **Longer lifespan** with reduced maintenance costs
- **Lower heat generation**, reducing HVAC system load[2]
- **Tunable color temperature** (typically 3000K–6500K range) to support circadian rhythms and task-specific needs[1]
- **High CRI (Color Rendering Index) bulbs** for accurate color accuracy, especially important for detailed visual work[1]

**Practical specification for home offices:** A compact setup combines overhead LED panels at **4000K color temperature** with a **high-CRI adjustable desk lamp** to reduce eye strain while maintaining general visibility.[1]

## Finding 3: Smart Lighting Controls & Energy Optimization

Smart lighting systems achieve energy efficiency through:

- **Occupancy sensors** that eliminate wasted energy in unused spaces
- **Daylight harvesting** to reduce artificial lighting when natural light is available[2]
- **Tunable smart bulbs** that adjust color temperature throughout the day (cooler 5000K+ in morning for focus; warmer 3000K in evening for relaxation)[5]
- **Smart scenes** that adjust multiple lights simultaneously for different work modes[5]
