## Thoracic Spine Mobility Drills
Seated shoulder rolls release neck/upper back tension: lift shoulders to ears, roll back, drop down; repeat 10 times with breath (inhale up, exhale down).[1]  
Seated chest opener counters rounded posture: clasp hands behind back (or chair sides), lift arms to open chest.[1]  
Controlled thoracic extension exercises, bodyweight rows, and scapular push-up variations target tight upper back from desk work (perform with intent, not just as warmup).[3]  
Source: https://thespinepro.com/blog/desk-friendly-stretches-for-back-pain-relief/[1]; https://purecalisthenics.com/tight-upper-back-desk-work-bodyweight-fix/[3]  

## Hip Flexor Recovery Exercises
Seated hip flexor stretch: scoot to chair edge, extend one leg back slightly, keep chest upright to stretch front hip.[1]  
Seated figure-four stretch: cross right ankle over left knee (ankle flexed), lean forward to stretch right hip/glute; switch sides.[1]  
08:20 Hip flexor stretch and 10:20 Fig 4 in 15-min routine.[2]  
Source: https://thespinepro.com/blog/desk-friendly-stretches-for-back-pain-relief/[1]; https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZHTQpZ_jCQ8[2]  

## Wrist Health for Heavy Typists
Wrist rolls → Fists to starfish (12:20 in routine): roll wrists, transition fists to open hands for mobility.[2]  
Short workday movement bouts reduce discomfort; integrate with 10-15 min stretches 3-5x/week.[1]  
Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZHTQpZ_jCQ8[2]; https://thespinepro.com/blog/desk-friendly-stretches-for-back-pain-relief/[1]
