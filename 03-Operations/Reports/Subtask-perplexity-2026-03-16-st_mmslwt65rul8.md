## Insulation Assessment Standards
- **R-value targets**: Attics should reach **R-30+** (10-15 inches of fiberglass or foam insulation); basement/crawl space floors need at least **R-25**. Check by inspecting attic joists—if visible, insulation is insufficient.[5]
- **Blower door test**: Gold standard using a fan in the door frame to depressurize the home, paired with infrared cameras to detect air leaks (shown as purple/blue streaks) and missing insulation.[5]
- **DOE Home Energy Score**: On-site evaluation measures insulation levels as part of a 1-10 score assessing permanent features like walls and roofs.[4]

## HVAC Efficiency Best Practices
- **Filter replacement**: Inspect furnace filters; replace if gray/fuzzy to avoid overworking the system.[6]
- **Efficiency ratings for upgrades**: Require **SEER, HSPF, AFUE** ratings; central AC, furnaces, boilers, and water heaters must meet **Energy Star** standards. Heat pumps need **ENERGY STAR Most Efficient** certification (2025).[1][3]
- **Water heater settings**: Set thermostat to **120°F** (not default 140°F) to reduce heat loss; natural gas/propane/oil models qualify under prior tax rules with Energy Star.[1][6]
- **Duct sealing**: Use mastic on ducts during audits.[5]

## Appliance Energy Ratings
- **Energy Star certification**: Mandatory for qualifying upgrades like water heaters (50% solar-derived for solar models), furnaces, and boilers. Check labels for **ENERGY STAR Most Efficient** on heat pumps.[1][3]
- **Phantom loads**: Audit appliances for standby power; use power strips or smart plugs to cut waste.[5]
- **Battery storage**: Minimum **3 kWh** capacity for tax-eligible systems (2025).[3]

## Smart Home Automation for Energy Savings
- **Real-time integration**: DOE Home Energy Score future updates may link with smart devices for dynamic consumption monitoring and behavior-based recommendations.[4]
- **Smart plugs**: Recommended for appliance phantom loads during DIY audits.[5]
