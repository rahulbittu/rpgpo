## Finding 1: US Primary Residence Exclusion for Foreign Real Estate Gains
US investors can exclude up to **$250,000** of capital gain tax-free on foreign property sales if it qualifies as their primary residence under Section 121, or **$500,000** for married couples filing jointly; this applies to sales in 2026 filed in 2027.[1]

## Finding 2: Tax Treaties Confirm Source Country Taxation on Real Estate Gains with US Saving Clause
US tax treaties (e.g., via IRS Publication 901) often allow the property's location country to tax real estate gains under Article 13, but US "saving clauses" preserve US taxation rights for citizens/residents, limiting relief to Foreign Tax Credit via **Form 1116** rather than full exemption.[1]  
States typically ignore treaties, taxing gains independently.[1]  
File **Form 8833** to disclose treaty positions overriding US rules; always report gains in USD.[1]
