## Finding 1: Coconino National Forest — Dark Sky Viewing Areas

**Location:** Multiple designated areas within Coconino National Forest surrounding Sedona

**Accessibility:** 
- Forest Road 525 (near Schnebly Hill Road) — paved access, pullouts available
- Forest Road 152 (Oak Creek Canyon rim) — gravel road, high-clearance vehicles recommended
- Parking areas typically open dawn to dusk; dispersed camping allowed in designated zones

**Special Features:**
- Minimal light pollution compared to Sedona town center
- Elevation 5,000–7,000 feet reduces atmospheric interference
- Clear views of Milky Way core (best April–October)
- No entrance fees; free public access

**Best Viewing Windows:** March–May and September–November (lowest humidity, clearest skies)

Source: https://www.fs.usda.gov/coconino (Coconino National Forest official site)

## Finding 3: Dead Horse Ranch State Park — Accessible Stargazing

**Location:** 6300 Dead Horse Ranch Road, Cottonwood, AZ 86326 (~20 miles north of Sedona)

**Accessibility:**
- Paved parking lot, wheelchair-accessible facilities
- Open year-round; day-use $7/vehicle, camping available ($35–$50/night)
- Minimal ambient light; elevation ~3,300 feet

**Special Features:**
- Verde River riparian zone provides scenic backdrop
- Designated camping areas with dark-sky orientation
- Ranger-led astronomy programs (seasonal; check park calendar)
- Picnic areas, restrooms, water access

**Best For:** Families, RV camping, accessible stargazing without high-elevation hiking

Source: https://azstateparks.com/dead-horse-ranch (Arizona State Parks)

## Finding 4: Sycamore Creek Overlook — High-Elevation Dark Sky Site

**Location:** Forest Road 525 near Schnebly Hill, elevation ~6,500 feet

**Accessibility:**
- Paved road (Schnebly Hill Road) to overlook; pullout parking
- 15-minute drive from Sedona town center
- No facilities (bring water, supplies)

**Special Features:**
- Panoramic views of Sedona red rocks + night sky
- Extremely low light pollution (Bortle Scale 3–4)
- Milky Way core visible June–September
- Popular with astrophotography groups

**Caution:** Road may close seasonally (winter snow); verify conditions via Coconino National Forest

Source: https://www.fs.usda.gov/coconino (Forest Service road conditions)
