## Plan

### Objective
Develop a routine to effectively relieve neck tension using self-massage, stretching, and breathing techniques.

### Components
1. **Self-Massage Techniques**
   - Suboccipital Release
   - Upper Trapezius and Levator Scapulae Compression

2. **Stretch Sequences**
   - Chin Tucks
   - Ear-to-Shoulder Stretches
   - Gentle Neck Rotations

3. **Breathing Integration**
   - Deep Breathing Techniques

4. **Daily Micro-Routines**
   - Quick exercises for daily integration

## Implementation Steps

### Self-Massage Techniques
1. **Suboccipital Release**
   - Lie on your back with a therapy ball at the base of your skull.
   - Rest your head fully on the tool for 60-90 seconds.
   - Slowly turn your head left and right while maintaining pressure.

2. **Upper Trapezius and Levator Scapulae Compression**
   - Stand against a wall with a therapy ball between your upper shoulder and the wall.
   - Lean in to control pressure, holding tender spots for 30-60 seconds.
   - Move the ball slightly and repeat, angling toward the spine for levator scapulae.

### Stretch Sequences
- Perform stretches immediately after self-massage.
- **Chin Tucks**: Gently pull your chin back to align the neck.
- **Ear-to-Shoulder Stretches**: Tilt your ear toward your shoulder slowly.
- **Gentle Neck Rotations**: Rotate your head side to side without force.

### Breathing Integration
- Incorporate deep breathing during stretches:
  - Inhale deeply through the nose, hold for a few seconds, and exhale slowly.
  - Focus on relaxing the neck and shoulders with each breath.

### Daily Micro-Routines
- **Morning Routine**: Perform a quick 5-minute routine combining chin tucks and ear-to-shoulder stretches.
- **Work Breaks**: Use therapy ball for quick suboccipital release during breaks.
- **Evening Wind Down**: Combine gentle neck rotations with deep breathing before bed.

## Tools/Resources
- Therapy balls or peanut tools for self-massage (cost-effective, under a single massage session).
- Access to a quiet space for uninterrupted practice.

## Review Schedule
- **Weekly Review**: Assess tension levels and adjust routine intensity.
- **Monthly Check-In**: Evaluate overall neck mobility and pain reduction.
- **Feedback Loop**: Record any persistent soreness or discomfort to adjust technique or frequency.

By following this structured routine, you can expect improved blood circulation, reduced tension, and increased range of motion. Begin with the self-massage techniques and integrate stretches and breathing exercises to maximize effectiveness.
