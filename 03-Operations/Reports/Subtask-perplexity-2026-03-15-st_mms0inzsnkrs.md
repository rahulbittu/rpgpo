## OSHA Guidelines Overview
OSHA does not publish highly specific desk ergonomics standards but emphasizes neutral body postures for comfortable seated work to prevent musculoskeletal disorders (MSDs), with general guidelines referencing standards like ANSI/HFES 100 and ISO 14738 for workstation dimensions.[1][2]

## Key Ergonomic Recommendations for Developers' Desks
Developers spending long hours coding benefit from adjustable setups to maintain elbow at 90° angles, reduce eye strain from multiple screens, and alternate sitting/standing. Core specs from OSHA-aligned standards:

| Criteria | Recommendation | Rationale for Developers |
|----------|----------------|--------------------------|
| Desk Depth | Min 32 in / 80 cm (44 in / 110 cm for >2 screens) | Fits monitors, keyboard, mouse without crowding.[1] |
| Desk Width | Min 63 in / 160 cm (71 in / 180 cm recommended) | Space for dual monitors common in coding.[1] |
| Desk Height (Fixed) | 29 in / 72 cm | Standard for typing without shoulder hike.[1] |
| Desk Height (Adjustable Sit-Stand) | 26-49 in / 65-125 cm | Switch positions every 30-60 min to cut MSD risk by 65%.[1][3] |
| Screen Distance | 20-28 in / 50-70 cm | Reduces neck strain during code reviews.[1] |
| Screen Position | Top edge at eye level | Prevents forward head posture from prolonged screen time.[1] |

**Source for table:** https://kwesk.com/en/blog/ergonomie/ergonomie-poste-de-travail [1]

## Practical Checklist for Developers' Workstations
Audit your setup weekly using this OSHA-informed list to hit neutral postures:

- **Chair:** Height adjustable (feet flat), lumbar support, armrests adjustable, seat depth with 2-3 finger gap at knee back.[1]
- **Desk:** Elbows at 90° typing, legroom clear, surface for keyboard/mouse away from edge.[1]
- **Screen/Monitor:** No glare, brightness matched to room, single/dual setup at arm's length.[1]
- **Additional for Developers:** Use headset for calls (not handheld), external keyboard if laptop to optimize posture.[3]
- **Movement:** Stand hourly, stretch wrists/shoulders to counter repetitive strain from typing/mousing.[1][3]
