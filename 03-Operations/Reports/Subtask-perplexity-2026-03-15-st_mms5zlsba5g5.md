## Sound Absorption vs Diffusion
**Absorption** uses porous materials like fiberglass, mineral wool, or open-cell foam to convert sound energy to heat, primarily effective for mid-to-high frequencies (500 Hz+ for 2-inch panels, 250 Hz+ for 4-inch); low frequencies (<250 Hz) need bass traps.[2] **Diffusion** scatters sound waves evenly to reduce echoes without deadening the space, often via irregular surfaces like wood slats; less emphasized in results but paired with absorption for balanced offices.[1][2] Absorption dominates office treatments for noise reduction, with NRC ratings ≥0.80 recommended (cheap foam often ≤0.50).[1]

## DIY vs Commercial Options
- **DIY**: Basic foam panels ($20–60/panel, low NRC for home studios); heavy soft decoration like thick curtains, upholstered artwork, or layered fabric—quick, no-construction install for home offices.[1][9] Example: Acoustic curtains in sky blue/orange for noise reduction.[2]
- **Commercial**: Fabric-wrapped fiberglass/mineral wool panels ($80–200/panel, NRC 0.80+); wall-mounted (51% market share by 2026), ceiling clouds/baffles, wood slat panels for absorption + design.[1][3][5] Custom in-house manufacturing for precise fit/thickness.[2] Eco-friendly fabric panels for small offices.[5]

## Budget-Friendly Solutions Under $300
- **6x Basic foam panels** (e.g., 2x4 ft): ~$120–360 total (covers 48–96 sq ft); low effectiveness for offices but quick DIY wall/ceiling mount.[1]
- **DIY heavy soft**: Thick acoustic curtains ($50–100/pair) + fabric-wrapped frames (build with $20 fiberglass + $10 fabric); layer for sound dampening without tools.[9]
- **Entry commercial**: 2–3 standard panels ($160–300 total) for primary reflections; prioritize NRC 0.80+ fiberglass cores over foam.[1] No sub-$300 full kits found; combine with free placement (e.g., corners for bass).[2]
