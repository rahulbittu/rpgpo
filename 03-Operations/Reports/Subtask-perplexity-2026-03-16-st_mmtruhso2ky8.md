## Self-Massage Methods
**Suboccipital Release** targets the four small suboccipital muscles at the base of the skull to relieve tension headaches: Lie on your back with a therapy ball or peanut tool positioned where your skull meets your neck. Let your head rest fully on the tool without holding tension, stay still for **60-90 seconds**, then slowly turn your head left and right while maintaining pressure until a deep release is felt.[1]

**Upper Trapezius and Levator Scapulae Compression** addresses the meaty part of the trapezius (upper shoulder) and levator scapulae (from upper shoulder blade to neck): Stand against a wall with a therapy ball between your upper shoulder and the wall, avoiding bone. Lean in to control pressure, hold tender spots for **30-60 seconds** until pain decreases by at least half, then move the ball slightly and repeat. Angle toward the spine for levator scapulae.[1]

These self-myofascial release techniques use tools like therapy balls (cost under a single massage session), improve blood circulation, reduce tension, and increase range of motion when done regularly at home or work.[1]

## Stretch Sequences
Post-release stretches enhance fascia release effectiveness:
- **Chin tucks**: Slow, gentle pulls to align the neck.
- **Ear-to-shoulder stretches**: Tilt ear toward shoulder slowly.
- **Gentle neck rotations**: Rotate head side to side without force.

Perform these immediately after self-massage; mild soreness may occur but resolves in **24-48 hours**. Drink plenty of water afterward to flush metabolic waste from released fascia.[1]
