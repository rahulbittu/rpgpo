# Real Estate Crowdfunding Platforms: Fundrise, CrowdStreet, and RealtyMogul

## Fundrise

**Minimum Investment & Scale**
Fundrise requires a **$10 minimum investment** and has grown to over **385,000 active investors** with **$7 billion in assets under management**.[1] Founded in 2012, the platform offers residential, commercial, and mixed-use properties.[1]

**Returns & Fees**
Annual advisory and management fees total **1% per year** (0.85% AUM fees).[1] The platform positions itself as an alternative to stocks and bonds through eREITs and eFunds.[6]

**Liquidity**
Fundrise offers **long-term holds with quarterly redemption options**, providing more flexibility than some competitors.[1]

**Accreditation Requirements**
Some investments require accreditation, though the platform is accessible to non-accredited investors for certain offerings.[1]

## CrowdStreet

**Minimum Investment & Focus**
CrowdStreet requires a **$25,000 minimum investment** and specializes exclusively in **commercial real estate (CRE)** including office space, retail space, and multifamily properties.[1] The platform launched in 2014.[6]

**Returns & Fees**
Each investment has its own fee structure and expenses; fees vary by project.[1] Management fees for real estate private equity-style investments typically range from 1.0–2.0% annually.[4]

**Liquidity**
CrowdStreet investments require **long-term holds** with limited liquidity options.[1]

**Accreditation Requirements**
**CrowdStreet is restricted to accredited investors only**, which significantly limits accessibility compared to competitors.[1][2]

Source: https://financebuzz.com/crowdstreet-review

## RealtyMogul

**Minimum Investment & Scale**
RealtyMogul offers the **lowest minimum investment at $5,000 for REITs**, though other investments generally start at **$25,000**.[3] The platform is one of the oldest in the space.[6]

**Returns & Fees**
- **RealtyMogul Income REIT**: Paid distributions monthly of approximately **4% annualized net of fees** for over nine years.[3]
- **RealtyMogul Apartment Growth REIT**: Paid distributions quarterly equating to roughly **4.5% annualized net of fees** since January 1, 2018.[3]
- Standard fees range from **1% to 1.25%** for most investments, with additional fees potentially applying.[3]

**Liquidity**
RealtyMogul offers a **Share Repurchase Program** allowing shareholders to sell RealtyMogul Income REIT and Apartment Growth REIT investments back to the platform **quarterly after the first year** at a reduced price, though buybacks are "subject to availability of capital."[3] Private placements have no redemption program.[3]

**Accreditation Requirements**
RealtyMogul offers investment opportunities for both accredited and non-accredited investors, though some investments require accreditation.[3]

Source: https://www.nerdwallet.com/investing/reviews/realty-mogul

## Comparative Summary

| Feature | Fundrise | CrowdStreet | RealtyMogul |
|---------|----------|------------|------------|
| **Minimum Investment** | $10 | $25,000 | $5,000 (REITs) |
| **Annual Fees** | 0.85% | Varies by project | 1–1.25% |
| **Property Types** | Residential, commercial, mixed-use | Commercial real estate only | Residential, commercial, REITs |
| **Accreditation Required** | Some investments only | Yes, all investments | Some investments only |
| **Liquidity** | Quarterly redemptions | Long-term hold | Quarterly buyback program (after year 1) |
| **AUM** | $7 billion | Not specified | Not specified |

**Key Insight for Passive Income**: If you're seeking the lowest barrier to entry with liquidity options, **Fundrise ($10 minimum with quarterly redemptions)** or **RealtyMogul ($5,000 minimum with quarterly buybacks)** are more accessible than CrowdStreet's $25,000 accredited-investor-only requirement.[1][2][3]
