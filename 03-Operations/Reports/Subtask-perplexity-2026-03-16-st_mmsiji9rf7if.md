## Key Anti-Inflammatory Foods
Specific foods repeatedly linked to reduced inflammation across recent sources include:
- **Berries** (blueberries, raspberries, strawberries): High in antioxidants and polyphenols; reduce oxidative stress and CRP levels[1][2][3][5][6].
- **Fatty fish** (salmon, mackerel, sardines, tuna): Rich in omega-3 fatty acids; strong anti-inflammatory effects, support immune modulation[2][3].
- **Leafy greens** (spinach, kale, collards): Provide antioxidants; lower inflammation markers like CRP[2][3].
- **Nuts and seeds** (almonds, walnuts, chia, flaxseeds): Contain healthy fats, fiber, antioxidants; reduce cardiovascular risk and inflammation[2][3][7].
- **Other specifics**: Tomatoes, olive oil, broccoli (sulforaphane), turmeric (curcumin), ginger, garlic (allicin), green tea (epigallocatechin gallate)[2][3][5].

**Source for lists**: Harvard Health (no specific date, accessed via search)[3]; Supersonic Food (no date)[2].

## Quantified Benefits from Studies
- Review of 13 RCTs (published Feb 17, 2026): Modified Mediterranean and Paleolithic diets reduced C-reactive protein (CRP) in 5/7 studies; largest drops in trials ≥6 months duration[1].
  - **Source**: https://multiplesclerosisnewstoday.com/news-posts/2026/02/17/certain-diets-help-reduce-inflammation-ms-new-review-finds/[1].
- Scoping review of RCTs (2026): Mediterranean and DASH diets improved depressive symptoms consistently; less consistent for anxiety, strongest in those with existing mental health issues[4].
  - **Source**: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/nutrition/articles/10.3389/fnut.2026.1795350/full[4].
- Nuts associated with lower inflammation markers, reduced cardiovascular disease and diabetes risk (Harvard review, no date)[3].
  - **Source**: https://www.health.harvard.edu/healthy-aging-and-longevity/foods-that-fight-inflammation[3].

No sources provided exact numerical reductions (e.g., "% CRP drop") beyond "decreased more" in diet groups vs. controls[1].

## Relevance for Tech Workers

- **Mental health/cognitive benefits**: Berries, green tea, blueberries improve brain function, reduce depression risk via antioxidants; relevant for burnout[4][5][6][7].
  - Phoenix Food Hub (March 2026): Color-coded produce (red/blue/purple) lowers heart disease/cancer risk, boosts cognition[6].
