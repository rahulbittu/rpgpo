## Finding 1: San Antonio Zoo

**Notable Features:** The zoo features diverse animal exhibits including the Cat Grotto (caracals and clouded leopards), Friedrich Aquarium, Reptile House, Neotropica (jaguars and Amazon wildlife), and Africa Live with world-famous animals Timothy and Uma, Nile crocodiles, okapi, and African wild dogs[1]. Currently on display through April 26, 2026 is "Pollinators," featuring larger-than-life sculptures throughout the park[5].

**Operating Hours & Entry:** Spring Break extended hours run March 6–22, 2026[5]. The San Antonio CityPASS includes one-time admission to the zoo for $63 (adult) or $53 (child, ages 3-11), valid through February 28, 2027[4].

**Additional Attractions:** Visitors can ride the Zootennial Carousel or Zoo Train, participate in giraffe feedings, and enjoy kangaroo petting[5].

## Finding 2: San Antonio River Walk

**Notable Features:** A 15-mile pedestrian path network following the San Antonio River with colorful Tex-Mex restaurants, cocktail bars, open-air music venues, and independent musicians performing rock, folk, and Mariachi music[6]. La Villita Historic Arts Village along the River Walk features local and international art exhibitions and over 30 boutique craft stores[6]. The Museum Reach cultural precinct includes the San Antonio Museum of Art and upscale riverside restaurants[6].

**Operating Hours & Entry:** The River Walk is accessible from multiple points in the city center with no entry fee for walking the paths[6]. Go Rio San Antonio River Cruises are included in the CityPASS[4].

**Source:** https://www.expedia.co.uk/San-Antonio-Downtown-San-Antonio.dx800120

## Finding 3: San Antonio Botanical Garden

**Notable Features:** Currently hosting "Otherwild" (March 7 – July 7, 2026), an immersive art installation by artist Patrick Shearn featuring *Flora Borealis*, a floating installation that shifts in the breeze, and *Enchanted Trees*, ten sculptural forms crafted from reflective materials[5].

**Operating Hours & Entry:** The San Antonio CityPASS includes one-time admission for $63 (adult) or $53 (child, ages 3-11), valid through February 28, 2027[4].
