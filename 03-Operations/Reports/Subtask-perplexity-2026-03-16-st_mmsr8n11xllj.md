## Cost Comparison: DIY vs Commercial Cleaning Products

DIY home cleaning solutions cost **$4–$8 less per bottle** than commercial all-purpose cleaners like major brands (e.g., Lysol or Clorox), with DIY batches using pantry staples at **$0.50–$2 per 16–32 oz** versus **$4–$8** retail.[4] For upholstery, DIY sessions run **$10–$50** (supplies + optional $35–$50 steam cleaner rental), saving **$80–$180** over pro services at **$200 average**.[5][6] Carpet cleaning DIY extracts dirt at 50–75% efficiency for **$50–$100 rental/session**, while pros charge **$100–$300** for 95%+ extraction.[2]

## Effectiveness Comparison: DIY vs Commercial Products/Services

Commercial and professional cleaning outperforms DIY by **20–45% in dirt/stain removal**: pros achieve **95%+ dirt extraction** and **85–95% stain success** (including set-in stains) on carpets/upholstery, versus DIY's **50–75% dirt removal** and **10–40% for old stains**.[2] DIY leaves residue attracting faster re-soiling and misses allergens (60–70% removal vs 90%+ pro), with temporary results lasting days vs weeks.[3][2] For general home surfaces, DIY recipes (e.g., vinegar-Dawn mix) handle visible grime but lack dwell-time for bacteria kill, risking damage from improper mixes.[3]

**Limitations**: Data focuses on carpets/upholstery (most quantified); general home cleaning lacks recent lab studies with exact % for DIY recipes vs brands like Method or Seventh Generation. Pro services excel for deep cleans but DIY suffices for maintenance.[1][3]
