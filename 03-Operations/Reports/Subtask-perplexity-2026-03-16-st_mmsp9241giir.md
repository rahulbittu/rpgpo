## Finding 1: ISO Exercise Windows and Vesting Limits for Tech Workers
Incentive Stock Options (ISOs) must be exercised within 10 years of the grant date (5 years for 10% shareholders) or within 3 months after termination, or they convert to NSOs and lose tax-favored treatment. ISOs have an annual vesting limit of $100,000 in value per year under IRC Section 422, with no federal ordinary income tax at exercise (though AMT applies on the spread).[2][4]
- Early exercise with 83(b) election within 30 days starts long-term capital gains clock when company value is low.
- RSUs (common in tech) tax full fair market value as ordinary income at vesting, with subsequent gains as capital gains.
Source: https://www.jdsupra.com/legalnews/tech-sector-guide-to-stock-options-9829881/ [2]; https://ietaxattorney.com/stock-option-taxes-in-california-iso-nso-and-rsu-tax-planning-for-tech-workers/ [4]

## Finding 2: Tax Reduction Strategies Involving Trusts and Deferral for Stock Options
Employees can reduce NSO exercise taxes via a Deferred Sale Trust to defer capital gains, or roll gains into an Opportunity Zone Fund; non-recourse funding covers exercise costs and taxes with no repayment if stock underperforms. Charitable contributions of appreciated RSU shares avoid capital gains tax and provide FMV deduction.[3][4]
- For high-net-worth tech executives, track vesting schedules and exercise windows in wealth planning to manage concentrated stock positions.
- Model withdrawal sequencing (taxable vs. tax-deferred vs. Roth) to cut lifetime taxes by hundreds of thousands to millions.
Source: https://www.esofund.com/blog/ways-to-reduce-stock-option-taxes [3]; https://ietaxattorney.com/stock-option-taxes-in-california-iso-nso-and-rsu-tax-planning-for-tech-workers/ [4]; https://defiantcap.com/high-net-worth-financial-planning-10-strategic-priorities-for-2026/ [6]

## Finding 3: Trust Structures like EOTs and ESOPs for Employee Ownership and Succession
Employee Ownership Trusts (EOTs) hold majority shares for long-term employee benefit; for disposals after November 26, 2025, 50% of gain is CGT-exempt (effective 12% rate at 24% main CGT), vs. 100% before—requires relinquishing control and modeling cashflow to avoid business strain. Employee Stock Ownership Plans (ESOPs) offer tax advantages for sellers and broad employee ownership vs. Management Buyouts (MBOs) for select key employees.[1][7]
- EOT payments to trustees risk HMRC reclassification as distributions; document commercial basis.
Source: https://www.saffery.com/insights/articles/business-succession-planning-exit-options-and-tax-changes-for-2025-26/ [1]; https://www.comerica.com/insights/wealth-management/business-ownership/management-buyout-vs-employee-stock-ownership-plan.html [7]
