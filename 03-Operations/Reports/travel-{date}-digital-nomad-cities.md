## Itinerary (day-by-day)

### Lisbon, Portugal
- **Week 1-4:**
  - **Monday-Friday:** Work from various coworking spaces (e.g., Second Home, Cowork Central) to experience different environments.
  - **Saturday:** Attend Lisbon Digital Nomads Meetup events to network with other nomads and locals.
  - **Sunday:** Explore cultural sites such as Belém Tower and Jerónimos Monastery.

- **Week 5-8:**
  - **Monday-Friday:** Continue work routine, incorporate visits to local cafes like LX Factory for a change of scenery.
  - **Saturday:** Join weekend trips organized by the digital nomad community to nearby locations like Sintra or Cascais.
  - **Sunday:** Relax at local beaches or parks like Parque Eduardo VII.

- **Week 9-12:**
  - **Monday-Friday:** Finalize projects and prepare for transition to the next city.
  - **Saturday:** Attend a farewell meetup or dinner with connections made.
  - **Sunday:** Final exploration day; visit any missed attractions or favorite spots.

## Costs & Budget

- **Accommodation:** $1,000–1,500/month for a central 1-bedroom apartment or $1,105/month for a coliving space.
- **Living Expenses:** €600–900/month excluding rent, covering groceries, transportation, and other essentials.
- **Internet:** $32–40/month for a fiber plan with up to 1 Gbps speeds.
- **Total Monthly Budget:** $2,700–3,400 for a comfortable setup.

## Logistics

- **Visa Requirements:** Secure a D8 Digital Nomad Visa, requiring proof of $3,975–$4,279/month income and a $11,923 bank balance.
- **Internet Setup:** Arrange a fiber internet plan with NOS or Vodafone upon arrival.
- **Transportation:** Obtain a transit pass for easy city navigation.

## Tips

- **Community Engagement:** Leverage the large digital nomad community (20,000+ members) for networking and social activities.
- **Cultural Immersion:** Participate in local events and explore Lisbon's rich history and vibrant neighborhoods.
- **Financial Planning:** Ensure you meet the financial requirements for the visa and have a buffer for unexpected expenses.
