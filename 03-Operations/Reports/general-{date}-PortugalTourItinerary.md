## Key Findings

### Michelin-Starred Restaurants
- **Lisbon**: Notable restaurants include Fifty Seconds, Belcanto, Henrique Sá Pessoa, and Encanto.
- **Porto**: New entries like dop, Éon, Gastro by Elemento, and In Diferente highlight Porto's culinary growth.
- **Évora**: A Cozinha do Paço, recognized with a Green Star for sustainability.

### Wine Regions
- **Douro Valley**: Renowned for its Port wine production and scenic vineyards.
- **Alentejo**: Known for robust reds and innovative winemaking.

### Cultural Activities
- **Tile Art Workshops**: Lisbon offers several workshops where visitors can learn about traditional Portuguese azulejos.
- **Surfing Spots**: Ericeira and Peniche are top surfing destinations, offering lessons and equipment rentals.

## Detailed Analysis

### Culinary Experiences
- **Lisbon**: Start your journey with a culinary exploration at Michelin-starred restaurants like Fifty Seconds and Belcanto. These establishments offer not only exquisite dining experiences but also a taste of modern Portuguese cuisine.
- **Porto**: The city's burgeoning food scene is exemplified by its new Michelin-starred entries, making it a must-visit for food enthusiasts.

### Wine Regions
- **Douro Valley**: A UNESCO World Heritage site, perfect for wine tastings and vineyard tours. Consider a river cruise to fully appreciate the landscape.
- **Alentejo**: Offers a contrasting experience with its expansive plains and unique wine profiles.

### Cultural Activities
- **Tile Art Workshops**: Engage in hands-on workshops in Lisbon to understand the history and craft of azulejos, a quintessential part of Portuguese culture.
- **Surfing**: Ericeira and Peniche provide excellent conditions for both beginners and experienced surfers, making them ideal stops for leisure and adventure.

## Recommended Actions

### Day 1-4: Lisbon
- **Day 1**: Arrive in Lisbon. Dinner at Fifty Seconds.
- **Day 2**: Explore Belém and Alfama districts. Lunch at Belcanto. Evening tile art workshop.
- **Day 3**: Day trip to Sintra. Return for dinner at Henrique Sá Pessoa.
- **Day 4**: Leisure day. Visit Encanto for dinner.

### Day 5-7: Douro Valley
- **Day 5**: Travel to Douro Valley. Vineyard tour and tasting.
- **Day 6**: River cruise along the Douro. Evening at a local wine estate.
- **Day 7**: Explore nearby towns. Depart for Porto in the evening.

### Day 8-12: Porto
- **Day 8**: Arrive in Porto. Dinner at dop.
- **Day 9**: Visit Livraria Lello and Ribeira district. Lunch at Éon.
- **Day 10**: Day trip to Vila Nova de Gaia for Port wine cellars. Dinner at The Yeatman Gastronomic.
- **Day 11**: Leisure day. Explore local markets and In Diferente for dinner.
- **Day 12**: Departure.

### Additional Activities
- **Surfing**: Consider a day trip to Ericeira or Peniche either before or after the Douro Valley segment for a surfing experience.
- **Cultural Enrichment**: Attend local festivals or fado performances if available during your visit.

### First Steps
1. **Booking**: Secure reservations at Michelin-starred restaurants and accommodations in advance.
2. **Transport**: Arrange for car rentals or train tickets between major destinations.
3. **Workshops and Tours**: Pre-book tile art workshops and vineyard tours to ensure availability.

This itinerary offers a balanced mix of culinary delights, cultural immersion, and leisure activities, providing a comprehensive experience of Portugal's rich offerings.
