## Detailed Analysis

### Feature Comparison
- **Dolby Atmos**: All three models support 5.2.2 configurations, making them suitable for entry-level Atmos setups.
- **HDMI 2.1 Support**: Denon and Yamaha offer partial HDMI 2.1 features, which are crucial for future-proofing against new video standards. Sony lacks these features.
- **Room Correction**: Denon's Audyssey is generally regarded as superior, offering better bass management. Yamaha's YPAO is reliable but less effective, while Sony's DCAC is basic.

### Performance Evaluations
- **Denon AVR-S660H**: Praised for its value and ease of setup, with strong room correction capabilities.
- **Yamaha RX-V4A**: Offers more HDMI inputs but falls short in room correction compared to Denon.
- **Sony STR-DH790**: Simplicity and sound quality are highlights, but it lacks advanced features.
