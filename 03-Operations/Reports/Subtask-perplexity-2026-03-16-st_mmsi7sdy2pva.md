# Flexibility Exercises for Desk Workers

Based on current sources, here are evidence-based flexibility exercises specifically designed to counteract prolonged sitting:

## Finding 1: Neck Side Glide for Screen-Related Tension

The **Neck Side Glide** targets tension from phone calls and screen gazing. Sit tall with relaxed shoulders, slowly tilt your right ear toward your right shoulder while keeping the opposite shoulder down, and hold for 20-30 seconds. Repeat on the left side.[2] This exercise directly addresses the forward head posture common in desk workers.

Source: https://www.hushkite.com/5-gentle-stretches-for-desk-workers/

## Finding 2: Shoulder Blade Squeeze to Counter Hunched Posture

The **Shoulder Blade Squeeze** counteracts the forward hunch from typing. Clasp your hands behind your back, straighten your arms slightly, and gently squeeze your shoulder blades together while lifting your chest. Hold for 15-20 seconds, then release slowly.[2] This directly reverses the rounded shoulder position desk workers develop.

Source: https://www.hushkite.com/5-gentle-stretches-for-desk-workers/

## Finding 3: Wrist Flexor Release for Mouse-Related Fatigue

The **Wrist Flexor Release** addresses forearm tightness from mouse use. Extend your right arm forward with palm up, use your left hand to gently pull your fingers back toward your body, and hold for 20 seconds. Switch arms and repeat.[2] This prevents the cumulative strain of repetitive clicking and typing.

Source: https://www.hushkite.com/5-gentle-stretches-for-desk-workers/

## Finding 4: Seated Spinal Twist for Spine Mobility

The **Seated Spinal Twist** improves spinal mobility after hours of stillness. Sit forward on your chair with feet flat, place your right hand on your left knee and left hand behind you on the chair, twist gently to the left while looking over your shoulder, and hold for 20-30 seconds. Repeat on the other side.[2]

Source: https://www.hushkite.com/5-gentle-stretches-for-desk-workers/

## Finding 5: Recommended Movement Frequency

Research indicates desk workers should aim for **2-5 minutes of stretching or light exercise every hour** to counteract the health risks of prolonged sitting, which include increased risk of heart disease, back pain, and metabolic dysfunction.[3]

Source: https://www.cleaneatzkitchen.com/a/blog/10-desk-exercises-for-sedentary-workers-1

## Finding 6: Seated Lower Back Stretch (Forward Fold)

The **Seated Lower Back Stretch** decompresses the lower spine. Sit near the edge of your chair with feet flat on the floor, slowly hinge forward at the hips letting your arms hang toward the ground, and hold for 20-30 seconds.[4] This directly targets lower back tension from sitting.

Source: https://thespinepro.com/blog/desk-friendly-stretches-for-back-pain-relief/

## Finding 7: Hip Flexor Stretch for Lower Body Mobility

The **Hip Flexor Stretch** can be done seated by scooting to the edge of your chair, extending one leg back slightly while keeping your chest upright. You should feel the stretch along the front of your hip.[4] This addresses the tightness that develops from prolonged sitting with bent hips.

Source: https://thespinepro.com/blog/desk-friendly-stretches-for-back-pain-relief/
