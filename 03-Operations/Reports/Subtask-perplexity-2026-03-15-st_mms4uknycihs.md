## Finding 1: Couch to 5K (C25K) Training Plan for Complete Beginners
- **Program Overview**: Free 8-week plan designed for zero-running experience to complete a 5K (3.1 miles); uses interval training with run/walk intervals to build endurance gradually, ideal for sedentary starters.
- **Frequency and Structure**: 3 runs per week; start with 5 minutes brisk walking warmup + dynamic stretching; Week 1 example: Brisk 5-min walk, then alternate 60 seconds running/90 seconds walking for 20 minutes total; end with cooldown walk.
- **Injury Prevention**: Include 3 short cross-training sessions weekly (weightlifting for runners, yoga, strength/mobility); ignore pace, focus on time; repeat weeks or take rest days if aches/burned out occur.
- **Customization**: Google Sheets version available for personal copy (C25K 8-Weeks tab).
- **Source**: https://marathonhandbook.com/couch-to-5k-training-plan/

## Finding 2: Run/Walk Method for Beginner Mile Pacing
- **Program Details**: Gold standard for novices; run 60 seconds, walk 90 seconds, repeat to 1 mile (5,280 feet); reduces joint impact and fatigue; 3-4 runs/week optimal for adaptation.
- **Pacing for Sedentary Beginners**: 12-15 minutes per mile typical for true novices using run/walk; increase run intervals/decrease walks as strength builds.
- **Injury Prevention and Recovery**: Prioritize consistency (e.g., 3x15-min runs/week over 1x60-min); stop for sharp pain (vs. soreness); accumulate activity in short bouts.
- **Source**: https://www.chalktalksports.com/blogs/running-life/how-long-to-run-a-mile-beginner-your-guide-to-pacing-and-progress

## Finding 3: Cross-Training and Recovery Tips (Relevant for Sedentary via Older Runner Insights)
- **Strategies for High Sit Time**: Add cross-training (cycling, swimming, strength, yoga) to running days for muscle balance/flexibility; reduces repetitive stress; examples: 3 run days/week + cross-training instead of 6 run days.
- **Warmup/Cooldown Focus**: Emphasize warmups and recovery; join non-competitive groups like New York Road Runners for community walks/runs.
- **General Prevention**: More rest days for longevity; all activity counts (e.g., short walks).
- **Source**: https://www.runnersworld.com/training/a70533327/tips-for-runners-over-fifty/

## Finding 4: Beginner Strength/Conditioning Template (Supports Running Transition)
- **Program Basics**: 5-week foundational template (machine/dumbbell/barbell); 2-3 days/week, 45-60 minutes; start light (6/10 effort); pairs with 150-300 min moderate cardio/week (e.g., short walks/runs).
- **Sedentary Fit**: Builds from novice for health/longevity; accumulate cardio in 10-min bouts; free downloadable template.
- **Injury Prevention**: Medical clearance first; comfortable clothes; combine with cardio guidelines for balanced start.
- **Source**: https://www.barbellmedicine.com/blog/the-beginner-prescription-blog/
