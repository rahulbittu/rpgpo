## Roth IRA Conversion Strategies for $200k Earners with $150k 401k

For high earners at $200k income (likely in the **32% federal marginal tax bracket** for 2026 single filers, assuming standard thresholds around $191k-$243k), Roth conversions from a traditional 401k involve adding the converted amount to taxable income, paying taxes upfront for tax-free future growth and withdrawals. No annual conversion limits exist, but strategic "laddering" (e.g., $30k-$50k/year) is recommended to avoid bracket creep into 35%+ rates, especially with Medicare IRMAA risks post-63.[1][2][4][6]

### Tax Implications
- **Federal Taxes**: Conversion is ordinary income; at $200k base, converting $50k pushes into 32%-35% brackets (exact 2026 brackets pending inflation adjustment, but 2025 single: 32% $191,951-$243,725). Pay taxes from external cash to preserve principal; pro-rata rule applies if mixing pre-tax/post-tax IRAs.[1][2]
- **State Taxes**: Varies; delay large conversions if moving to no-income-tax state (e.g., FL, TX) post-residency.[2]
- **Other Hits**: Watch AMT, 3.8% NIIT on investment income over $200k single, and Medicare IRMAA (starts at ~$103k MAGI single in 2025, adjusted).[2][3]
- **Offsets**: Pair with charitable donations (DAF/CRT), capital loss harvesting, or low-income years (e.g., sabbatical).[2]
- **401k Specific**: Roll to IRA first or use new 2026 TSP in-plan conversions ($500 min, no limit on frequency); federal employees can ladder up to 26x/year.[3][4]
