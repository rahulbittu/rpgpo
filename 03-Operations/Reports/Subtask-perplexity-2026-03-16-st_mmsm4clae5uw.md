## Visa Requirements for Working Holiday/Digital Nomads
- **Visa on Arrival (VoA)**: $35 USD (IDR 550,000), valid 30 days for tourism/business/short-term work; extendable once for 30 more days at immigration offices (fee ~IDR 500,000). Available at Denpasar airport for 90+ nationalities.[4]
- **B211A Single-Entry Visit Visa**: For 60 days + 1x 60-day extension (total 120 days), apply online via imigrasi.go.id, costs IDR 2.2 million (~$145 USD) + sponsor fee; allows remote work but no local employment. Source: https://www.immigration.go.id/en/visa/b211a-2026/ (official, updated Feb 2026).
- No dedicated digital nomad visa as of March 2026; VoA suffices for 2 weeks. Overstay fine: IDR 1 million/day. Source: https://bali.com/bali-visa-requirements-2026/.

## Internet Reliability
- **Average speeds**: Canggu/Ubud fiber 50-300Mbps download (Telkomsel/IndiHome), but outages common during rain (2-3x/week); Starlink widely used in coworkings (200Mbps, 99% uptime, IDR 800,000/month).[1][2]
- **Mobile data**: Telkomsel 4G/5G plans IDR 150,000/50GB (reliable 20-50Mbps); eSIM via Airalo IDR 300,000/unlimited for 15 days. Downtime peaks rainy season (Dec-Mar). Source: https://www.speedtest.net/global-index/indonesia/bali#mobile (Feb 2026 data, median 45Mbps).
- Coworking backups: Most use dual ISP + generators; 95% uptime reported. Source: https://nomadlist.com/bali/internet (March 2026).
