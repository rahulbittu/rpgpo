## Finding 1: Sprint Demo Presentation Template Best Practices (Gambia College Guide)
- **Core structure**: Start with completed user stories/features as the main content; for each, include brief description, problem solved, user benefits, and visuals like screenshots, short video demos, or animated GIFs (keep demos concise, under 1-2 minutes per feature, focused on key functionality from user perspective).
- **Delivery tips**: Practice rehearsal with team for smooth delivery and feedback; keep total demo concise (aim for 15-30 minutes total); engage audience with questions and feedback prompts to foster collaboration.

## Finding 3: Actionable Demo Follow-Up from Sprint Retros (Asana 2026 Guide)
- **Link to demos**: In sprint retros (post-demo), set 1 action item per pain point with owner/deadline (e.g., "Gather task context from stakeholders before backlog refinement" if demo feedback highlights info gaps); share screen, take notes during demo/review.
- **Prep**: Collect feedback 1-2 days pre-retro with questions like "What went well? What to do differently?" to inform next demo improvements.
- **Limit**: Focus on 1-3 actions per sprint to avoid overload (echoed in related tools).
- **Date**: Updated 2026 guide.
- **Source**: https://asana.com/resources/sprint-retrospective[2]
