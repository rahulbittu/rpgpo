## Finding 2: Bad News Delivery for Engineering Leaders

**Recommended Approach:** Engineering leaders should deliver bad news (missed deadlines, technical debt impact, team attrition) using the **SBI framework** (Situation-Behavior-Impact):

1. **Situation:** State facts objectively (e.g., "We discovered a critical security vulnerability in the payment module")
2. **Behavior:** Explain what happened (e.g., "Our automated security scanning missed this because we hadn't updated the ruleset")
3. **Impact:** Quantify the business consequence (e.g., "This requires 2 weeks of remediation work, pushing the Q2 launch by 10 days")
4. **Mitigation:** Provide immediate action plan with timeline and owner

**Why it works:** Separates emotion from facts, demonstrates accountability, and shows forward momentum.

## Finding 3: Cross-Functional Alignment Rituals

**Effective Rituals for Engineering Leaders:**

- **Weekly Dependency Sync (30 min):** Engineering, Product, Design leads review blockers, timeline risks, and decision log. Owner: Engineering Lead. Output: Updated risk register.

- **Bi-weekly Executive Steering (45 min):** VP Engineering, Product Lead, Finance present: ship metrics, burn-down, top 3 risks, resource requests. Format: Slide deck with 3-slide limit.

- **Monthly All-Hands Technical Talk (60 min):** Engineering lead presents architecture decisions, technical debt status, hiring pipeline to full organization. Builds transparency and attracts talent.

- **Quarterly Business Review (2 hours):** Engineering presents: delivered features, technical debt reduction, team productivity metrics, hiring plan, budget forecast.
