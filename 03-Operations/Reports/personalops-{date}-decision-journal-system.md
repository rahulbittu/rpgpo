## Plan

### Objective
To create a structured decision journal system that aids in improving judgment for life and career decisions by tracking, reviewing, and analyzing decision-making patterns.

### Components
1. **Decision Template**: A comprehensive form to document each decision.
2. **Review Cadence**: A schedule for regular reviews of past decisions.
3. **Pattern Recognition Methodology**: A process for analyzing decision outcomes and identifying biases.

## Implementation Steps

### Decision Template
1. **Basic Information**
   - **Date and Time**: Record when the decision is made.
   - **Decision Made**: Clearly state the decision (e.g., "Invest in SaaS project A").

2. **Contextual Analysis**
   - **Facts/Reality Known**: List 5-10 key facts at the time of decision.
   - **Assumptions**: Document any assumptions (e.g., "AI market growth at 15% YoY").
   - **Alternatives Considered**: List 2-3 other options with pros and cons.

3. **Decision Metrics**
   - **Confidence Level**: Rate confidence in the decision (1-10 scale).
   - **Probability Estimates**: Estimate success probability (e.g., 70% chance of positive ROI).

4. **Outcome Documentation**
   - **Outcome**: Record the result after a set period (success/fail, with reasons).
   - **Delta Analysis**: Compare expected vs. actual outcomes at 30/90/180 days.

### Review Cadence
- **Monthly Review**: Analyze 10-20 past entries to assess recent decision quality.
- **Quarterly Review**: Conduct a deeper analysis of decision patterns over the past quarter, focusing on long-term outcomes.

### Pattern Recognition Methodology
- **Bias Identification**: Compare assumptions vs. reality to identify recurring biases (e.g., over-optimism).
- **OODA Loop Integration**: Use Andy McKee’s OODA loop framework to refine decision-making processes:
  - **Observe**: Gather data on past decisions.
  - **Orient**: Analyze context and biases.
  - **Decide**: Adjust future decision criteria based on insights.
  - **Act**: Implement changes and document new decisions.

## Tools/Resources

- **Google Sheets Template**: Use Andy McKee’s OODA loop decision journal template for structured documentation.
- **Farnam Street Blog Resources**: Reference Shane Parrish’s decision journal insights for additional guidance.
- **Pattern Analysis Software**: Consider tools like Tableau or Power BI for visualizing decision patterns and outcomes.

## Review Schedule

- **Monthly**: Set a recurring calendar event to review recent decisions. Focus on immediate outcomes and short-term biases.
- **Quarterly**: Schedule a more comprehensive review session. Analyze long-term patterns, success rates, and adjust decision-making strategies accordingly.

### First Step
- **Setup**: Download and customize the Google Sheets template from Andy McKee to fit personal decision-making needs. Begin documenting decisions immediately to build a robust dataset for future analysis.
