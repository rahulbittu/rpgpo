# Medieval Historical Sites and Castles in Czech Republic

## Finding 1: Prague Castle — 9th Century Origins and Current Status

**Prague Castle** is the official residence of the Czech Republic's president and originated in the late 9th century during the reign of Bořivoj, the first Christian prince of Bohemia[1]. Key architectural highlights include:

- **St. George's Basilica** (begun ~920): One of the oldest standing structures, representing Romanesque architecture[1]
- **St. Vitus Cathedral**: Foundation stone laid in 1344 by Charles IV; completed in 1929 after nearly 600 years of construction[1][7]. The cathedral features late Gothic style designed by architect Petr Parléř and houses Bohemia's crown jewels in a vault[1]
- **Old Royal Palace**: The original seat of the Czech kingdom, featuring late Gothic and Renaissance architecture[1][5]
- **Golden Lane**: A picture-perfect alleyway of cottages that once housed castle servants, goldsmiths, and soldiers[5]
- **Summer Palace (1538–60)**: Also called the Belvedere or Queen Anna's Summer Palace, built in Renaissance style[1]
- **Ball Game Hall (1567–69)**: Originally built as a gymnasium, now used for exhibits and concerts[1]

Source: https://www.britannica.com/place/Prague-Castle

## Finding 2: Houska Castle — Late 13th Century Fortress

**Houska Castle** was constructed between 1270–1280 during the reign of Ottokar II of Bohemia[6]. Located in a limestone karst region, the castle is notable for its unusual construction in an area with no nearby water source, major trade route, or significant settlement[6]. The castle chapel contains distinctive frescoes depicting centaur-like figures and demonic forms, unusual for typical medieval Christian imagery[6].

## Finding 3: Český Krumlov — UNESCO-Listed Medieval Town

**Český Krumlov** features the second-largest castle in the Czech Republic with magnificent baroque gardens[2]. The town center is UNESCO-listed and contains over 300 historic houses[2]. Notable features include:

- **Oldest Baroque Theater in the World**: Part of the castle complex with a rotating auditorium[2]
- Historical seat of noble families including the Rosenbergs, Eggenbergs, and Schwarzenbergs[2]
- Preserved medieval atmosphere with picturesque narrow streets[2]

## Finding 4: Regional Castle Sites — Okres Teplice Region

The Teplice region contains several historically significant castles:

- **Teplice Castle and Castle Park**: Renaissance castle (baroque style) evolved from a 12th-century Benedictine convent; park created in 17th century and redesigned in 18th century[4]
- **Kostomlaty Castle Ruins**: Gothic castle built by lords from Rýzmburk in early 14th century; purchased by Margrave Karel (later Karel IV) in 1335[4]
- **Krupka Castle (Burg Krupka)**: Castle ruins on a rocky promontory built around 1300, offering views of the Bohemian Basin[4]
- **Doubravka Castle**: Striking ruin with panoramic views of Teplice[4]
- **Duchcov Castle**: Renowned for Baroque architecture and connection to Giacomo Casanova[4]

## Finding 5: Additional Regional Historical Sites

**Olomouc Region** features:
- **Javoříč Caves**: Among the most beautiful karst caves in the Czech Republic with exceptionally rich stalactite decoration; largest known bat and wren wintering site in Czechia[3]
- **Archbishop's Palace in Olomouc**: Recalls the city's importance as historical center of Moravian church administration[3]
