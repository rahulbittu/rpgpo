## Finding 1: Monarch Money – Best for Couples and Shared Finances

**Specific Features:**
- **Net worth tracking** (identified as the strongest feature)[3]
- **Goals 3.0 system** with automatic debt payoff projections and savings calculators[1]
- **AI-powered features** including weekly recaps, transaction categorization, and spending explanations[2]
- **Account connectivity**: Connects to 13,000+ financial institutions via Plaid, Felicity, and Amex[4]

**Tradeoff:** One of the more expensive options; overkill for solo users[5]

## Finding 2: YNAB (You Need A Budget) – Structured Envelope Budgeting

**Approach:** Budgeting-first tool with strict zero-based budgeting methodology[6]

**Key Difference from Monarch:** Unlike Monarch's flexible monthly targets with rollover options, YNAB enforces envelope-style budgeting where every dollar is assigned a purpose[3]

**Tradeoff:** Steep learning curve despite powerful budgeting discipline[5]

**Pricing:** Paid subscription[5]

Source: https://monavio.app/blog/monarch-money-vs-ynab/

## Finding 4: Manual Spreadsheet Approach – Baseline for Comparison

One reviewer replaced their spreadsheet system entirely after 30 days of Monarch testing, connecting 9 financial accounts (checking, savings, 3 credit cards, brokerage, retirement, mortgage) and tracking net worth weekly[3]. This suggests spreadsheets require manual data entry and lack automated account synchronization.

**Spreadsheet limitations:** No real-time account syncing, manual transaction categorization, no automated insights or projections[3][4]
