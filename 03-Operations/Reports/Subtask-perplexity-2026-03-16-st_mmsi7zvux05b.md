## AI-Driven Throughput Gains Not Reaching Production (Waydev, 2026)

AI boosted engineering throughput by **59%** overall, with **15.2% increase** on feature branches, but main branch throughput dropped **6.8%** due to outdated delivery systems and quality checks failing to scale.[1]

## Talent Shortage Worsened by Hiring Practices (Quest Global, Ravalnath Kamath)

Engineering leaders over-rely on external hires for emerging skills like GenAI, but universities produce stream-based graduates while industry needs cross-boundary expertise; retirements accelerate gaps.[2]  
Audit reveals heavy external dependency: calculate external hire ratio vs. internal transitions, time-to-productivity, and retention—most orgs vulnerable to supply constraints.[2]  
**Next steps**: (1) Map high-potential engineers for rotational training/mentorship; (2) Launch knowledge-transfer from seniors via expert networks and pairings before retirements; shift to internal capability-building.[2]  
Source: https://www.questglobal.com/insights/thought-leadership/why-hiring-harder-makes-the-engineering-talent-shortage-worse/
