# 529 Plan Optimization & Education Savings Strategies (2026)

## Finding 1: 529 Plan FAFSA Impact & Ownership Optimization

**Key Strategy:** Parent-owned 529 accounts are assessed at **5.64%** on FAFSA, while student-owned accounts face **20% assessment**. Converting student-owned to parent-owned 529s can significantly increase aid eligibility.[1]

**Specific Tactics:**
- **Timing liquidations:** Paying a $15,000 tuition bill before FAFSA filing reduces reportable cash assets by $15,000, decreasing Student Aid Index (SAI) by roughly $850.[1]
- **Grandparent 529 strategy:** Grandparent-owned 529 accounts don't appear on FAFSA at all, allowing distributions anytime without reducing aid eligibility. Use parent-owned 529s for the student's final year when remaining aid matters less.[1]
- **Spend student assets first:** When both student and parent assets are available, deplete student assets (20% assessment) before parent assets (5.64% assessment) to maximize remaining aid eligibility in subsequent years.[1]

Source: https://www.saxonfinancialgroup.com/how-529-plans-affect-fafsa/

## Finding 2: 529-to-Roth IRA Rollovers (SECURE 2.0 Rule)

**New Option (Effective 2024):** Federal law permits rolling up to **$35,000 lifetime** from a 529 into a Roth IRA for the beneficiary.[1]

**Requirements:**
- 15-year holding period on the original 529 account
- Five-year rule on contributions
- Removes excess funds from future FAFSA calculations while providing retirement benefits[1]

This is particularly valuable for families with unused 529 funds after a student completes education.[1]

This expansion significantly increases the utility of 529 plans for families with school-age children before college.

## Finding 4: Expanded Qualified Education Expenses

**Qualified 529 expenses now include:**[3]
- Tuition and room & board
- Laptops and technology
- Student loan interest repayment
- Qualified apprenticeship programs
- K-12 tuition (up to $20,000/year in 2026)

**Gift Tax Advantage:** Individuals can contribute up to **$95,000** ($190,000 for married couples) and elect to treat contributions as made ratably over five years, maximizing the annual gift tax exclusion.[3]

## Finding 5: Unused 529 Funds & Beneficiary Changes

**No Expiration:** Unused 529 plan funds do not expire and are not subject to mandatory withdrawal ages.[5]

**Flexibility Options:**
- Leave funds in place and adjust investment strategy for longer time horizons
- Change beneficiary to another child or grandchild (unlimited beneficiary changes allowed)
- Roll into Roth IRA (up to $35,000 lifetime under SECURE 2.0)
- Use for student loan repayment[5]

This flexibility makes 529s valuable for multigenerational education and estate planning strategies.[5]

Source: https://hbwealth.com/insights/unused-529-plan-funds-explained-flexible-planning-strategies-for-families/
