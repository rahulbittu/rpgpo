## Tool Storage Best Practices
- **Pegboards for lightweight tools**: Install pegboards on walls above workbenches or near doors for hanging tools, coats, or bags using customizable hooks, shelves, and baskets. This keeps items visible and accessible without floor space.[2]
- **Magnetic strips for metal tools**: Mount magnetic strips above or beside workbenches to hold wrenches, chisels, screwdrivers, and scissors, eliminating drawer searches.[2]
- **PVC pipes for long-handled tools**: Cut PVC pipes to length and mount horizontally on walls to store brooms, rakes, garden tools, or screwdrivers securely at low cost.[2]
- **Wall-mounted power tool racks**: Use Anqtovp 2-Pack 66-inch wide steel racks (holds 18 cordless drills, 600 lb capacity per rack, $114.98 at Lowe's) for heavy-duty organization; modular with DIY side panels.[3]
- **Modular panel systems**: Snap-together plastic or steel panels (e.g., from Home Depot) with hooks, shelves, and bins for power tools; place behind workbenches for reachability; no tools needed for most assembly.[4]
- **Toolboxes and carts**: Eastwood offers Extreme Tools, Homak, and Milwaukee brands in various sizes with drawer liners, dividers; portable carts for mobility in garages.[1]
- **Wood pallets for yard tools**: Anchor pallets vertically against walls for durable floor or wall storage of rakes and shovels when space is limited.[2]

## Workbench Design
- Position workbenches centrally or against walls with surrounding cabinets, leaving space for panel systems behind to hang frequently used tools within arm's reach.[4]
- Incorporate magnetic strips or pegboards directly above or on sides for immediate tool access; pair with drawer organizers like screwdriver holders (DIY or purchased).[2][4]
