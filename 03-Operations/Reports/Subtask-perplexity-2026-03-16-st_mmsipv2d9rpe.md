## Remote Work Trends (2026)

**75% of employees engaged in some remote work in 2025, with 40% willing to quit if forced fully on-site.** Hybrid models persist amid "hybrid creep," where 30% of remote/hybrid workers fear stunted career growth; remote workers earn 12% more than in-office peers, especially senior staff.[1][3] Remote hiring surged as a competitive edge, with PwC noting 72% of companies planning expansion for cost savings and global talent; McKinsey reports distributed teams scale 3x faster.[2]

- **RTO Pushback**: Companies like NBCUniversal mandated 4 office days/week from Jan 2026 (offering severance); Truist shifted to 4-5 days; Sherwin-Williams allows 12 remote days/year max (2/month).[4]
- **Geographic Shifts**: Remote work declined in large US cities (15.7% to 13.45%, 2023-2024); top spots: Frisco, TX (33.7%), Berkeley, CA (31.5%). Growth in St. George, UT (10.3% to 17.0%).[5]
- **Worker Changes**: 56% report altered communication, 53% changed hours, 51% more video meetings; 28% have side hustles. Mental health focus: 50%+ say firms address it seriously.[1]
- **Hiring Efficiency**: Nearshore replaces offshoring; remote roles integrate into core ops, cutting time-to-hire and overhead.[2]
