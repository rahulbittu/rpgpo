# Delegation Frameworks for Engineering Managers

Based on the search results provided, here are the key delegation frameworks and strategies currently used by engineering managers:

## Finding 1: Delegation Framework in Engineering Management Operating Systems

**Specific Framework:** RACI (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed) is recommended as a core delegation tool for engineering managers managing cross-team projects.[1]

**Context:** According to em-tools.io, a mature engineering manager operating system typically includes RACI for any project involving more than two teams. This framework is part of a complementary set that also includes OKRs for quarterly goal-setting, RICE scoring for roadmap prioritization, DORA metrics for delivery health, and quarterly team health checks.[1]

**Application:** The framework helps structure delegation decisions by clarifying roles across multiple teams, reducing ambiguity in who owns what responsibility.

Source: https://www.em-tools.io/engineering-management-frameworks

## Finding 2: Agentic AI Delegation Strategy (Build, Buy, or Delegate)

**Framework Name:** The "Build, Buy, or Delegate" decision matrix for agentic AI projects.[4]

**When to Delegate:** Delegation is recommended when:[4]
- Running large-scale change requiring internal teams to focus on security, data, and integration
- Wanting business results backed by service level agreements (e.g., contact centers handling millions of interactions)
- Testing agentic approaches without long-term platform commitments or heavy upfront build costs

**Key Principle:** Delegation works best when your organization owns goals and metrics while the partner owns day-to-day delivery and continuous tuning.[4]

**90-Day Action Step:** Run a one-hour working session with direct reports to list all active and planned AI/agent projects, map each to "core, context, or commodity" categories, and mark whether you're building, buying, or delegating today.[4]

Source: https://www.presidio.com/blogs/the-right-agentic-framework-strategy-build-buy-or-delegate/

## Finding 3: Delegation in AI-Augmented Engineering Teams

**Framework Shift:** Microsoft's approach emphasizes that **engineers remain accountable for outcomes even when delegating to AI agents**. AI output is reviewed, tested, and validated like any other engineering input.[3]

**Key Practice:** Rather than lowering standards, embedding AI raised expectations around judgment, oversight, and data quality. Leadership became more deliberate about responsibility and governance.[3]

**Measurement Change:** The delegation model is shifting from individual productivity metrics to **hybrid-centric measurement** that tracks:[5]
- Human contribution (strategic decisions, context provision, quality oversight)
- AI contribution (code generation, test coverage, initial review)
- Human-AI interaction quality (how well engineers orchestrate AI agents)

Source: https://www.microsoft.com/insidetrack/blog/powering-the-new-age-of-ai-led-engineering-in-it-at-microsoft/

Source: https://waydev.co/when-ai-writes-the-code-what-do-we-measure/

## Finding 4: Legal Engineering Delegation Model

**Real-World Example:** Latham & Watkins' Manager of Legal Engineering role (updated February 26, 2026) demonstrates delegation in practice.[2]

**Delegation Responsibilities:**
- Leads and develops a team of legal engineers and knowledge engineers
- Establishes prompt engineering standards and workflow templates
- Oversees translation of practice requirements into AI workflow specifications

**Required Skills:** Proven leadership in managing high-performing professionals, including coaching, performance oversight, delegation, and succession planning.[2]
