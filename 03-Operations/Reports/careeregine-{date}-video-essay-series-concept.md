## Key Findings

### Psychological and Cognitive Effects
- **Natural Light and Spatial Design**: Environments with abundant daylight and green elements are shown to reduce stress and enhance cognitive performance. This is increasingly applied in corporate and educational settings to improve mood and productivity[1].
- **Acoustic Design**: Sound management in spaces influences focus and relaxation, guiding movement and mood subtly[1].

### Neurophysiological Evidence
- **Urban Design and Neurophysiology**: Mobile EEG studies in Calicut, Kerala show that urban settings with varying street enclosure, natural features, and activity levels affect neurophysiological states like excitement, engagement, and stress. This highlights real-time cognitive and affective experiences shaped by urban design[3].

### Cultural and Environmental Communication
- **Material Choices**: The materials used in architecture communicate cultural values and environmental priorities, influencing how spaces are perceived and interacted with[1].
