## Emails and Subscriptions
- **Unsubscribe from newsletters and alerts**: Review and cancel unused subscriptions manually on iOS/Android settings; deleting apps does not auto-cancel rolling subscriptions—check app stores for instructions.[1][2][3]
- **Ongoing maintenance**: Participate in annual **Digital Cleanup Day** to delete unused apps, archive old messages, and cancel subscriptions; set monthly/quarterly reminders for repeat cleanups.[2]
- No specific email automation tools found in results; manual review recommended alongside general app deletion.

## Files
- **Organize with logical folder structures**: Use hierarchy like *Work Documents > Graphic Design > Clients > Harry Miller > Final Logo Designs*; keep desktop clear via scheduled cleanups.[1]
- **Consistent naming**: Prefix filenames with ISO 8601 date format (e.g., **2024-12-25-filename**); use **TextExpander** (`filedate` snippet from Sample Date Formats group) for auto-insertion.[1]
- **Declutter tips**: Delete overflow folders and untitled documents; back up data regularly.[1][3]
- No automation tools specified beyond TextExpander for naming.

## Photos
- **Declutter albums**: Scan folders for unnecessary photos/screenshots (e.g., one user found **254 screenshots**); delete outdated ones to free storage.[1][2][3]

## General Best Practices
- **Apps and home screen**: Delete unused apps monthly/yearly; arrange home screen after cleanup.[1]
- **Notes**: Sort oldest first, delete temporaries, file into folders (e.g., Personal, Work), add tags.[1]
- **Device-wide**: Clear cache for speed, review contacts/passwords, uninstall outdated software.[3]
- **Time-bound methods**: No digital-specific timers found; physical decluttering analog ("just one song" ~4-5 minutes/song) suggests short bursts for motivation.[4]
  - Source: https://www.goodhousekeeping.com/uk/house-and-home/household-advice/a70532742/the-just-one-song-decluttering-method/
- **Rituals**: Adopt Cal Newport's capture method (jot tasks) and shutdown ritual (end-day closure) to reduce overload.[2]
  - Source: https://josebriones.substack.com/p/digital-hoarding-is-making-you-sick
- **Frequency**: Monthly to quarterly reviews; combine with backups and physical cleaning (e.g., speakers, screens).[1][2][3]
  - Source: https://onenet.net/lets-get-cleaning-a-guide-to-spring-cleaning-digital-devices/
