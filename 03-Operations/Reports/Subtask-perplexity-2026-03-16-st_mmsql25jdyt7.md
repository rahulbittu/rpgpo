## Stressors Among Tech Workers

Tech workers face high burnout rates driven by constant connectivity, AI fears, heavy workloads, and after-hours demands. In 2026, **58% of technology sector workers report moderate-to-extreme burnout**, the third-highest industry after retail (62%) and healthcare (61%), per DHR Global's 2026 Workforce Trends Report.[2] Additionally, **72% of employees report stress from constant email and mobile connectivity** (Gallup, 2020), **43% feel overwhelmed by work communications** (Buffer, 2023), **50% cite email overload** (Gallup, 2022), and **60% feel pressure to respond outside hours** (Harvard Business Review, 2022).[1] **13% specifically cite AI impact on roles as a burnout cause** in a 2026 report, amid rapid tech adoption creating "technological stress" like cognitive overload from new systems.[3]

**High workloads delay recovery**: New research (March 10, 2026) in Journal of Applied Psychology, based on 10,000+ hours of observation, shows stressful days push recovery later into evenings; 50% start evenings with low recovery due to rumination on unfinished tasks.[5] Broader stats: **83% of U.S. workers experience work stress** (OSHA), **91% of UK adults face high/extreme stress** (Mental Health UK, 2026), with tech/knowledge workers at **83% burnout** (DHR Global).[2][4]

**Age-specific**: Gen Z (74% moderate-severe burnout) and 18-24-year-olds (40% take stress-related time off; 39% uncomfortable discussing with managers).[2] Ages 18-34 worry most about AI (65%).[4]

**Costs**: Job stress costs U.S. $300B+ yearly in absenteeism/turnover; global depression/anxiety $1T in lost productivity (WHO, 2024).[4]

## Coping Strategies for Tech Workers

**Schedule short tech exploration**: For cognitive overload from new tools/AI, dedicate 15-20 minutes daily to "play" with systems, reducing performance anxiety (District Counseling Center, March 2, 2026).[3]

**Prioritize evening recovery timing**: High workloads delay recovery; end days with accomplishment sense. Organizations should manage loads; individuals counter rumination by starting recovery early post-5 PM (Journal of Applied Psychology, March 10, 2026).[5]

**Broader actions from stats**: Only 40% admit stress to employers, with 60% getting no help; top stressors are deadlines (55%), relationships (53%), management (50%)—address via open talks (ADAA, 2025).[4] 56% say stress hits performance; vacations aid well-being per meta-analysis (Jan 2025).[5]

**Next Steps**:
- Track personal recovery: Log evening stress 1 week using free app like Daylio; aim for pre-8 PM unwind (inferred from [5]).
- Discuss AI fears: Email manager template—"Per DHR 2026 report, 13% cite AI burnout; suggest 15-min weekly upskill session" [3][2].
- Benchmark: Compare to 58% tech burnout benchmark; if >50% days overwhelmed, seek EAP (56% performance impact stat [4]).
