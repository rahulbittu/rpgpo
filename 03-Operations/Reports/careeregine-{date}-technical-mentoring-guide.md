## Key Findings

### Finding 1: Structured Program Components for Technical Mentorship
- **Orientation:** Essential for introducing program goals, expectations, and resources.
- **Regular Meetings:** Consistent and purposeful interactions are crucial.
- **Evaluation:** Feedback mechanisms are necessary to track progress and adjust strategies.
- Clear expectations and guidelines are vital, including roles, communication guidelines, goal-setting frameworks, and feedback mechanisms.

### Finding 2: SMART Goal-Setting Framework
- Mentors should guide mentees in setting SMART goals to ensure clarity and measurable outcomes in skill development.

### Finding 3: Reverse and Reciprocal Mentoring
- With AI skills gaps expected to reach 20-40% by 2028, reverse mentoring and reciprocal mentoring are effective strategies for mutual learning and bridging knowledge gaps.

## Recommendations

### Setting Expectations
- **What to Do:** Clearly define roles, responsibilities, and communication guidelines at the start.
- **Why:** Ensures both mentor and mentee understand their commitments and the program's objectives.
- **Expected Outcome:** A structured and efficient mentorship relationship.
- **First Step:** Conduct an orientation session to outline these elements.

### Structuring Sessions
- **What to Do:** Schedule regular, purposeful meetings with a clear agenda.
- **Why:** Consistent interactions foster a productive mentorship environment and allow for timely feedback.
- **Expected Outcome:** Steady progress and a strong mentor-mentee relationship.
- **First Step:** Agree on a regular meeting schedule and prepare agendas in advance.

### Giving Feedback
- **What to Do:** Implement structured feedback mechanisms.
- **Why:** Provides mentees with actionable insights and areas for improvement.
- **Expected Outcome:** Mentees can make informed adjustments and track their development.
- **First Step:** Use feedback forms or tools to facilitate structured feedback sessions.

### Measuring Mentee Progress
- **What to Do:** Utilize the SMART goal-setting framework to track progress.
- **Why:** Ensures goals are clear, measurable, and aligned with professional development.
- **Expected Outcome:** Mentees achieve tangible skill improvements.
- **First Step:** Collaborate with the mentee to set initial SMART goals and establish check-in points.

## Action Items

1. **Orientation Session (Week 1):** Conduct an initial meeting to set expectations and outline program components.
2. **Meeting Schedule (Week 2):** Establish a regular meeting schedule and prepare agendas.
3. **Feedback Mechanism (Week 3):** Develop and implement a structured feedback process.
4. **SMART Goals (Week 4):** Assist mentees in setting SMART goals and plan subsequent check-ins to monitor progress.
