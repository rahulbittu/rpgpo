2. **Deal Flow Sources**: Effective deal flow can be sourced by attending accelerator demo days, joining accelerators as a mentor, building a strong online presence, and using platforms like AngelList and Expedite.Ventures.

3. **Due Diligence**: While specific checklists were not found, evaluating founder-market fit, including domain expertise and complementary skills, is crucial.

## Detailed Analysis
### Minimum Investment Amounts
- **Range**: $25,000 to $250,000 per startup.
- **Return Expectations**: Target annual returns of 20-30% to compensate for the high risk and illiquidity.
- **Investment Horizon**: Expect a 5-10 year period before potential liquidity events.

### Deal Flow Sources
- **Accelerators and Events**: Engage with accelerators by attending demo days and becoming a mentor to access early-stage companies.
- **Online Presence**: Build a following on platforms like X (formerly Twitter) to attract inbound deals.
- **Investment Platforms**: Utilize AngelList and Expedite.Ventures for structured deal flow and community engagement.
- **Communities**: Join angel investor communities for shared insights and opportunities.

### Due Diligence
- **Focus Areas**: Assess the founder's domain expertise and team skills. Look for complementary skills such as technical and commercial acumen.

### Portfolio Strategies
- **Diversification**: Invest in a broad range of startups to mitigate individual investment risks.
- **Risk Management**: Balance high-risk investments with more stable ones to maintain portfolio health.

## Recommended Actions
1. **Set Investment Budget**: Determine a personal investment budget within the $25,000 to $250,000 range per startup, based on financial capacity and risk tolerance.
   - **First Step**: Review personal financials to establish a maximum investment limit.

2. **Enhance Deal Flow**: Actively participate in startup ecosystems by attending events and joining accelerators as a mentor.
   - **First Step**: Register for upcoming accelerator demo days and apply to become a mentor at a local accelerator.

3. **Leverage Platforms**: Create profiles on AngelList and Expedite.Ventures to access a wide array of investment opportunities.
   - **First Step**: Set up an AngelList account and explore current investment opportunities.

4. **Develop Due Diligence Skills**: Focus on understanding founder-market fit and team dynamics.
   - **First Step**: Attend workshops or webinars on startup evaluation and due diligence.

5. **Diversify Portfolio**: Plan to invest in a variety of startups to spread risk.
   - **First Step**: Identify a target number of startups to invest in over the next year and allocate funds accordingly.

By following these actionable steps, tech workers can strategically enter the angel investing space, leveraging their industry knowledge and networks to build a diversified and potentially lucrative investment portfolio.
