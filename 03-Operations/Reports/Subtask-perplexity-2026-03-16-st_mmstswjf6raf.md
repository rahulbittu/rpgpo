## Equipment Prioritization by Budget
For a single-car garage (typically 10x20 ft or ~200 sq ft), prioritize space-saving, foldable items starting with essentials for strength training.

- **$150–$500 (Minimal/Corner Setup, 6x8 ft/48 sq ft):** Rubber stall mats (Tractor Supply, $45 each, 4x6 ft; 3 mats = $135 for 12x6 ft padded area), resistance bands, bodyweight tools. Best for beginners focusing on consistency over heavy loads.[1]
- **$800–$2,500 (Half-Garage, 8x12 ft/96 sq ft):** Fold-back wall-mounted rack (Rogue RML-3W or PRx Profile Rack), adjustable bench (Rep FB-5000 ~$200), adjustable dumbbells (Bowflex 552 or PowerBlock Elite), compact bike for cardio. Position rack opposite garage door for pull depth.[1]
- **Higher Budget Add-Ons:** All-in-one machines like RitFit M1 Series (rack + Smith + cable in one footprint) or ANCORE Pro cable ($750 each; two = $1,500 for 130 lbs resistance, no power needed).[2][3]

## Flooring Solutions
Use interlocking rubber stall mats for drop protection and vehicle compatibility in a shared space.

- Tractor Supply 4x6 ft mats at $45 each; three cover 12x6 ft for $135 total. Provides padding for deadlifts and stalls car tires.[1]
- Stall mats in "Hybrid Lane" for deadlifts/lunges; keep open floor clear for parking.[2]

## Storage Systems
Maximize vertical space and integrate with equipment to preserve parking area.

- **Weight Trees & Rack Attachments:** Store 300+ lbs plates on RitFit M1 rear/side horns or rack-integrated horns for stability; no separate trees needed.[2]
- **Vertical/Wall Systems:** Tiered dumbbell racks, wall hooks for jump ropes/yoga mats, high shelves for cardio/mobility gear. Barbell holders built into all-in-ones.[2]
- **Zone-Specific:** Power Zone (rack/plates on trees), Accessory Zone (tiered racks), Cardio Zone (wall hooks).[2]
- **Mounting:** 3-inch lag screws into wall studs (not drywall anchors) for shear force from plates.[2]

## Programming for Strength and Conditioning
Results emphasize hybrid strength-cardio layouts over full programs; structure around zones for compounds + accessories.

- **Power Zone (Heavy Compounds):** Rack/barbell/plates for squats/deadlifts; 3-5 sets of 3-5 reps.[1][2]
- **Accessory Zone (Hypertrophy/Arms):** Dumbbells/bench; 3 sets of 8-12 reps.[2]
- **Cardio/Mobility Zone:** Jump rope, bike, yoga mat; 20-30 min sessions post-strength.[1][2]
- **Cable Integration:** Add DIY pulley or ANCORE Pro for pulldowns/rows (65 lbs/unit); bands as low-cost alternative.[3]
- **Sample Session:** Warm-up mobility (5 min), compounds (rack, 45 min), accessories (20 min), cardio (15 min). Train 4x/week, rest hybrid lane for car access.[1][2]
