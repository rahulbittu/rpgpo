## Finding 1: 90-Minute Work Sprints Aligned with Ultradian Rhythms for Energy Management
Work in 90-minute focused blocks followed by 10-15 minute renewal breaks involving movement, nature, or social connection to match natural 90-120 minute cycles, preventing energy depletion during long workdays. Limit to 3-4 sprints per day for sustainable performance; forcing more leads to diminishing returns. After three weeks, users report 30-40% increases in deep work output and reduced afternoon crashes.[1]  
**Source:** https://www.lifehack.org/991281/managing-energy-not-time

## Finding 2: Energy ROI Matrix for Task Scheduling During 12-Hour Days
Schedule high-impact, high-energy tasks (e.g., strategic thinking) during peak energy hours; batch high-impact low-energy tasks in medium windows; delegate/eliminate low-impact high-energy traps; automate low-impact tasks during dips. Use 7 minutes of morning planning and 5-minute end-of-day reflection to track drains/energizers and refine iteratively.[1]  
**Source:** https://www.lifehack.org/991281/managing-energy-not-time
