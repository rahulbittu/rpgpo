## Finding 1: Leverage Online Platforms and Thoughtful Direct Messages for Introverts
NPA Worldwide guide from March 9, 2026, recommends introverts use online platforms like LinkedIn for controlled networking: craft concise, personalized direct messages mentioning a specific piece of someone's work and asking one well-thought-out question to spark dialogue, often leading to virtual coffee chats that feel less intimidating than in-person events.[1]  
**Source:** https://npaworldwide.com/blog/2026/03/09/how-to-network-like-a-pro-even-if-youre-an-introvert/

## Finding 2: Set a Goal of Initiating Conversations with Three New People
Hilborn Charity eNews suggests introverts attend learning sessions with peers also seeking knowledge, setting a simple goal like "initiate conversations with three new people" by asking what they've found useful; use a buddy system at events but actively engage beyond sticking together, inspired by a structured approach from the movie *Party Monster*.[2]  
**Source:** https://hilborn-charityenews.ca/networking-strategies-for-people-who-hate-networking/

## Finding 3: Collect Contacts and Follow Up Intentionally
Same Hilborn article advises collecting business cards or using a phone QR code for LinkedIn during conversations, then following up via LinkedIn, email, or text within days: reference where you met, express enjoyment, and propose coffee or an informational interview on a shared interest; afterward, take time to absorb the experience by jotting down thoughts.[2]  
**Source:** https://hilborn-charityenews.ca/networking-strategies-for-people-who-hate-networking/

## Finding 4: Prepare, Arrive Early, and Focus on Depth Over Volume
Workplace Journal article from February 2026 outlines prep steps like researching attendees and businesses; top tips include: (1) arrive early when the room is quieter for easier starts, (2) prioritize one meaningful connection over ten cards, (3) lead with listening to make others feel heard, (4) take short breaks to reset energy, and (5) follow up within 24 hours referencing a specific discussion.[3]  
**Source:** https://workplacejournal.co.uk/2026/02/why-networking-is-harder-for-introverts-but-worth-it/

## Finding 5: Meet Just Three People as an Introvert Strategy
YouTube video "The Art of Networking for Introverts" by Nicholas White promotes a core strategy for introverts: meet just three people at events to build quality connections without overwhelm.[5]  
**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2TWT7zpRRaQ
