## Finding 1: Therapeutic Associates' 5 Bulletproof Ankle Exercises for Preventing Re-Sprains in Runners
Therapeutic Associates outlines evidence-based exercises targeting posterior tibialis, fibularis, calves, and intrinsic foot muscles to rebuild ankle stability post-sprain, essential for trail runners with lateral ankle sprain history. Key exercise: **Banded Posterior Tibialis Strengthening** – sit in figure-four position, loop light resistance band around ball of foot (anchor under planted foot), perform 15-20 slow inversion reps per side, progressing band resistance as strength builds; promotes arch strength and overall stability. Additional protocols include proprioceptive retraining, plyometric progressions, manual therapy for joint stiffness, and graduated loading with sport-specific agility drills. Published 2023 (accessed 2026), emphasizes active restraints over bracing alone.  
**Source:** https://www.therapeuticassociates.com/bulletproof-ankles-how-do-i-prevent-repeating-an-ankle-sprain/[1]

## Finding 2: Runner's World Ankle Injury Prevention Exercises and Evidence
Runner's World (medically reviewed by John Vasudevan, M.D., Penn Medicine Sports Medicine Director, 2024 update) reports 50% of recreational runners face injuries, with ankles common; recommends strength/mobility exercises, proper landing mechanics, and over-the-counter orthotics for excessive pronation (as effective as custom per Reed Ferber, Ph.D., University of Calgary). No specific trail runner exercises listed, but general protocols include form tweaks and mileage management to cut re-injury risk; orthotics advised first for flat arches. Lacks numbered reps/sets or sprain-specific rehab phases.  
**Source:** https://www.runnersworld.com/health-injuries/a70521462/exercises-to-prevent-common-running-injuries/[3]

## Finding 3: Blood Flow Restriction (BFR) Training for Ankle/Athletic Recovery
Physical TheraPT (2025) endorses BFR with cuffs like SAGA/VALD for post-sprain rehab: preserves muscle mass, reduces healing tissue stress, accelerates return-to-sport (e.g., Frontiers in Physiology study, He C et al., March 2025, doi:10.3389/fphys.2025.1683442 on athletic populations; American Journal of Sports Medicine reviews 2020/2024 confirm safety and efficacy vs. standard rehab). Compression stockings and anteroposterior joint mobilization also supported for faster recovery; applicable to runners but not trail-specific. No direct ankle sprain exercise reps provided.  
**Source:** https://www.physicaltherapt.com/field-research-blog[2]
