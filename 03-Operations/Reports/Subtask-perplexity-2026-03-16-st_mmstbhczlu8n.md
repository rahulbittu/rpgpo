## Finding 1: ISO 9241-5:2024 Standard Recommends Top 1/3 of Primary Screen at Eye Level for Neutral Posture
For screen-heavy workers like data entry professionals, position the **top 1/3 of the primary monitor at eye level** to support neutral neck alignment and prevent cumulative flexion, per **ISO 9241-5:2024** workstation standards. In dual setups, center a larger primary monitor (e.g., 27-inch) directly ahead, angle secondary (e.g., 24-inch) at **30-45 degrees** to minimize neck rotation; distance **18-24 inches** for high-PPI screens reduces eye strain.
- **Actionable steps**: Measure eye level seated; adjust via stand/arm; test with 10-minute trial for comfort.
Source: https://eurekaergonomic.com/blogs/eureka-ergonomic-blog/ergonomic-screen-positioning-data-entry[1]

## Finding 2: Arm's Length Distance (20-26 Inches) with 0-10° Downward Gaze Prevents Neck Strain
Position monitor at **arm's length (20-26 inches)** from eyes, top at or slightly below eye level for a **0-10° downward gaze**, aligning with natural eye rest per OSHA guidelines and Mayo Clinic recommendations. Dual monitors: center primary, angle secondary **30 degrees**; forward head posture adds **10 pounds effective neck weight per inch** leaned forward.
- **Actionable steps**: Extend arm to elbow crease for distance check; tilt screen back **10-20 degrees** to cut glare; bifocal users lower 1-2 inches.
Source: https://www.contourdesign.com/workstations/most-ergonomic-desk-setup[3]

## Finding 3: 20-30 Inches Distance with Top at Eye Level Reduces Tech Neck and Eye Fatigue
Screens at **20-30 inches** with top aligned to **natural eye level** (slight downward gaze) prevent unconscious leaning and asymmetric neck strain in dual setups; center seam for equal use or primary monitor ahead with secondary toward dominant eye. Laptop users: raise screen via stand, add external keyboard to avoid wrist/neck trade-off.
- **Actionable steps**: For >20-inch monitors, push back extra 4-6 inches; standardize with adjustable stand like Odinlake for **eye-level lift**.
Source: https://www.doctronic.ai/blog/beyond-the-chair-advanced-office-ergonomics-to-prevent-tech-neck/[7]

## Finding 4: 20-28 Inches (50-70 cm) with Top at or Below Eye Level Cuts Neck Pain
Place screen **20-28 inches (50-70 cm)** away, top edge at or slightly below eye level so gaze hits **1/3 down screen**; dual: side-by-side centered if equal use, primary front if favored. Perpendicular to windows avoids glare; **68-79°F (21-26°C)** room temp supports comfort.
- **Actionable steps**: Laptop fix: **laptop stand + external keyboard**; weekly posture audit for 8+ hour screen workers.
Source: https://kwesk.com/en/blog/ergonomie/ergonomie-poste-de-travail[5]
