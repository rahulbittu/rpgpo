## Finding 1: Omega-3 Fatty Acids, Proteins, Whole Grains, Magnesium, and Antioxidants Support Synaptic Plasticity and Neuronal Growth
Healthy fats like **omega-3s** (from fish, seeds, avocados) improve neuronal growth and reduce inflammation; proteins (amino acids) boost neurotransmitters dopamine/serotonin for mood/cognition; whole grains provide stable glucose for cognitive function; **magnesium** (nuts, whole grains, dark chocolate) enhances synaptic plasticity and stress management; antioxidants (vitamins C/E, polyphenols in berries, citrus, nuts, green tea) protect against oxidative damage and aid neuronal repair. Consistent intake via balanced diet nourishes brain cells for regeneration and fewer illnesses.  
**Source**: https://lonestarneurology.net/others/the-influence-of-diet-and-nutrition-on-brain-plasticity/[1]

## Finding 2: Dietary Polyphenols and Exercise Synergistically Boost BDNF, Neurogenesis, and Reduce Hippocampal Inflammation (Narrative Review, No Specific Date)
Polyphenols/flavonoids (natural bioactives) promote hippocampal neurogenesis, synaptic plasticity, and anti-inflammation via BDNF signaling, oxidative stress reduction, and cytokine modulation; combined with exercise, they enhance cognitive function in aging/neurodegeneration. Preclinical data strongest for synergy; recommends lifestyle integration for preventive brain health without quantified doses.  
**Source**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12969489/[2]

## Finding 3: Mediterranean Diet (MD) Linked to Slower Cognitive Decline and Lower Alzheimer's Risk (Cites 2019-2024 Studies)
**Mediterranean diet** (fruits/veg, whole grains, legumes, olive oil, fish, nuts; moderate wine) slows cognitive decline and cuts AD risk via anti-inflammatory/antioxidant effects, MUFA/PUFA profile, vascular support. Additional benefits from **ketogenic diet** (high fat/protein, low carb) for mitochondrial function/excitotoxicity reduction; plant-based polyphenol-rich diets lower neuroinflammation but need B12/iron/omega-3 supplementation. Antioxidants (vit C/E, polyphenols, selenium), omega-3s, vitamin D target oxidative stress/inflammation. Published 2026.  
**Source**: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/pharmacology/articles/10.3389/fphar.2026.1765786/full[3]

## Finding 4: MIND, Mediterranean, DASH Diets Improve Cognition; Fish > Omega-3 Supplements (Harvard Review, No Specific Date)
**MIND, Mediterranean, DASH diets** (rich in omega-3s from fish like salmon/mackerel, not supplements) associate with better cognitive function and lower decline risk; omega-3s (EPA/DHA) build brain membranes, reduce inflammation—eat 2+ fish servings/week. No RCT evidence for isolated omega-3/fish oil supplements matching whole-diet benefits.  
**Source**: https://www.health.harvard.edu/brain-health/dont-buy-into-brain-health-supplements[4]
