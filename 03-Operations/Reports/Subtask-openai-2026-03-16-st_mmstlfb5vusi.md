## Plan

To optimize mental performance through a morning routine, we will integrate evidence-based practices focusing on wake timing, cold exposure, sunlight, caffeine timing, and movement sequences. Each component is designed to enhance alertness, cognitive function, and overall well-being.

## Implementation Steps

### 1. Wake Timing
- **What to Do**: Use a dynamic wake-time protocol based on circadian rhythm data.
- **Why**: Aligning wake times with your natural circadian rhythm can improve alertness and cognitive function.
- **Expected Outcome**: Improved mental clarity and energy levels throughout the day.
- **First Step**: Implement the CRS App Protocol to adjust your wake times based on real-time sleep data.

### 2. Cold Exposure
- **What to Do**: Incorporate cold showers or cold water immersion for 1-3 minutes.
- **Why**: Cold exposure can increase norepinephrine levels, enhancing mood and focus.
- **Expected Outcome**: Increased alertness and mood stabilization.
- **First Step**: Start with a 30-second cold shower, gradually increasing duration over a week.

### 3. Sunlight Exposure
- **What to Do**: Get at least 20 minutes of sunlight exposure within the first hour of waking.
- **Why**: Morning sunlight exposure helps regulate your circadian rhythm and boosts serotonin production.
- **Expected Outcome**: Better mood and sleep quality.
- **First Step**: Plan a morning walk or outdoor activity shortly after waking.

### 4. Caffeine Timing
- **What to Do**: Delay caffeine intake by 90 minutes after waking.
- **Why**: This timing aligns with natural cortisol peaks, avoiding interference with your body's natural alertness cycle.
- **Expected Outcome**: Sustained energy levels without mid-morning crashes.
- **First Step**: Set a reminder to have your first cup of coffee 90 minutes post-wake-up.

### 5. Movement Sequences
- **What to Do**: Engage in a 5-10 minute morning exercise routine.
- **Why**: Physical activity increases blood flow to the brain, enhancing cognitive performance.
- **Expected Outcome**: Enhanced focus and energy for morning tasks.
- **First Step**: Begin with a simple routine like jumping jacks, push-ups, or yoga stretches.

## Review Schedule

- **Weekly Review**: Assess energy levels and cognitive performance. Adjust wake times and exposure durations as needed.
- **Monthly Check-In**: Evaluate overall routine effectiveness and make necessary adjustments based on personal feedback and performance metrics.

By following this structured morning routine, you can expect to see improvements in mental performance, mood, and overall productivity.
