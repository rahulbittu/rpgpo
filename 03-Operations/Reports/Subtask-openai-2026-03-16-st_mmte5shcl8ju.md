### GP vs LP Roles

- **General Partner (GP):**
  - **Responsibilities:** Manages the syndication, makes decisions, and oversees property management. Typically responsible for sourcing deals, securing financing, and executing the business plan.
  - **Compensation:** Earns a management fee (1-2% of the equity raised) and a share of the profits (often 20-30% after preferred returns are paid to LPs).

- **Limited Partner (LP):**
  - **Responsibilities:** Provides capital for the investment. LPs have limited liability and do not participate in day-to-day management.
  - **Compensation:** Receives preferred returns and a portion of profits after GPs have been compensated.

### Preferred Return Structures

- **Preferred Return:** Typically ranges from 6-10% annually. LPs receive this return before any profit split with GPs.
- **Example:** An 8% preferred return means LPs are paid 8% on their investment before profits are split, often in a 70/30 ratio (LP/GP).

### Waterfall Distributions

- **Structure:** Determines how profits are distributed among investors. Commonly involves:
  - **Return of Capital:** Initial investment returned to LPs.
  - **Preferred Return:** Paid to LPs.
  - **Catch-Up Clause:** GPs receive a portion of profits until they reach a certain percentage.
  - **Split Profits:** Remaining profits split, e.g., 70% to LPs, 30% to GPs.

- **Example:** A deal might return capital to LPs, then pay an 8% preferred return, followed by a 20% catch-up for GPs, and finally split remaining profits 70/30.

### K-1 Tax Implications

- **K-1 Forms:** Issued to LPs, detailing their share of income, deductions, and credits. Important for tax reporting.
- **Passive Loss Limitations:** Losses reported on K-1 can offset other passive income but not active income, unless the investor qualifies as a real estate professional.

### Due Diligence Checklist for Sponsors

- **Track Record:** Review past performance and deal history.
- **Financial Health:** Assess the sponsor's financial stability and capital reserves.
- **Transparency:** Evaluate the clarity and detail of financial projections and business plans.
- **References:** Contact previous investors for feedback on their experience.
- **Legal Review:** Ensure all legal documents are reviewed by a qualified attorney.

## Comparison
