# Emergency Financial Planning: Current Guidance for March 2026

**Build a 3-6 month emergency fund covering essential expenses** (rent/mortgage, utilities, food, insurance, healthcare) in an easily accessible, interest-bearing account.[1][2] This is the foundational first step before investing or paying down debt.[2]

## Key Emergency Fund Targets

**Fund size:** Aim for 3-6 months of essential (non-discretionary) expenses for most people; retirees and those with variable income should target 6-12 months.[8] Calculate this by adding up all non-discretionary monthly expenses and multiplying by your target month range.[6]

**Account type:** Use a Money Market account or high-yield savings account. Specific options mentioned include VIO Bank and Bask Bank, which offer better returns than traditional savings accounts.[5]

**Starting point:** Begin with whatever amount you can spare—$20, $50, or $75 monthly—and automate transfers to ensure consistent contributions.[6][9] Even small amounts compound over time.[1]

1. **Set savings goals** with realistic timelines[6]
