## Key Findings

### Calibration Methods
- **Standardization**: Calibration sessions ensure consistent performance ratings by requiring managers to present evidence for proposed ratings and engage in group discussions to reconcile differences. This process helps in adjusting ratings based on comparisons with other projects and team contributions[1][2].
- **Bias Training**: Managers are trained to recognize and mitigate biases such as recency, halo effect, leniency/strictness, and affinity bias. Regular documentation and specific evidence are emphasized to support ratings[1][2].
- **Integration with OKRs**: For engineering teams, combining Objectives and Key Results (OKRs) with peer feedback and quarterly reviews helps balance quantitative metrics like velocity with qualitative aspects like mentorship and architectural contributions[2].

### Feedback Frameworks
- **Behaviorally Anchored Rating Scales (BARS)**: These scales define specific, observable behaviors for each rating level, providing clear examples of what constitutes different performance levels. This framework helps in setting clear expectations and providing actionable feedback[1].

### Growth Planning Templates
- **SMART Goals**: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound goals are essential for creating effective growth plans. These goals should align with both individual career aspirations and organizational objectives.
- **Development Plans**: Include specific actions, resources needed, timelines, and metrics for success. For example, a plan might include attending a leadership workshop, completing a certification, or leading a cross-functional project.

## Recommendations

1. **Implement Calibration Sessions**: Organize regular calibration sessions to ensure fair and consistent performance ratings across teams. This will help in aligning ratings with organizational standards and reducing bias.
   - **Expected Outcome**: More equitable performance evaluations and improved trust in the review process.
   - **First Step**: Schedule quarterly calibration meetings and train managers on bias recognition.

2. **Adopt BARS for Feedback**: Use Behaviorally Anchored Rating Scales to provide clear, actionable feedback. This will help employees understand specific areas for improvement and recognize their strengths.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Enhanced clarity in performance expectations and improved employee development.
   - **First Step**: Develop BARS specific to engineering roles with input from senior engineers.

3. **Develop Growth Planning Templates**: Create templates for growth plans that include SMART goals and specific development actions. This will support career development and align individual goals with company objectives.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Increased employee engagement and career progression.
   - **First Step**: Design a template and conduct a workshop for managers on how to use it effectively.
