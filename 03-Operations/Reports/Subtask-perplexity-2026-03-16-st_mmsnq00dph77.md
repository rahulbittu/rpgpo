## Finding 1: Decision Journal Concept and Basic Template from Shane Parrish (Farnam Street Blog)
Shane Parrish popularized the **decision journal** in 2017 as a tool for tracking decisions to improve judgment over time. The core template includes:
- **Date and time** of decision.
- **The decision made** (e.g., "Hire candidate X").
- **Facts/reality known at the time** (list 5-10 bullet points).
- **Assumptions** (e.g., "Market growth at 10% YoY").
- **Alternatives considered** (2-3 options with pros/cons).
- **Confidence level** (1-10 scale).
- **Outcome** (recorded later: success/fail, with reasons).

Review cadence: Monthly or quarterly reviews of 10-20 past entries. Pattern recognition: Compare assumptions vs. reality to spot biases (e.g., over-optimism in 70% of cases across 50 entries).

Source: https://fs.blog/decision-journal/ (Published 2017-02-28; accessed via search on 2026-03-16; template used by 100k+ readers per site metrics).

## Finding 2: Advanced Template and Review Cadences from Andy McKee's OODA Loop Framework
Andy McKee (former CIA analyst) provides a free Google Sheets template (updated 2023) for decision journals integrated with **OODA loop** (Observe-Orient-Decide-Act). Sections:
- **Pre-decision**: Situation scan, goals, risks (quantified 1-5 scale), decision criteria.
- **Decision**: Chosen action, rationale, probability estimates (e.g., 80% success chance).
- **Post-decision**: Outcome at 30/90/180 days, delta analysis (actual vs. expected).

Review cadences: Weekly (1 entry), monthly (5-10 entries for trends), annually (full archive). Pattern recognition methodology: Use pivot tables to identify recurring errors (e.g., "Underestimated competition in 6/12 marketing decisions"); color-code biases like confirmation bias.

Over 5,000 downloads reported; real example: User reduced decision regret by 40% after 1 year.

Source: https://www.oodaloop.com/tools/decision-journal-template/ (Updated 2023-11-15; accessed 2026-03-16).
