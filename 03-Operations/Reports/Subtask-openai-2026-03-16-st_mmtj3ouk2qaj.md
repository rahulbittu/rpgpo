- **Expected Outcome**: A balanced term sheet that aligns with both the founder's and investors' interests.

#### Board Composition
- **What to Do**: Establish a board structure that includes both investor representatives and independent directors.
- **Why**: A well-composed board provides strategic guidance and oversight while protecting founder interests.
- **Expected Outcome**: A diverse and effective board that supports company growth and governance.

#### Liquidation Preferences
- **What to Do**: Negotiate liquidation preferences that protect both the founders and investors in exit scenarios.
- **Why**: Liquidation preferences determine the payout order in the event of a company sale or liquidation.
- **Expected Outcome**: A fair agreement that ensures founders receive a reasonable share of proceeds after investors.
- **First Step**: Understand common structures like 1x non-participating preference and discuss these with your legal advisor.

#### Anti-Dilution Protection
- **What to Do**: Address anti-dilution clauses to protect against future down rounds.
- **Why**: Anti-dilution provisions adjust investor equity in the event of a down round, impacting founder ownership.
- **Expected Outcome**: A balanced clause that minimizes founder dilution while satisfying investor protection.
- **First Step**: Familiarize yourself with weighted average and full ratchet provisions, and negotiate terms that align with your long-term goals.
