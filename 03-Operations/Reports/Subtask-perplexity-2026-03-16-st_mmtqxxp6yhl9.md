## Finding 1: Desk-Friendly Seated Shoulder Rolls for Upper Back Tension Release
Seated shoulder rolls target neck and upper back tension from desk work: Lift shoulders toward ears, roll them back, and drop down; repeat 10 times, inhaling on lift and exhaling on drop. This counters rounded posture from prolonged sitting.[1]  
**Source:** https://thespinepro.com/blog/desk-friendly-stretches-for-back-pain-relief/

## Finding 2: Seated Thoracic Spinal Twist for Spine Decompression
Perform a seated spinal twist: Sit upright, place right hand on back of chair, rotate torso right while keeping hips forward; hold briefly, repeat on left side. Designed for desk workers to decompress lower spine and relax tight back muscles; hold seated forward fold variant for 20-30 seconds by hinging forward at hips with arms hanging.[1]  
**Source:** https://thespinepro.com/blog/desk-friendly-stretches-for-back-pain-relief/
