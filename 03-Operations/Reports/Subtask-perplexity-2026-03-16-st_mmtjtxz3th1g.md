## Finding 2: Water Heater Maintenance Protocol

**Annual flushing requirement:** Flush your water heater **once per year** to remove sediment buildup that reduces efficiency.[1] If you have **hard water in your area, flush twice annually**.[1]

**Temperature setting:** Set your water heater to **48 degrees Celsius** (approximately 118°F) per Australian Standard AS/NZS 3500—hot enough for household needs while preventing scalding and reducing energy consumption.[3]

**Warning signs requiring professional service:**
- Rusty or discolored hot water
- Unusual noises from the tank
- Inconsistent water temperature
- Water pooling around the unit[4]

## Finding 3: Leak Detection & Prevention (Monthly Task)

**Inspection frequency:** Check for leaks **at least once monthly** under sinks, around toilets, and at faucet handles.[1][3]

**What to look for:**
- Water stains, dampness, or puddles under sinks and toilets
- Leaking handles when turning water off (described as "sneaky culprits")[1]
- Water stains under pipes while water runs[1]

**Impact:** Unnoticed leaks waste significant water, increase utility bills, cause water damage, promote mold growth, and can lead to costly repairs if left unchecked.[5]

## Finding 4: Drain Prevention & Professional Cleaning

**Precision maintenance approach:** Use **camera inspections and hydro jetting** for targeted drain maintenance rather than reactive emergency repairs.[2]

**How hydro jetting works:** Calibrated water pressure clears the entire pipe wall diameter—not just poking a hole through clogs—which:
- Reduces repeat visits by fully clearing pipes
- Restores design flow, lowering pump strain and extending system life
- Supports accurate planning for future repairs[2]

**When to schedule:** For kitchens, food-service facilities, and older residential stacks, **jetting at set intervals is often more cost-effective than waiting for emergencies**.[2]

**Benefits of camera inspection:** Shows whether the issue is sludge, scale, offsets, or root intrusion, enabling informed repair planning.[2]

## Finding 5: Pipe Insulation & Winterization

**Outdoor fixture shutdown:** Shut off the water supply to outdoor fixtures—hose bibs, faucets, sprinkler systems, and backflow preventers—and **drain and insulate them in the fall before freezing temperatures start**.[1]

**Faucet dripping strategy:** Faucets requiring dripping are determined by location:
- Faucets attached to exposed pipes
- Faucets near poorly insulated walls
- Each showerhead in bathrooms
- At least one faucet in each section of your home[1]

**Spring inspection for winter damage:** Winter weather causes pipes to expand and contract, potentially creating small cracks or weakened joints.[4] Inspect outdoor hose bibs, irrigation connections, garden hoses, and outdoor drains for leaks or weak water flow.[4]

## Finding 6: Toilet & Sump Pump Maintenance

**Sump pump testing procedure:**
1. Pour a bucket of water into the sump pit
2. Check if the float switch activates the pump
3. Ensure water drains properly away from your home

If the pump doesn't turn on or struggles to remove water, it needs professional servicing or replacement.[4]

## Finding 7: Advanced Repair Options (Trenchless Methods)

**Trenchless sewer line replacement:** If your property has slow drains, recurring backups, or known root intrusion near the main line, **trenchless sewer line replacement restores flow with minimal surface disruption**.[2]

**Advantages over traditional digging:**
- Faster repair timelines
- Eliminates collateral damage to yards, driveways, or commercial entryways
- Reduces material waste and restoration costs
- Uses pipe bursting, lining, and targeted segment replacement through small access points[2]

## Finding 8: When to Call a Professional

**Call a plumber when you notice:**
- Persistent leaks despite visual inspection
- Rusty or discolored hot water
- Unusual tank noises or temperature inconsistencies
- Water pooling around the water heater
- Recurring drain backups or slow drains
- Sump pump failure or weak performance
- Weak outdoor water flow after winter[4]

**Professional assessment value:** A local plumber can assess your specific plumbing setup, recommend a customized maintenance schedule, identify corrosion or blockages before they escalate, and provide advice on proper usage and care.[5]

## Actionable Maintenance Calendar

- **Monthly:** Visual leak inspections under sinks, toilets, and around faucets
- **Seasonally (Fall):** Shut off and drain outdoor fixtures; insulate exposed pipes
- **Annually:** Flush water heater; schedule professional inspection (or bi-annually for newer systems)
- **Every 2 Years:** Comprehensive professional plumbing inspection
- **As needed:** Drain camera inspection and hydro jetting for recurring issues
