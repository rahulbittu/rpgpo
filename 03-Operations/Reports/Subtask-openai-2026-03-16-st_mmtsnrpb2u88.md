## Plan (Week-by-Week)

### Week 1-4: Base Building Phase
- **Objective:** Establish a strong foundation with increased volume and technique focus.
- **Frequency:** 4 days/week
  - **Day 1:** Deadlift (3x5), Squat (3x5), Assistance (e.g., Romanian Deadlift 3x8)
  - **Day 2:** Upper Body Maintenance (Bench Press 3x5, Pull-ups 3x8)
  - **Day 3:** Deadlift Speed Work (5x3 @ 60% 1RM), Technique Drills
  - **Day 4:** Deadlift Block Pulls (3x5), Squat Assistance (e.g., Front Squat 3x8)
- **Progression:** Increase volume weekly by adding 1 set to main lifts.
- **Safety:** Focus on form, use submaximal weights to avoid overtraining.

### Week 5-8: Specialization Phase
- **Objective:** Peak deadlift strength through extreme specialization.
- **Frequency:** 4 days/week
  - **Day 1:** Deadlift Heavy (5x3, build weights weekly)
  - **Day 2:** Upper Body Maintenance (Bench Press 3x5, Rows 3x8)
  - **Day 3:** Deadlift Speed Work (5x3 @ 70% 1RM), Technique Refinement
  - **Day 4:** Deadlift Block Pulls (4x3), Squat (3x5)
- **Progression:** Use the Eddy Coan method—incrementally increase weights each week.
- **Safety:** Incorporate easier days to manage fatigue, avoid failure.

### Week 9-10: Deload and Assessment
- **Objective:** Recovery and performance assessment.
- **Frequency:** 3 days/week
  - **Day 1:** Light Deadlift (3x5 @ 50% 1RM), Mobility Work
  - **Day 2:** Upper Body Light (Bench Press 3x5 @ 50% 1RM)
  - **Day 3:** Technique Drills, Active Recovery
- **Safety:** Prioritize recovery, assess progress, plan next cycle.

## Evidence Basis

- **Extreme Specialization (Andy Bolton):** Focuses on deadlift with varied intensity and volume to boost strength without overtraining. Suitable for recreational lifters due to manageable workload and focus on technique (Source: StrongFirst).
- **Progressive Overload:** Gradual increase in volume and frequency enhances hypertrophy and work capacity, minimizing injury risk (Source: Fick PT and Performance).
- **Conjugate Method:** Rotating effort methods weekly prevents accommodation, ensuring continuous strength gains (Source: Westside Barbell).

## Progression

- **Weeks 1-4:** Build volume and technique, foundational strength.
- **Weeks 5-8:** Intensify focus on deadlift, peak strength.
- **Weeks 9-10:** Recovery, assess gains, plan adjustments.

## Safety Notes

- **Form and Technique:** Crucial throughout to prevent injury.
- **Avoid Failure:** Use submaximal weights, especially on harder days.
- **Monitor Fatigue:** Adjust intensity based on recovery and performance.
- **Consultation:** Consider consulting a coach for personalized adjustments.

This program leverages periodization techniques to safely and effectively enhance deadlift performance while maintaining overall strength and conditioning.
