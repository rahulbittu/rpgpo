## Challenges in Time Management for Makers Attending Meetings

Makers (solo builders like indie developers or creators) face disruptions from meetings that fragment focus time, with common issues including unexpected overruns, context-switching costs, and rigid schedules that ignore real-time changes.[1]  
- **Meeting overruns derail deep work:** A meeting running long can cascade into the day, as non-adaptive systems fail to reschedule tasks automatically.[1]  
- **Lack of buffers amplifies stress:** Without 10-15 minute gaps between blocks, small interruptions snowball, making it hard to protect maker time for building.[1]  
- **Reactive tasks invade focus:** Emails and calls from meetings spill over, interrupting high-value deep work unless batched into dedicated slots.[1]

## Best Practices and Strategies

**Adaptive time-blocking** tops recommendations: Use tools that auto-adjust schedules for meeting changes, prioritizing 1-3 deep work tasks during peak energy (e.g., mornings).[1]  
- **Build buffers:** Insert 10-15 minute gaps post-meetings for transitions, mental resets, or overruns to maintain momentum.[1]  
- **Daily reviews:** 5-minute morning adjustment of blocks based on meeting priorities; evening reflection on what failed to refine tomorrow.[1]  
- **Batch reactive work:** Group post-meeting emails/calls into 30-60 minute slots, reserving mornings for uninterrupted making.[1]  

**Pomodoro Technique** for post-meeting recovery: Break maker tasks into 25-minute sprints (Pomodoro sessions) with 5-minute breaks; aim for 16 sessions in an 8-hour day but buffer extras for safety.
