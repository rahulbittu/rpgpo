## Foliage Viewing Spots
- **Kancamagus Highway (New Hampshire)**: A premier scenic drive through White Mountain National Forest with storybook fall colors; peak foliage typically early to mid-October.[4][6]
- **Route 100 (Vermont)**: Iconic byway from southern to northern Vermont, renowned for vibrant foliage; aim for first or second week of October in southern areas, last week of September to first week of October in north.[3][4]
- **Smuggler’s Notch Road (Vermont Rte 108)**: 15-mile mountain pass with boulders, waterfalls, and hiking like Barnes Camp Trail (easy views) or Sterling Pond Trail (3 miles); gondola at Mt. Mansfield open until mid-October.[2]
- **Franconia Notch State Park (New Hampshire)**: Features river gorges, The Basin (15-foot-deep ice age bowl), waterfalls, glacial boulders; adjacent Loon Mountain gondola for elevated foliage views.[1][9]
- **Lost River Gorge & Boulder Caves (New Hampshire)**: 1-mile boardwalk loop with caves, treehouse, viewpoints amid foliage; advance reservations required as fall days sell out.[1]
