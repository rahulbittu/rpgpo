## Finding 1: 2026 VSP Vision Care Workplace Vision Health Report Reveals 100 Hours Weekly Screen Time for Desk Workers, Driving Dry Eye Risks
Desk workers average nearly **100 hours of screen time per week**, with **93% of weekday waking hours** on screens, increasing dry eye syndrome risks via reduced blinking and tear film disruption; **1 in 4 employees** take time off due to digital eye strain, averaging **4.5 days/year**. Only **32%** of workers report employer guidance on setups like lighting or ergonomics to minimize strain. Prevent Blindness resources for March 2026 Workplace Eye Wellness Month include fact sheets on digital devices and eyes.
- **Actionable steps**: Use artificial tears; blink frequently; optimize workstation with screen at arm’s length, below eye level.
Source: https://preventblindness.org/workplace-eye-wellness-month-2026/[2]

## Finding 2: 20-20-20 Rule Reduces Dry Eye by Relaxing Muscles; Blink Rate Drops by Half on Screens
Follow the **20-20-20 rule**—every **20 minutes**, look **20 feet** away for **20 seconds**—to relax eye muscles and combat dry eyes from reduced blinking (drops by half during screen use, thinning tear film). West Georgia Eye Care and Lakeville Family Eye Care recommend combining with frequent full blinks, screen at arm’s length/slightly below eye level, and anti-reflective coatings. Glare from lights/windows worsens evaporation.
- **Actionable steps**: Set timers/apps for breaks; position screen to encourage downward gaze (reduces blinking inhibition); take breaks away from screens.
Source: https://wgecc.com/work-smart-see-better-eye-wellness-in-the-modern-workplace/[1]

## Finding 3: Humidifiers, Hydration, and Artificial Tears Counter Screen + Dry Air Effects on Tear Production
Screens slow blink rate, causing tear evaporation and dry eye symptoms like burning/grittiness after prolonged focus; add **humidifiers** at work/home, drink water throughout day, and use **lubricating drops** (artificial tears) matched to symptoms. Mountain West Eye Care notes arid climates + screens accelerate this for office workers; Innovative Eye NJ links air conditioning to worsened dryness.
- **Actionable steps**: Install desk humidifier; apply drops 4x/day; track hydration (aim 8 glasses water).
Source: https://www.mountainwesteyecare.com/article/dry-eye-syndrome-how-screen-time-climate-worsen-symptoms/[5]

## Finding 4: Annual Eye Exams Detect Dry Eye Early; Only 25% of Workers Feel Employers Prioritize Eye Health
**Routine eye exams** identify dry eye, strain, or prescription needs for screen workers; VSP’s 2026 report shows **25%** of workers believe companies care greatly about eye health, despite **93% weekday screen time**. Prevent Blindness urges exams + employer support like ergonomic setups during March 2026 Workplace Eye Wellness Month.
- **Actionable steps**: Schedule comprehensive exam (detects tear film issues); request workplace adjustments like glare filters.
Source: https://ophthalmologymanagement.com/news/2026/prevent-blindness-announces-workplace-eye-wellness-month[3]

## Finding 5: Workstation Optimization Cuts Glare and Fatigue; Dry Air Weakens Tear Film
Position screen at **arm’s length**, slightly below eye level; adjust brightness/contrast; reduce glare with filters/lighting. Lakeville Eye Care reports dry air + high screens reduce blinking, leading to afternoon dryness; posture affects neck/eye strain.
- **Actionable steps**: Angle gaze downward 15-20°; use anti-glare screen; elevate humidity >40%.
Source: https://www.lakevilleeye.com/workplace-eye-wellness-month-how-to-ease-eye-strain-at-work/[4]
