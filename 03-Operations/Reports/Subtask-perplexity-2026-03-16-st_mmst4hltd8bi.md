## Finding 1: Fixture Selection Best Practices
- **Frameless glass enclosures** are the gold standard for 2026 NJ bathroom renovations, combining accessibility and luxury without conflict; standard or semi-frameless options recommended over custom to avoid doubling costs ($2000–$12000 total tub-to-shower conversion range).[2][3]

## Finding 2: Tile Layout Design Best Practices
- Use **large-format porcelain (12×24 or 24×24 inches)** on shower walls, **2×2 mosaic or small hex** on floors for slip resistance on sloped pans; hand-painted tiles, natural stone with visible texture, micro-cement, and brushed finishes gain popularity for texture/depth.[1][2]
- **Epoxy or stain-proof grout** (e.g., Laticrete SpectraLOCK) essential in hard water areas like Wayne, Clifton, Parsippany, Bergen County NJ to prevent staining; standard cement grout fails over time.[2]
- Layout tip: Prioritize faster-install materials like acrylic panels over tile to cut labor (51% of costs); tile labor often exceeds material costs despite lower per sq ft price.[3]

## Finding 3: Waterproofing Best Practices
- Apply **full Schluter KERDI waterproofing membrane** to all shower walls and pan substrate after plumbing rough-in (drain at 48 inches for valve, 78–80 inches for showerhead); prevents mold/water damage found in 15–20 year old improper installs.[2]
- Inspect walls pre-demolition for hidden damage; common in 70% of cost overruns from materials/fixtures tied to water issues.[3]
