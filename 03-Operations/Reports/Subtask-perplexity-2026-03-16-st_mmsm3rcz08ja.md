## Finding 2: Property Type Criteria
- **Single-family homes**: Simpler management, easier financing via BTL loans, stable capital growth in strong cities; lower regulatory complexity than HMOs.[2][6]
- **Multi-unit flats/blocks (MUFBs)**: 7-10% gross yields, scalable for portfolios, lower per-unit management; min unit values apply for specialist mortgages (e.g., 8-unit Midlands block).[2]
- **Semi-commercial/mixed-use**: 8-10% yields with diversification (dual income reduces risk), tax perks like no SDLT surcharge; suits balanced growth-income investors.[2]
- **HMOs**: Highest cashflow for central locations but high management intensity; target well-located for max income relative to capital.[2]
- **Short-term rentals (STRs)**: Prioritize year-round tourism spots; larger properties (e.g., 3+ beds) for groups/families if market data supports revenue vs. costs; arbitrage viable if STR income >138% of long-term rent.[5][7][9]
- **Energy-efficient upgrades**: Smart thermostats, LED lighting, insulation command 5-10% rent premiums, attract tenants (20% surge in interest 2025).[6]

## Finding 3: Market Trends and Financial Screening Rules
- **Yield benchmarks**: Gross 7-10% typical (net after costs); Price-to-Rent Multiple (GRM) = purchase price / annual net rent (lower is better, compare locally).[2][3]
- **Quick rules**: 1% rule (monthly rent ≥1% of purchase price); 50% rule (operating expenses ~50% gross rent excl. mortgage for NOI estimate); Cap rate (NOI/property value); Cash-on-cash (annual cash flow/total cash invested).[4]
- **2026 trends**: Single-family rentals up 4.4% YoY prices, 71% landlord optimism; focus revenue optimization on existing properties, sustainability for premiums; declining rates boost valuations.[4][5][6]
- **Screening consistency**: Non-negotiable tenant criteria (e.g., 650+ credit, 3x rent income) for low turnover (40-60% fewer issues); CMA on 5-10 comps for pricing (slightly below market for faster leasing).[1]
