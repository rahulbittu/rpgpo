- **Morning**: Visit the Geysir geothermal area.
  - **Photography Focus**: Capture Strokkur geyser eruptions with steam against the snow.
  - **Camera Settings**: Fast shutter speed (1/1000s) to freeze motion, ISO 400-800.
- **Afternoon**: Head to Gullfoss Waterfall.
  - **Photography Focus**: Shoot the partially frozen waterfall with mist and potential rainbows.
  - **Camera Settings**: ISO 100-200, aperture f/11, use a polarizing filter to manage reflections.
- **Evening**: Return to Reykjavik for overnight stay.

### Day 3: Snæfellsnes Peninsula and Kirkjufell
- **Morning**: Drive to Snæfellsnes Peninsula.
- **Afternoon**: Photograph Kirkjufell Mountain.

  - **Camera Settings**: ISO 100, aperture f/8, use ND filter for long exposure effects.
- **Evening**: Stay in a local guesthouse and prepare for aurora shots.
  - **Activity**: Northern Lights photography with Kirkjufell as a foreground.
  - **Camera Settings**: ISO 1600-3200, aperture f/2.8, 15-25 second exposure.

### Day 4: Jökulsárlón Lagoon and Diamond Beach
- **Morning**: Travel to Jökulsárlón Lagoon.
  - **Photography Focus**: Icebergs and reflections in the lagoon.
  - **Camera Settings**: ISO 100, aperture f/11, use a polarizer.
- **Afternoon**: Visit Diamond Beach.
  - **Photography Focus**: Ice chunks on black sand with aurora potential.
  - **Camera Settings**: ISO 200, aperture f/8, use a wide-angle lens.
- **Evening**: Stay in a nearby accommodation.

### Day 5: Hornstrandir Nature Reserve (Optional)

  - **Camera Settings**: ISO 1600-3200, aperture f/2.8, 10-20 second exposure.
- **Evening**: Return to Reykjavik for departure.
