## Finding 1: Core Workout Frequency for General Health and Beginners
BUBS Naturals recommends **2-3 core sessions per week** for general health, posture improvement, or beginners, using 10-15 minutes of focused exercises at session ends. This provides muscle tone and spinal stability without overtraining; include at least 1-2 full rest days weekly, especially with heavy full-body training like squats.[1]  
**Source:** https://www.bubsnaturals.com/blogs/workouts-and-training/optimal-frequency-how-many-times-a-week-should-you-do-core-workouts

## Finding 2: Frequency by Experience Level
**Beginners: 2 days/week** to adapt without excessive soreness; **intermediate: 3 days/week**; **advanced: 4-6 days/week** with varied intensity (e.g., heavy weighted crunches Monday, isometric planks Tuesday). Avoid daily high-intensity to prevent fatigue impacting other lifts.[2]  
**Source:** https://www.bubsnaturals.com/blogs/workouts-and-training/how-many-days-a-week-should-i-do-core-workouts

## Finding 3: Sample Weekly Schedule with Rest Days
Monday: Full-body strength + 10 min core (spinal flexion).  
Tuesday: Active recovery (walking/yoga).  
Wednesday: 15 min core (rotation/anti-rotation) + cardio.  
Thursday: Full-body strength + 10 min core (planks).  
Friday: Active recovery/mobility.  
Saturday: Outdoor activity + 5 min core finisher.  
Sunday: Full rest. This hits core directly 3x/week with 2 recovery days.[2]  
**Source:** https://www.bubsnaturals.com/blogs/workouts-and-training/how-many-days-a-week-should-i-do-core-workouts

## Finding 4: Rest Day Activities and Volume Guidelines
On rest days, do active recovery (20-40 min low-intensity cardio), mobility/foam rolling, or light bodyweight practice; include complete rest if fatigued. Core volume: beginners 8-12 sets/week, intermediate 12-18, advanced 16-25, spread over 2-3 sessions.[3]  
**Source:** https://setgraph.app/es/ai-blog/muscle-group-workout-schedule-weekly-splits

## Finding 5: Deload for Recovery
Incorporate deload weeks every 4-6 weeks, reducing volume/intensity by 40-60% for one week to cut fatigue; pair with 1 full rest day in heavy 5-6 day splits.[6]  
**Source:** https://www.mensjournal.com/health-fitness/a-trainers-guide-to-recovery-periodization
