## Finding 1: Portfolio Management Tools for Angel Investors
AngelList and Carta are primary tools for tracking angel portfolios, centralizing deal terms, ownership stakes, cap table updates, follow-on funding, and founder communications. AngelList manages $171B in assets across ~25k investment vehicles as of recent data, handling complexities like valuation markups, side letters, and membership changes[4]. Carta streamlines cap table management, SPV accounting, tax compliance, investor subscriptions, and waterfall calculations for venture deals[1][8].

**Specific features and pricing:**
- **AngelList**: Fund administration with automated ledgers for portfolio monitoring; used in over half of top-tier U.S. VC deals[4].
- **Carta**: Supports SPV formation automation and portfolio monitoring; part of a $1.04B VC management software market projected for 2026[8].
- **Excel-based trackers**: Customized for KPIs, valuation updates, and cash flow; free but manual[1].

## Finding 2: Startup Databases for Portfolio Tracking and Monitoring
Crunchbase, PitchBook, Tracxn, Dealroom, and AngelList enable ongoing portfolio tracking with real-time updates on funding rounds, valuations, and benchmarks. PitchBook provides cap table data, deal comparables, and financial modeling for due diligence; AngelList integrates discovery, execution, and monitoring for seed-stage portfolios[3]. These tools support full lifecycle tracking, with many investors combining them (e.g., Crunchbase for scanning + PitchBook for depth)[3].

**Pricing and focus (2026 data):**
- **AngelList**: Early-stage focus, accessible for angels[3].
- **PitchBook** (Morningstar-owned): Deep private market data[3].
- **Usage tip**: Pair with networks for qualitative insights[3].

**Source:** https://qubit.capital/blog/startup-databases-investors[3]
