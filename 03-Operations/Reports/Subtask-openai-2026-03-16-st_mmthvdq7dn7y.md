## Detailed Analysis

### Weatherstripping
Weatherstripping is a cost-effective solution for reducing drafts from operable windows. However, it should be combined with other sealing methods for comprehensive weatherproofing, especially in older homes where structural air leaks are common.

### Insulation: Spray Foam vs. Fiberglass
- **Spray Foam**: Offers an R-value of approximately 6.5 per inch, making it highly effective for energy savings. It expands to fill gaps, providing excellent air sealing. However, it is more costly, with prices ranging from $0.44 to $0.65 per board foot.
- **Fiberglass**: Offers an R-value of about 2.2 to 2.7 per inch. It is less expensive, with costs around $0.50 to $1.50 per square foot, but requires precise installation to avoid gaps and air leaks.

### Storm Windows
Storm windows provide an additional layer of insulation and can significantly reduce energy loss. Interior storm windows are easier to manage but may not withstand harsh weather as well as exterior options.

### Thermal Curtains
While specific data on 2026 effectiveness is unavailable, thermal curtains are generally known to reduce heat loss by up to 25% when properly installed, making them a worthwhile investment for drafty windows.
