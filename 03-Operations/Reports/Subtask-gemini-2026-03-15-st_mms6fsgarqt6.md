## Strategic Recommendations: Building Psychological Safety in Engineering 1-on-1s

This report outlines strategic recommendations for fostering psychological safety within engineering team 1-on-1 meetings, drawing directly from the provided subtask results. The focus is on actionable steps with quantified impacts where possible, presented in a comparative format.

### Analysis of Psychological Safety Drivers in 1-on-1s

Based on the prior subtask results, the following elements are critical for building psychological safety:

*   **Open and Non-Judgmental Inquiry:** The ability to explore root causes of underperformance or challenges without fear of immediate reprisal is paramount. This requires managers to ask open-ended questions and create a safe space for honest discussion. (Finding 1)
*   **Supportive Environment for Growth and Challenges:** Engineers need to feel that their managers are invested in their career development, are available to help navigate blockers, and will address performance concerns constructively and privately. (Finding 2)
*   **Clear Expectations and Timely, Kind Feedback:** Knowing what is expected and receiving feedback promptly, delivered privately and with kindness, reduces anxiety and builds trust. (Finding 1)
*   **Confidentiality and Defined Success:** Explicitly setting expectations around confidentiality and how success in the 1-on-1 relationship will be defined is crucial for building trust, especially in virtual/hybrid settings. (Finding 3)
*   **Consistent Follow-Up and Momentum:** Demonstrating that discussions lead to action and progress, through micro-goals and reviews, reinforces that the engineer's input is valued and acted upon. (Finding 3)
