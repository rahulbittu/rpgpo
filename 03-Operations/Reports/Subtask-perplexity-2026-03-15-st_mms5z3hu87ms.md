## Finding 1: 72-Hour Post-Trip Recalibration Period to Avoid Major Decisions and Burnout
Sharleen recommends a **72-hour post-trip period** where no major life decisions are made; prioritize sleep, nutrition, and gentle movement to manage physiological "fog" and prevent malaise from rushing back to work. Louis-Philippe describes this as a natural recalibration needing slower re-entry, with strategies like quieter mornings, fewer inputs, journaling notes, and touch-basing with friends to ease isolation—harder without social integration.

## Finding 2: Mental Integration and Habit Extension from Vacation Experiences
Mentally integrate vacation lessons by asking what was learned, how it shaped you, and sharing photos/stories with friends; continue one trail rhythm (e.g., reflective walks) for a week to sustain adventure momentum and combat post-trip blues. From longevity vacations, experts like Emily Laurence, Jessica Tenzer, and Duffy emphasize bringing home **hyper-specific habits** (e.g., Duffy added weekly saunas post-Scandinavia trip; author gained a 100-day protocol from The Proper/Hundred retreat) for daily integration to avoid exhaustion.
