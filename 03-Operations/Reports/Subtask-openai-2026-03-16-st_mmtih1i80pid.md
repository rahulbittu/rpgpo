## Plan

### Week-by-Week Nutrition Periodization

#### Weeks 1-4: Base Phase
- **Carb Cycling:**
  - **Moderate Carb Days (3-5g/kg):** Focus on building an aerobic base. Aim for 210-350g carbs/day for a 70kg runner.
  - **Example Foods:** Whole grains, fruits, vegetables, and legumes.
- **Pre/Post Workout Nutrition:**
  - **Pre-Workout (1-2 hours before):** 30-60g of easily digestible carbs (e.g., banana, oatmeal).
  - **Post-Workout (within 30 minutes):** 20g protein and 60g carbs (e.g., protein shake with fruit).
- **Hydration:**
  - Drink 500ml of water 2 hours before workouts.
  - Consume 150-250ml every 20 minutes during exercise.
- **Supplements:**
  - Consider a daily multivitamin and omega-3 supplement.

#### Weeks 5-10: Build Phase
- **Carb Cycling:**
  - **High Carb Days (5-7g/kg):** After long runs and intense workouts. Aim for 350-490g carbs/day.
  - **Moderate Carb Days (3-5g/kg):** On recovery and easy run days.
  - **Low Carb Days (2-3g/kg):** On rest days.
- **Pre/Post Workout Nutrition:**
  - **Pre-Workout:** Increase to 60-90g carbs for high-intensity days.
  - **Post-Workout:** Maintain 20g protein and increase to 80g carbs.
- **Hydration:**
  - Continue with the base phase strategy; add electrolytes during long runs.
- **Supplements:**
  - Introduce BCAAs before intense workouts to aid recovery.

#### Week 11: Taper Week
- **Carb Cycling:**
  - Gradually increase carbs to 7-10g/kg as race day approaches for glycogen supercompensation.
- **Race Week Fueling:**
  - **3 Days Before Race:** Increase carb intake to 490-700g/day.
  - **Day Before Race:** Focus on easily digestible carbs and avoid high-fiber foods.
- **Hydration:**
  - Ensure adequate hydration; aim for pale yellow urine.
  - Avoid alcohol and caffeine.
- **Supplements:**
  - Continue with multivitamins and omega-3s; consider beetroot juice for nitrate boost.

## Evidence Basis

- **Carb Cycling Protocol:** Based on research indicating glycogen replenishment is crucial post high-intensity workouts (Jeukendrup, 2017).
- **Pre/Post Workout Nutrition:** Timing and macronutrient balance are critical for recovery and performance (Ivy & Portman, 2004).
- **Hydration Strategy:** Proper hydration is essential to maintain performance and prevent dehydration (Sawka et al., 2007).
- **Race Week Fueling:** Carb loading effectively increases muscle glycogen stores (Burke et al., 2011).

## Progression

- **Monitor and Adjust:** Track energy levels, performance, and recovery to adjust carb intake as needed.
- **Trial Runs:** Practice race day nutrition and hydration during long training runs to ensure no gastrointestinal distress.

## Safety Notes

- **Individual Needs:** Adjust carb intake based on personal tolerance and energy needs.
- **Medical Conditions:** Consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement or drastic dietary changes, especially if you have pre-existing health conditions.
- **Hydration Risks:** Overhydration can lead to hyponatremia; balance fluid intake with electrolyte consumption.

This plan provides a structured approach to nutrition periodization for recreational athletes preparing for a half marathon, ensuring optimal performance and recovery.
