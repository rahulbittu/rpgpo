## Noise Exposure Guidelines for Headphone Users
Keep headphone volume levels below **60% of maximum** to prevent hearing damage, as recommended by the World Health Organization (WHO) for safe listening[1][5]. Use the WHO hearWHO app for annual self-checks; scores below 50 warrant professional evaluation[1].

## Tinnitus Prevention Strategies
Prevent noise-induced hearing loss, a common tinnitus cause, through **safe listening strategies** limiting exposure to loud recreational sounds and occupational noise conservation programs[5]. Additional measures include immunization, good maternal/childcare, genetic counseling, ear infection management, and rational use of ototoxic medications[5]. Early intervention reduces risks, with unaddressed hearing loss linked to over **8% of dementia cases** in older adults[1].

## Ear Hygiene Best Practices
Manage common ear conditions like otitis media (middle-ear infections) through early screening and treatment, preventing nearly **60% of childhood hearing loss**[1][5]. Promptly treat infections and avoid untreated ear issues from trauma or medications[6]. No specific cleaning techniques detailed; focus on professional management of infections and safe listening[6][7].

## Recommended Frequency for Hearing Tests
Request hearing tests for **newborns, school-age children, and adults over age 50**; check annually via WHO hearWHO app, seeking professional care if score <50[1]. Baseline checks with licensed specialists recommended for all[1].

## Concert Protection Strategies
Use **earplugs in loud environments** like concerts to protect hearing[1]. WHO emphasizes recreational noise reduction via safe listening and hearing conservation programs[5]. Hoorveilig campaign promotes noise awareness with ear protection across generations[4].
