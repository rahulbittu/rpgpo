## Finding 1: 1-on-1 Meeting Cadence and Structure for Engineering Managers
Engineering Managers should schedule **fortnightly 1:1s** (every two weeks) for remote teams, keeping them short and valuable to avoid meeting fatigue; use them to explore root causes of underperformance with open, non-judgmental questions after confirming clear expectations and recent feedback have been communicated. Deliver tough feedback privately within **48 hours** of the event, being direct but kind, then listen, agree on actionable next steps, and follow up in the next 1:1.  
Source: https://www.em-tools.io/managing-teams

## Finding 2: Core Activities in Regular 1-on-1 Meetings
Engineering Managers spend significant time in **regular one-to-one meetings** focused on coaching, mentoring, supporting career development, addressing performance concerns early, and helping engineers navigate challenges or blockers; this builds delivery by ensuring engineers feel supported, challenged, and clear on expectations.  
Source: https://www.platform-recruitment.com/blog/what-does-an-engineering-manager-typically-do-in-a-day-

## Finding 3: Follow-Up and Momentum in Mentoring-Style 1-on-1s
In long-term 1:1 relationships, set micro-goals and reviews to discuss changes since last interaction, create clear actions or next steps between meetings, and refer back to stated goals to maintain momentum toward outcomes; for virtual/hybrid, explicitly set expectations, confidentiality, cadence, and success definitions upfront.  
Source: https://chronus.com/blog/the-mentor-definition-for-2026-and-the-future-of-mentorship-for-leaders
