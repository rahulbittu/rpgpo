## Influence Maps and Stakeholder Management Best Practices

Influence maps visualize stakeholder power, interest, and relationships using a **power-interest grid** (high/low power vs. high/low interest) to prioritize engagement: manage high-power/high-interest closely, monitor low-power/low-interest.[1]

### Creating Influence Maps: Specific Strategies
- **Map stakeholders by attributes**: Plot on a 2x2 grid based on power (decision-making authority) and interest (project impact). High-power/high-interest need close management; low-power/low-interest require minimal monitoring. Prepare for category shifts in dynamic environments like ecommerce expansion.[1]
- **Ecommerce-specific example**: For platforms expanding market reach (e.g., increased suppliers/customers), enhance analysis to handle diverse groups like in luxury brand or electronics scenarios, identifying risks (strategic/financial).[1]
- **Iterative process**: Reevaluate maps periodically for attitude/impact changes, aligning with operational excellence frameworks.[1]

### Communication Recommendations for Engineering Roles
- **Tailor information sharing**: Identify essential project details stakeholders need, communicate frequency based on their role (e.g., weekly updates for high-interest). Handle team issues independently when not relevant.[2]
- **Transparent and timely**: Use effective communication to build buy-in, foster trust, and mitigate conflicts, per Peter Drucker principles. In engineering projects, share progress on efficiencies or innovations.[1][2]
- **Persuasion tactics**: When convincing stakeholders (e.g., on efficient task strategies), use data-backed arguments, negotiation, and highlight results like project success.[2]

**Source**: https://ca.indeed.com/career-advice/interviewing/stakeholder-management-interview-questions[2]

### Trust-Building and Conflict Tactics
- **Build relationships**: Receive feedback regularly via structured channels (e.g., surveys/meetings). Demonstrate reliability through consistent delivery.[2]
- **Resolve conflicts**: Mediate by appreciating perspectives, blend ideas (e.g., cautious + risky approaches into moderate strategy with contingency plans). Research compromises for engineering decisions like resource allocation.[2]
- **Risk integration**: Identify/mitigate stakeholder risks (operational/reputational) proactively, especially in shopping domains with regulators/suppliers.[1]

### Actionable Next Steps for Engineering in Shopping Domain
- **Week 1**: Build initial influence map for your project using power-interest grid; list 10+ stakeholders (e.g., product leads, merchants, devs).
- **Week 2**: Schedule feedback sessions with top 3 high-power stakeholders; test one communication template.
- **Ongoing**: Quarterly remap; track one metric like stakeholder satisfaction score (target 80%+ via post-meeting surveys).
