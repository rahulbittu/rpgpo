## South Rim from Laguna Meadows Loop
- **Trail Name**: South Rim from Laguna Meadows Loop (also called South Rim and Emory Peak Loop).
- **Distance**: 24.1 km (15 miles).
- **Elevation Gain**: 1,070 m (over 2,000 feet from Laguna Meadows intersection).
- **Difficulty**: Hard; requires very good fitness, sure-footedness, sturdy shoes, and alpine experience. Includes highly dangerous, technical terrain needing specialist equipment and prior experience.[1]

## Lost Mine Trail
- **Trail Name**: Lost Mine Trail.
- **Distance**: 4.8 miles round trip.
- **Difficulty**: Medium.
- **Description**: Offers big mountain views over the desert; recommended as a marquee hike, best with early start in warm weather. Parking may be limited.[2]

## Grapevine Hills / Balanced Rock
- **Trail Name**: Grapevine Hills Trail to Balanced Rock.
- **Distance**: 2.2 miles round trip.
- **Difficulty**: Easy.
- **Description**: Features fun rock formations; good for shorter hikes.[2]

## Mule Ears Trail
- **Trail Name**: Mule Ears Trail.
- **Distance**: 3.82 miles (3.8 miles).
- **Difficulty**: Easy.
- **Description**: Showcases distinctive rock formations and a desert oasis; up to 2 hours and 1,000 ft. elevation in Brewster County (Big Bend area).[3]

## Mariscal Rim Trail
- **Trail Name**: Mariscal Rim Trail.
- **Distance**: 6.5 miles out and back.
- **Difficulty**: Not specified; involves open desert hike climbing to canyon rim.
- **Description**: Runs through desert to the rim of Mariscal Canyon (1,400-foot deep chasm with sheer walls).[4]
