## Key Findings

1. **Meeting Cadence and Structure**: Engineering Managers should hold 1-on-1 meetings every two weeks, especially for remote teams, to maintain engagement without causing fatigue. Meetings should be concise, valuable, and focus on exploring issues and performance with open questions.
2. **Core Activities**: These meetings are crucial for coaching, mentoring, career development, and addressing performance issues early. They help engineers feel supported and clear about their roles and expectations.
3. **Follow-Up and Momentum**: Setting micro-goals and reviewing progress in each meeting helps maintain momentum. It's important to establish clear actions and refer back to goals to ensure continuous progress.

## Detailed Analysis

- **Cadence and Structure**: Fortnightly meetings are optimal for balancing engagement and avoiding fatigue. They should focus on understanding root causes of issues through open-ended questions, ensuring expectations are clear, and feedback is timely.
- **Core Activities**: One-on-ones are a platform for mentoring and coaching, which are essential for career development and performance management. Addressing challenges early helps prevent larger issues and supports engineers in overcoming blockers.
- **Follow-Up and Momentum**: Consistent follow-ups with micro-goals keep the team aligned and motivated. This approach also reinforces accountability and tracks progress effectively.

## Recommended Actions

1. **Schedule Regular 1-on-1s**:
   - **What to Do**: Schedule 1-on-1 meetings every two weeks for each team member.
   - **Why**: To ensure regular engagement and address any issues promptly.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Improved team morale and performance through consistent support.
   - **First Step**: Use a calendar tool to set recurring 1-on-1 meetings with each team member.

2. **Prepare a Question Bank**:
   - **What to Do**: Develop a list of open-ended questions to guide discussions.
   - **Why**: To explore issues deeply and encourage open communication.
   - **Expected Outcome**: More insightful conversations that address root causes.
   - **First Step**: Start with questions like "What challenges have you faced since our last meeting?" or "How can I support your goals?"

3. **Establish Clear Goals and Follow-Ups**:
   - **What to Do**: Set micro-goals for each team member and review them in subsequent meetings.
   - **Why**: To maintain momentum and ensure accountability.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Continuous progress towards personal and team goals.
   - **First Step**: Agree on specific, measurable goals at the end of each meeting and document them.

4. **Deliver Timely Feedback**:
   - **What to Do**: Provide feedback within 48 hours of any event requiring it.
   - **Why**: To address issues promptly and constructively.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Clear understanding and resolution of performance issues.
   - **First Step**: Develop a feedback template to ensure feedback is consistent and constructive.

5. **Use a Meeting Template**:
   - **What to Do**: Utilize a structured template for each 1-on-1.
   - **Why**: To ensure all critical areas are covered and meetings are efficient.
   - **Expected Outcome**: More productive meetings with clear outcomes.
   - **First Step**: Implement a template with sections for agenda, discussion points, action items, and follow-up notes.

### Sample Meeting Template

- **Agenda**:
  - Review of past meeting notes
  - Current challenges and blockers
  - Career development and goals
  - Feedback and performance discussion
  - Action items and next steps

- **Discussion Points**:
  - Open-ended questions to explore issues
  - Specific feedback on recent performance
  - Progress on micro-goals

- **Action Items**:
  - List of agreed-upon actions
  - Deadlines for each action

- **Follow-Up Notes**:
  - Summary of discussion
  - Any additional notes or observations

This structured approach ensures that 1-on-1 meetings are effective, supportive, and aligned with both personal and organizational goals.
