## Itinerary

### Day 1: Arrival in Inverness
- **Activity**: Settle in and explore Inverness.
- **Why**: Inverness is the gateway to the Highlands, offering a blend of modern amenities and historical sites.
- **First Step**: Check into your accommodation and explore the city center.

### Day 2: Inverness to Dalwhinnie
- **Activity**: Visit Dalwhinnie Distillery.
- **Why**: Experience one of Scotland's highest distilleries known for its light, floral single malts.
- **First Step**: Book a tour in advance. Tours typically cost around £12-£25.

### Day 3: Dalwhinnie to Fort William
- **Activity**: Explore the West Highland Way and visit Glenfinnan Viaduct.
- **Why**: The West Highland Way offers stunning hiking routes, and the Glenfinnan Viaduct is a must-see for Harry Potter fans.
- **First Step**: Plan a short hike on the West Highland Way and visit the viaduct.

### Day 4: Fort William to Oban
- **Activity**: Visit Oban Distillery.
- **Why**: Discover the coastal flavors of Oban's 14-year-old single malt.
- **First Step**: Schedule a distillery tour. Tours cost approximately £10-£20.

### Day 5: Oban to Isle of Skye
- **Activity**: Travel to Isle of Skye, stopping at Eilean Donan Castle.
- **Why**: Eilean Donan is one of the most iconic castles in Scotland, offering picturesque views.
- **First Step**: Allocate time for a tour (£10 entry fee).

### Day 6: Isle of Skye Photography
- **Activity**: Visit the Quiraing and Old Man of Storr.
- **Why**: These locations offer some of the best photography spots on the Isle of Skye.
- **First Step**: Start early to capture sunrise shots.

### Day 7: Isle of Skye to Talisker Distillery
- **Activity**: Tour Talisker Distillery.
- **Why**: Experience the unique peaty flavor of Talisker's single malt.
- **First Step**: Book a tour in advance (£10-£20).

### Day 8: Isle of Skye to Inverness
- **Activity**: Return to Inverness, stopping at Urquhart Castle.
- **Why**: Urquhart Castle offers stunning views of Loch Ness.
- **First Step**: Plan a visit and allocate a couple of hours (£12 entry fee).

### Day 9: Inverness to Aberdeen
- **Activity**: Explore castles in Aberdeenshire, such as Balmoral Castle.
- **Why**: Aberdeenshire is known as "Scotland's Castle Country."
- **First Step**: Check opening times and book tickets (£15 entry fee).

### Day 10: Aberdeen to Edinburgh
- **Activity**: Travel to Edinburgh for a ceilidh experience.
- **Why**: Experience traditional Scottish music and dance.
- **First Step**: Reserve a spot at a local ceilidh event (costs vary, typically £10-£20).

### Day 11: Edinburgh Exploration
- **Activity**: Tour Edinburgh Castle and explore the city.
- **Why**: Edinburgh offers rich history and vibrant culture.
- **First Step**: Purchase tickets for Edinburgh Castle in advance (£18 entry fee).

### Day 12: Departure from Edinburgh
- **Activity**: Final day for shopping and relaxation.
- **Why**: Enjoy the last moments in Scotland's capital.
- **First Step**: Plan your route to the airport or next destination.

## Costs & Budget
- **Accommodation**: Budget £100-£150 per night for mid-range hotels.
- **Food**: Estimate £30-£50 per day for meals.
- **Transport**: Car rental costs approximately £30-£50 per day.
- **Total Estimated Cost**: £2,000-£3,000 for 12 days, including activities, accommodation, and transport.

## Logistics
- **Transport**: Rent a car for flexibility and convenience.
- **Accommodation**: Book in advance, especially in remote areas.
- **Weather**: Prepare for variable weather; pack layers and waterproof gear.

## Tips
- **Advance Booking**: Essential for distillery tours and popular attractions.
- **Local Events**: Check local listings for additional ceilidh events and festivals.
- **Photography**: Sunrise and sunset offer the best lighting for photography on the Isle of Skye.
