## Detailed Analysis

### Room Measurement Tools
While specific tools were not identified, common industry tools for room measurement include:
- **REW (Room EQ Wizard):** A free tool for measuring and analyzing room acoustics.
- **Acourate:** A more advanced tool for detailed acoustic analysis.
- **ARTA:** Provides a suite of audio measurement tools.

### Bass Trap Placement
- **Corners:** Bass traps are most effective when placed in the corners of the room, as this is where bass frequencies tend to accumulate.
- **Wall-Ceiling Junctions:** Additional traps can be placed at the junctions of walls and ceilings for further bass control.

### Diffuser vs. Absorber Ratios
- **Small Rooms:** Typically require more absorption to control reflections and prevent echo.
- **Larger Rooms:** Can benefit from a higher ratio of diffusion to maintain a lively sound without excessive reverberation.

### Budget Options
- **$500 Tier:** Focus on DIY solutions or entry-level products like foam panels and basic bass traps.
- **$1500 Tier:** Mid-range options including a mix of commercial bass traps, absorbers, and diffusers.
- **$5000 Tier:** High-end treatment with custom solutions and professional-grade materials.

## Recommended Actions

### Room Measurement
1. **What to Do:** Use tools like REW to measure room dimensions and identify reflection points.
   - **Why:** Accurate measurements are essential for effective treatment planning.
   - **Expected Outcome:** A clear understanding of room acoustics and problem areas.
   - **First Step:** Download and install REW, and follow online tutorials to perform initial measurements.

### Bass Trap Placement
1. **What to Do:** Install bass traps in all corners and consider additional traps at wall-ceiling junctions.
   - **Why:** To reduce low-frequency build-up and improve overall sound clarity.
   - **Expected Outcome:** Smoother bass response and reduced muddiness in sound.
   - **First Step:** Purchase or construct bass traps suitable for your room size and budget.

### Diffuser vs. Absorber Ratios
1. **What to Do:** Balance absorbers and diffusers based on room size and acoustic goals.
   - **Why:** To achieve a balanced sound that is neither too dead nor too lively.
   - **Expected Outcome:** Improved sound quality with controlled reflections and enhanced clarity.
   - **First Step:** Assess room size and decide on an initial ratio, adjusting as needed based on listening tests.
