## Onboarding Best Practices (First 100 Days)
Structured onboarding over 90-100 days boosts productivity, with 92% of new hires in professional services firms fully productive by day 90 using 30/60/90 plans[1]. Programs typically span 90 days, extending to 6 months for complex roles, per Gallup research[3][8].

- **Pre-Boarding (7-3 Days Before Day 1):** Send welcome email with first-day schedule, team intro, and document upload requests; prepare tech setup (laptop, email, access); notify team. Automate via platform for HR (docs/ID), IT (config), manager (buddy/goals)[5].
- **Day 1 Agenda:** Morning: IT setup, paperwork, manager 1-on-1 (share excitement, week plan); include office/virtual tour, welcome kit handover, social activity (lunch/coffee); afternoon: company overview (mission/values), compliance training (security, harassment)[1][2][3][4].
- **Week 1:** Daily 10-min check-ins (questions/blockers); role expectations (30/60/90 success metrics, written/shared); intros to 3-5 stakeholders + cross-functional (IT/finance); buddy daily check-ins; 1-2 small tasks for early win; team rituals/values stories[1][2][3][4][6].
- **Days 1-30 (Learn Phase):** Shadow teammates, complete training/tools, understand workflows/OKRs; frequent manager/buddy 1-on-1s on styles/feedback[3][4].
- **Days 31-60 (Contribute Phase):** Solo tasks with clear scope; regular 1-on-1 cadence; surface blockers[3].
- **Days 61-90+:** Goal adjustment, celebrate milestones; full integration. Schedule check-ins at offer acceptance[1][2].

**Next Steps:** Customize template per role (send 3-5 days pre-start); use shared doc for "Who's Who" contacts and roadmap[1][4].

## Relationship Building
Assign buddy/mentor for Week 1 daily check-ins (higher frequency for remote: daily first week); facilitate 1-on-1s with manager (working styles/comms) and 3-5 stakeholders Day 1-Week 1; cross-functional intros (finance/IT) beyond team; virtual team-building for remote[3][4][6].

- **Buddy Program Scope:** 30-90 days (longer for seniors); pre-boarding welcome kit to check-ins/activities; define responsibilities/check-in cadence[6].
- **Pro Tip:** Record leadership video for remote personalization[2].

**Next Steps:** Manager schedules team intro + buddy on hire; buddy facilitates virtual intros Week 1[5][6].

## Quick Win Identification
Identify 1-2 small, achievable tasks Week 1 aligned with skills (not steep learning curve); set short-term goals first month, celebrate milestones; meaningful work with clear expectations/outcomes for early achievement sense[2][4].

- **Examples:** Complete small task Week 1; solo tasks Days 31-60[2][3].

**Next Steps:** Manager discusses 30-day direction Week 1, assigns tasks tied to team OKRs[4].

## Learning Plans
Share 30/60/90-day roadmap Day 1 (success looks like: Day 30 understand role/tools; 60 contribute; 90 full productivity); phases: Days 1-30 learn (shadow/train), 31-60 contribute; role-specific training, KPIs/long-term goals Week 1[1][3][4].

- **Check-Ins:** 30/60/90 developmental (progress/support, document actions); daily Week 1-2[1][3].

**Next Steps:** Offer acceptance: calendar check-ins; provide handbook highlights, org structure[1][4].
