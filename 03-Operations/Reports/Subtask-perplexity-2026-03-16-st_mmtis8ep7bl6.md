- Reachbacks

These exercises specifically target the ability to achieve a good shoulder turn while keeping your lower body stable—a critical component of swing sequence.

## Finding 3: Dynamic Warm-Up & Flexibility Protocol

Pre-round mobility work should focus on the largest muscle groups[1]:

- **Hip openers** and **torso twists** to improve rotational mobility
- **Shoulder stretches** for upper body range of motion
- **Lower back stretches** for spinal mobility

The recommended frequency is **3-4 workouts per week** combining both strength and flexibility exercises[1]. A specialized daily mobility routine can be completed in **15 minutes**[2].

Source: https://dungenessgolf.com/golf-fitness-exercises-for-flexibility-and-power/

## Finding 4: Hip Mobility Progressions

Advanced hip mobility work includes **Hip 90/90 stretches with controlled rotations** to open internal and external rotation bilaterally[6]. This addresses the foundational mobility needed for weight transfer and rotational power in the golf swing.
