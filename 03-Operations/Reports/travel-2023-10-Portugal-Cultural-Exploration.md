## Itinerary

### Day 1: Lisbon
- **Morning:** Visit **Sao Jorge Castle** for panoramic views. Arrive before 10 AM to avoid crowds. Explore the castle grounds and enjoy the vistas of the Tagus River and Lisbon's red rooftops.
- **Afternoon:** Stroll through the **Alfama neighborhood**. Discover its Visigothic roots and Arabic influence. Visit **Commerce Square** and enjoy the vibrant atmosphere.
- **Evening:** Dinner at a fado house in Alfama or Chiado. Experience live fado music, a UNESCO-listed cultural heritage, while enjoying traditional Portuguese cuisine.

### Day 2: Lisbon
- **Morning:** Explore the **National Tile Museum** to appreciate Portugal's azulejo tile art.
- **Afternoon:** Head to **Belém Tower**. Enjoy a pastel de nata at a local café in Belem.
- **Evening:** Take a 2-hour sunset and wine sailing tour on the Tagus River for a unique perspective of Lisbon.

### Day 3: Sintra
- **Morning:** Travel to Sintra (30-minute train from Lisbon). Visit the **Pena Palace** and its colorful Romanticist architecture.
- **Afternoon:** Explore the **Quinta da Regaleira** and its mystical gardens.
- **Evening:** Return to Lisbon. Enjoy a relaxed dinner at a riverside restaurant.

### Day 4: Porto
- **Morning:** Travel to Porto (3-hour train from Lisbon). Visit **Sao Bento Station** to see its famous azulejo tiles.
- **Afternoon:** Climb the **Clerigos Tower** for panoramic city views. Visit the **Livraria Lello** bookstore.
- **Evening:** Enjoy a traditional meal and try **Ginjinha**, a local cherry liqueur.

### Day 5: Porto
- **Morning:** Explore the **Ribeira District**, a UNESCO World Heritage site, and enjoy the riverside views.
- **Afternoon:** Visit a port wine cellar in Vila Nova de Gaia for a tasting tour.
- **Evening:** Dinner at a local seafood restaurant, sampling fresh catches from the Atlantic.

### Day 6: Algarve Coast
- **Morning:** Travel to the Algarve (train from Porto to Faro, approximately 5 hours). Visit **Ponta da Piedade** for stunning rock formations.
- **Afternoon:** Relax on **Praia da Marinha**, one of the most beautiful beaches in Europe.
- **Evening:** Enjoy a seafood dinner at a local beachside restaurant.

### Day 7: Algarve Coast
- **Morning:** Explore the historic town of **Lagos** and its old city walls.
- **Afternoon:** Take a boat tour to explore hidden sea caves and grottos.
- **Evening:** Return to Lisbon or Porto for departure.

## Costs & Budget
- **Accommodation:** Budget €100-€150 per night for mid-range hotels.
- **Transport:** Train tickets (Lisbon-Porto ~€30, Porto-Faro ~€40).
- **Food:** Average meal cost €15-€30.
- **Activities:** Entry fees (e.g., Livraria Lello €5, Pena Palace €14).

## Logistics
- **Transport:** Use trains for intercity travel (Lisbon-Sintra, Lisbon-Porto, Porto-Faro).
- **Accommodation:** Book centrally located hotels or Airbnb for easy access to attractions.
- **Language:** Portuguese is the official language, but English is widely spoken in tourist areas.

## Tips
- **Advance Booking:** Book popular attractions like Pena Palace and Livraria Lello in advance.
- **Local Cuisine:** Try local specialties like bacalhau (codfish) and pastel de nata.
- **Weather:** Check the weather forecast and pack accordingly, especially for coastal areas.

This itinerary provides a balanced mix of cultural exploration, culinary experiences, and relaxation, ensuring a comprehensive experience of Portugal's diverse offerings.
