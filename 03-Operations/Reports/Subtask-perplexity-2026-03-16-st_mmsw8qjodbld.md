# Mexico City Culinary Attractions: Current Offerings

## Finding 1: Guided Food Tours with Multiple Tastings

**Sherpa Food Tour - Roma Norte (3.5 hours, 10+ tastings)**
This traditional food tour features Michelin-recognized dishes and regional Mexican cuisine. The itinerary includes a farm-to-table experience with cactus ceviche, fideo seco, and silky plantain gnocchi paired with a michelada, followed by a feast in a 100-year-old building featuring guacamole with chapulines, two types of mole, blue corn quesadillas, and tres leches cake. The tour concludes at a speakeasy in Roma. Meeting point: Gonzalitos taquería at Colima 76.
Source: https://www.getyourguide.com/mexico-city-l194/mexico-city-food-tour-with-10-tastings-local-favorites-t1140666/

**Historic Center Food Tour (half-day walking tour)**
This tour covers pre-Hispanic to contemporary Mexican cuisine through Mexico City's 700-year-old historic center. Specific stops include Kaaj (Oaxaca-inspired tostada with shrimp), Café de Raíz (three types of tamales), La Pitahaya Vegana (hibiscus fried rolled taco), Mux (huitlacoche quesadilla), and Café Barajas (cold brew, café de olla, and Casa D'Aristi Xtabentún Decorado liqueur). The tour includes visits to the Aztec market and a traditional cantina.
Source: https://www.tripadvisor.com/AttractionProductReview-g150800-d11455237-Historic_Center_Food_Tour_in_Mexico_City-Mexico_City_Central_Mexico_and_Gulf_Coast.html

**Roma Neighborhood Food Tour - Context Travel (3 hours)**
This expert-led tasting tour focuses on Mexican culinary foundations, including corn-based dishes with pre-Colombian history, Spanish colonial influences (pork, dairy, wheat, almonds), and regional or fusion cuisine depending on group interests and venue availability.
Source: https://www.contexttravel.com/cities/mexico-city/tours/mexico-city-food-tour

## Finding 4: Cooking Classes with Market Visits

**Essence of Mexico: Authentic Mexican Cooking Class & Market Tour**
This experience includes a hands-on cooking class in an inviting kitchen space, a tour through Medellin market with stops at Oaxaca specialty stands to sample cultivated and prepared insects, and visits to multiple tortillerías for fresh corn and flour tortillas. The class covers ingredient mastery and cooking techniques, accommodating both novice and professionally trained chefs.
Source: https://www.tripadvisor.com/Attractions-g150800-Activities-c41-t185-Mexico_City_Central_Mexico_and_Gulf_Coast.html

**Small Group Multi Variety Mole Cooking Class in La Condesa**
This intimate cooking class focuses on mole preparation and includes mezcal pairings. Participants cook together in a family-style setting.
