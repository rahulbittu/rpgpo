## Finding 1: Angel Due Diligence Timelines
Angel due diligence is typically lighter and faster than VC processes, ranging from **2-6 weeks** for most deals. This includes verifying founder backgrounds (via LinkedIn and references), business registration, customer validation, financial hygiene (bank statements, GST filings), and cap table clarity[1][2]. Legal documentation post-due diligence adds **2-6 weeks** for simple rounds like iSAFE or priced equity, with total closing timelines of **4-6 weeks** after term sheet signing to cover diligence, legal drafting, and negotiations[7].

## Finding 2: Syndicate Participation in Angel Investments
Angel syndicates enable co-investment, often starting with small lead rounds of **$200K–$500K** to build traction before VC seed rounds 6–12 months later; platforms like AngelList facilitate targeting aligned investors by industry, stage, and region[2][5]. Networks such as Alliance of Angels host pitch events for vetted deals, with typical individual check sizes of **$25K–$100K** and portfolio allocation limits of **10-15%** across 5+ startups for diversification; syndicates require SEC accredited status (net worth >$1M excluding home or income >$200K/$300K joint)[prior]. Hybrid paths are common in 2026, with angels tolerating pre-product stages via prototypes or demos, emphasizing founder conviction over metrics[2].

**Source:** https://www.spectup.com/resource-hub/angel-investment-trends[2]; https://financialmodelslab.com/blogs/blog/capital-raising-tips-angel-investors[5]
