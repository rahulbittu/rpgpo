## Finding 1: Bear Brook Murders - Rea Rasmussen Identified in 2025
Authorities used genetic genealogy to identify the fourth victim, a young girl found in a barrel near New Hampshire's Bear Brook State Park in 2000 (originally discovered between 1985-2000), as **Rea Rasmussen**, daughter of serial killer **Terry Peder Rasmussen**. The other victims were **Marlyse Elizabeth Honeychurch** (24), **Marie Elizabeth Vaughn** (6), and **Sarah Lynn McWaters** (1), all murdered by Rasmussen, who died in 2010 after killing at least six women and two children. Identification occurred in **2025** via genetic genealogy, per the National Center for Missing and Endangered Children.

## Finding 2: 1987 Double Homicide Solved via GEDmatch - William Talbott Identified
Investigators solved the **1987 double homicide** of two women by uploading crime scene DNA to public genealogy site **GEDmatch**, which matched two second cousins of suspect **William Talbott**. They built a family tree to identify him, confirmed by DNA from his discarded coffee cup matching scene evidence. Case solved through genetic genealogy pointing to relatives.
