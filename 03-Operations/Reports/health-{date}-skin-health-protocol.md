## Plan

### Week 1: Screen Protection and Hydration
- **Screen Protection:**
  - **Blue Light Filters:** Install blue light filtering software on all devices to reduce potential skin damage from prolonged exposure. Apps like f.lux or built-in settings on devices can be utilized.
  - **Anti-Reflective Screen Protectors:** Use anti-reflective screen protectors to minimize glare and reduce strain.
  - **First Step:** Download and configure a blue light filter app on your primary work device.

- **Hydration:**
  - **Daily Water Intake:** Aim for at least 3 liters of water per day to maintain skin hydration.
  - **Humidifier Use:** Place a humidifier in your workspace to combat dry indoor air.
  - **First Step:** Purchase a desk humidifier and set reminders to drink water every hour.

### Week 2: Nutrition and Dermatologist Visit Schedule
- **Nutrition:**
  - **Antioxidant-Rich Foods:** Incorporate foods like berries, nuts, and green leafy vegetables to combat oxidative stress.
  - **Omega-3 Fatty Acids:** Consume fish or flaxseeds to support skin barrier function.
  - **First Step:** Plan weekly meals including at least two servings of fatty fish and daily portions of fruits and vegetables.

- **Dermatologist Visit Schedule:**
  - **Annual Check-Ups:** Schedule a dermatology appointment annually for a full skin evaluation.
  - **First Step:** Contact a board-certified dermatologist to book an initial consultation.

### Week 3: Continued Education and Routine Adjustment
- **Educate on Verified Information:**
  - **Follow Certified Professionals:** Subscribe to content from board-certified dermatologists for reliable advice.
  - **Avoid Unverified Treatments:** Be cautious of treatments promoted on social media without professional endorsement.
  - **First Step:** Identify and follow at least three board-certified dermatologists on social media platforms.

- **Routine Adjustment:**
  - **Evaluate and Adjust:** After implementing the above steps, assess any skin changes and adjust the routine accordingly.
  - **First Step:** Keep a weekly journal of skin health observations to discuss with your dermatologist.

## Evidence Basis

- **Screen Protection and Hydration:** The emphasis on blue light protection and hydration is derived from general skin health practices, acknowledging the indoor working environment of tech workers.
- **Nutrition:** Antioxidant-rich and omega-3 fatty acid foods are well-documented for their skin health benefits, supporting barrier function and reducing inflammation.
- **Dermatologist Visits:** Regular professional evaluations are crucial for early detection and prevention of skin issues.
- **Education:** The prevalence of misinformation on social media highlights the need for following certified professionals.

## Progression

- **Short-Term:** Implement immediate changes in screen protection and hydration to observe initial improvements in skin comfort and hydration.
- **Medium-Term:** Establish a balanced diet and regular dermatologist visits to maintain skin health and prevent future issues.
- **Long-Term:** Continue education and routine adjustments to adapt to any new skin health trends or personal changes.

## Safety Notes

- **Avoid Over-Reliance on Non-Professional Advice:** Ensure all treatments and routines are vetted by professionals to prevent adverse skin reactions.
- **Monitor Skin Reactions:** Be vigilant about any adverse reactions to new products or routines and consult a dermatologist if necessary.
