## Detailed Analysis

### Ergonomic Features
- **Herman Miller Aeron:** Offers a breathable mesh back and precise adjustments, ideal for users who prefer a firm, supportive seat. Its design caters to specific body sizes, providing a tailored fit.
- **Steelcase Leap V2:** Features a flexible backrest and seat that move with the user, making it suitable for those who frequently change positions. Offers comprehensive adjustability.
- **Secretlab Titan:** Focuses on a firmer seating experience with built-in lumbar support, appealing to users who prioritize a gaming chair aesthetic.

### Lumbar Support
- **Herman Miller Aeron:** Provides targeted support with adjustable pads, beneficial for users with specific lumbar pain points.
- **Steelcase Leap V2:** Offers dynamic support that adjusts with movement, ideal for users who need continuous lumbar support.
- **Secretlab Titan:** Integrated lumbar support is adjustable but may not offer the same level of precision as the Aeron or flexibility as the Leap V2.

### Pricing and Warranty
- **Herman Miller Aeron:** Higher upfront cost with long-term warranty; refurbished options offer cost savings.
- **Steelcase Leap V2:** Similar price range to Aeron, with potential savings on refurbished models.
- **Secretlab Titan:** More affordable, with a shorter warranty period.

### User Feedback
- **Herman Miller Aeron:** Highly rated for long-term comfort and breathability, especially for users in static positions.
- **Steelcase Leap V2:** Praised for adaptability and comfort across various postures.
- **Secretlab Titan:** Generally well-received for short to medium durations, but some find it too firm for extended use.
