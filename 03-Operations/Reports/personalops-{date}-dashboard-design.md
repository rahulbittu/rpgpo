## Key Findings

1. **Focus on Key Metrics**: Limit KPIs to 5-7 per view to avoid cognitive overload. This aligns with cognitive psychology research by George Miller, which suggests that working memory can handle about 7 items at a time.

2. **Decision-Oriented Design**: Metrics should prompt actionable decisions. If a KPI doesn't influence a decision or action, it should not be included.

3. **Visual Hierarchy**: Place the most critical KPIs in the top-left quadrant of the dashboard, as this is where users naturally focus first.

## Detailed Analysis

### Layout Suggestions
- **Top-Left Quadrant**: Reserve this space for your most critical KPI. For example, if financial growth is a priority, display your net worth or investment returns here.
- **Central Area**: Use this space for secondary but still important metrics such as monthly savings rate, current project progress, or health metrics like weekly exercise hours.
- **Bottom Area**: Display less critical, more detailed information that supports the main KPIs, such as breakdowns of spending categories or detailed habit tracking.

### Feature Recommendations
- **Customizable Widgets**: Allow users to select which KPIs to display and where, enhancing personalization and relevance.
- **Integration with Tools**: Link with financial apps (e.g., Mint, YNAB), health trackers (e.g., Fitbit), and project management tools (e.g., Trello) for real-time data updates.
- **Goal Setting and Alerts**: Enable users to set goals and receive alerts when they are close to achieving or missing them.

### Specific KPIs for Tracking
- **Goals**: Progress towards specific goals (e.g., savings target, weight loss).
- **Habits**: Daily habit completion rate (e.g., meditation, exercise).
- **Finances**: Net worth, monthly savings rate, investment returns.
- **Health**: Weekly exercise hours, sleep quality score.
- **Projects**: Task completion rate, project milestones achieved.

### Weekly Review Format
- **Summary Section**: Provide a quick overview of the past week's performance across all KPIs.
- **Trends and Insights**: Highlight any significant trends or changes in data.
- **Action Items**: List actionable steps based on the week's data, such as adjusting a budget or modifying a workout routine.

## Recommended Actions

1. **Define Key Metrics**: Identify the 5-7 most impactful KPIs for your personal goals. Prioritize metrics that will directly influence your decisions and actions.
   - **Why**: To ensure focus and prevent information overload.
   - **Expected Outcome**: A more streamlined and effective dashboard.
   - **First Step**: List your primary goals and determine which metrics best represent progress towards these goals.

2. **Design the Layout**: Use the visual hierarchy to place critical KPIs in the top-left quadrant and organize supporting data logically.
   - **Why**: To enhance user experience and ensure quick access to important information.
   - **Expected Outcome**: A visually appealing and intuitive dashboard.
   - **First Step**: Sketch a layout draft and test it with real data to ensure usability.

3. **Integrate Tools and Automate Data**: Connect your dashboard with existing apps and tools to automate data collection and ensure real-time updates.
   - **Why**: To save time and reduce manual data entry errors.
   - **Expected Outcome**: A more accurate and up-to-date dashboard.
   - **First Step**: Identify which tools you currently use and explore their integration capabilities with your dashboard platform.

4. **Set Up a Weekly Review Routine**: Establish a process for reviewing your dashboard weekly to track progress and make necessary adjustments.
   - **Why**: To maintain momentum and ensure alignment with your goals.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Continuous improvement and goal achievement.
   - **First Step**: Schedule a specific time each week for this review and create a template for consistency.
