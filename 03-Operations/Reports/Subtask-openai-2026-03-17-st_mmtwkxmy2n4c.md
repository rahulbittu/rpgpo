## Recommendations

### Building Executive Presence

1. **Translate Technical Achievements**: Regularly present technical work in terms of its strategic value to the organization. This involves linking project outcomes to business objectives and using language that resonates with non-technical stakeholders.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Improved visibility and recognition among executive peers.
   - **First Step**: Identify key business goals and map current projects to these objectives.

2. **Engage in Executive Forums**: Actively participate in executive meetings and forums to increase visibility and influence.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Enhanced reputation as a strategic leader.
   - **First Step**: Schedule attendance at the next executive meeting and prepare to discuss how your team’s work aligns with strategic goals.

3. **Develop Structured Communication**: Practice delivering messages that are clear, concise, and aligned with organizational priorities.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Increased clarity and impact of communication with stakeholders.
   - **First Step**: Enroll in a communication workshop focused on executive-level presentations.

### Managing Stakeholders

1. **Propose and Prioritize Strategic Initiatives**: Clearly outline the strategic initiatives your team will focus on, including trade-offs and what will not be prioritized.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Reduced ambiguity and increased stakeholder buy-in.
   - **First Step**: Draft a list of current projects with proposed priorities and trade-offs.

2. **Enhance Documentation Practices**: Treat documentation as an integral part of the engineering process, ensuring it is as robust and accessible as code.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Improved knowledge sharing and continuity, especially in remote environments.
   - **First Step**: Implement a system for maintaining ADRs and runbooks.
