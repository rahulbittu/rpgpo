## Itinerary (Day-by-Day)

### Day 1-3: Copenhagen
- **Day 1: Arrival and Design Museum Visit**
  - **Activity**: Visit the Design Museum Denmark.
  - **Why**: Explore Danish design heritage and contemporary work.
  - **Expected Outcome**: Gain insights into Denmark's design evolution.
  - **First Step**: Book tickets online at [Design Museum Denmark](https://designmuseum.dk/).

- **Day 2: Furniture Showrooms and Nordic Food Hall**
  - **Activity**: Visit Hay House and Illums Bolighus for modern Danish furniture.
  - **Why**: Experience cutting-edge Scandinavian furniture design.
  - **Expected Outcome**: Discover furniture trends and potential home decor ideas.
  - **First Step**: Schedule showroom visits via their respective websites.
  - **Activity**: Dinner at Torvehallerne Food Hall.
  - **Why**: Sample a variety of Nordic cuisines in a vibrant setting.
  - **Expected Outcome**: Enjoy authentic Danish culinary experiences.
  - **First Step**: Arrive early to avoid crowds.

- **Day 3: Day Trip to Roskilde**
  - **Activity**: Visit the Viking Ship Museum.
  - **Why**: Learn about Denmark’s maritime history.
  - **Expected Outcome**: Understand the historical context of Scandinavian seafaring.
  - **First Step**: Purchase train tickets to Roskilde from Copenhagen Central Station.

### Day 4-6: Stockholm
- **Day 4: Arrival and Stockholm Design Days**
  - **Activity**: Attend Stockholm Design Days at Fatburen Showroom.
  - **Why**: Engage with Nordic design brands and exhibitions.
  - **Expected Outcome**: Network with designers and gather inspiration.
  - **First Step**: Register for the event through [Stockholm Design Days](https://scandinaviandesign.com/category/exhibitions/).

- **Day 5: Swedish Museum of Architecture and Design**
  - **Activity**: Visit the museum on Skeppsholmen island.
  - **Why**: Explore exhibitions on industrial and decorative arts.
  - **Expected Outcome**: Deepen understanding of Swedish design innovations.
  - **First Step**: Check museum hours and plan your visit.

- **Day 6: Archipelago Day Trip**
  - **Activity**: Take a boat tour of the Stockholm Archipelago.
  - **Why**: Experience the natural beauty of Sweden’s islands.
  - **Expected Outcome**: Relax and enjoy scenic views.
  - **First Step**: Book a tour with a local operator like Stromma.

### Day 7-10: Helsinki
- **Day 7: Arrival and Kansallismuseo Visit**
  - **Activity**: Explore the National Museum of Finland.
  - **Why**: Learn about Finnish design in the context of cultural history.
  - **Expected Outcome**: Appreciate the intersection of design and ecology.
  - **First Step**: Plan your visit around museum opening hours.

- **Day 8: Sauna Experience**
  - **Activity**: Visit Löyly, a public sauna in Helsinki.
  - **Why**: Experience traditional Finnish sauna culture.
  - **Expected Outcome**: Relax and rejuvenate in a unique setting.
  - **First Step**: Make a reservation online.

- **Day 9: Furniture Showrooms and Nordic Cuisine**
  - **Activity**: Visit Artek and Marimekko for Finnish design.
  - **Why**: Discover iconic Finnish furniture and textiles.
  - **Expected Outcome**: Explore unique design aesthetics.
  - **First Step**: Schedule visits via their websites.
  - **Activity**: Dinner at Old Market Hall.
  - **Why**: Taste Finnish specialties in a historic setting.
  - **Expected Outcome**: Enjoy a diverse range of local foods.
  - **First Step**: Plan to visit during less busy hours.

- **Day 10: Departure**
  - **Activity**: Final shopping and departure.
  - **Why**: Purchase souvenirs and prepare for departure.
  - **Expected Outcome**: Return with memories and mementos.
  - **First Step**: Check flight times and arrange transport to the airport.

## Costs & Budget
- **Accommodation**: Estimate $150 per night for mid-range hotels.
- **Meals**: Budget $50 per day for meals.
- **Transport**: Public transport passes (e.g., Copenhagen Card, SL Access Card in Stockholm, HSL Card in Helsinki) can save costs.
- **Attractions**: Museum entries and event tickets vary; budget $20 per attraction.
- **Total Estimated Cost**: Approximately $2,500 - $3,000 per person.

## Logistics
- **Flights**: Book multi-city flights to optimize travel time.
- **Transport**: Use city cards for unlimited public transport.
- **Currency**: Ensure you have local currencies (DKK, SEK, EUR).

## Tips
- **Weather**: February can be cold; pack warm clothing.
- **Language**: English is widely spoken, but learning basic phrases in Danish, Swedish, and Finnish can enhance your experience.
- **Connectivity**: Consider a local SIM card for data and calls.

This itinerary provides a balanced mix of cultural, design, and natural experiences across Copenhagen, Stockholm, and Helsinki, tailored to your interests in Scandinavian design and culture.
