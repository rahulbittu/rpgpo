## Wake-Up Timing
Optimal wake-up aligns with chronotype and circadian rhythm for peak cognitive performance; morning types benefit from 5-6 AM wake-ups, while forcing it for evening types increases social jetlag, impairing academic performance and raising risks of diabetes, high blood pressure, and obesity.[3][4]  
Research shows 7-8 hours of sleep nightly supports memory, executive function, and visuospatial ability; sleeping <5 or >9 hours worsens cognition, with long sleep linked to depressive symptoms and brain changes.[2]

## Light Exposure
Get bright natural sunlight within first hour after waking to boost alertness, mood, and circadian alignment; reduce blue light (phones/screens) at least 1 hour before bed to avoid melatonin suppression and cognitive misalignment.[4]  
Morning light counters darker mornings post-time changes, reducing jetlag-like effects on cognition; prioritize over evening adjustments.[4][5]  
**Next step:** Set 10-15 min outdoor walk post-wake; use f.lux or Night Shift app for evening blue light filter starting 8 PM if bedtime ~9-10 PM.[4]
