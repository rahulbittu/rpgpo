## The 20-20-20 Rule
The **20-20-20 rule** is a widely recommended guideline for reducing digital eye strain: every **20 minutes**, look at an object **20 feet** away for at least **20 seconds**.[1][2][3][4][5][6][7][8] Developed by optometrists, it relaxes eye focusing muscles during prolonged screen use.[8]

## Eye Care Guidelines for Desk Workers
Multiple sources from eye care professionals and clinics outline these specific, actionable habits for desk workers to combat eye strain, dryness, and fatigue:

- **Optimize screen position**: Place screen at **arm's length** (20-28 inches) and **15-20 degrees below eye level**.[3][4][6]
- **Blink frequently**: Screen time halves blink rates, causing dryness; consciously blink fully and often, or use artificial tears.[2][4][6]
- **Adjust lighting and glare**: Use soft ambient light, position screen with light to the side, add anti-glare covers or anti-reflective lens coatings.[6]
- **Tweak display settings**: Match brightness to room, increase text size, enable night/dark mode to cut blue light.[2][4][6]
- **Take hourly breaks**: Stand, stretch, and focus on distant objects beyond 20-20-20 sessions to ease neck/shoulder tension.[4][6]
- **Contact lens tips**: Switch to glasses 1-2 days/week for heavy screen days; never sleep in contacts; use fresh solution and rub-clean extended lenses.[3]
- **Regular exams**: Get annual comprehensive eye exams to check for strain, update prescriptions.[2][3]

**Blue light glasses** offer limited benefits (e.g., reduced glare for some); habits like 20-20-20 are more effective per research cited.[2]
