## Analysis

### Secured Credit Cards
Secured credit cards are a foundational tool for building credit from scratch. They require a refundable security deposit that serves as your credit limit, making them accessible to individuals with no credit history. Key data points include:

- **Approval Ease:** Due to the security deposit, approval rates are higher compared to unsecured cards[4].
- **Impact on Credit Score:** Users of secured cards like OpenSky® and Chime Card's Credit Builder have reported average credit score increases of 47 points and 28 points, respectively, within 6 to 8 months[2].
- **Timeline:** A minimum of 6 months is needed to establish a credit score with at least one open account[1].

### Credit Builder Loans
Credit builder loans are designed to help individuals establish credit by borrowing a small amount that is held in a savings account until the loan is paid off. These loans report to credit bureaus, helping to build a positive payment history.

### Authorized User Strategies
Being added as an authorized user on someone else's credit card can help build credit history without the need for a personal credit card. This strategy can be beneficial if the primary cardholder has a good credit history.

## Comparison

- **Secured Cards vs. Credit Builder Loans:** Secured cards typically offer quicker access to a credit line and potential rewards, whereas credit builder loans focus on building savings while establishing credit.
- **Authorized User vs. Secured Cards:** Becoming an authorized user can be a risk-free way to build credit if the primary account is well-managed, but it offers less control over individual credit-building efforts compared to secured cards.

### Authorized User Strategy
- **What to Do:** Request to become an authorized user on a family member's or trusted friend's credit card.

- **Authorized User:** Your credit score can be negatively affected if the primary cardholder mismanages the account.
- **General Disclaimer:** Credit score improvements are subject to individual financial behavior and external economic factors. Always read the terms and conditions of any financial product thoroughly before committing.
