## Quibi: $1.7B Streaming Flameout

Launched April 2020 by Hollywood heavyweight Jeffrey Katzenberg and ex-HP CEO Meg Whitman with $1.7B funding and A-list talent, Quibi targeted "quick bites" (10-minute mobile videos) for on-the-go viewers. Pandemic lockdowns killed the core use case—no one was commuting—while it failed to land hit shows amid Netflix/Disney+ dominance; shut down October 2020 after 6 months, returning $350M to investors.  
**Podcast Hook**: "Two billionaires bet $1.7 billion on phone videos during a lockdown—gone in 6 months. What hubris?"[1][6]

Source: https://entrepreneurship.babson.edu/lessons-from-billion-dollar-failure/

## Pets.com: Dot-Com Darling's Super Bowl Sock Puppet Implosion

Raised $82.5M including Amazon backing, Pets.com hyped pet e-commerce with iconic sock puppet ads (12 Super Bowl XXXIV spots in Jan 2000 cost $2M+ total for dot-coms). Burned cash on unprofitable shipping amid bubble burst; IPO'd Feb 2000 at $11/share, crashed 75% by Nov 2000, bankrupt after 9 months, wiping $1.755T in dot-com value.  
**Podcast Hook**: "Super Bowl stardom to sock puppet funeral—how Pets.com's puppet outlived the company by months."[3]

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dot-com_bubble

## Jawbone: Wearables Giant Crushed by Fitbit

Pioneered fitness trackers with $930M funding (peak $3B valuation), Jawbone dominated early wearables market 2011-2015. Lost to Fitbit/Apple via poor battery life, buggy apps, and failed pivots (e.g., speakers); outcompeted as incumbents scaled, liquidated 2017 after layoffs and lawsuits.  
**Podcast Hook**: "From wristband king to ghost company—Jawbone's $930M grave marked by a speaker that no one wanted."[6]

Source: https://ideaproof.io/startup-failure-database

## MoviePass: Unlimited Movies Bankrupted by Free Buffets

Launched 2011, peaked 2018 at 3M subscribers with $10/month unlimited movies (valued $1.2B+). Unlimited model exploded costs ($30-40/film vs. $10 sub); parent Helios & Matheson diluted shares, faked metrics, restricted access amid backlash; shut down Sept 2019 after $8M losses/week.  
**Podcast Hook**: "See any movie unlimited for $10? MoviePass's dream turned into a $100M+ nightmare of empty seats and SEC probes."[6]

Source: https://ideaproof.io/startup-failure-database

## Secret: Anonymous App's Toxic Spiral

Launched 2014 as "anti-Facebook" anonymous sharing, hit 15M users in months with $35M funding (peak $100M valuation). Imploded from cyberbullying, hate speech floods, and doxxing scandals; co-founder banned users arbitrarily, leading to mass exodus; shut down 2015.  
**Podcast Hook**: "The app where secrets killed the startup—15M users, then a cesspool of rage that lasted 16 months."[6]
