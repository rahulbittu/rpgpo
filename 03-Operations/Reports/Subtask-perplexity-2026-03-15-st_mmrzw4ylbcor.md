## Finding 1: DASH Diet and Low Hyperinsulinemia/Inflammation Diets Linked to Lower Cognitive Decline Risk
- JAMA Neurology cohort study (published February 2026) analyzed dietary patterns in relation to subjective cognitive decline and objective cognitive function.
- **DASH diet** (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension) associated with lower risk of cognitive decline and better cognitive performance; diets with lower hyperinsulinemia potential (low glycemic load) and lower inflammation potential also showed benefits.
- Study underscores healthy diets for long-term cognitive health; no specific foods quantified, but emphasizes overall patterns over isolated nutrients.
- Source: https://www.eurekalert.org/news-releases/1117225 and https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jamaneurology/fullarticle/10.1001/jamaneurol.2026.0062 (DOI: 10.1001/jamaneurol.2026.0062)

## Finding 2: 2025-2030 USDA Dietary Guidelines Emphasize Protein, Whole Foods, Gut Health for Overall Health (Indirect Brain Benefits)
- Released January 2026; increases protein to **1.2-1.6 g/kg body weight per day** (doubled from prior 80-year standard); prioritizes animal proteins, full-fat dairy (e.g., butter, beef tallow), whole grains over refined.
- Gut health focus: fermented foods like **kimchi** and **kefir**; half plate fruits/veggies, lean meats, healthy fats; notes 90% of Americans lack fiber despite mentions of high-fiber foods.
- Critics note weak evidence for higher animal protein/full-fat dairy on health outcomes; strong support for whole grains reducing processed carbs.
- Next steps: Start with daily fruits/veggies, 3 balanced meals, water; track protein via app to hit 1.2-1.6 g/kg (e.g., 84-112g for 70kg person).
- Source: https://www.rochesterregional.org/hub/2026-dietary-guidelines and https://publichealth.gmu.edu/news/2026-02/tip-sheet-plate-pyramid-understanding-new-dietary-guidelines

## Finding 3: MIND Diet Blend Reduces Alzheimer's Risk, Enhances Cognitive Health
- **MIND diet** (Mediterranean + DASH hybrid) supports brain health; reduces Alzheimer's risk by up to 53% with strict adherence (prior studies), focuses on brain-protective foods.
- Promotes green leafy veggies, berries, nuts, whole grains, fish, poultry, olive oil; limits red meat, butter, pastries, fried foods.
- Event details from May 6, 2026, highlight its role in cognitive maintenance.
- Next steps: Adopt 3+ servings/week leafy greens (e.g., spinach), 5+ servings/week nuts/berries; source recipes from extension.illinois.edu.
- Source: https://extension.illinois.edu/events/2026-05-06-introduction-mind-diet-eating-brain-health

## Finding 4: Fruits, Vegetables, Minimally Processed Foods Boost Mental Health; Whole Grains for Sustained Energy
- APA research (March 2026) links diets high in **fruits, vegetables, minimally processed foods** to positive mental health outcomes, including cognitive function.
- American Heart Association (March 9, 2026) recommends MyPlate/pyramid: half plate **fruits/veggies**, 25% **whole grains** (e.g., oats, quinoa for fiber/energy), 25% lean proteins (chicken, turkey, beans, soy); fiber under-consumed, aids sustained energy.
- Whole grains provide steady energy via fiber; plant proteins add fiber/healthy fats.
- Next steps: Plate audit—fill half with produce (e.g., 2 cups veggies/day), quarter whole grains (1/2 cup cooked oats); limit added sugars/salt.
- Source: https://www.apa.org/monitor/2026/03/nutrition-food-mental-health and https://www.heart.org/en/news/2026/03/09/struggling-to-sort-through-nutrition-advice-dont-forget-the-joy-this-expert-says

## Finding 5: Probiotics/Prebiotics Counter Early Unhealthy Diets for Brain/Feeding Regulation (Sustained Energy Indirectly)
- High-fat/sugar early diets disrupt hypothalamus (appetite/energy balance); **Bifidobacterium longum APC1472** probiotic and **FOS+GOS prebiotics** (fructooligosaccharides + galactooligosaccharides) restore gut microbiota, improve eating behaviors/brain function, especially in females.
- Targets long-term metabolism/feeding for sustained energy.
- Next steps: Supplement with B. longum APC1472 (consult doctor); add prebiotic foods like onions/garlic (FOS sources).
- Source: https://www.nutritioninsight.com/news/early-diet-impact-brain-health-feeding.html
