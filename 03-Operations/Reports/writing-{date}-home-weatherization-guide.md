# DIY Home Weatherization: Techniques, Priorities, and ROI

## Air Sealing Priorities

### Key Areas for Air Sealing:

1. **Attics**: 
   - **Why**: Heat loss primarily occurs through the attic due to rising warm air.
   - **First Step**: Inspect and seal around attic hatches and access doors.

2. **General Air Sealing**:
   - **Why**: Prevents air from escaping through gaps around wiring, plumbing, and framing.
   - **First Step**: Use caulk or spray foam around window and door frames, and electrical outlets.

3. **Basement or Crawlspace Rim Joists**:
   - **Why**: Cold air infiltration is common in these areas.
   - **First Step**: Insulate and seal rim joists with rigid foam and caulk.

4. **Problem Zones**:
   - **Why**: Areas like kneewalls, rooms over garages, and window and door frames are often overlooked.
   - **First Step**: Inspect and seal these areas using appropriate materials like weatherstripping or foam.

### Expected Savings:
- **DOE Estimate**: Up to 15% reduction in heating and cooling costs.
- **EPA Estimate**: Average of 11% energy cost reduction.
- **Potential Savings**: Up to 30% on energy bills with comprehensive air sealing and insulation.

## Insulation R-Value Selection by Climate Zone

### R-Value Recommendations:

- **Zone 1 (Warmest)**: R-30 to R-49 for attics; R-13 to R-15 for walls.
- **Zone 2**: R-30 to R-60 for attics; R-13 to R-15 for walls.
- **Zone 3**: R-30 to R-60 for attics; R-19 to R-21 for walls.
- **Zone 4**: R-38 to R-60 for attics; R-19 to R-21 for walls.
- **Zone 5**: R-49 to R-60 for attics; R-19 to R-21 for walls.
- **Zone 6 (Coldest)**: R-49 to R-60 for attics; R-19 to R-21 for walls.

### First Step:
- Determine your climate zone using the U.S. Department of Energy's climate zone map and select insulation accordingly.

## Window Assessment

### Steps for Window Assessment:

1. **Inspect for Drafts**:
   - **Method**: Use a candle or smoke pen to detect air movement around window frames.
   - **First Step**: Seal detected leaks with weatherstripping or caulk.

2. **Check Window Glazing**:
   - **Why**: Single-pane windows are less efficient.
   - **First Step**: Consider upgrading to double or triple-pane windows for better insulation.

3. **Evaluate Window Frames**:
   - **Why**: Warped or damaged frames can lead to air leaks.
   - **First Step**: Repair or replace damaged window frames.

## Draft Detection Methods

### Techniques:

- **Visual Inspection**: Look for visible gaps or cracks.
- **Smoke Test**: Use incense or a smoke pen to identify air movement.
- **Thermal Camera**: Identify cold spots indicating leaks.

### First Step:
- Conduct a thorough inspection using these methods to identify and prioritize areas for sealing.

By following these specific steps and focusing on high-impact areas, you can effectively improve your home's energy efficiency and achieve significant cost savings. Prioritize air sealing and insulation based on your climate zone for the best results.
