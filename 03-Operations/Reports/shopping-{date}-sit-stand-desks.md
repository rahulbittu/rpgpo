## Key Findings

1. **Uplift V3 Standing Desk**: Offers best-in-class stability and a high weight capacity, making it ideal for heavy dual monitor setups. Priced from $599, it features a commercial-grade steel frame and advanced motor system.

2. **FlexiSpot E6 Standing Desk**: Known for its affordability and stability, especially at full height, with prices ranging from $420 to $480. It supports dual monitors effectively with its robust motor system and oval leg design.

3. **IKEA Bekant Desk**: While not detailed in the prior results, IKEA's Bekant series is typically known for its budget-friendly options and simplistic design, generally priced under $500.

## Detailed Analysis

### Uplift V3 Standing Desk
- **Pros**: 
  - High stability and weight capacity (355 lbs).
  - Advanced features like a digital keypad and optional Bluetooth control.
  - Fast lift speed (1.5 in/sec) and quiet operation (<50dB).
- **Cons**: 
  - Higher price point, especially with additional features.
  - Assembly may require assistance due to the desk's weight and complexity.

### FlexiSpot E6 Standing Desk
- **Pros**: 
  - Cost-effective with a strong emphasis on stability due to its oval leg design.
  - Adequate height range and weight capacity for dual monitor setups.
- **Cons**: 
  - Limited desktop size options compared to Uplift.
  - Fewer advanced features like Bluetooth connectivity.

### IKEA Bekant Desk
- **Pros**:
  - Budget-friendly and widely available.
  - Simple design suitable for basic setups.
- **Cons**:
  - Generally less stable than Uplift and FlexiSpot, especially at full height.
  - Limited weight capacity and fewer customization options.
