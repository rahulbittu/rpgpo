# Digital Nomad Life in Medellín, Colombia: 2026 Guide

## Finding 1: Cost of Living & Monthly Budget

**Monthly expenses range from $1,000–$1,600 for a comfortable lifestyle.**[2] A more detailed breakdown shows daily budgets of $46–$93 (approximately $1,380–$2,790/month).[3] The Digital Nomad Visa (V type) requires proof of $3,000/month income.[2]

**Housing costs:** Home internet in major cities typically runs $20–$40/month for 100–300 Mbps fiber through providers like Claro, Tigo, or ETB.[2]

**Transportation:** Metro rides cost approximately $1.16 per trip (COP 4,292).[3] Uber/Beat/Cabify rides range from $5.44–$14 (COP 20,172–COP 51,504).[3]

**Coworking spaces:** Dedicated desks with reliable high-speed internet cost $80–$150/month.[2]

## Finding 2: Top Neighborhoods for Digital Nomads

**El Poblado & Parque Lleras** is the primary digital nomad hub, featuring tree-lined streets, shopping malls (El Tesoro, Santafé), international restaurants, hostels, and concentrated coworking spaces.[3] Parque Lleras is the nightlife epicenter with rooftop bars, salsa clubs, and reggaeton venues open until 03:00 Thursday–Saturday.[3]

**Laureles** is where many long-term expats relocate after their El Poblado phase, offering a more residential feel.[2]

## Finding 3: Coworking Spaces & Internet Infrastructure

**Recommended coworking spaces:**

- **Selina** (El Poblado): All-in-one accommodation, coworking, food, and social events.[1]
- **Siembra Coworking/Ofizen** (El Poblado): Strong community focus with regular quizzes, workshops, networking nights, and free beer Fridays.[1]
- **Tinkko** (business district, 15th floor): High-rise location with proper meeting rooms for startups; strong business focus.[1]
- **WeWork:** Part of Medellín's mature coworking scene.[2]

**Internet speeds:** Average download speed via wi-fi is 10 Mbps, though faster connections are readily available.[1] Colombia has been rolling out 4G coverage for several years with decent access in urban areas.[1]

## Finding 5: Safety Tips

**Transportation safety:** Avoid random street-hailed taxis due to scams and danger; use Uber, Beat, or Cabify instead.[3] Walking is safe in El Poblado.[3]

**General caution:** While safety has improved significantly, caution is still required in certain areas.[3]

**Urban regeneration context:** Medellín has undergone substantial urban regeneration from its violent past, with neighborhoods like Comuna 13 now featuring public art, electric escalators, and cultural tourism.[3]

## Finding 6: Transportation Infrastructure

Medellín has **Colombia's only Metro system**, making getting around efficient and affordable.[2] The Metro includes 2 lines plus the Metrocable cable car (integrated system).[3] Metrocable Line K reaches Santo Domingo with valley views; Metro to San Javier connects to Comuna 13 escalators.[3]

Recharge a Cívica card (~$1.16/ride) for Metro access.[3]

## Finding 7: Language & Cultural Considerations

**Spanish proficiency is essential.** To get the best out of living in Medellín, you'll need to speak good Spanish, which also opens doors when networking with other entrepreneurs.[1] The city is known for exceptionally warm "paisa hospitality" where locals genuinely greet strangers on streets.[3]
