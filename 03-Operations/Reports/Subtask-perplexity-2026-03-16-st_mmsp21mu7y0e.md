## Lisbon

**Cultural Attractions:** Sao Jorge Castle offers panoramic views of red rooftops, Tagus River, and Ponte 25 de Abril; arrive before 10am to avoid crowds. Alfama neighborhood features Visigothic roots, Arabic influence, and fishing heritage with 21,435 Tripadvisor reviews (4.5/5 rating). Top sites include Commerce Square (Praça do Comércio), Carmo Convent (Carmo Archaeological Museum), Belém Tower (Torre de Belém, 38,301 reviews, 4.2/5), and National Tile Museum (Museu Nacional do Azulejo).[1][3][6]

**Dining Options:** Pasteis de nata at Belem; fresh seafood in riverside promenades; fado houses in Alfama and Chiado serve meals with live UNESCO-listed fado music on saudade themes.[1][5]

**Unique Experiences:** Evening fado shows; 2-hour sunset and wine sailing tour on Tagus River; Best of Lisbon Small-Group Walking Tour covers old quarter highlights.[1][3]

## Porto

**Cultural Attractions:** Sao Bento Station displays over 20,000 hand-painted azulejo tiles on Portuguese history (free entry). Clerigos Tower (76m tall, 240 steps) provides 360-degree views. Livraria Lello bookstore (entry €5, redeemable on books) inspired J.K. Rowling's Hogwarts.[1]

**Dining Options:** Ginjinha (cherry liqueur) in nearby Obidos on routes to Lisbon.[1]

**Unique Experiences:** Spiritus Multimedia Show at Clérigos Church (audiovisual light/music in 75m Baroque tower by OCUBO).[2]

Source: https://www.talesofthemap.com/en/blog/8-days-in-portugal-itinerary[1]; https://www.getyourguide.com/portugal-l169015/family-friendly-activities-tc1094/[2]
