**Key Practice:** Begin training in controlled pool environments before transitioning to open water. Start with a 50-meter pool to enable continuous swimming without frequent wall turns, which reduces reliance on pool walls for confidence-building.[1]

**Open Water Progression:** Get in open water before race day to acclimate to conditions like currents, temperature, and visibility. This race-specific preparation is essential despite pool training.[1]

**Wetsuit Benefits:** Wetsuits eliminate form drag by positioning your body horizontally in the water without effort—approximately 99.999% of people will float vertically when taking a breath without proper body position support.[1]

## Finding 2: Freestyle Technique and Form Development

**Form-Focused Training (FF):** Dedicate sessions to technique work and developing "feel for the water" through drill work, followed by normal swimming to reinforce improved form.[1]

**Aerobic Base Building (AE):** Train at comfortable but smooth effort using intervals of 100s and 200s with short rest periods to build your aerobic engine—the foundation for sustained swimming performance.[1]
