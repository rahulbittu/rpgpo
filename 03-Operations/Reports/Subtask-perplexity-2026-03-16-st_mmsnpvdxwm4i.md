## Reversible Soundproofing Methods for Rental Apartments
Non-invasive techniques like removable adhesives, tension rods, over-the-door hangers, furniture placement, rugs, curtains, and blankets allow soundproofing without permanent wall damage or drilling, making them ideal for renters.[1][2] These methods block 20-50% of noise transmission depending on implementation, using mass addition and absorption without structural changes.[1]

- **Door upgrades**: Hang heavy moving blankets or quilts over thin apartment doors using adhesive hooks or over-the-door hangers; add draft stoppers at the base. Cost: $20–$30 per blanket. Effectiveness: ★★★☆☆.[1][3]
- **Window treatments**: Install thick thermal/blackout curtains or interior acrylic panels with tension rods. Cost: $25–$50 per panel. Effectiveness: ★★★☆☆.[1]
- **Wall mass addition**: Position bookshelves filled with books against shared walls; hang rugs, tapestries, or DIY panels (old towels/denim behind fabric frames) via removable hooks. Effectiveness boosted by irregular surfaces and density.[1][2]
- **Floor solutions**: Layer rugs with thick underlay for downstairs noise; use felt pads under furniture legs for upstairs impact reduction. Cost: $10–$30.[1]
