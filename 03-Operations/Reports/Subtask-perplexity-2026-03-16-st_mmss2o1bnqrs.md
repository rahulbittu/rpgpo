## Torres del Paine W Trek

The W Trek in Torres del Paine National Park, Chile, is a 5-day self-guided hike (approx. 70-100 km total) open for independent trekkers from October to end of April; book park entry and refugio/campsite permits 3-6 months in advance via Fantasy or Vertice websites (not detailed in results but standard per [5])[5].

### Gear Requirements
- Warm layered clothing, waterproof jacket/pants, sturdy hiking boots, trekking poles, tent (if camping), sleeping bag rated to 0°C, food, and glacier-viewing essentials like binoculars; big rucksacks can be dropped at stations like Guardería Italiano for side hikes[5].
- Specific hikes: Torres lookout (22 km, 8-10 hours, 900m elevation, rocky moraine); French Valley (panoramic glacier views); Grey Glacier (3-4 hours to viewpoints, 1km forest walk to ice chunks)[1][5].

### Permit Booking Timelines
- Self-guided season: October-April; entry bus from Puerto Natales ($25,000 CLP return, 2-4 hours to park); refugios book out fast for peak Nov-Mar—secure 4-6 months ahead (results highlight 2026 trips selling out, e.g., March 2026 tours)[3][5][8].

### Optimal Weather Windows
- Peak trekking: November-March (summer, milder winds); March 2026 tours feature day hikes to Towers base and French Valley; high winds/precipitation common year-round—check forecasts daily[4][5].

### Emergency Evacuation Options
- No specific services named; rangers at Guardería Italiano/El Chileno; park accessible by bus/catamaran from Puerto Natales for quick exit; guided tours (e.g., EcoCamp stays) include support[3][4][5].

## El Chaltén Trekking (Fitz Roy Area)

El Chaltén, Argentina ("Trekking Capital"), offers day hikes like Laguna de los Tres (to Fitz Roy, 7 hours, 11 miles, 2107 ft max elev.) and Cerro Torre/Laguna Torre; no permits needed for day hikes, but guided glacier treks (e.g., Perito Moreno) have age limits (under 65)[2][7].

### Gear Requirements
- Moderate difficulty: Warm layers, rain gear, boots for 7-hour out-and-backs with climbs (e.g., De las Vueltas river valley to Fitz Roy); lunch/snacks for lakeside breaks; glacier hikes need crampons/ice axes via guides[2][7].

### Permit Booking Timelines
- Day hikes free/no permits; full-day guided tours from El Calafate bookable same-day or advance (e.g., private/shared to El Chaltén); March 2026 group tours sold out—book 3+ months early[7][8].

### Optimal Weather Windows
- February-March 2026 ideal (e.g., 8-day trips with Fitz Roy/Cerro Torre hikes); clear days for panoramas, but variable steppe weather[2][8].

### Emergency Evacuation Options
- Guided tours from El Calafate (3-hour drive) include transport; park trails like Loma del Pliegue Tumbado (18km, 9 hours) near roads; no air evac details, but estancias/ranch lodges (e.g., Blanca Patagonia Hostería) provide support[2][3][7].

**Next Steps**: Book El Calafate transfers via getyourguide.com; check Fitz Roy trail conditions at elchalten.com; prioritize Feb-Mar 2026 slots.
