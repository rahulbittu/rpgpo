## Comprehensive Mobility Routine for Golfers

### Plan

#### Day 1: Focus on Thoracic Rotation
- **Exercises**: 
  - **Reachbacks**: 3 sets of 10 reps per side
  - **Half-Kneeling Rotation with Side Bend**: 3 sets of 8 reps per side
  - **Half-Kneeling Resisted Rotation**: 3 sets of 10 reps per side
  
  **Why**: These exercises enhance thoracic mobility, crucial for achieving a full shoulder turn without compromising lower body stability.
  
  **First Step**: Begin with a light warm-up to increase blood flow to the upper body.

#### Day 2: Hip Internal Rotation
- **Exercises**:
  - **Dumbbell Romanian Deadlifts (RDL)**: 3 sets of 12 reps
  - **Dumbbell Split Squats**: 3 sets of 10 reps per leg
  - **Double Leg Seated Box Jumps**: 3 sets of 8 reps
  - **Medicine Ball Slams**: 3 sets of 12 reps
  
  **Why**: These exercises target hip mobility, crucial for maintaining posture and preventing swing faults like swaying.
  
  **First Step**: Start with dynamic stretches focusing on the lower body.

#### Day 3: Wrist Mobility
- **Exercises**:
  - **Wrist Circles**: 3 sets of 15 reps in each direction
  - **Wrist Flexor Stretch**: 3 sets of 30-second holds
  - **Wrist Extensor Stretch**: 3 sets of 30-second holds
  
  **Why**: Improved wrist mobility aids in better club control and reduces the risk of injury.
  
  **First Step**: Warm up wrists with light resistance bands.

#### Day 4: Dynamic Warm-Up Sequence
- **Routine**:
  - **Arm Circles**: 2 sets of 15 reps
  - **Leg Swings**: 2 sets of 15 reps per leg
  - **Torso Twists**: 2 sets of 15 reps
  - **High Knees**: 2 sets of 30 seconds
  
  **Why**: Prepares the body for physical activity by increasing heart rate and blood flow to muscles.
  
  **First Step**: Perform these exercises in sequence before practice or play.

#### Day 5: Post-Round Recovery
- **Routine**:
  - **Foam Rolling**: 5 minutes focusing on back, hips, and legs
  - **Static Stretching**: 30 seconds per stretch focusing on major muscle groups
  - **Hydration and Nutrition**: Replenish with water and a protein-rich snack
  
  **Why**: Facilitates muscle recovery and reduces soreness after a round of golf.
  
  **First Step**: Begin with foam rolling immediately after your round.
