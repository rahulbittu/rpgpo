## Plan for Eye Health Program for Heavy Screen Users

### Week 1: Implement the 20-20-20 Rule
- **What to Do:** Every 20 minutes, take a break to look at something 20 feet away for at least 20 seconds.
- **Why:** This practice helps reduce digital eye strain by allowing the eyes to relax and refocus, preventing fatigue.
- **Expected Outcome:** Improved comfort and reduced eye strain during prolonged screen use.
- **First Step:** Set a timer or use a reminder app to prompt breaks every 20 minutes.

### Week 2: Conscious Blinking Exercises
- **What to Do:** Practice conscious blinking every 30 minutes during screen time. Try to blink 10 times slowly to rehydrate the eyes.
- **Why:** Screen use reduces blink rate, leading to dry eyes. Conscious blinking helps maintain moisture and comfort.
- **Expected Outcome:** Reduced dryness and irritation.
- **First Step:** Incorporate blinking reminders into your daily routine using phone alarms or sticky notes on your monitor.

### Week 3: Adjust Screen Settings
- **What to Do:** Adjust screen brightness to match the surrounding environment and use blue light filters, especially in the evening.
- **Why:** Proper screen settings can reduce glare and blue light exposure, minimizing eye strain and potential sleep disruption.
- **Expected Outcome:** Enhanced visual comfort and reduced risk of digital eye strain.
- **First Step:** Access your device's display settings to adjust brightness and enable night mode or blue light filtering.

### Week 4: Schedule an Annual Eye Checkup
- **What to Do:** Schedule an annual eye exam with an optometrist or ophthalmologist.
- **Why:** Regular checkups can detect early signs of eye conditions and ensure corrective measures are taken promptly.
- **Expected Outcome:** Early detection and management of potential vision issues.
- **First Step:** Contact a local eye care provider to book an appointment, ideally aligning with your annual health checkup schedule.
