## Detailed Analysis

### Foam Roller Sequences
- **Foam Roller Thoracic Extensions**: Lie on your back with a foam roller placed horizontally under the upper back. Support your head with your hands and gently extend your spine over the roller. Perform 8-10 slow extensions to improve thoracic spine mobility.
- **Foam Roller Upper Back Roll**: Position the foam roller under your upper back and cross your arms over your chest. Roll back and forth slowly, focusing on the thoracic spine area. Spend 1-2 minutes on this exercise to release tension.

### Wall Slide Progressions
- **Basic Wall Slides**: Stand with your back against a wall, feet shoulder-width apart. Slide your arms up and down the wall, keeping elbows and wrists in contact. Perform 10 repetitions to enhance shoulder and upper back mobility.
- **Advanced Wall Slides with Rotation**: Add a rotational component by turning your torso slightly as you slide your arms up. This variation targets both mobility and rotational strength in the thoracic spine.

### Thread-the-Needle Variations
- **Basic Thread-the-Needle**: Start on all fours, reach your right arm under your body and across to the left, allowing your shoulder to rest on the ground. Hold for 20-30 seconds, then switch sides. This stretches and mobilizes the thoracic spine.
- **Dynamic Thread-the-Needle**: Perform the same movement but add a dynamic component by reaching the arm up towards the ceiling after threading it through, then returning to the start position. Repeat 5-8 times per side.
