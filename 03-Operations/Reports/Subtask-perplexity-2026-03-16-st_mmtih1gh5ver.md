## Finding 1: Carb Cycling Periodization for Half Marathon Training

**Specific Protocol:**
- **High carb days (5-7g/kg body weight)**: Scheduled after long runs and high-intensity workouts to maximize glycogen replenishment
- **Moderate carb days (3-5g/kg)**: On easy run days and recovery days
- **Lower carb days (2-3g/kg)**: On complete rest days or very light activity days

For a 70kg (154 lb) recreational runner:
- High carb day = 350-490g carbs
- Moderate carb day = 210-350g carbs
- Low carb day = 140-210g carbs

**Timing within periodization:**
- Base phase (weeks 1-4): Emphasis on moderate carbs to build aerobic base
- Build phase (weeks 5-10): Increase high carb days to 3-4x/week as mileage increases
- Peak phase (weeks 11-14): Maintain high carbs post-long run, reduce volume on other days
- Taper phase (final 2 weeks): Shift to moderate carbs with strategic loading 3 days pre-race

## Finding 2: Pre-Workout Nutrition Timing & Composition

**Pre-run fueling (1.5-3 hours before):**
- **Carbs**: 1-4g per kg body weight (70kg runner = 70-280g)
- **Protein**: 0.25g per kg body weight (70kg = 17.5g)
- **Fat & fiber**: Minimize to reduce GI distress
- **Hydration**: 400-600ml fluid 2-3 hours prior

**Specific examples for 70kg runner:**
- Light run (easy pace, <60 min): 150-200g carbs + 10-15g protein (banana + Greek yogurt, or oatmeal with berries)
- Moderate run (tempo/threshold, 60-90 min): 200-250g carbs + 15-20g protein (toast with peanut butter + orange juice)
- Long run (>90 min): 250-300g carbs + 20-25g protein (bagel with honey + egg whites, or pasta with lean meat)

**Timing rule**: Larger meals (>3g carbs/kg) = 3 hours before; smaller meals (<1.5g carbs/kg) = 30-60 minutes before

## Finding 4: Hydration Strategy During Training & Racing

**Daily baseline hydration:**
- **Minimum**: 35ml per kg body weight (70kg = 2.45L daily)
- **With training**: Add 500-1000ml per hour of exercise depending on sweat rate

**Sweat rate calculation (critical for personalization):**
- Weigh yourself before and after a 1-hour run in typical conditions
- Formula: (pre-weight - post-weight + fluid consumed) = sweat rate per hour
- Example: Runner loses 1.2kg, drank 400ml = 1.6L/hour sweat rate

**During-run hydration (for runs >60 minutes):**
- **Fluid**: 400-800ml per hour (adjust based on sweat rate, not exceeding 150% of sweat rate)
- **Carbs**: 30-60g per hour (6-8% carbohydrate solution)
- **Sodium**: 300-600mg per liter
- **Electrolytes**: Sodium, potassium, magnesium in 3:1 ratio

**Practical example for runner with 1.6L/hour sweat rate:**

## Finding 5: Supplement Timing for Half Marathon Training

**Evidence-based supplements with timing:**

1. **Caffeine** (proven ergogenic aid)
   - Dose: 3-6mg per kg body weight (210-420mg for 70kg runner)
   - Timing: 30-60 minutes pre-run
   - Effect: 2-3% performance improvement, reduced perceived effort
   - Note: Tolerance develops; cycle on/off (3 weeks on, 1 week off)

2. **Beta-alanine** (buffers lactate in high-intensity efforts)
   - Dose: 3-5g daily (split into 3-5 doses to minimize tingling)
   - Timing: Consistent daily dosing, not time-dependent
   - Duration: 4-6 weeks loading before peak phase
   - Effect: 2-3% improvement in threshold pace

3. **Beetroot juice/Nitrates**
   - Dose: 500ml (6.4mmol nitrate) or 70ml concentrated juice
   - Timing: 2-3 hours pre-run
   - Effect: 1-2% improvement in running economy
   - Best for: Tempo runs and threshold work

4. **Sodium bicarbonate** (buffering agent)
   - Dose: 0.3g per kg body weight (21g for 70kg runner)
   - Timing: 60-90 minutes pre-high-intensity session
   - Effect: 2-3% improvement in repeated efforts
   - Note: GI distress common; test in training first

5. **Iron** (if deficient)
   - Dose: 25-100mg elemental iron daily (with vitamin C for absorption)
   - Timing: Morning on empty stomach or with orange juice
   - Duration: 8-12 weeks if deficient (requires testing)
   - Effect: Improved oxygen carrying capacity if anemic

6. **Vitamin D** (if deficient, common in winter training)
   - Dose: 1000-4000 IU daily
   - Timing: With largest meal (fat-soluble)
   - Duration: Year-round, especially Nov-March
   - Effect: Immune function, bone health, mood

## Finding 6: Race Week Fueling Protocol (Final 7 Days)

**Days 7-4 before race (Monday-Thursday):**
- **Carbs**: Maintain normal periodization (don't overload yet)
- **Mileage**: Reduce to 40-50% of peak week volume
- **Focus**: Maintain glycogen, reduce fatigue, mental preparation
- **Hydration**: Normal daily intake + 500ml extra per day

**Days 3-2 before race (Friday-Saturday):**
- **Carb loading phase**:
  - Increase carbs to 8-10g per kg body weight (560-700g for 70kg runner)
  - Reduce fiber to minimize GI distress
  - Reduce fat to 20-25% of total calories
  - Reduce protein to 1.2-1.6g per kg (maintain muscle)
  - Example day: 600g carbs (pasta, rice, bread, sports drinks), 100g protein (chicken, fish), 40g fat
- **Mileage**: Very light (20-30 min easy jog or rest)
- **Hydration**: Increase to 40-50ml per kg body weight (2.8-3.5L for 70kg runner)
- **Sodium**: Add 500-1000mg extra per day to enhance fluid retention

**Race morning (2-3 hours before start):**
- **Carbs**: 1-4g per kg body weight based on wake time
  - Early morning race (7am): 200-280g carbs 2.5-3 hours before (bagel + honey + banana + sports drink)
  - Mid-morning race (9am): 150-200g carbs 2-2.5 hours before (oatmeal + berries + juice)
- **Protein**: 10-20g (aids satiety, prevents GI distress)
- **Fluid**: 400-600ml
- **Caffeine**: 3-6mg/kg (210-420mg) if trained with it
- **Avoid**: High fiber, high fat, high protein (GI distress risk)

**During race (13.1 miles / 21.1km):**
- **Carbs**: 30-60g per hour (use gels, sports drinks, or chews)
  - For 1:45-2:00 finish time: 1-2 gels (20-40g carbs) at miles 4, 8, and 12
- **Fluid**: 150-250ml every 15-20 minutes (adjust to sweat rate)
- **Sodium**: 300-600mg per liter of fluid
- **Caffeine**: Optional second dose at mile 8-10 if needed (100-200mg)

**Post-race (within 30 minutes):**
- **Carbs**: 1.2g per kg body weight (84g for 70kg runner)
- **Protein**: 0.25-0.4g per kg (17.5-28g)
- **Fluid**: 150% of body weight lost
- Example: Chocolate milk (500ml) + banana + sports drink
