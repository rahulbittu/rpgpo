## Finding 1: Preparation and Expertise for Introvert Public Speaking
Public speaking for introverts is mastered through **preparation and topic expertise** rather than natural charisma, allowing structured sharing of knowledge in comfortable formats.[1]  
- **Techniques**: Develop personal stories for meaningful contributions; use prepared topics and question-based engagement to guide conversations without dominating.  
- **Advanced application**: Leverage listening skills for group facilitation and conflict resolution, extending to presentations.  
Source: https://theartofcharm.com/art-of-personal-development/social-skills-for-introverts/

## Finding 2: Stanford Professor Matt Abrahams' 6-Word Framework for Speech Improvement
Stanford professor **Matt Abrahams** outlines a **6-word framework**—"Calm, Unlock, Redefine, Listen, Focus, Connect"—to improve any speech by taming anxiety and reframing challenges (article published around 2023-2024, relevant as timeless technique).[2]  
- **Step 1: Calm** – Tame anxiety.  
- **Step 2: Unlock** – Aim for “good enough.”  
- **Step 3: Redefine** – View problems as opportunities (e.g., audience questions as connection chances; use “Yes, And” improv technique to build on interruptions).  
- **Step 4: Listen** – Pace, space, grace in responses.  
- **Step 5/6**: Focus and connect (framework completes with these for full delivery).  
Source: https://www.upworthy.com/6-word-framework-stanford-professor-matt-abrahams/

## Finding 3: Susan Cain's Insights on Introvert Strengths in Speaking
Author **Susan Cain** (from "Quiet" book) notes introverts **listen more, think before speaking**, and excel in writing, making them strong in deliberate public contributions over constant talking.[2]  
- **Application for speaking**: Prepare thoughts in advance; trust timing for insightful input, as quiet observation leads to high-impact responses.
