## 7-Day Whole Foods Eating Plan

### Day 1: Monday
- **Breakfast**: Oatmeal with sliced bananas and walnuts
- **Lunch**: Quinoa salad with mixed greens, cherry tomatoes, chickpeas, and lemon-tahini dressing
- **Dinner**: Grilled salmon with steamed broccoli and brown rice
- **Snacks**: Apple slices with almond butter

### Day 2: Tuesday
- **Breakfast**: Smoothie with spinach, mango, flaxseeds, and almond milk
- **Lunch**: Lentil soup with whole-grain bread
- **Dinner**: Stir-fried tofu with bell peppers, carrots, and quinoa
- **Snacks**: Carrot sticks with hummus

### Day 3: Wednesday
- **Breakfast**: Avocado toast on whole-grain bread with a poached egg
- **Lunch**: Whole grain wrap with turkey, avocado, lettuce, and tomato
- **Dinner**: Baked chicken with sweet potato and green beans
- **Snacks**: Mixed berries

### Day 4: Thursday
- **Breakfast**: Greek yogurt with mixed berries and chia seeds
- **Lunch**: Black bean and corn salad with lime vinaigrette
- **Dinner**: Vegetable curry with brown rice
- **Snacks**: Celery sticks with peanut butter

### Day 5: Friday
- **Breakfast**: Whole grain pancakes with blueberries
- **Lunch**: Spinach and feta stuffed portobello mushrooms
- **Dinner**: Grilled shrimp with quinoa and asparagus
- **Snacks**: Orange slices

### Day 6: Saturday
- **Breakfast**: Smoothie bowl with banana, spinach, and almond milk topped with granola
- **Lunch**: Chickpea and avocado salad
- **Dinner**: Whole wheat pasta with tomato and basil sauce
- **Snacks**: Cucumber slices with tzatziki

### Day 7: Sunday
- **Breakfast**: Scrambled eggs with tomatoes and spinach
- **Lunch**: Mixed bean salad with olive oil and vinegar dressing
- **Dinner**: Roasted vegetable medley with grilled chicken
- **Snacks**: Pear slices

## Comprehensive Grocery List
- **Fruits**: Bananas, apples, mixed berries, oranges, mango, pears
- **Vegetables**: Spinach, mixed greens, broccoli, bell peppers, carrots, tomatoes, avocado, green beans, asparagus, sweet potatoes, celery, cucumbers
- **Proteins**: Salmon, tofu, chicken, turkey, shrimp, eggs
- **Grains**: Oatmeal, quinoa, brown rice, whole-grain bread, whole wheat pasta, whole grain wraps
- **Legumes**: Chickpeas, black beans, lentils
- **Nuts/Seeds**: Walnuts, flaxseeds, chia seeds, almond butter, peanut butter
- **Dairy/Alternatives**: Greek yogurt, almond milk
- **Other**: Olive oil, lemon-tahini dressing, hummus, tzatziki, granola

## Prep Schedule
- **Sunday**: 
  - Prepare quinoa, brown rice, and lentils in bulk
  - Chop vegetables for salads and snacks
  - Make lemon-tahini dressing and store
- **Midweek**: 
  - Refresh vegetable prep
  - Prepare additional grains if needed

## Timeline of Expected Benefits
- **Week 1**: Improved digestion and energy levels due to increased fiber intake
- **Week 2-4**: Noticeable weight management and enhanced mental clarity
- **Month 2-3**: Reduced risk factors for chronic diseases, improved metabolic health
- **Ongoing**: Sustained mental health improvements, reduced grocery costs, and environmental impact

## Evidence Basis
- **Gut Health**: 30 different plant foods weekly improve microbiome diversity (American Gut Project)
- **Weight/Metabolic Health**: Fiber-rich diets linked to obesity and diabetes risk reduction (Tufts University)
- **Mental Health**: Positive outcomes from increased fruit and vegetable intake (APA research)
- **Cost/Environmental Impact**: Plant-forward diets reduce costs and emissions (Planetary Health Diet outcomes)

## Progression
- **Start**: Implement the plan on Day 1, follow prep schedule
- **Monitor**: Track energy, mood, and physical changes weekly
- **Adjust**: Modify recipes based on preferences and seasonal produce

## Safety Notes
- Ensure adequate protein intake, especially if plant-based
- Consult a healthcare provider if you have dietary restrictions or health conditions
