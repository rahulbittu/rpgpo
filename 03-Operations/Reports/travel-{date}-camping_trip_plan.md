## Key Findings

1. **Campground Options**: Consider Emma Long Metropolitan Park for its proximity to Turkey Creek Trail and urban amenities. McKinney Falls State Park is another option with access to multiple trails and planned improvements.
2. **Gear Checklist**: Focus on lightweight, weather-resistant gear suitable for a weekend trip, including a tent, sleeping bags, and a portable stove.
3. **Meal Plan**: Prioritize easy-to-prepare meals using non-perishable ingredients, with options for breakfast, lunch, and dinner.
4. **Hiking Trails**: Barton Creek Greenbelt and River Place Nature Trail are top choices for their scenic views and varying difficulty levels.

## Detailed Analysis

### Campground Options

- **Emma Long Metropolitan Park**: Offers access to Turkey Creek Trail, suitable for easy to moderate hikes. It's dog-friendly and provides creek access for cooling off. Ideal for those wanting a quick escape from Austin while still enjoying nature[1].
  
- **McKinney Falls State Park**: Provides a more extensive trail network and is part of a greenway plan. It offers scenic views and opportunities for swimming in Onion Creek. Planned improvements may enhance trail connectivity[1][3].

### Gear Checklist

- **Shelter and Sleeping**:
  - 2-person, lightweight, weather-resistant tent
  - Compact sleeping bags rated for the season
  - Sleeping pads or inflatable mattresses
  - Ground tarp or footprint

- **Cooking and Food Storage**:
  - Portable camping stove with fuel canisters
  - Lightweight cookware set
  - Biodegradable soap and sponge
  - Cooler or insulated bag

- **Clothing and Footwear**:
  - Layered clothing (moisture-wicking)
  - Hiking boots with good traction
  - Sun protection gear (hat, sunglasses)
  - Rain gear

- **Safety and Navigation**:
  - First aid kit
  - Map and compass or GPS
  - Multi-tool or knife
  - Headlamp or flashlight

### Meal Plan

- **Breakfast**: Instant oatmeal with dried fruits and nuts; coffee or tea.
- **Lunch**: Wraps with canned tuna or chicken, cheese, and tortillas.
- **Dinner**: Pasta with pre-made sauce or dehydrated meals; hot chocolate or tea.
- **Snacks**: Trail mix, granola bars, and dried fruits.

### Ranked Hiking Trails

1. **Barton Creek Greenbelt**: Offers a 12.68-mile trail with intermediate difficulty, featuring limestone pools and scenic views. Ideal for a full day of hiking with shorter options available[1].
2. **River Place Nature Trail**: A moderate 5.5-mile trail with elevation gain and waterfall views, perfect for a half-day hike[1].
3. **Turkey Creek Trail**: Easy-moderate, dog-friendly trail within Emma Long Metropolitan Park, suitable for a relaxed hike[1].
