## Easy Trails
- **West Fork Trail (Oak Creek Canyon)**: 6.9 miles round-trip, 600 feet elevation gain. Lush canyon with creek crossings, red rock walls, sycamores; family-friendly, shaded, great for photography especially in fall.[1]
- **Fay Canyon Trail**: 1-2 miles round-trip, minimal elevation gain. Beginner-friendly with epic views near Uptown Sedona; designated easy by Coconino National Forest.[2]

## Moderate Trails
- **Soldier Pass Trail**: 4.5 miles round-trip, 600 feet elevation gain. Features Seven Sacred Pools (rainwater basins), Devil’s Kitchen sinkhole (collapsed sandstone cavern from 1800s); connects to Brins Mesa loop. Parking closed Thu-Sun; use Sedona Shuttle.[1]
- **Doe Mountain Trail**: 1.6 miles round-trip, 500 feet elevation gain. Short steep climb to flat mesa with panoramic views of western canyons; underrated local favorite.[1]
- **Boynton Canyon Trail**: 6.9 miles, 1158 feet elevation gain. Explores red rock formations and diverse landscapes in Yavapai County.[3]

## Moderate-Strenuous to Strenuous Trails
- **Cathedral Rock Trail**: 1.2 miles round-trip, 750 feet elevation gain. Iconic red rock climb. Parking closed Thu-Sun; use Sedona Shuttle from Back O’ Beyond Road trailhead.[1]

**Notes**: Sedona has over 200 trails covering 400+ miles for all levels; check U.S. Forest Service Sedona Trails Map for regulations. Data from recent guides (no specific publication dates in results).[1][2][3]
