## Finding 1: One-Month Early Time-Restricted Eating (eTRE) Enhances Cognition via White Matter Changes
- **Study Details**: In a 2026 randomized controlled trial, 1 month of eTRE (eating window restricted to early daytime) in healthy adults led to significant cognitive improvements: immediate recall and delayed recall on Rey Auditory Verbal Learning Test (RAVLT) increased (p < 0.01), processing speed reaction time shortened (p = 0.002), and executive function enhanced. Tract-based spatial statistics (TBSS) showed fractional anisotropy (FA) increases in left anterior thalamic radiation (ATR_L, r = -0.513 with reduced fasting glucose, p = 0.017) and bilateral corticospinal tracts. Mediation analysis confirmed reduced right dorsolateral prefrontal cortex (DLPFC) thickness mediated FA-delayed recall link (ACME = 10.07, 95% CI 1.10–37.84, p = 0.021). Metabolic benefits: body weight, BMI, fasting glucose, insulin, HOMA-IR decreased (all p < 0.01); QUICKI increased[1].
- **Source**: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/nutrition/articles/10.3389/fnut.2026.1753462/full

## Finding 2: UCSD Ongoing Trial on 14-Hour Nightly Fasting for MCI/AD
- **Trial Details**: UCSD trial (recruiting in 2025-2026, La Jolla/San Diego, CA) tests 14-hour nightly fasting (e.g., stop eating 8 PM, resume 10 AM) for 3-6 months in MCI/early-moderate AD patients vs. <12-hour fasting controls. Primary outcomes: AD pathology markers, cognitive tests, sleep quality at 3/6/12 months. Measures adherence via diary/app and activity tracker; explores partner fasting for compliance. No results available yet[2].
- **Source**: https://clinicaltrials.ucsd.edu/intermittent-fasting

## Finding 3: 3-Year IF Study in MCI Older Adults Shows Sustained Cognitive Gains
- **Study Details**: 3-year observational study (published ~2025) in older adults with mild cognitive impairment (MCI): regular IF practitioners had improved Mini-Mental State Examination (MMSE) and Montreal Cognitive Assessment (MoCA) scores at 36 months, vs. declines in non-practitioners. IF group showed lower oxidative stress (higher superoxide dismutase, reduced DNA damage, C-reactive protein). Pilot on 14-hour prolonged nightly fasting (PNF) in memory decline: improved Memory and Attention Phone Screener scores and reduced insomnia after 8 weeks[3].
- **Source**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12954078/

## Finding 4: Systematic Review on IF for Cognitive/Mental Health in Older Adults
- **Review Details**: 2026 systematic review found potential for IF/time-restricted eating to improve cognitive function and mental health in older adults, though evidence is preliminary[4].
