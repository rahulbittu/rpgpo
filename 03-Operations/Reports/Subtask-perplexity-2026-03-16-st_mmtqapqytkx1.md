## Wrist Mobility and Strength Exercises for Climbers and Programmers

Wrist mobility and strength exercises are crucial for climbers to prevent injuries from gripping and for programmers to counter desk-induced stiffness and repetitive strain. Below are specific exercises drawn from targeted sources, with descriptions, benefits, and progressions tailored to these groups.

### Wall Walks for Wrist and Forearm Strength
- **Description**: Start in a plank position facing a wall, walk hands up the wall while lifting feet, distributing weight evenly across palms and fingers to build wrist resilience. Move one hand at a time for stability; use parallettes if wrists hurt to reduce extension.
- **Benefits for Climbers**: Builds grip strength and wrist endurance for supporting bodyweight in overhangs and dynamic moves, mimicking climbing positions.
- **Benefits for Programmers**: Improves wrist extension tolerance lost from prolonged typing and mouse use; addresses pain from uneven palm pressure.
- **Progression**: Weeks 1-2: Weight shifts (3x30s); Weeks 3-4: Partial climbs (5x3); Weeks 5+: Full walks (3-5x2-3 reps).[1]

### Wrist Mobility Drills (Pre-Wall Walk Warmup)
- **Description**: Perform dynamic stretches like wrist circles, finger extensions, and prayer stretches (palms together, elbows out) for 1-2 minutes per side before loading exercises.
- **Benefits for Climbers**: Enhances full wrist range for crimps and pinches, reducing injury risk during sessions.
- **Benefits for Programmers**: Restores flexion/extension lost from static desk postures, easing typing fatigue.
- **Usage**: Do before planks, bear crawls, or climbing; pair with even weight distribution.[1]

### Planks and Bear Crawls for Wrist Strengthening
- **Description**: Hold high plank (shoulders over wrists, body straight) for 20-60s, or bear crawl (on all fours, move forward/backward) for 3x20m, focusing on palm pressure.
- **Benefits for Climbers**: Develops isometric wrist hold for sustained grips on holds.
- **Benefits for Programmers**: Builds baseline strength to prevent RSI from keyboard work.
- **Progression**: Start with 3x20s planks, advance to bear crawls under fatigue.[1]

**Next Steps**:
- Test wrist pain: If present during planks, add 5min daily mobility drills for 2 weeks.
- Integrate 3x/week: 10min session before climbing or coding breaks.
- Track: Use app like Strong for logging reps; reassess wrist comfort after 4 weeks.[1]
