## Plan

### Week 1: Initial Recovery Phase
- **Days 1-2:**
  - **Gentle Range of Motion Exercises:**
    - Ankle circles, dorsiflexion, plantarflexion, and alphabet tracing with your foot.
    - Goal: Reduce swelling and maintain joint flexibility.

- **Days 3-7:**
  - **Progress to Pain-Free Walking:**
    - Begin weight-bearing as tolerated.
    - **Strengthening Exercises:**
      - Calf raises: 3 sets of 10 reps.
      - Resistance band exercises for peroneal muscles: 15–20 reps per side.
      - Single-leg balance work: 30 seconds per leg.

### Week 2: Strengthening and Proprioception
- **Strengthening:**
  - **Banded Fibularis Longus/Brevis Strengthening (Eversion):** 15–20 reps per side.
  - **Banded Posterior Tibialis Strengthening (Inversion):** 15–20 reps per side.
  - **Single-Leg Heel Raise with Pause:** 3 sets of 10 reps with a 2-second pause at the top.

- **Proprioception Training:**
  - Balance on an unstable surface (e.g., balance board or pillow) for 1-2 minutes per leg.

### Week 3: Advanced Strengthening and Dynamic Proprioception
- **Dynamic Balance Exercises:**
  - Single-leg hops: 3 sets of 10 reps per leg.
  - Lateral jumps over a line: 3 sets of 10 reps.

- **Increased Resistance:**
  - Increase resistance band strength as tolerated.
  - Continue single-leg heel raises with added weight if possible.

### Week 4: Return-to-Running Preparation
- **Taping Techniques:**
  - Use ankle taping or a brace during initial running sessions to provide support.
  - Techniques such as the "figure-eight" or "heel lock" are recommended.

- **Return-to-Running Criteria:**
  - Pain-free walking and hopping.
  - Full range of motion restored.
  - Strength at least 90% of the uninjured ankle.

### Week 5 and Beyond: Return to Running and Prevention
- **Gradual Return to Running:**
  - Start with short, easy runs on even surfaces.
  - Gradually increase distance and intensity over 2-3 weeks.

- **Prevention Strategies:**
  - Continue strengthening and proprioception exercises twice a week.
  - Incorporate agility drills and varied terrain running to improve adaptability.

## Progression
- **Week 1-2:** Focus on reducing swelling and regaining basic mobility.
- **Week 3:** Emphasize strength and proprioception to prepare for dynamic activities.
- **Week 4-5:** Transition to running with support, ensuring readiness through specific criteria.

## Safety Notes
- **Monitor Pain Levels:** Avoid exercises that cause pain beyond mild discomfort.
- **Consult a Professional:** If swelling or severe pain persists, seek medical advice.
- **Avoid Overloading:** Gradually increase exercise intensity to prevent overuse injuries.

By following this structured plan, runners can effectively rehabilitate their ankles post-sprain and safely return to their running routine.
