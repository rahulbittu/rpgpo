## Analysis

### Minimum Investment Amounts
- **Investment Range**: Real estate syndications in 2026 typically require investments between **$50,000 to $250,000**. This increase from previous years is due to tighter lending standards and higher borrowing costs. Some multifamily syndications accept as low as **$25,000**.
- **Accreditation Requirement**: Investors generally need to be accredited, with a net worth of at least $1 million or an income of $200,000 or more. Verification can be done through SEC Form D filings on EDGAR.

### Fee Structures
- **Waterfall Distribution**: This structure prioritizes LPs with a **6-8% preferred return** annually. Profits above this are split, often **70/30** between LPs and GPs.
- **Promote Interests**: GPs earn additional profit shares (20-30%) after preferred returns, alongside acquisition/disposition fees (1-2% of deal size).
- **Target IRR**: Common equity aims for a **15-25% IRR**, though it bears the first losses and lacks guaranteed payments.

### Tax Benefits
- Syndications offer tax advantages like **depreciation deductions**, **cost segregation**, and **bonus depreciation**, which can significantly reduce taxable income for LPs.

## Comparison

- **Investment Thresholds**: The current investment threshold is notably higher than in previous years, reflecting market adjustments and economic conditions.
- **Fee Structures**: The waterfall model remains a standard, but the specifics of promote interests and acquisition fees vary by sponsor.
- **Tax Benefits**: These remain a strong incentive for investors, though the phase-down of bonus depreciation could impact future returns.
