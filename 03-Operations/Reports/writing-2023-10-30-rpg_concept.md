## Tabletop RPG Concept: "Chronicles of the Librarium"

### Overview
"Chronicles of the Librarium" is a tabletop RPG where players take on the roles of librarians tasked with maintaining the integrity of narratives across various book worlds. Players navigate through parallel timelines and interact with characters within these worlds to prevent narrative paradoxes that could unravel entire stories.

### Core Mechanics

#### Parallel Timeline System
- **Mechanic Description**: Players can access multiple parallel timelines within a book world. Each timeline offers different outcomes based on player decisions, and knowledge from one timeline can influence actions in another.
- **Implementation**: Players use a "Chrono-Compass" to shift between timelines. Each shift costs a "Chrono Token," which can be earned through successful narrative interventions.
- **Outcome**: Encourages players to explore moral and strategic complexity by making choices that affect multiple timelines.

#### Morality Without Judgment
- **Mechanic Description**: Players face morally complex decisions that influence narrative outcomes without punitive measures. Choices are binary but lead to unexpected and wholesome twists.
- **Implementation**: Situations are presented as dilemmas (e.g., "Save the character or preserve the artifact"), with each choice leading to a unique narrative development.
- **Outcome**: Fosters a narrative-driven experience where players feel empowered to explore different moral perspectives.

### Character Classes

1. **Archivist**
   - **Role**: Keeper of knowledge, specializing in uncovering hidden truths within narratives.
   - **Abilities**: "Lore Dive" to reveal hidden plot elements, "Script Swap" to alter minor details in a timeline.
   - **First Step**: Choose a specialty in either historical or fictional archives to gain unique insights.

2. **Narrative Weaver**
   - **Role**: Expert in manipulating story threads to prevent paradoxes.
   - **Abilities**: "Thread Stitch" to repair narrative inconsistencies, "Plot Pivot" to redirect story arcs.
   - **First Step**: Select a primary genre (e.g., fantasy, mystery) for enhanced manipulation abilities.

3. **Chrono Guardian**
   - **Role**: Protector of timelines, adept at navigating parallel worlds.
   - **Abilities**: "Temporal Shield" to protect against timeline collapses, "Time Leap" for quick timeline shifts.
   - **First Step**: Develop a bond with a specific timeline for increased stability during interventions.

4. **Ink Mage**
   - **Role**: Wielder of magical ink, capable of creating and erasing narrative elements.
   - **Abilities**: "Ink Conjure" to summon narrative elements, "Erase" to remove paradox-inducing details.
   - **First Step**: Craft a signature ink with unique properties to enhance spellcasting.

### Starter Adventure: "The Tale of the Fractured Fable"

#### Adventure Synopsis
The players are summoned to the world of "The Enchanted Forest," where a narrative paradox threatens to split the story into conflicting versions. A character known as the "Lost Prince" is simultaneously a hero and a villain in different timelines.

#### Adventure Structure
- **Introduction**: Players receive a distress call from the Librarium's central archive, detailing the emergence of a paradox in "The Enchanted Forest."
- **Act 1: Investigation**: Players must gather information about the Lost Prince and his dual roles. Use "Lore Dive" and "Thread Stitch" to uncover clues.
- **Act 2: Timeline Navigation**: Employ the "Chrono-Compass" to explore parallel timelines. Players must decide whether to save the prince or preserve the forest's magic.
- **Act 3: Resolution**: Resolve the paradox by merging the timelines or choosing one definitive narrative. Use "Time Leap" and "Ink Conjure" to influence the outcome.

#### Expected Outcome
- **Resolution**: Players successfully prevent the narrative collapse, preserving the integrity of "The Enchanted Forest."
- **Rewards**: Gain "Chrono Tokens" and unique narrative artifacts to aid in future adventures.
