## Best Practices for Personal Retrospectives

Personal retrospectives involve structured self-reflection to assess progress, identify patterns, and plan adjustments, typically using simple formats like "What went well? What didn't? What to change?" to build self-awareness without self-criticism. Best practices emphasize monthly timing for course correction, implementing at least one actionable change per session, and dedicating 30 minutes to write down insights for sustainability.[1]

## Specific Prompts

### Monthly Review Prompts
Use these at month-end for leadership and performance reflection:
- What went well this month?[1]
- What did you handle poorly?[1]
- What pattern are you noticing in your behavior or dynamics?[1]
- What is one thing you want to do differently next month?[1]

Additional team-oriented format adaptable for personal use:
- What should we/you keep doing?[1]
- What should we/you stop doing?[1]
- What should we/you start doing?[1]

### Quarterly Review Prompts
From OKR cycles (adapt for personal goals):
- What worked in achieving key results?[2]
- What didn't work?[2]
- What did we/you learn?[2]

Separate retrospective on process:
- Were the goals well-written?[2]
- Did check-ins happen regularly?[2]
- Was the cadence right for focus?[2]

### Annual Review Prompts
No direct personal annual prompts in recent results; quarterly OKRs serve as stepping stones to annual ambitions, with end-of-year reflection mirroring quarterly: score achievements, reflect on learnings, and adjust process for the next cycle.[2] For pulse-style checks, track sentiment shifts quarterly instead of annually to catch issues early.[3]
