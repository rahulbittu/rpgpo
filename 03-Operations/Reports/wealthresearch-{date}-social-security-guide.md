## Analysis

### Claiming Strategies
- **Early Claiming Impact**: Claiming Social Security benefits at age 62 can reduce benefits by up to 30% if your Full Retirement Age (FRA) is 67. Conversely, delaying benefits past FRA until age 70 increases them by 8% annually. This strategy can maximize annual benefits to $62,172 by 2026.[4][6]
- **Investment Opportunity**: For high-net-worth individuals, claiming benefits early (at 62 or FRA) and investing them conservatively at a 4-5% pre-tax return can yield approximately $220,000 in liquid capital by age 70. This strategy may outperform delaying benefits until age 88-90.[2]
- **Cost of Living Adjustment (COLA)**: The 2026 COLA is projected at 2.8%, which adds roughly $50 monthly to average checks.[7]

### Spousal Benefits
- **Benefit Maximization**: A spouse can claim up to 50% of the higher earner's FRA benefit. For example, a lower-earning spouse receiving $874/month on their own record at FRA can switch to 50% of the higher earner's benefit once they claim at 70. If the primary earner claims early, the spousal benefit is reduced by 35%.[5][6]

## Comparison
- **Early Claiming vs. Delayed Benefits**: Early claiming offers immediate liquidity and potential investment growth, but reduces long-term benefits. Delaying increases monthly payouts significantly, providing greater long-term security.
- **Spousal Coordination**: Coordinating spousal benefits can maximize household income, especially if the higher earner delays claiming until age 70.

## Recommendation

1. **Verify Earnings Record**:
   - **What to Do**: Log into SSA.gov/myaccount to review your earnings statement for accuracy.
   - **Why**: Errors in your earnings record can reduce your benefits, as Social Security averages your top 35 earning years.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Ensure maximum eligible benefits based on accurate earnings history.
   - **First Step**: Access and review your Social Security earnings statement online.

2. **Model Claiming Scenarios**:
   - **What to Do**: Use SSA calculators to model different claiming scenarios.
   - **Why**: Understanding the financial impact of claiming at different ages helps in making informed decisions.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Clear understanding of how different claiming ages affect your benefits.
   - **First Step**: Run projections using the SSA calculators available at ssa.gov.

3. **Coordinate Spousal Benefits**:
   - **What to Do**: Discuss claiming strategies with your spouse, especially if one spouse has significantly higher earnings.
   - **Why**: Coordinating benefits can optimize household income and provide financial security.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Maximize combined household Social Security benefits.
   - **First Step**: Use the SSA spousal benefits calculator and discuss scenarios with your spouse.

## Risks & Disclaimers
- **Market Risks**: Investing early claimed benefits involves market risk, and actual returns may vary.
- **Longevity Risk**: Delaying benefits increases monthly payments but requires living longer to break even compared to early claiming.
- **Policy Changes**: Future changes in Social Security policy could impact benefits and claiming strategies. Always stay informed of legislative updates.
