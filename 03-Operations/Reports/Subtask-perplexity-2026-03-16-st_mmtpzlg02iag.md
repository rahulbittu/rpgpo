## 401(k) Catch-Up Contributions (2026 Limits)
- **Standard employee deferral**: $24,500 (up from $23,500 in 2025).[1][2][3]
- **Age 50+ catch-up**: Additional $8,000 (total $32,500); up from $7,500 in 2025.[1][2][3][4][5]
- **Ages 60-63 super catch-up**: Additional $11,250 (total $35,750); unchanged from 2025.[1][2][3][4][5][7]
- **Total combined limit (employee + employer, excluding catch-up)**: $72,000 (or 100% compensation); rises to $80,000 (age 50+) or $83,250 (ages 60-63).[2][3][4]
- **Roth-only rule (effective Jan 1, 2026)**: Catch-ups must be Roth if prior-year wages >$150,000 (2025 wages for 2026 contributions); applies to 401(k)/Solo 401(k).[4][5][8]

## IRA Catch-Up Contributions (2026 Limits)
