# TopRanker Operating Playbook

## Role of TopRanker inside RPGPO

TopRanker is the flagship business mission inside RPGPO.
It is the primary product bet and one of the main revenue opportunities.

RPGPO exists in part to accelerate TopRanker’s growth, quality, execution speed, and decision quality.

---

## TopRanker Objectives

- improve product usefulness
- improve trust and brand clarity
- reduce onboarding friction
- increase user conversion
- improve retention
- find repeatable growth channels
- test monetization intelligently
- ship consistently

---

## Daily TopRanker Loop

### 1. Review current state
Check:
- current feature progress
- bugs
- blockers
- recent changes
- pending decisions

### 2. Prioritize next work
Choose the highest-value next moves based on:
- user value
- product leverage
- speed of execution
- revenue potential
- trust and clarity impact

### 3. Research and compare
Review:
- competitors
- adjacent products
- user expectations
- pricing
- onboarding ideas
- messaging patterns

### 4. Build or refine
Execute on:
- code
- copy
- onboarding
- analytics
- content
- outreach

### 5. Review and critique
Use the critic function to ask:
- what is weak?
- what is unclear?
- what may not convert?
- what is overbuilt?
- what creates friction?

### 6. Log and learn
Capture:
- what changed
- what worked
- what failed
- what should be tested next

---

## TopRanker Decision Priorities

When tradeoffs appear, prioritize:

1. user trust
2. product usefulness
3. conversion clarity
4. speed of learning
5. shipping velocity
6. visual polish after core value is clear

---

## Key Operating Questions

TopRanker work should repeatedly answer:
- why would a user care?
- what pain is being reduced?
- what makes this credible?
- where is the friction?
- where will users discover it?
- why would they pay?
- what would make them stay?

---

## Daily Deliverables for TopRanker

At minimum, RPGPO should try to produce one or more of:
- prioritized next task
- improved feature plan
- better copy
- research memo
- growth experiment
- bug fix plan
- onboarding improvement
- monetization idea
- launch content draft

---

## Weekly Review Questions

Every week, ask:
- what moved the product forward?
- what wasted time?
- what is still unclear about users?
- what should be cut?
- what should be doubled down on?
- where is trust weak?
- what is the next highest-leverage move?

---

## Red Flags

Watch for:
- building without distribution thinking
- adding features without trust
- vague messaging
- too many simultaneous priorities
- no feedback loop
- no monetization learning
- spending time polishing what users do not value

---

## Final Rule

TopRanker should be treated like a serious business mission, not a side distraction.
Every week it should become clearer, sharper, more useful, or more monetizable.
