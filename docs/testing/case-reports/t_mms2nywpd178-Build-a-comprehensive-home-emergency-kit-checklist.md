## Detailed Analysis

### Water and Food Supplies
- **Water**: Each person needs 1 gallon per day, totaling 3 gallons for 72 hours. Replace bottled water every time you change your clocks (twice a year) to ensure freshness.
- **Food**: Prepare a 3-day supply of non-perishable items like canned goods, energy bars, and shelf-stable meals. A manual can opener is essential for accessing canned food.

### First Aid Kit
- **Basic Items**: Include gloves, gauze pads, bandages, antiseptic, scissors, and tweezers. A survival blanket and instant ice packs are also recommended.
- **Advanced Items**: Consider adding a needle and thread for emergency stitching and additional medical supplies based on family needs.

### Important Documents
- **Identification and Legal Papers**: Store passports, driver's licenses, birth certificates, and other critical documents in a waterproof container. Keep digital copies in secure cloud storage.
- **Financial and Insurance Docs**: Include bank details, credit card info, and insurance policies. Ensure these are easily accessible during emergencies.
- **Medical Records**: Maintain an updated list of prescriptions, allergies, and doctor's contacts. Include a supply of medications for at least 7-14 days.

### Communication Plan
- **5-Step Plan**: Assign a communication lead, develop message templates, and schedule regular updates. Test the plan quarterly to ensure effectiveness.

### Maintenance Schedule
- **Daylight Saving Time**: Use the "spring forward" change in March to check and update your emergency kit. This aligns with preparations for potential spring hazards.
- **Severe Weather Preparedness Week**: Participate in activities during the first week of March, such as statewide drills, to ensure readiness.
