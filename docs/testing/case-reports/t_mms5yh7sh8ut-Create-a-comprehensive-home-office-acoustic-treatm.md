## Sound Absorption vs Diffusion
**Absorption** uses porous materials like fiberglass, mineral wool, or open-cell foam to convert sound energy to heat, primarily effective for mid-to-high frequencies (500 Hz+ for 2-inch panels, 250 Hz+ for 4-inch); low frequencies (<250 Hz) need bass traps.[2] **Diffusion** scatters sound waves evenly to reduce echoes without deadening the space, often via irregular surfaces like wood slats; less emphasized in results but paired with absorption for balanced offices.[1][2] Absorption dominates office treatments for noise reduction, with NRC ratings ≥0.80 recommended (cheap foam often ≤0.50).[1]
