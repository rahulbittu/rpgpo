## Steps to Incorporate a Texas LLC

Forming a Texas LLC involves filing Form 205 (Certificate of Formation) with the Texas Secretary of State, appointing a registered agent, obtaining an EIN, creating an operating agreement, and setting up a bank account. Processing is typically 3-5 business days online, with approval confirmed via email including a Certificate of Filing.[2]

### 1. Choose a Compliant Business Name
- Name must be distinguishable from existing Texas entities and include "LLC" or "Limited Liability Company".[1][2]
- Check availability via Texas Secretary of State name search tool before filing to avoid rejection.[1][2]
- **Next step**: Search at https://mycpa.cpa.state.tx.us/coa/ (Texas SOS entity search).

### 2. Appoint a Registered Agent
- Must be an individual or entity with a physical Texas street address (no P.O. boxes) to receive legal notices.[1][2][3]
- Can be a member, employee, or hired service like Tailor Brands or GoDaddy.[3][4]
- Include agent's name and address in Form 205.[2]

### 3. File Certificate of Formation (Form 205)
- Download Form 205 from Texas Secretary of State website; file online via SOSDirect or mail.[2]
- **State filing fee**: $300 for online/mail filing (as of 2026).[3]
- Key details: LLC name, registered agent info, management structure (member-managed or manager-managed), organizer signature and date, effective date (immediate via Line A).[2]
- Track status with Texas Business Filing Tracker on SOS website; approval sends Welcome Letter, Certificate of Filing, and stamped Form 205.[2]
- **Next step**: File at https://direct.sos.state.tx.us/help/help-corp-filing.asp (SOS filing portal).

### 4. Obtain EIN
- Apply free via IRS website (Form SS-4 online) after LLC approval; required for taxes, banking, employees.[1][5]
- Takes minutes online; use LLC name, address, responsible party (e.g., organizer), business code.[5]
- **Next step**: Apply at https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online.

### 5. Create Operating Agreement
- Not required by Texas law but recommended for multi-member LLCs to outline ownership percentages, management, profit distribution, roles, decisions, exits.[1][4][5]
- No official state template; use customizable templates from services like Tailor Brands or free online generators (covers members' responsibilities, profit/loss splits).[4][5]
- **Next step**: Draft using Tailor Brands tool at https://www.tailorbrands.com/llc-formation or Rocket Lawyer free template.

### 6. Set Up Bank Account
- Open after EIN and Certificate of Filing; keep business finances separate from personal.[1]
- Banks require EIN, Certificate of Formation, operating agreement, ID; options include Chase, Bank of America (Texas branches).[1]
- **Next step**: Visit bank with documents; e.g., Chase business account at local Texas branch.

**Total startup cost**: $300 state fee + optional service fees ($0 + state fees via Tailor Brands).[
