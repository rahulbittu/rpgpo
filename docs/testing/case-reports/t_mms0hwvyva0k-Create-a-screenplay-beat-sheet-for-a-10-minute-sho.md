## Typical Structures in Short Film Beat Sheets

Short film beat sheets adapt feature-length structures like **three-act** or **Save the Cat** frameworks, compressing them into 8-15 key beats to fit 5-15 minute runtimes, emphasizing quick setup, escalating conflict, and tight resolution.[2][3][6]

- **Three-Act Structure (most common for shorts)**: Act 1 (0-25%, ~1-2.5 min): Setup with exposition and inciting incident. Act 2 (25-75%, ~5-7.5 min): Rising action, midpoint crisis, plot point 2. Act 3 (75-100%, ~2-2.5 min): Climax and denouement.[2][3]
- **Four-Act Structure**: Divides into quarters with plot points at 25%, 50% (midpoint), 75%; scalable for shorts by focusing on 8 beats including bookends (hook, inciting incident, midpoint, climax).[2]
- **Save the Cat Beat Sheet**: 15 beats including opening image (0-1%), catalyst (10%), midpoint (50%), all-is-lost (75%), finale (99-100%); used for shorts by prioritizing emotional turns over subplots.[3][8]

## Elements in Short Film Beat Sheets

Beat sheets list 10-20 milestones with page/minute estimates, one-sentence descriptions, and character stakes; rated on character, dialog, plot, theme, resolution for feedback.[1][7]

- Core elements: Hook/opening image, inciting incident, stakes raise, midpoint reversal, climax confrontation, resolution payoff.
- Per beat: Scene summary, emotional change, visual action; e.g., "Protagonist discovers secret (inciting, min 1), responds with denial (rising, min 3)."[2][6]
- Visual aids: Plot diagrams label exposition, rising action (crises waves), climax, falling action.[4]
