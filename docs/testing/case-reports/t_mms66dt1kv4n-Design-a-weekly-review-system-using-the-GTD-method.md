## GTD Weekly Review Overview
The GTD (Getting Things Done) weekly review is a core practice for maintaining clarity and control, structured into phases like 'Get Clear,' 'Get Current,' and 'Get Creative' to process tasks, update lists, and spark creativity.[1][4] It involves reviewing all commitments, including the "Someday/Maybe" list, to keep ideas alive without immediate action pressure.[3]

## Trigger Questions and Process Steps
Specific trigger questions guide the review to ensure completeness:
- **Get Clear phase**: Review inboxes, calendars, and lists—ask "What has my attention?" to capture everything outstanding.[1]
- **Get Current phase**: Update next actions, projects, and waiting-for lists; check "Is everything current and actionable?"[1][4]
- **Get Creative phase**: Brainstorm new ideas and horizons; trigger with "What could I do next week?" or scan Someday/Maybe for relevance.[1][3]
