## 2026 Tax Law Changes Impacting Charitable Giving
Effective for tax year 2026 under the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA), key changes include a **0.5% AGI floor** for itemized charitable deductions (only amounts exceeding 0.5% of AGI are deductible), a **35% cap** on deduction value for top 37% bracket taxpayers (reducing benefit from 37 cents to 35 cents per dollar), **standard deductions** of $16,100 (single) or $32,200 (married filing jointly), and a new **above-the-line cash deduction** up to $1,000 (single) or $2,000 (married) for non-itemizers (excludes DAFs and supporting organizations).[1][3][4][8]

## Donor-Advised Funds (DAFs)
DAFs enable bunching multi-year contributions in one year to exceed the 0.5% AGI floor, with grants distributed over time; ineligible for the new above-the-line deduction for non-itemizers.[1][7]

## Qualified Charitable Distributions (QCDs)
For those 70½+, QCDs allow direct IRA transfers up to **$108,000** (one source) or **$111,000** (2026 limit, another source) to charities, excluding the amount from taxable income, satisfying RMDs, and bypassing the 0.5% AGI floor entirely.[2][3][5]

## Appreciated Stock Donations
Donate long-term appreciated assets (stocks, bonds, crypto held >1 year) directly for a deduction of full fair market value, avoiding capital gains tax; example: stock bought for $1,000 now worth $5,000 yields $5,000 deduction with no gain recognized.[2][3][4]

## Charitable Remainder Trusts (CRTs)
No specific 2026 updates found in results for CRTs; general strategy involves transferring assets for income stream and remainder to charity, but lacks new data here.

## Bunching Strategies
Combine multiple years' donations into one (e.g., via DAFs) to surpass 0.5% AGI floor and standard deduction threshold, then take standard deduction alternate years; maximizes multi-year benefits under OBBBA.[4][6][7]

## Detailed Analysis

### Donor-Advised Funds (DAFs)
DAFs are an effective tool for high earners to manage and optimize their charitable giving. By "bunching" contributions, donors can exceed the 0.5% AGI floor and maximize their tax benefits in a single year. This strategy is particularly useful for those who may not itemize every year due to high standard deductions.

### Qualified Charitable Distributions (QCDs)
QCDs offer a unique opportunity for older taxpayers to make tax-efficient donations directly from their IRAs. By excluding the distribution from taxable income, donors can effectively lower their AGI, which may have additional tax benefits, such as reducing Medicare premiums.

### Appreciated Stock Donations
Donating appreciated stocks or other long-term assets is highly tax-efficient, as it allows donors to deduct the full market value of the asset without incurring capital gains tax. This can significantly increase the effective value of the donation compared to selling the asset and donating the proceeds.

### Charitable Remainder Trusts (CRTs)
CRTs can be an excellent option for those looking to balance income needs with charitable goals. They allow donors to receive a stream of income for life or a term of years, with the remainder going to charity. This can also provide estate tax benefits and diversify income sources.

## Recommended Actions

### Donor-Advised Funds (DAFs)
- **What to Do**: Consider opening a DAF if you plan to make significant charitable contributions.
- **Why**: To maximize tax benefits through bunching and strategic grant distribution.
- **Expected Outcome**: Increased tax efficiency and flexibility in charitable giving.
- **First Step**: Research and select a reputable DAF provider that aligns with your philanthropic goals.

### Qualified Charitable Distributions (QCDs)
- **What to Do**: Utilize QCDs if you are eligible and have RMDs.
- **Why**: To reduce taxable income and satisfy RMDs in a tax-efficient manner.
- **Expected Outcome**: Lower AGI and potential reduction in other tax liabilities.
- **First Step**: Consult with your financial advisor to coordinate QCDs with your IRA custodian.

### Appreciated Stock Donations
- **What to Do**: Identify appreciated assets in your portfolio for donation.
- **Why**: To leverage full market value deductions and avoid capital gains tax.
- **Expected Outcome**: Enhanced tax savings and increased donation impact.
- **First Step**: Work with your financial advisor to select the best assets for donation and coordinate with the receiving charity.

### Charitable Remainder Trusts (CRTs)
- **What to Do**: Explore setting up a CRT if you seek income and estate planning benefits.
- **Why**: To secure an income stream while supporting charitable causes.
- **Expected Outcome**: Tax benefits, income diversification, and charitable impact.
- **First Step**: Consult with an estate planning attorney to draft and establish a CRT tailored to your needs.
