## Escape Room Designs Based on Scientific Discoveries

### 1. High-Pressure Chemistry Simulation
**Theme:** AI Framework for High-Pressure Chemistry

**Overview:** This escape room immerses players in the role of scientists tasked with simulating chemical reactions under extreme planetary core pressures. The goal is to discover a new high-density material.

**Puzzles:**
- **Molecular Bonding Puzzle:** Players use magnetic pieces to simulate atomic bonding. These pieces must be assembled under timed "pressure," which is physically represented by inflating balloons or weights that must be balanced.
  - **Science Understanding:** Players learn about atomic bonding and pressure effects on materials, reflecting the AI's role in predicting these reactions.
  - **First Step:** Introduce the concept of atomic structures and bonding with a brief tutorial or visual guide.

- **UV-Reactive Formula Discovery:** Once the bonding puzzle is completed, players use UV lights to reveal hidden formulas on props, simulating the discovery of new materials.
  - **Science Understanding:** This puzzle illustrates the process of uncovering new material properties, akin to the AI's predictive capabilities.
  - **First Step:** Equip players with UV flashlights and provide a brief on how UV light can reveal hidden features.

**Outcome:** Successfully assembling the molecular structure and revealing the formula unlocks a safe containing a "new alloy" key, symbolizing the discovery of a high-density material.

### 2. Protein Drug Design Challenge
**Theme:** MIT AI for Protein Drug Design

**Overview:** Participants are scientists in a race to develop a new drug by correctly folding proteins to match biological targets, reducing the need for extensive lab trials.

**Puzzles:**
- **3D Protein Folding:** Players manipulate glow-in-the-dark strings or hinged cardboard models to fold proteins. The correct configuration matches a target model displayed in the room.
  - **Science Understanding:** This task demonstrates the complexity of protein folding and its impact on drug design, mirroring the AI's ability to predict these structures.
  - **First Step:** Provide a basic introduction to protein structures and folding techniques.

- **Laser Sensor Activation:** Correctly folded proteins activate a laser sensor, releasing a vial of "drug serum" that neutralizes a simulated hazard, such as fog or flashing lights.
  - **Science Understanding:** This represents the successful creation of a drug that can target and neutralize specific biological threats.
  - **First Step:** Set up a demonstration of how laser sensors can be used in scientific applications.

**Outcome:** Completing the protein folding and neutralizing the hazard signifies the creation of an effective drug, highlighting the AI's potential to revolutionize drug development.

### 3. Neuromorphic Computers for Physics Simulations
**Theme:** Neuromorphic Computing for Advanced Physics

**Overview:** Players explore the capabilities of neuromorphic computers in simulating complex physical systems, aiming to solve a critical physics problem.

**Puzzles:**
- **Circuit Connection Challenge:** Players must connect a series of circuits using conductive pathways to simulate the neural networks of a neuromorphic computer.
  - **Science Understanding:** This illustrates how neuromorphic systems mimic brain functions to process information efficiently.
  - **First Step:** Provide a brief on neuromorphic computing and its applications in physics simulations.

- **Physics Simulation Puzzle:** Using interactive screens, players adjust variables in a simulated environment to achieve a stable system, demonstrating the computer's simulation capabilities.
  - **Science Understanding:** This task shows how neuromorphic computers can model complex physical phenomena.
  - **First Step:** Offer a tutorial on basic physics principles relevant to the simulation.

**Outcome:** Successfully completing the circuit and simulation challenges unlocks a final clue, representing the breakthrough in physics research facilitated by neuromorphic computing.
