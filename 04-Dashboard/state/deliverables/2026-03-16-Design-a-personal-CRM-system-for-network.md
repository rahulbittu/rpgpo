## Research existing CRM systems
## Affinity CRM
Affinity is a top CRM for relationship management in private equity and networking-heavy sectors, emphasizing automated interaction capture and network visualization.

- **Contact categorization**: Network graphs and relationship scoring to map connections and prioritize contacts.
- **Interaction tracking**: Automatic email and meeting capture with contact/company enrichment, activity timelines, notes, and AI-powered summaries.
- **Follow-up automation**: Deal pipelines with reminders, task tracking, and natural-language search across interactions.
- **Integrations**: Native with Gmail, Outlook, LinkedIn (implied via social capture), PitchBook, Preqin, Salesforce.
- **Pricing**: Contact sales; notable clients include Samsung NEXT, Redalpine.
- **Source**: https://collegian.com/sponsored/2026/03/4-best-private-equity-crm-solutions-for-2026/[3]

## Nimble CRM
Nimble is a relationship-focused CRM that aggregates interactions from multiple channels into unified profiles, ideal for small teams emphasizing networking.

- **Contact categorization**: Automatic enrichment from 160+ sources including social profiles; unified profiles with relationship intelligence.
- **Interaction tracking**: Unified inbox for email, Twitter, LinkedIn messages; social listening for mentions and engagement.
- **Follow-up automation**: Pipeline management with customizable stages; Today page with prioritized action items and relationship reminders; group messaging.
- **Integrations**: Email, LinkedIn, Twitter; browser extension (Nimble Prospector) overlays CRM data on websites.
- **Pricing**: Not specified in results; best for 1-10 person teams.
- **Source**: https://lagrowthmachine.com/crm-software-examples-2026/[6]

## Salesforce PRM (Partner Relationship Management)
Salesforce PRM excels in networking via partner ecosystems, with deep CRM integration for relationship tracking.

- **Contact categorization**: Multi-tier partner support; performance predictions via Einstein AI.
- **Interaction tracking**: Native CRM data sync; partner community portals with forums and collaboration.
- **Follow-up automation**: Automated lead routing by territory/certification; next-best-action recommendations.
- **Integrations**: Built on Salesforce Sales Cloud (email/LinkedIn via ecosystem); Experience Cloud portals.
- **Pricing**: From $25/user/month.
- **Source**: https://www.guideflow.com/blog/best-prm-software[1]

## HubSpot CRM
HubSpot supports relationship management through its free tier and marketing/sales integrations, with strong automation for follow-ups.

- **Contact categorization**: Deal pipeline management; customer segmentation via Breeze AI.
- **Interaction tracking**: Email tracking/notifications; website engagement insights; full account views.
- **Follow-up automation**: Sales automation, meeting scheduling, quote-to-pay workflows.
- **Integrations**: 1,500+ apps including email; LinkedIn via marketplace (not explicitly detailed here).
- **Pricing**: Free; paid Starter from $15/seat/month.
- **Source**: https://meetingnotes.com/blog/best-crm-software[2]

## Key Findings

### Affinity CRM
- **Contact Categorization**: Utilizes network graphs and relationship scoring to map connections and prioritize contacts.
- **Interaction Tracking**: Automates email and meeting capture, enriches contact/company data, and provides activity timelines and AI-powered summaries.
- **Follow-up Automation**: Includes deal pipelines with reminders, task tracking, and natural-language search.
- **Integrations**: Integrates with Gmail, Outlook, LinkedIn, PitchBook, Preqin, Salesforce.
- **Pricing and Clients**: Contact sales for pricing; clients include Samsung NEXT, Redalpine.

### Nimble CRM
- **Contact Categorization**: Enriches contacts from over 160 sources, creating unified profiles with relationship intelligence.
- **Interaction Tracking**: Offers a unified inbox for email and social media interactions; includes social listening for mentions and engagement.
- **Follow-up Automation**: Features pipeline management, a Today page with action items, and group messaging.
- **Integrations**: Integrates with email services and social media platforms.

## Recommendations

1. **Contact Categorization**
   - **What to Do**: Implement a system of network graphs and relationship scoring similar to Affinity to visualize connections and prioritize contacts.
   - **Why**: Helps in identifying key relationships and focusing on high-priority contacts.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Enhanced ability to manage and leverage your network effectively.
   - **First Step**: Develop or integrate a tool for network visualization and scoring.

2. **Interaction Tracking**
   - **What to Do**: Automate the capture of emails, meetings, and social interactions, and enrich contact data from various sources.
   - **Why**: Streamlines data collection and provides a comprehensive view of interactions.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Improved relationship management through a detailed interaction history.
   - **First Step**: Integrate with email and social media APIs for automatic data capture.

3. **Follow-up Automation**
   - **What to Do**: Create deal pipelines with reminders and task tracking, using AI for natural-language processing.
   - **Why**: Ensures timely follow-ups and reduces manual tracking effort.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Increased efficiency in managing follow-ups and tasks.
   - **First Step**: Design a pipeline management interface with task automation features.

4. **Integrations**
   - **What to Do**: Ensure seamless integration with Gmail, Outlook, LinkedIn, and other relevant platforms.
   - **Why**: Facilitates data flow and interaction tracking across platforms.
   - **Expected Outcome**: A unified and comprehensive CRM experience.
   - **First Step**: Identify and prioritize key integrations based on user needs.

## Action Items

4. **Week 7-8**: Finalize integrations with key platforms like LinkedIn and email services.
5. **Week 9-10**: Launch a beta version and gather user feedback for further refinement.
