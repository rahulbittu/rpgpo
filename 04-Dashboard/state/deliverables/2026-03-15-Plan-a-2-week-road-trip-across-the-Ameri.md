## Popular Road Trip Routes
The **Southwest Loop** is a popular 10-day RV itinerary starting in Phoenix, AZ, covering Sedona, Grand Canyon South Rim (Days 2-3), Zion National Park, Bryce Canyon National Park, Arches National Park, Canyonlands National Park, and optionally Mesa Verde National Park, with reasonable drive days under 2 hours between key stops like Bryce[1].  
A 7-day non-hiker itinerary from Los Angeles totals over 1,500 miles through Utah, Arizona, Colorado, and New Mexico: Day 1 Zion NP (stay Hurricane, UT); Day 2 Bryce Canyon NP (stay Hatch, UT); Day 3 Horseshoe Bend (stay Mexican Hat, UT); Day 4 Monument Valley tour and Mesa Verde NP drive (stay Gallup, NM); Day 5 Petrified Forest NP (stay Sedona, AZ); Day 6 Grand Canyon NP (stay Kingman, AZ); Day 7 return to LA[2].  
**Desert View Drive** along Grand Canyon South Rim offers quieter vistas with stops at Desert View Watchtower[3].

## National Parks and Key Attractions
- **Grand Canyon NP (South Rim, AZ)**: 2-3 days recommended; Desert View Drive for panoramas[1][3].
- **Zion NP (UT)**: Day hikes like West Fork Trail (mini-Zion feel, cool off in Oak Creek)[1]; accessible for non-hikers[2].
- **Bryce Canyon NP (UT)**: Queen's Garden/Navajo Combination Loop (moderate, hoodoos and switchbacks, top for first-timers)[1][2].
- **Arches NP and Canyonlands NP (UT)**: Core loop stops[1].
- **Mesa Verde NP (CO)**: Cliff dwellings of Ancestral Pueblo (700+ years), low-key drives/tours[1][2].
- **Sedona (AZ)**: Cathedral Rock (short, rewarding views), Devil's Bridge (challenging), Oak Creek Canyon[1].
- Others: Horseshoe Bend, Monument Valley, Petrified Forest NP[2].

## Recommended Actions

1. **Plan Route and Book Accommodations**: Confirm the route and book accommodations in advance to secure the best rates and availability. Start with booking in high-demand areas like Moab and Sedona.
   
2. **Purchase a National Parks Pass**: Consider buying an America the Beautiful pass for $80, which covers entrance fees for all national parks, saving $130 if visiting six parks.

3. **Prepare Vehicle**: Ensure the vehicle is road-trip ready with a maintenance check, especially tires and brakes. Consider renting an RV if preferred for convenience and accommodation savings.

4. **Pack Accordingly**: Bring hiking gear, a cooler for food storage, and a camera for capturing scenic views. Prepare for varying weather conditions across different parks.

5. **Review Park Regulations**: Check each park's website for specific regulations, trail closures, and safety tips to ensure a smooth visit.
