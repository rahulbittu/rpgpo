## Synthesize Plantar Fascia Recovery Program
## Plan

### Week 1-2: Initial Recovery and Pain Management
- **Objective:** Reduce pain and inflammation, initiate gentle mobility.
- **Exercises:**
  - **Toe Curls:** 3 sets of 10 reps daily to engage intrinsic foot muscles.
  - **Towel Scrunches:** 3 sets of 10 reps daily to strengthen foot arches.
  - **Calf Stretching:** Hold each stretch for 30 seconds, 3 times per leg, twice daily.
- **Orthotics:**
  - Use insoles with arch support and a heel-to-toe drop of 8-12mm, such as Superfeet or PCSsole.
- **Safety Note:** Avoid high-impact activities; focus on low-intensity exercises to prevent exacerbating pain.

### Week 3-4: Progressive Loading
- **Objective:** Begin tissue loading to stimulate collagen production.
- **Exercises:**
  - **High-Load Strength Training (HLST):** Calf raises on a step, 3 sets of 12 reps, every other day. Increase weight gradually.
  - **Foot Doming:** 3 sets of 10 reps daily to activate intrinsic foot muscles.
- **Orthotics:**
  - Continue using supportive insoles; adjust based on comfort and support needs.
- **Safety Note:** Monitor pain levels closely; reduce intensity if pain increases.

### Week 5-6: Advanced Strengthening
- **Objective:** Enhance load tolerance and tissue capacity.
- **Exercises:**
  - **Single-Leg Calf Raises:** 3 sets of 10 reps per leg, three times a week.
  - **Resisted Toe Flexion:** Use resistance bands, 3 sets of 10 reps daily.
- **Orthotics:**
  - Evaluate the need for custom orthotics if over-the-counter options are insufficient.
- **Safety Note:** Ensure proper form during exercises to avoid injury.

### Week 7-8: Return-to-Activity
- **Objective:** Gradually reintroduce activities and assess readiness for full activity.
- **Exercises:**
  - **Plyometric Drills:** Light jumping exercises, 2 sets of 10 reps, twice a week.
  - **Balance Exercises:** Single-leg stands for 30 seconds, 3 sets per leg, daily.
- **Criteria for Return-to-Activity:**
  - Pain-free during and after exercises.
  - Full range of motion in the ankle and foot.
  - Ability to perform daily activities without discomfort.
- **Safety Note:** Gradually increase the intensity of activities; avoid sudden increases in load.
