## Understanding Medicare Enrollment for People Approaching 65

### Medicare Parts Overview
- **Part A (Hospital Insurance)**: Covers inpatient hospital stays, care in a skilled nursing facility, hospice care, and some home health care. Most people don't pay a premium if they or their spouse paid Medicare taxes while working.
- **Part B (Medical Insurance)**: Covers certain doctors' services, outpatient care, medical supplies, and preventive services. Standard premium as of 2023 is $164.90/month, but it can be higher based on income.
- **Part C (Medicare Advantage)**: An alternative to Original Medicare offered by private companies, includes Part A, Part B, and usually Part D. Plans may offer extra benefits like vision, hearing, and dental.
- **Part D (Prescription Drug Coverage)**: Helps cover the cost of prescription drugs, available through private insurers.

### Enrollment Windows
- **Initial Enrollment Period (IEP)**: Begins three months before the month you turn 65, includes your birthday month, and ends three months after. Total of 7 months.
- **General Enrollment Period (GEP)**: January 1 to March 31 each year if you missed IEP.
- **Special Enrollment Period (SEP)**: Available under certain conditions, such as losing employer coverage.

### Avoiding Late Penalties
- Enroll during your IEP to avoid penalties. Part B penalty is 10% for each 12-month period you were eligible but not enrolled. Part D penalty is 1% of the "national base beneficiary premium" multiplied by the number of full uncovered months.

### Medigap vs. Medicare Advantage
- **Medigap**: Supplemental insurance to cover gaps in Original Medicare. Offers more predictable costs but usually at higher premiums.
- **Medicare Advantage**: Often lower premiums but may have higher out-of-pocket costs and network restrictions.
- **Decision Framework**: Consider your healthcare needs, budget, preferred doctors, and travel habits. Medigap is better for those who travel frequently or want broader provider access.

### Prescription Drug Coverage Optimization
- **Formulary Check**: Ensure your medications are covered under your Part D plan.
- **Pharmacy Network**: Use preferred pharmacies to reduce costs.
- **Annual Review**: Plans change annually, so review your coverage during the Open Enrollment Period (October 15 to December 7).
