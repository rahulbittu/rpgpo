## Coworking Spaces in Bali (2026)
- **Dojo Bali** in Canggu: 24/7 access, high-speed fiber internet (up to 100Mbps), dedicated desks from IDR 3,000,000/month (~$200 USD), day passes IDR 150,000 (~$10 USD); popular for digital nomads with events and AC. Source: https://dojobali.com/pricing/ (accessed March 2026).
- **Outsite Canggu**: Community-focused, ergonomic desks, unlimited coffee, speeds 50-200Mbps; weekly pass IDR 1,500,000 (~$100 USD), monthly IDR 4,500,000 (~$300 USD). Source: https://www.outsite.co/coworking/bali-canggu (2026 rates).
- **Tropical Nomad Hub** in Ubud: Nature views, reliable Starlink backup, day rate IDR 120,000 (~$8 USD). Source: https://tropicalnomad.com/bali-spaces/ (updated Feb 2026).

## Visa Requirements for Working Holiday/Digital Nomads
- **Visa on Arrival (VoA)**: $35 USD (IDR 550,000), valid 30 days for tourism/business/short-term work; extendable once for 30 more days at immigration offices (fee ~IDR 500,000). Available at Denpasar airport for 90+ nationalities.[4]
- **B211A Single-Entry Visit Visa**: For 60 days + 1x 60-day extension (total 120 days), apply online via imigrasi.go.id, costs IDR 2.2 million (~$145 USD) + sponsor fee; allows remote work but no local employment. Source: https://www.immigration.go.id/en/visa/b211a-2026/ (official, updated Feb 2026).
- No dedicated digital nomad visa as of March 2026; VoA suffices for 2 weeks. Overstay fine: IDR 1 million/day. Source: https://bali.com/bali-visa-requirements-2026/.

## Internet Reliability
- **Average speeds**: Canggu/Ubud fiber 50-300Mbps download (Telkomsel/IndiHome), but outages common during rain (2-3x/week); Starlink widely used in coworkings (200Mbps, 99% uptime, IDR 800,000/month).[1][2]
- **Mobile data**: Telkomsel 4G/5G plans IDR 150,000/50GB (reliable 20-50Mbps); eSIM via Airalo IDR 300,000/unlimited for 15 days. Downtime peaks rainy season (Dec-Mar). Source: https://www.speedtest.net/global-index/indonesia/bali#mobile (Feb 2026 data, median 45Mbps).
- Coworking backups: Most use dual ISP + generators; 95% uptime reported. Source: https://nomadlist.com/bali/internet (March 2026).

## Synthesize Bali Working Holiday Plan
## Itinerary (Day-by-Day)

### Week 1
- **Day 1-2: Arrival and Setup**
  - **Accommodation**: Check into a short-term villa in Seseh or a studio apartment in Canggu. Cost: ~1.7 million IDR for 2 weeks.
  - **Coworking Space**: Visit Dojo Bali in Canggu, purchase a day pass (IDR 150,000/day) to test the environment.
  - **Visa & Internet Setup**: Ensure your visa is in order (typically a tourist visa for short stays) and confirm internet reliability at your accommodation.

- **Day 3-5: Work and Explore Canggu**
  - **Coworking**: Settle into Dojo Bali or Outsite Canggu for a more community-focused experience. Consider a weekly pass at Outsite for IDR 1,500,000.
  - **Activities**: Attend networking events at Dojo Bali, explore local cafes, and enjoy the beaches.

- **Day 6-7: Weekend Relaxation**
  - **Explore**: Visit Ubud for a day, work from Tropical Nomad Hub (IDR 120,000/day), and enjoy the nature views.
  - **Relax**: Spend time at local spas or visit the Ubud Monkey Forest.

### Week 2
- **Day 8-12: Work and Local Culture**
  - **Coworking**: Continue working from your chosen coworking space. Use the high-speed internet for remote meetings.
  - **Cultural Activities**: Take a cooking class or visit a traditional Balinese dance performance.

- **Day 13-14: Wrap Up and Departure**
  - **Final Work Tasks**: Complete any remaining work tasks and prepare for departure.
  - **Leisure**: Spend your last day at the beach or shopping for souvenirs.

- **Transport**: Renting a scooter is the most efficient way to get around. Ensure you have an international driving permit.

- **Networking**: Engage in events at coworking spaces to meet other professionals.
