## Common Injuries in Overhead Athletes
Overhead athletes, such as throwers in baseball, lacrosse, or other sports, face elevated risks of shoulder and elbow injuries due to repetitive high-velocity motions, particularly during the deceleration phase of throwing where stress peaks.[2]  
No specific injury prevalence numbers from the last 30 days were found in search results; general risks include rotator cuff strains, labral tears, and elbow overuse from poor mechanics, fatigue, or inadequate scapular stability.[2]

## Recommended Exercises
Search results emphasize exercises targeting **rotator cuff strength**, **scapular stability**, **rear deltoids**, and overall shoulder stability using tools like resistance bands, light-moderate dumbbells (start with modifications), plyometric balls, and medicine balls.[1][2]  
Key exercises include:  
- **Barbell Overhead Press**: Stand feet shoulder-width, press barbell from shoulder height to full extension (core engaged, no back arch); 8-12 reps.[1]  
- **Barbell Landmine Face Pull**: Secure barbell in landmine/corner, pull toward face with elbows out, squeeze shoulder blades; 8-12 reps.[1]  
- **Plank Dumbbell Shoulder Raise**: From plank, raise one dumbbell (use hex for stability); 8-12 reps per side; modify on knees.[1]  
- Scapular activation, single-leg balance, and plyometrics for tissue elasticity.[2]

## Progression Plans
Progress from light to moderate resistance, high to moderate reps (e.g., 8-12 reps), with controlled movements and tracked strength to counter seasonal losses.[2]  
Incorporate:  
- Lower body/core/scapular work first for full-body foundation.[2]  
- Gradual plyometrics for high-speed stress tolerance.[2]  
- Monitor via routine logging; add dynamic elements post-basics.[2]

## Safety Guidelines and Prehabilitation Tips
**Prehabilitation** focuses on whole-body programs: lower body strength (glutes/hips/legs), core control, scapular stability, and rotator cuff work to prevent injuries.[2]  
- **Warm-up**: Dynamic (light cardio, arm/shoulder swings, scapular activation, gradual throwing) over static stretching.[2]  
- **Load management**: Scheduled rest days, off-seasons (several months/year for youth), avoid overlapping sports, monitor fatigue/soreness.[2]  
- **Mechanics**: Emphasize proper throwing form to distribute forces.[2]  
- Modifications: Lighter weights, knee-supported planks, hex dumbbells for stability.[1]

## Synthesize Shoulder Prehabilitation Program
## Plan

### Week 1-2: Foundation
- **Objective**: Establish baseline strength and stability.
- **Exercises**:
  - **Barbell Overhead Press**: 8-12 reps, 2-3 sets. Focus on form and core engagement.
  - **Barbell Landmine Face Pull**: 8-12 reps, 2-3 sets. Emphasize scapular retraction.
  - **Plank Dumbbell Shoulder Raise**: 8-12 reps per side, 2-3 sets. Start on knees if necessary.
  - **Scapular Activation Drills**: 10-15 reps, 2 sets. Focus on control and muscle engagement.
  - **Single-Leg Balance with Arm Reach**: 10 reps per leg, 2 sets. Enhance proprioception and stability.

### Week 3-4: Build Strength
- **Objective**: Increase resistance while maintaining form.
- **Exercises**:
  - **Barbell Overhead Press**: Increase weight slightly, maintain 8-12 reps, 3 sets.
  - **Barbell Landmine Face Pull**: Increase resistance, maintain 8-12 reps, 3 sets.
  - **Plank Dumbbell Shoulder Raise**: Progress to full plank, maintain 8-12 reps per side, 3 sets.
  - **Medicine Ball Throws**: 10 reps, 2-3 sets. Focus on explosive power.
  - **Plyometric Push-Ups**: 5-10 reps, 2 sets. Enhance explosive shoulder strength.

### Week 5-6: Enhance Stability and Power
- **Objective**: Integrate dynamic movements and plyometrics.
- **Exercises**:
  - **Barbell Overhead Press**: Further increase weight, 8-10 reps, 3 sets.
  - **Barbell Landmine Face Pull**: Maintain resistance, 8-10 reps, 3 sets.
  - **Plank Dumbbell Shoulder Raise**: Add instability (e.g., BOSU ball), 8-10 reps per side, 3 sets.
  - **Medicine Ball Slams**: 10 reps, 3 sets. Focus on full-body coordination.
  - **Plyometric Push-Ups**: Increase to 10-15 reps, 3 sets.

## Evidence Basis
- **Rotator Cuff and Scapular Stability**: Emphasized for injury prevention in overhead athletes due to their role in controlling shoulder movements during high-velocity actions.[2]
- **Progressive Overload**: Gradual increase in resistance and complexity ensures adaptation without overloading the shoulder joint prematurely.[1][2]
- **Plyometrics**: Included to enhance tissue elasticity and explosive power, crucial for athletic performance.[2]

## Progression
- **Monitoring**: Track progress weekly, adjusting resistance based on comfort and performance.
- **Feedback**: Incorporate athlete feedback to tailor exercise difficulty and prevent overuse.
- **Adaptation**: Modify exercises if pain or discomfort arises, focusing on maintaining proper form.

## Safety Notes
- **Warm-Up**: Essential to prevent injury; include dynamic stretches and light cardio.
- **Form**: Prioritize technique over weight to avoid stress on shoulder joints.
- **Recovery**: Allow 48 hours between sessions targeting the same muscle groups to ensure recovery.
- **Consultation**: Engage a physiotherapist or coach if unfamiliar with exercises or if pain persists.
