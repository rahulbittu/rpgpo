## Volcano Hiking

## Itinerary (Day-by-Day)

### Day 1: Arrival in San José
- **Activity**: Arrive at Juan Santamaría International Airport.
- **Accommodation**: Stay overnight in San José to rest from travel.
- **Next Steps**: Book a hotel in downtown San José for easy access to transport and dining options.

### Day 2: Travel to La Fortuna
- **Activity**: Travel to La Fortuna, the gateway to Arenal Volcano.
- **Accommodation**: Check into a hotel or eco-lodge near Arenal Volcano.
- **Next Steps**: Arrange transportation, either by renting a car or booking a shuttle service.

### Day 3: Arenal Volcano National Park
- **Activity**: Hike the Arenal 1968 Trail (2.5 miles/4 km) for wildlife spotting and views of the volcano.
- **Cost**: Park entry $15 USD; guided tours $65-85 USD.
- **Next Steps**: Book a guided tour if desired, which includes transport and a naturalist guide.

### Day 4: Hot Springs Relaxation
- **Activity**: Visit one of the many hot springs resorts in the area, like Tabacón or Baldi.
- **Cost**: Entry fees range from $35-70 USD depending on the resort.
- **Next Steps**: Reserve a spot at your chosen hot spring in advance.

### Day 5: Monteverde Cloud Forest
- **Activity**: Travel to Monteverde for a zip-lining adventure in the cloud forest.
- **Cost**: Zip-lining tours typically cost around $50-70 USD.
- **Next Steps**: Book a zip-lining tour in advance; consider a combo tour with hanging bridges.

### Day 6: Monteverde Exploration
- **Activity**: Explore the Monteverde Cloud Forest Reserve on foot.
- **Cost**: Entry fee approximately $25 USD.
- **Next Steps**: Purchase tickets online to avoid lines and ensure availability.

### Day 7: Travel to Manuel Antonio
- **Activity**: Head to the Pacific coast to Manuel Antonio.
- **Accommodation**: Check into a beachfront hotel or lodge.
- **Next Steps**: Arrange transportation from Monteverde to Manuel Antonio.

### Day 8: Manuel Antonio National Park
- **Activity**: Explore the park's beaches and trails.
- **Cost**: Entry fee around $16 USD.
- **Next Steps**: Buy tickets in advance, as the park has a daily visitor limit.

### Day 9: Surfing at Manuel Antonio
- **Activity**: Take a surfing lesson or rent a board.
- **Cost**: Lessons range from $50-75 USD.
- **Next Steps**: Book a lesson with a local surf school.

### Day 10: Return to San José
- **Activity**: Travel back to San José for departure.
- **Next Steps**: Plan your travel to ensure timely arrival for your flight.

## Costs & Budget

- **Accommodation**: Budget around $60-120 USD per night for mid-range hotels.
- **Food**: Estimate $30-50 USD per day for meals.
- **Activities**: Total activity costs approximately $300-500 USD.
- **Transportation**: Shuttle services cost $40-60 USD per trip; car rentals start at $30 USD/day.

## Logistics

- **Transportation**: Consider renting a car for flexibility or use shuttle services for convenience.
- **Currency**: Costa Rican Colón; credit cards widely accepted, but carry cash for small vendors.
- **Weather**: Pack for warm, humid conditions and rain, especially in the cloud forests.

## Tips

- **Early Bookings**: Secure accommodations and tours in advance, especially during peak season.
- **Local Guides**: Hiring local guides enhances wildlife spotting and cultural understanding.
- **Safety**: Stay on marked trails and follow local safety guidelines, especially near active volcanoes.
