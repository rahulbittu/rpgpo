## Neck Exercises
- **Neck Side Stretch**: Sit or stand comfortably, tilt your head to one side bringing ear toward shoulder, hold for 10-30 seconds per side; targets screen-induced tightness in programmers. Repeat 3-5 times daily.[3]

## Shoulder Exercises
- **Shoulder Roll Stretch**: Sit or stand, roll shoulders forward in circles 10 times, then backward 10 times; relieves mouse/typing tightness. Do every 1-2 hours.[3]
- Desk Yoga app: Chair-based shoulder stretches for tight shoulders, sitting or standing options, no equipment needed.[2]

## Hip Exercises
- **Bodyweight Squat Hold**: Stand feet shoulder-width, lower into squat keeping heels down, hold 10 seconds, repeat 3-5 times; improves hip flexibility and reduces lower back tension from sitting 6-10 hours daily.[3]

## Lower Back Exercises
- **Seated Spinal Twist**: Sit straight, place right hand on back of chair, twist gently right, hold 10 seconds; repeat left. Do 3 times per side to relieve prolonged sitting stiffness.[3]
- **Forward Bend Stretch**: Sit at chair edge, bend forward reaching toward feet, hold 10 seconds; targets lower back tension.[3]
- **Open the Book Stretch** (tested daily for 1 month by author, recommended by osteopath Andy McIntyre, The Livewell Clinic, London): Lie on side, knees bent, lift top arm over body rotating torso; hold 20-30 seconds per side. Improves thoracic mobility, identifies imbalances (e.g., right-side limitation common in desk workers).[4]

## Key Findings

1. **Neck Mobility**: Incorporate the Neck Side Stretch to alleviate tension from prolonged screen use. This exercise is simple and effective for reducing neck stiffness.
2. **Shoulder Mobility**: Utilize the Shoulder Roll Stretch to relieve tightness from extended periods of typing and mouse use. This exercise can be performed frequently throughout the day.
3. **Wrist Mobility**: Engage in wrist strain stretches from the Desk Yoga app, which are specifically designed for programmers and can be done without interrupting work.
4. **Hip Flexibility**: Perform the Bodyweight Squat Hold to improve hip flexibility and reduce lower back tension from long hours of sitting.
5. **Lower Back Mobility**: Use the Seated Spinal Twist to alleviate lower back tension and improve spinal flexibility.

## Detailed Analysis

- **Neck Side Stretch**: This exercise targets the lateral neck muscles, which often become tight due to prolonged periods of looking at screens. Holding the stretch for 10-30 seconds helps in lengthening these muscles and can be repeated multiple times a day for best results.

- **Shoulder Roll Stretch**: By rolling the shoulders forward and backward, this exercise helps in loosening the shoulder girdle, which can become tense from repetitive typing and mouse use. Performing this exercise every 1-2 hours can significantly reduce shoulder discomfort.

- **Wrist Strain Stretches**: The Desk Yoga app provides specialized stretches for the wrists, which are crucial for programmers who experience strain from continuous typing. These exercises can be seamlessly integrated into the workday without requiring additional time or space.

- **Bodyweight Squat Hold**: This exercise not only targets the hips but also engages the core and lower back, providing a comprehensive stretch that counters the effects of prolonged sitting. Repeating this exercise 3-5 times can enhance hip mobility.

- **Seated Spinal Twist**: This exercise is effective for the lower back and helps in maintaining spinal flexibility. It can be easily performed at the desk and repeated multiple times to keep the lower back muscles relaxed.
