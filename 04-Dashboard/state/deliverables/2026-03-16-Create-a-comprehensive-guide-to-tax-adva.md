## Backdoor Roth Strategies
- **Process**: Contribute to traditional IRA (non-deductible if over income limits), then convert to Roth IRA; no income limit on conversions.[1][3]
- **2026 relevance**: Viable for high earners phased out of direct Roth ($153,000+ single); pro-rata rule applies if pre-tax IRA balances exist—taxes gain on conversion.
- **Caution**: Consult tax pro; recharacterization allowed until April 15, 2026 for 2025 contributions.[1][3]

## Analysis

### 401(k) Plans
- **Contribution Limits (2026)**:
  - Employee elective deferrals: $24,500 (under age 50), catch-up $8,000, "super" catch-up $11,250 for high earners (must be Roth).
  - Total defined contribution limit: $72,000 (including employer contributions).
  - Annual compensation limit: $360,000.
- **Benefits**:
  - Tax-deferred growth.
  - Employer matching contributions.
  - Higher contribution limits compared to IRAs.
- **Drawbacks**:
  - Limited investment options.
  - Early withdrawal penalties before age 59½.
- **Practical Strategy for Tech Workers**:
  - Maximize employer match to benefit from "free money."
  - Consider Roth contributions if expecting higher future tax rates.

### Roth IRA
- **Contribution Limits (2026)**:
  - Standard: $7,500 (under age 50), catch-up $1,100.
  - Income phase-outs: Single under $153,000, partial $153,000-$168,000; joint under $242,000, partial $242,000-$252,000.
- **Benefits**:
  - Tax-free growth and withdrawals.
  - No required minimum distributions (RMDs).
- **Drawbacks**:
  - Contribution limits are lower than 401(k).
  - Income limits restrict eligibility.
- **Practical Strategy for Tech Workers**:
  - Use if expecting higher income in retirement.
  - Consider backdoor Roth if income exceeds limits.

### Health Savings Account (HSA)
- **Contribution Limits (2026)**: Not provided in prior results.
- **Benefits**:
  - Triple tax advantage: tax-deductible contributions, tax-free growth, tax-free withdrawals for qualified medical expenses.
  - Funds roll over year to year.
- **Drawbacks**:
  - Must be enrolled in a high-deductible health plan (HDHP).
- **Practical Strategy for Tech Workers**:
  - Maximize contributions if eligible for HDHP.
  - Use as an additional retirement account due to tax advantages.

### 529 Plans
- **Contribution Limits**: Not provided in prior results.
- **Benefits**:
  - Tax-free growth and withdrawals for qualified education expenses.
  - High contribution limits.
- **Drawbacks**:
  - Penalties for non-qualified withdrawals.
- **Practical Strategy for Tech Workers**:
  - Use for children's education savings.
  - Consider state tax deductions or credits.

### Backdoor Roth IRA
- **Strategy**:
  - Contribute to a Traditional IRA, then convert to a Roth IRA.
- **Benefits**:
  - Circumvents Roth IRA income limits.
- **Drawbacks**:
  - Potential tax implications during conversion.
- **Practical Strategy for Tech Workers**:
  - Use if income exceeds Roth IRA limits.
  - Plan conversions to minimize tax impact.

## Comparison

- **401(k) vs. Roth IRA**: Higher contribution limits in 401(k), but Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals.
- **HSA vs. 401(k)/IRA**: HSA offers triple tax advantage, but requires HDHP enrollment.
- **529 vs. Roth IRA**: 529 is education-focused, while Roth IRA is more flexible for retirement.
- **Backdoor Roth**: Ideal for high-income earners unable to contribute directly to Roth IRA.

## Recommendation

- **401(k)**: Maximize contributions, especially to secure employer match. Consider Roth 401(k) for tax diversification.
- **Roth IRA**: Contribute if eligible; use backdoor strategy if income is too high.
- **HSA**: Maximize contributions if eligible for HDHP for additional retirement savings.
- **529**: Use for education savings if applicable.
- **First Step**: Review current contributions and adjust based on eligibility and financial goals.

## Risks & Disclaimers

- **401(k) and Roth IRA**: Early withdrawals can incur penalties.
- **HSA**: Requires HDHP, which may not suit all healthcare needs.
- **529**: Non-qualified withdrawals incur taxes and penalties.
- **Backdoor Roth**: Be aware of tax implications during conversion. Consult a tax advisor.
