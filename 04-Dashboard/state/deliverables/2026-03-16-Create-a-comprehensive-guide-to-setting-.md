- **Pour-Over**: Typically involves a dripper (e.g., Hario V60, ~$20) and a gooseneck kettle. Offers clean, nuanced flavors.

- **French Press**: Simple, robust flavor, ~$20-50. Uses immersion brewing, resulting in a heavier body.

Each brewing method offers distinct advantages:
- **Pour-Over**: Best for those who enjoy a clean cup with distinct flavor notes. Requires more precision and practice.
- **AeroPress**: Ideal for quick, strong coffee. Great for travel due to its compact size.
- **French Press**: Offers a full-bodied cup. Best for those who prefer a more robust flavor and simpler brewing process.

### Water Quality Optimization
Using high-quality water can significantly enhance coffee flavor. Filtered or spring water is recommended to avoid off-flavors from tap water. Investing in a simple filtration system or coffee-specific water additives can ensure consistent quality.

### Storage Solutions
Proper storage of coffee beans is essential to maintain freshness. Airtight containers prevent oxidation and preserve flavor. Organizing equipment on a dedicated cart or shelf can streamline the brewing process.

### Cost-Per-Cup Analysis
Understanding the cost per cup helps in budgeting and appreciating the value of home-brewed coffee. While initial equipment costs can be high, the long-term savings compared to café purchases are substantial.
