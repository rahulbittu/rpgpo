## Implementation Steps

### Cable Protection
- **Objective**: Prevent pets from chewing on cables and causing damage or injury.
- **Action**: Use PVC conduits to cover and protect cables. This method is durable and prevents pets from accessing the cables.
- **Expected Outcome**: Reduced risk of electrical hazards and cable damage.
- **First Step**: Purchase PVC conduits from a local hardware store and install them around exposed cables in the home office.

### Plant Safety
- **Objective**: Ensure plants in the office are non-toxic to pets.
- **Action**: Incorporate known pet-safe plants such as spider plants (Chlorophytum comosum) into the office decor.
- **Expected Outcome**: Aesthetic enhancement of the office without risking pet health.
- **First Step**: Acquire spider plants from a nursery or online retailer, ensuring they are free from pesticides.

### Furniture Choices
- **Objective**: Select furniture that withstands pet activity and is easy to maintain.
- **Action**: Invest in Homebody sectionals or Home Reserve sectionals, which are durable, pet-friendly, and come with washable covers.
- **Expected Outcome**: Long-lasting furniture that maintains its appearance and functionality despite pet interactions.
- **First Step**: Evaluate space requirements and budget, then purchase the selected sectional online or in-store.

### Creating a Comfortable Pet Space
- **Objective**: Provide a dedicated area for pets within the home office.
- **Action**: Designate a corner of the office with a comfortable pet bed and toys, ensuring the area is free from hazards.
- **Expected Outcome**: Pets have a safe and inviting space, reducing disruptions during work.
- **First Step**: Choose a pet bed that fits the office decor and place it in a quiet corner, away from electrical outlets and cables.
