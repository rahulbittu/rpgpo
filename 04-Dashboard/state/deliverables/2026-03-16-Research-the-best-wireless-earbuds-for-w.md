# Wireless Earbuds Comparison: Jabra Elite 8 Active, Beats Fit Pro 2, and Sony WF-1000XM5

## Jabra Elite 8 Active

**IP Rating & Durability:** IP68-rated, offering military-grade durability that withstands rain, dust, and mud[1][2].

**Secure Fit Assessment:** The earbuds are comfortable to wear but have a notable weakness—they're "not the securest fit for workout earbuds"[1]. While praised for all-weather resilience, they're described as "more home-listening than gym-friendly" despite their durability[1].

**Heart Rate Monitoring:** No heart rate monitoring capability mentioned in available reviews[1][2].

**Multipoint Connectivity:** Not specified in search results.

**Additional Specs:**
- Battery life: 8 hours (buds with ANC active), 32 hours total with case[1]
- Weight: 5g per bud, 46g case[1]
- Noise cancelling: Yes, with decent ANC performance[1]
- Widely considered "the best workout headphones on the market"[2]

---

## Beats Fit Pro 2 (Powerbeats Pro 2)

**IP Rating & Durability:** IP57-rated, capable of handling sweat and rain[2].

**Secure Fit Assessment:** Excellent secure fit with ear hook design. Testing confirmed they "didn't budge, even during sprints or circuit training," making them ideal for intense workouts[4].

**Heart Rate Monitoring:** Yes—includes built-in heart-rate monitoring capability[4]. However, heart rate measurements have been found to be "consistently higher than those measured by our chest strap monitor"[2].

**Multipoint Connectivity:** No Bluetooth Multipoint support[4].

**Additional Specs:**
- Battery life: Around 8 hours with noise cancelling enabled[2]
- Active noise cancelling: Yes[4]
- Apple H2 chip integration[4]
- Sound quality drops noticeably when ANC is disabled[4]

---

## Sony WF-1000XM5

**IP Rating & Durability:** IPX4 water-resistant rating[3].

**Secure Fit Assessment:** Redesigned shape with improved fit—"stayed secure and comfortable, even during exercise" during testing[2]. Notably lighter and smaller than predecessor models[2].

**Heart Rate Monitoring:** No heart rate monitoring capability mentioned[3][5].

**Multipoint Connectivity:** Not specified in search results.

**Additional Specs:**
- Battery life: Excellent battery life with improved performance over XM4 models[2]
- Noise cancelling: Outstanding ANC performance; "ability to immerse in almost complete silence"[3]
- Sound quality: Upgraded audio drivers, sophisticated audio processing, support for lossless streaming codecs[3]
- Price: $300[3]
- Newer model announced: Sony WF-1000XM6 promised "even better sound and ANC performance," but WF-1000XM5 still considered "the better buy right now"[3]
