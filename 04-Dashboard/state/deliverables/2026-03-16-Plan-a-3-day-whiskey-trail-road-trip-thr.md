## Compile 3-Day Itinerary
## Itinerary

### Day 1: Arrival and Exploration in Bardstown
- **Morning**: 
  - **Willett Distillery Tour**: Start your whiskey trail with an in-depth small-batch tour at the Willett Distillery in Bardstown. Enjoy tastings and explore The Bar at Willett, which has been rated the best restaurant/bar on the Bourbon Trail. 
  - **Booking**: Reserve your tour in advance through their website.
- **Lunch**: 
  - **Mammy’s Kitchen & Bar**: Located in downtown Bardstown, this local favorite offers classic Southern dishes. 
- **Afternoon**: 
  - **Heaven Hill Springs Distillery**: Experience the new $200M facility and join the Heritage Rising Tour. This small-group tour offers insights into the bourbon-making process and the history of Heaven Hill.
  - **Booking**: Book early as spots fill up quickly.
- **Evening**: 
  - **Dinner at The Rickhouse Restaurant**: Known for its bourbon-inspired menu and located in Bardstown, it's the perfect place to end your day.

### Day 2: Loretto and Frankfort
- **Morning**: 
  - **Maker’s Mark Distillery Tour**: Head to Loretto to tour the historic Maker’s Mark Distillery. Enjoy the scenic views of the rolling hills and learn about the bourbon-making process from mash to bottle.
  - **Booking**: Secure your spot online well in advance.
- **Lunch**: 
  - **The Talbott Tavern**: Located in Bardstown, this historic inn offers a unique dining experience with a menu that features local favorites.
- **Afternoon**: 
  - **Buffalo Trace Distillery**: Drive to Frankfort for a complimentary tour at the Buffalo Trace Distillery. Choose from the Trace Tour, Hard Hat Tour, or Old Taylor Tour, each offering a unique perspective on the distillery's history and production methods.
  - **Booking**: Reservations are recommended; check their website for availability.
- **Evening**: 
  - **Dinner at Bourbon on Main**: Located in Frankfort, this restaurant offers a variety of bourbon-infused dishes and a great selection of local bourbons.

### Day 3: Scenic Drives and Final Tastings
- **Morning**: 
  - **Preservation Distillery Tour**: Visit this intimate distillery located on a historic tobacco farm. The tour focuses on 100% pot-distilled bourbon and sustainability practices.
  - **Booking**: Book your tour in advance to ensure availability.
- **Lunch**: 
  - **Kurtz Restaurant**: A family-run establishment in Bardstown offering traditional Kentucky fare.
- **Afternoon**: 
  - **Scenic Drive**: Take a leisurely drive through the countryside, exploring the scenic routes between distilleries. Consider the Bluegrass Parkway for picturesque views.
- **Evening**: 
  - **Dinner at Harrison-Smith House**: Located in Bardstown, this restaurant offers a farm-to-table dining experience with a focus on local ingredients.

## Costs & Budget
- **Distillery Tours**: Expect to spend $10-$25 per person per tour, with some complimentary options like Buffalo Trace.
- **Meals**: Budget around $15-$30 per person per meal at local restaurants.
- **Accommodation**: Consider staying at a local bed and breakfast or hotel in Bardstown, with prices ranging from $100-$200 per night.

## Logistics
- **Transportation**: Rent a car for the duration of the trip to easily navigate between distilleries and scenic routes.
- **Accommodation**: Book accommodations in Bardstown for easy access to most distilleries.
- **Reservations**: Make all necessary tour and restaurant reservations in advance to secure your spots.

## Tips
- **Timing**: Start your days early to maximize the number of tours and tastings.
- **Designated Driver**: Consider having a designated driver or use a local transportation service for safety.
- **Packing**: Bring comfortable clothing and shoes for walking tours, and consider packing a cooler for any bourbon purchases.

By following this itinerary, you can enjoy a comprehensive and enjoyable whiskey trail experience in Kentucky, filled with tastings, history, and scenic beauty.
