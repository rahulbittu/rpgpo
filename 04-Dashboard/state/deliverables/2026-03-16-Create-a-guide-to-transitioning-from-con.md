## Relevant Regulatory Context (India - Fixed Term Employment Shift)
India's Labour Codes, particularly the Occupational Safety, Health and Working Conditions Code, 2020 (OSH Code), restrict contract labor in core operations, pushing toward **Fixed Term Employment (FTE)** as an alternative to permanent roles. FTE workers receive benefits comparable to permanent employees (e.g., gratuity) but no end-of-term notice pay or retrenchment compensation, balancing flexibility for employers with worker protections.[1]

## UK Employment Rights Act Updates (Contract Changes)
From **1 January 2027**, UK employers face restrictions on varying contract terms like pay, hours, pensions, and time-off, with changes potentially triggering automatic unfair dismissal claims. Employers should update contracts before this date for flexibility, ideally via employee consent tied to pay reviews; collective consultation may apply for larger changes.[2]

## US DOL Proposed Independent Contractor Rule (Feb 26, 2026)
