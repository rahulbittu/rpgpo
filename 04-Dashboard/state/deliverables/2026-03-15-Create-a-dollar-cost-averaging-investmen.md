## Detailed Analysis

- **Projected Values with Dollar-Cost Averaging**:
  - Assuming a monthly investment of $500 over 20 years:
    - **At 7% annual return**: The future value of the investment would be approximately $261,000.
    - **At 10% annual return**: The future value would be approximately $380,000.
    - **At 12% annual return**: The future value would be approximately $500,000.
  - These projections are based on compound interest calculations using the formula for future value of a series: 
    \[
    FV = P \times \frac{(1 + r)^n - 1}{r}
    \]
    where \( P \) is the monthly investment, \( r \) is the monthly interest rate (annual rate/12), and \( n \) is the total number of investments (months).

- **Market Volatility Consideration**: The historical data shows that VTI can experience significant fluctuations, which can be advantageous for dollar-cost averaging as it allows for purchasing more shares when prices are lower.
