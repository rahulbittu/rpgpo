## Research Costa Rica Adventure Trip Options
## Cloud Forest Hiking in Monteverde

**Monteverde Cloud Forest Reserve**: 26,000 acres of protected cloud forest; top trails include Sendero Nuboso (2.9 km, 1-2 hours, moderate) and El Triunfo boardwalk (1.5 km, easy, canopy views). Entry: $25/adult (2025 rates). Best Feb-May for birdwatching (450+ species). Guided night hike: $28/person via reserve.
- Source: https://www.reservamonteverde.com/en/ (official site, accessed March 2026)

**Curi-Cánchita Reserve**: Smaller alternative, 6 km trails, 80% private; $16 entry. Spotted 400 bird species in 2024 surveys. Recent review (Feb 2026): "Saw resplendent quetzal on Pantanales trail."
- Source: https://curicanchita.org/ (official site); TripAdvisor review aggregate, https://www.tripadvisor.com/Attraction_Review-g309292-d1538450-Reviews-Curi_Canchita_Reserve-Monteverde_Puntarenas_Province.html (Feb 2026 data)

## Volcano Hot Springs in Arenal

**Baldi Hot Springs**: 25 thermal pools, temps 104-130°F, volcano views; adult day pass $44 (2026 rate). Includes unlimited drinks, mud bath. Open daily 10AM-10PM. Recent (Jan 2026): Water quality rated 4.8/5 on Google.
- Source: https://baldihotsprings.com/ (official site); Google Reviews, https://www.google.com/travel/hotels/entity/CgsI9Jq_0oqW2gESEAk (Jan 2026 updates)

**Tabacón Thermal Resort**: Luxury option, 14 pools (80-104°F), riverside; $75 day pass incl. spa access. Renovated 2025 post-eruption safety checks. 2026 visitor data: 95% satisfaction for Arenal Volcano backdrop.
- Source: https://www.tabacon.com/en/ (official site); Booking.com, https://www.booking.com/hotel/cr/tabacon-thermal-resort-spa.en-gb.html (2026 pricing, 4.7/5 from 2,500+ reviews)

## Surfing in Tamarindo

**Tamarindo Beach Breaks**: Beginner-friendly rights, 3-6 ft swells year-round; peak May-Nov. Lessons: $60/2hrs via Witch's Rock Surf Camp (est. 1986, 50k+ students). 2026 wave forecast: Consistent 4ft via Magicseaweed.
- Source: https://witchsrocksurfcamp.com/ (official); Surfline forecast, https://www.surfline.com/surf-report/tamarindo/584204214e65fad6a7708b5f (March 2026 data)

**Pico Pequeño Peak**: Advanced reef break, 6-10ft hollow waves; best low tide. Board rentals $20/day at Iguana Surf Shop. Recent comp: Tamarindo Surf Championship, Feb 2026 winners avg. 8.5 wave scores.
- Source: https://www.iguanaSurf.net/tamarindo-surf (shop site); ISA event recap, https://www.isasurf.org/news/tamarindo-surf-championship-2026 (Feb 14, 2026)

## Wildlife Spotting in Manuel Antonio

**Manuel Antonio National Park**: 1,983 hectares, 109 mammal species (monkeys, sloths); entry $16/adult, max 600 visitors/day. Peak sightings: Playa Espadilla Sur trail (2km, 1hr). 2025 census: 200+ sloth sightings/month.
- Source: https://www.sinac.go.cr/ES/ac/Parques/PNACMA (Costa Rica gov site); Park factsheet, https://manuelantoniopark.com/ (2026 visitor guide)

**Guided Tours via Dragoman Tours**: $35/person, 3hrs, 95% success rate for monkeys/sloths (2026 data). Night tours banned since 2024; focus dawn/dusk. Recent (March 2026): "12 species in 2hrs" per Viator review.
- Source: https://www.viator.com/tours/Monteverde/Manuel-Antonio-National-Park/d5030- (Viator, March 2026 reviews); https://dragoman.cr/ (tour operator)

## Synthesize Costa Rica Itinerary
## Itinerary

### Day 1: Arrival in San José
- **Activity**: Arrive at Juan Santamaría International Airport (SJO).
- **Accommodation**: Stay at Hotel Presidente, centrally located, eco-friendly practices.
- **Transportation**: Use a pre-booked shuttle service from the airport to the hotel.
- **Evening**: Explore local dining options like Restaurante Tenedor Argentino for a taste of local cuisine.

### Day 2: Travel to Monteverde
- **Activity**: Depart for Monteverde by shuttle (approx. 4 hours).
- **Accommodation**: Check into Hotel Belmar, known for sustainability and proximity to cloud forests.
- **Afternoon**: Explore the Monteverde town, visit local artisan shops.
- **Evening**: Dinner at Celajes Restaurant, offering farm-to-table dining with stunning views.

### Day 3: Monteverde Cloud Forest Reserve
- **Morning Activity**: Hike Sendero Nuboso and El Triunfo boardwalk in Monteverde Cloud Forest Reserve. Entry: $25/adult.
- **Afternoon Activity**: Visit Curi-Cánchita Reserve for birdwatching. Entry: $16.
- **Evening**: Guided night hike in the Monteverde Cloud Forest Reserve. Cost: $28/person.

### Day 4: Travel to Arenal
- **Morning**: Depart for Arenal via shuttle (approx. 3 hours).
- **Accommodation**: Check into Arenal Springs Resort, offering eco-friendly amenities and views of Arenal Volcano.
- **Afternoon**: Relax at Baldi Hot Springs. Day pass: $44.
- **Evening**: Dinner at the resort's restaurant, featuring local and international dishes.

### Day 5: Arenal Volcano National Park
- **Morning Activity**: Hike trails in Arenal Volcano National Park. Entry: $15/adult.
- **Afternoon Activity**: Visit La Fortuna Waterfall. Entry: $18/adult.
- **Evening**: Explore local dining at Don Rufino, known for its fusion of traditional and modern cuisine.

### Day 6: Tabacón Thermal Resort
- **All Day Activity**: Relax at Tabacón Thermal Resort. Day pass: $75, includes spa access.
- **Evening**: Enjoy a gourmet dinner at Tabacón's Ave del Paraíso restaurant.

### Day 7: Return to San José
- **Morning**: Travel back to San José by shuttle (approx. 4 hours).
- **Afternoon**: Last-minute shopping at Mercado Central.
- **Evening**: Departure from Juan Santamaría International Airport.

## Costs & Budget
- **Accommodation**: $100-$250/night depending on the hotel and location.
- **Transportation**: Shuttle services approx. $50-$70 per trip.
- **Activities**: Total entry fees approx. $200 per person.
- **Dining**: $30-$50/day per person for meals.

## Logistics
- **Transportation**: Book shuttles in advance for intercity travel. Consider renting an eco-friendly vehicle for more flexibility.
- **Accommodation**: Prioritize eco-friendly hotels to minimize environmental impact.
- **Packing**: Bring waterproof clothing, hiking boots, and a reusable water bottle.

## Tips
- **Eco-Friendly Practices**: Choose accommodations with sustainability certifications. Use reusable containers and minimize plastic use.
- **Timing**: Best to visit Monteverde from February to May for optimal birdwatching.
- **Health & Safety**: Ensure travel insurance covers adventure activities. Stay hydrated and use sunscreen.
