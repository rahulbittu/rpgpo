## Itinerary (Day-by-Day)

### Day 1: Arrival and Evening Jazz
- **Morning/Afternoon**: Arrive in New Orleans. Check into your hotel. Consider staying near the French Quarter for easy access to attractions.
- **Evening**: Visit **The Joy Theater** on Canal Street for the "Fest After Dark" series. If your visit coincides with April 23-24, catch STS9, or on May 2, enjoy "Queens Night in NOLA" with Big Freedia and Dawn Richard. 
  - **Cost**: Tickets vary, starting around $30. Purchase in advance ([Joy Theater](https://www.thejoytheater.com/)).
  - **Logistics**: The theater is a short walk from the French Quarter.

### Day 2: Jazz Fest and Local Cuisine
- **Morning/Afternoon**: Head to the **Fair Grounds Race Course & Slots** for the New Orleans Jazz & Heritage Festival. Enjoy performances from renowned artists like Preservation Hall Jazz Band and Trombone Shorty.
  - **Cost**: General admission starts at $85. Discounts available on April 23 for locals.
  - **Logistics**: Located at 1751 Gentilly Blvd., accessible by public transport or rideshare.
- **Evening**: Explore local dining options. Try a classic Creole dinner at **Commander's Palace** or **Cochon** for Cajun cuisine.
  - **Cost**: Expect to spend around $50 per person for dinner.

### Day 3: Cultural Exploration and Jazz at the Sandbar
- **Morning**: Visit the **National WWII Museum** or the **New Orleans Museum of Art** for a cultural deep dive.
  - **Cost**: WWII Museum tickets are $28, NOMA is $15.
- **Afternoon**: Stroll through the French Quarter, visit Jackson Square, and enjoy beignets at **Café du Monde**.
  - **Cost**: Beignets and coffee around $10.
- **Evening**: Attend **Jazz at the Sandbar** at the 410 Lounge, Earl K. Long Library, University of New Orleans. Enjoy free performances by student-professional collaborations.
  - **Logistics**: Check the schedule for specific artists ([UNO Jazz](https://www.uno.edu/)).

### Day 4: Garden District and Departure
- **Morning**: Explore the Garden District. Take a walking tour to see historic mansions and Lafayette Cemetery No. 1.
  - **Cost**: Walking tours range from $20 to $30.
- **Afternoon**: Last-minute shopping at the **French Market** or **Magazine Street**.
- **Evening**: Departure from New Orleans.

## Costs & Budget
- **Accommodation**: $150-$300 per night depending on location and amenities.
- **Food**: $50-$100 per day per person.
- **Attractions and Events**: $150-$200 total for tickets and tours.
- **Transportation**: $20-$40 per day for public transport or rideshare.

## Logistics
- **Transportation**: Use public transit or rideshares (Uber/Lyft) for convenience. Consider a Jazzy Pass for unlimited streetcar and bus rides ($3 for 1 day, $9 for 3 days).
- **Accommodation**: Book early, especially during Jazz Fest. Look for hotels in the French Quarter or Central Business District for central access.

## Tips
- **Tickets**: Purchase event and attraction tickets in advance to secure your spot, especially for popular events like Jazz Fest.
- **Weather**: April-May is warm; pack accordingly and stay hydrated.
- **Safety**: Stay in well-lit, populated areas, especially at night. Be mindful of your belongings in crowded places.

This itinerary provides a balanced mix of music, culture, and culinary experiences, tailored to make the most of New Orleans' vibrant atmosphere.
