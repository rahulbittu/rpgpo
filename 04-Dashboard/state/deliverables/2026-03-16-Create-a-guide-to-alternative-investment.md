- **Overview**: Private credit involves non-bank lending to private companies. It typically offers higher yields compared to traditional fixed-income investments due to its illiquid nature and the risk associated with lending to less established entities.

- **Liquidity & Correlation**: Private credit is generally illiquid, with investments often locked in for several years. It shows low correlation with public markets, offering diversification benefits.

- **Overview**: Wine futures involve purchasing wine before it is bottled, typically at a discount. The investment matures as the wine ages and appreciates in value.
- **Market Dynamics**: The fine wine market has grown steadily, with the Liv-ex Fine Wine 1000 index showing a 5-year annualized return of approximately 7%.

### Startup Secondaries
- **Overview**: Startup secondaries involve purchasing shares from existing shareholders of private companies. This provides liquidity to early investors or employees.
- **Market Growth**: The secondary market for private equity has expanded, with transactions reaching $130 billion in 2023, according to PitchBook.
- **Liquidity & Correlation**: While more liquid than primary startup investments, secondaries still involve significant holding periods. They have a moderate correlation with public markets, depending on the company's stage and sector.
