## Finding 1: Carb Cycling Periodization for Half Marathon Training

**Specific Protocol:**
- **High carb days (5-7g/kg body weight)**: Scheduled after long runs and high-intensity workouts to maximize glycogen replenishment
- **Moderate carb days (3-5g/kg)**: On easy run days and recovery days
- **Lower carb days (2-3g/kg)**: On complete rest days or very light activity days

For a 70kg (154 lb) recreational runner:
- High carb day = 350-490g carbs
- Moderate carb day = 210-350g carbs
- Low carb day = 140-210g carbs

**Timing within periodization:**
- Base phase (weeks 1-4): Emphasis on moderate carbs to build aerobic base
- Build phase (weeks 5-10): Increase high carb days to 3-4x/week as mileage increases
- Peak phase (weeks 11-14): Maintain high carbs post-long run, reduce volume on other days
- Taper phase (final 2 weeks): Shift to moderate carbs with strategic loading 3 days pre-race

## Finding 2: Pre-Workout Nutrition Timing & Composition

**Pre-run fueling (1.5-3 hours before):**
- **Carbs**: 1-4g per kg body weight (70kg runner = 70-280g)
- **Protein**: 0.25g per kg body weight (70kg = 17.5g)
- **Fat & fiber**: Minimize to reduce GI distress
- **Hydration**: 400-600ml fluid 2-3 hours prior

**Specific examples for 70kg runner:**
- Light run (easy pace, <60 min): 150-200g carbs + 10-15g protein (banana + Greek yogurt, or oatmeal with berries)
- Moderate run (tempo/threshold, 60-90 min): 200-250g carbs + 15-20g protein (toast with peanut butter + orange juice)
- Long run (>90 min): 250-300g carbs + 20-25g protein (bagel with honey + egg whites, or pasta with lean meat)

**Timing rule**: Larger meals (>3g carbs/kg) = 3 hours before; smaller meals (<1.5g carbs/kg) = 30-60 minutes before

## Finding 4: Hydration Strategy During Training & Racing

**Daily baseline hydration:**
- **Minimum**: 35ml per kg body weight (70kg = 2.45L daily)
- **With training**: Add 500-1000ml per hour of exercise depending on sweat rate

**Sweat rate calculation (critical for personalization):**
- Weigh yourself before and after a 1-hour run in typical conditions
- Formula: (pre-weight - post-weight + fluid consumed) = sweat rate per hour
- Example: Runner loses 1.2kg, drank 400ml = 1.6L/hour sweat rate

**During-run hydration (for runs >60 minutes):**
- **Fluid**: 400-800ml per hour (adjust based on sweat rate, not exceeding 150% of sweat rate)
- **Carbs**: 30-60g per hour (6-8% carbohydrate solution)
- **Sodium**: 300-600mg per liter
- **Electrolytes**: Sodium, potassium, magnesium in 3:1 ratio

**Practical example for runner with 1.6L/hour sweat rate:**
- Drink 600-800ml/hour (staying below sweat rate to avoid overhydration)
- Use sports drink with 6-8% carbs (6-8g per 100ml)
- Consume 400-500mg sodium per hour

## Plan

### Week-by-Week Nutrition Periodization

#### Weeks 1-4: Base Phase
- **Carb Cycling:**
  - **Moderate Carb Days (3-5g/kg):** Focus on building an aerobic base. Aim for 210-350g carbs/day for a 70kg runner.
  - **Example Foods:** Whole grains, fruits, vegetables, and legumes.
- **Pre/Post Workout Nutrition:**
  - **Pre-Workout (1-2 hours before):** 30-60g of easily digestible carbs (e.g., banana, oatmeal).
  - **Post-Workout (within 30 minutes):** 20g protein and 60g carbs (e.g., protein shake with fruit).
- **Hydration:**
  - Drink 500ml of water 2 hours before workouts.
  - Consume 150-250ml every 20 minutes during exercise.
- **Supplements:**
  - Consider a daily multivitamin and omega-3 supplement.

#### Weeks 5-10: Build Phase
- **Carb Cycling:**
  - **High Carb Days (5-7g/kg):** After long runs and intense workouts. Aim for 350-490g carbs/day.
  - **Moderate Carb Days (3-5g/kg):** On recovery and easy run days.
  - **Low Carb Days (2-3g/kg):** On rest days.
- **Pre/Post Workout Nutrition:**
  - **Pre-Workout:** Increase to 60-90g carbs for high-intensity days.
  - **Post-Workout:** Maintain 20g protein and increase to 80g carbs.
- **Hydration:**
  - Continue with the base phase strategy; add electrolytes during long runs.
- **Supplements:**
  - Introduce BCAAs before intense workouts to aid recovery.

#### Week 11: Taper Week
- **Carb Cycling:**
  - Gradually increase carbs to 7-10g/kg as race day approaches for glycogen supercompensation.
- **Race Week Fueling:**
  - **3 Days Before Race:** Increase carb intake to 490-700g/day.
  - **Day Before Race:** Focus on easily digestible carbs and avoid high-fiber foods.
- **Hydration:**
  - Ensure adequate hydration; aim for pale yellow urine.
  - Avoid alcohol and caffeine.
- **Supplements:**
  - Continue with multivitamins and omega-3s; consider beetroot juice for nitrate boost.

## Evidence Basis

- **Carb Cycling Protocol:** Based on research indicating glycogen replenishment is crucial post high-intensity workouts (Jeukendrup, 2017).
- **Pre/Post Workout Nutrition:** Timing and macronutrient balance are critical for recovery and performance (Ivy & Portman, 2004).
- **Hydration Strategy:** Proper hydration is essential to maintain performance and prevent dehydration (Sawka et al., 2007).
- **Race Week Fueling:** Carb loading effectively increases muscle glycogen stores (Burke et al., 2011).

## Progression

- **Monitor and Adjust:** Track energy levels, performance, and recovery to adjust carb intake as needed.
- **Trial Runs:** Practice race day nutrition and hydration during long training runs to ensure no gastrointestinal distress.

## Safety Notes

- **Individual Needs:** Adjust carb intake based on personal tolerance and energy needs.
- **Medical Conditions:** Consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement or drastic dietary changes, especially if you have pre-existing health conditions.
- **Hydration Risks:** Overhydration can lead to hyponatremia; balance fluid intake with electrolyte consumption.
