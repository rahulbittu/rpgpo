## Thoracic Spine Mobility Drills
Seated shoulder rolls release neck/upper back tension: lift shoulders to ears, roll back, drop down; repeat 10 times with breath (inhale up, exhale down).[1]  
Seated chest opener counters rounded posture: clasp hands behind back (or chair sides), lift arms to open chest.[1]  
Controlled thoracic extension exercises, bodyweight rows, and scapular push-up variations target tight upper back from desk work (perform with intent, not just as warmup).[3]  
Source: https://thespinepro.com/blog/desk-friendly-stretches-for-back-pain-relief/[1]; https://purecalisthenics.com/tight-upper-back-desk-work-bodyweight-fix/[3]  

## Hip Flexor Recovery Exercises
Seated hip flexor stretch: scoot to chair edge, extend one leg back slightly, keep chest upright to stretch front hip.[1]  
Seated figure-four stretch: cross right ankle over left knee (ankle flexed), lean forward to stretch right hip/glute; switch sides.[1]  
08:20 Hip flexor stretch and 10:20 Fig 4 in 15-min routine.[2]  
Source: https://thespinepro.com/blog/desk-friendly-stretches-for-back-pain-relief/[1]; https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZHTQpZ_jCQ8[2]  

## Wrist Health for Heavy Typists
Wrist rolls → Fists to starfish (12:20 in routine): roll wrists, transition fists to open hands for mobility.[2]  
Short workday movement bouts reduce discomfort; integrate with 10-15 min stretches 3-5x/week.[1]  
Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZHTQpZ_jCQ8[2]; https://thespinepro.com/blog/desk-friendly-stretches-for-back-pain-relief/[1]

## Synthesize Joint Mobility Program
## Plan

### Week 1: Introduction and Routine Establishment
- **Day 1-3**: Start with the 15-minute seated mobility routine each morning. Focus on learning the flow and correct posture.
- **Day 4-5**: Introduce thoracic spine mobility drills. Perform seated shoulder rolls and chest openers twice daily.
- **Day 6-7**: Begin hip flexor recovery with static hip flexor stretches, holding each stretch for 30 seconds, twice a day.

### Week 2: Routine Expansion and Consistency
- **Day 1-3**: Continue the morning flow routine. Add wrist health exercises (wrist rolls and fists to starfish) in the afternoon.
- **Day 4-5**: Increase thoracic spine drills frequency to three times daily. Introduce bodyweight rows and scapular push-ups.
- **Day 6-7**: Add dynamic hip flexor exercises, such as lunges with a twist, to the routine.

### Week 3: Intensity and Variation
- **Day 1-3**: Intensify the morning flow by holding each position for longer. Focus on deeper stretches.
- **Day 4-5**: Add resistance bands for thoracic spine drills to increase challenge.
- **Day 6-7**: Incorporate yoga poses like the Pigeon pose for hip flexor flexibility.

### Week 4: Mastery and Maintenance
- **Day 1-3**: Master the morning flow routine by performing it without guidance. Focus on fluidity and breath control.
- **Day 4-5**: Perform thoracic spine drills with added resistance and focus on form.
- **Day 6-7**: Maintain hip flexor flexibility with a mix of static and dynamic exercises.

## Evidence Basis

- **Morning Flow Routine**: A structured 15-minute routine improves range of motion and energy, focusing on key areas affected by prolonged sitting.[2]
- **Thoracic Spine Mobility**: Exercises like shoulder rolls and chest openers counteract desk posture, improving upper back flexibility and reducing tension.[1]
- **Hip Flexor Recovery**: Static and dynamic stretches target hip tightness, common in desk workers, promoting flexibility and reducing discomfort.
- **Wrist Health Strategies**: Wrist rolls and dynamic exercises like fists to starfish enhance wrist mobility, crucial for desk work involving typing.[2]

## Progression

- **Week 1**: Establish a baseline routine and correct form.
- **Week 2**: Build consistency and introduce new exercises.
- **Week 3**: Increase intensity and add variations for challenge.
- **Week 4**: Achieve mastery and focus on maintaining flexibility and mobility.

## Safety Notes

- **Equipment**: Use a stable chair for seated exercises. Resistance bands can be added for increased difficulty in later weeks.
- **Form**: Ensure proper form to prevent injury. Use mirrors or record yourself to check posture.
- **Breathing**: Focus on controlled breathing during exercises to enhance relaxation and effectiveness.
- **Consultation**: If you experience pain or discomfort, consult a healthcare professional before continuing.
