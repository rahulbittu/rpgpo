## Impact-Effort Matrix
The primary method from search results is the **Impact-Effort Matrix** (also called Value vs Effort Matrix), which plots tasks on a 2x2 grid: x-axis for impact (low to high), y-axis for effort (low to high). Prioritize **low effort, high impact** first, then **high effort, high impact**; deprioritize or eliminate **high effort, low impact** and **low effort, low impact**[1][2][6].

**Criteria to evaluate** (score each task low/medium/high or 1-10):
- **Effort**: Time, energy, resources needed (e.g., hours, skills required)[1][2][6].
- **Impact**: Potential value/reach toward goals (e.g., revenue, progress, outcomes); use RICE variant: multiply Reach x Impact x Confidence, divide by Effort for score[1].

## Additional Methods
- **RICE Scoring**: Reach (users affected) x Impact (benefit level) x Confidence (%) / Effort. Example: Task with Reach=100, Impact=3, Confidence=80%, Effort=5 scores (100x3x0.8)/5=48 (high=prioritize)[1].
- **MIT (Most Important Task)**: Pick 1-3 tasks/day with max goal impact; e.g., TopRanker notifications as MIT[4].
- Regular review: Reassess weekly as goals shift[2].
