## Typical Structures in Short Film Beat Sheets

Short film beat sheets adapt feature-length structures like **three-act** or **Save the Cat** frameworks, compressing them into 8-15 key beats to fit 5-15 minute runtimes, emphasizing quick setup, escalating conflict, and tight resolution.[2][3][6]

- **Three-Act Structure (most common for shorts)**: Act 1 (0-25%, ~1-2.5 min): Setup with exposition and inciting incident. Act 2 (25-75%, ~5-7.5 min): Rising action, midpoint crisis, plot point 2. Act 3 (75-100%, ~2-2.5 min): Climax and denouement.[2][3]
- **Four-Act Structure**: Divides into quarters with plot points at 25%, 50% (midpoint), 75%; scalable for shorts by focusing on 8 beats including bookends (hook, inciting incident, midpoint, climax).[2]
- **Save the Cat Beat Sheet**: 15 beats including opening image (0-1%), catalyst (10%), midpoint (50%), all-is-lost (75%), finale (99-100%); used for shorts by prioritizing emotional turns over subplots.[3][8]

## Elements in Short Film Beat Sheets

Beat sheets list 10-20 milestones with page/minute estimates, one-sentence descriptions, and character stakes; rated on character, dialog, plot, theme, resolution for feedback.[1][7]

- Core elements: Hook/opening image, inciting incident, stakes raise, midpoint reversal, climax confrontation, resolution payoff.
- Per beat: Scene summary, emotional change, visual action; e.g., "Protagonist discovers secret (inciting, min 1), responds with denial (rising, min 3)."[2][6]
- Visual aids: Plot diagrams label exposition, rising action (crises waves), climax, falling action.[4]

## Detailed Analysis

- **Setup (Act 1: 0-2.5 min)**:
  - **Opening Image**: Introduce the protagonist, an engineer, in a typical work setting, showcasing their meticulous nature and reliance on precise communication in code reviews.
  - **Inciting Incident**: The engineer receives a new task to review an AI's code, which is being developed to assist in automating code reviews.

- **Conflict (Act 2: 2.5-7.5 min)**:
  - **Rising Action**: The engineer begins reviewing the AI's code, providing detailed comments meant to guide improvements. The AI, however, interprets these comments literally, leading to unexpected and humorous changes in the codebase.
  - **Midpoint Crisis**: The engineer realizes the AI is executing commands based on their comments verbatim, causing chaos in the system. This realization occurs during a critical project deadline.
  - **Plot Point 2**: The engineer attempts to correct the AI's actions by adjusting their communication style, but the AI's literal interpretation continues to escalate the situation.

- **Climax and Resolution (Act 3: 7.5-10 min)**:
  - **Climax**: In a race against time, the engineer devises a creative solution by using humor and metaphor in their comments, tricking the AI into correcting its own errors.
  - **Denouement**: The AI successfully implements the changes, and the system stabilizes just in time for the project deadline. The engineer learns the importance of clear communication and adaptability when working with AI.

## Recommended Actions

1. **Develop the Opening Scene**:
   - **What to Do**: Create a scene that establishes the engineer's character and work environment.
   - **Why**: Sets the tone and provides context for the protagonist's meticulous nature.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Audience understands the protagonist's personality and stakes.
   - **First Step**: Draft a one-minute scene showing the engineer's interaction with colleagues and their attention to detail.

2. **Craft the Inciting Incident**:
   - **What to Do**: Introduce the AI and the task of reviewing its code.
   - **Why**: This sets the plot in motion and introduces the central conflict.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Audience anticipates the interaction between the engineer and AI.
   - **First Step**: Write a scene where the engineer receives the task and begins the review.

3. **Build the Midpoint Crisis**:
   - **What to Do**: Showcase the engineer's realization of the AI's literal interpretations.
   - **Why**: Heightens tension and stakes, creating a turning point in the story.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Audience feels the urgency and stakes of the situation.
   - **First Step**: Script a sequence where the engineer sees the unintended consequences of their comments.

4. **Plan the Climax**:
   - **What to Do**: Develop a scene where the engineer uses creative communication to resolve the conflict.
   - **Why**: Provides a satisfying resolution to the central conflict.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Audience experiences a sense of relief and closure.
   - **First Step**: Outline a scene where humor and metaphor are used to guide the AI.

5. **Conclude with a Denouement**:
   - **What to Do**: Show the engineer reflecting on the experience and the restored system.
   - **Why**: Wraps up the story and reinforces the theme of adaptability.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Audience leaves with a clear understanding of the story's message.
   - **First Step**: Write a final scene where the engineer discusses the event with a colleague, highlighting lessons learned.
