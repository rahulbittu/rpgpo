## Finding 1: University Meeting Hygiene Practices with Pre-Meeting Agendas and Post-Meeting Outcomes
A university implemented disciplined meeting hygiene requiring agendas sent the day prior with documented desired outcomes for all meetings, from cabinet to one-on-one supervisor sessions; meetings without these were canceled. Meetings ended with reviews of decisions, outcomes, next steps, and follow-up communications, with minutes streamlined to only capture outcomes and next steps rather than dialogue. Agenda templates and guidelines were disseminated university-wide, and cabinet minutes were published to all employees, leading to cultural shifts toward more outcome-oriented meetings; teams were encouraged to retreat 1-2 times per year off-campus with funding for facilitators to build relationships.
- **Pre-meeting**: Agenda with outcomes required 1 day prior.
- **During-meeting**: Structured reviews.
- **Post-meeting**: Minutes on outcomes/next steps only; published publicly.
- **Protection**: Regular retreats to prevent relational drift.

## Finding 2: WHO Hand Hygiene Protocols for Healthcare Meetings and Workflows

Objectives include embedding hand hygiene in national IPC plans and facility SOPs per WHO Global Action Plan targets by 2030, with hand hygiene compliance monitoring as a key national indicator in reference hospitals by 2026; advocacy tools like badges and slides for presentations/virtual meetings provided.

## Finding 3: Healthcare Sanitation Protocols for Conference Rooms and Administrative Spaces
Healthcare cleaning checklist mandates sanitizing conference room tables **between uses**, disinfecting shared equipment like copiers/printers, and cleaning administrative offices with dusting, trash emptying, floor cleaning, and high-touch surface disinfection multiple times daily (e.g., door handles, pens). Terminal cleaning for high-risk areas includes thorough room disinfection with fresh materials, documentation checklists, and privacy curtain replacement per policy; professional teams note terminal cleaning takes significantly more time than daily tasks to reduce HAI risks.
- **Pre-meeting**: Fresh materials, check high-touch surfaces.
- **During-meeting**: N/A directly.
- **Post-meeting**: Disinfect tables between uses; complete checklists.
- **Protection**: Sequential clean-then-disinfect with EPA-registered products.

## Synthesize Meeting Hygiene System
## Plan

### Objective:
Develop a comprehensive meeting hygiene system that enhances productivity, ensures clarity, and protects valuable time for deep work and strategic thinking.

### Key Components:
1. **Pre-Meeting Preparation**
2. **During-Meeting Execution**
3. **Post-Meeting Follow-up**
4. **Strategies for Protecting Meeting-Free Time**

## Implementation Steps

### Pre-Meeting Preparation
- **Agenda Requirement**: Ensure that every meeting has a detailed agenda sent at least one day in advance. This agenda should include:
  - Purpose of the meeting
  - Desired outcomes
  - List of participants
  - Time allocations for each topic
  - Any preparatory materials or readings
  - **First Step**: Create a standardized agenda template and distribute it to all team members. Use the university's model as a guide.

### During-Meeting Execution
- **Structured Reviews**: Implement a structured review process at the end of each meeting to summarize decisions made, outcomes achieved, and assign next steps.
  - Use a designated timekeeper to ensure adherence to the agenda.
  - Encourage active participation and keep discussions focused on the agenda.
  - **First Step**: Train meeting facilitators on effective time management and engagement techniques.

### Post-Meeting Follow-up
- **Streamlined Minutes**: Document only the outcomes and next steps rather than detailed dialogue. This should be shared with all participants promptly.
  - Publish minutes publicly within the organization to ensure transparency and accountability.
  - **First Step**: Develop a template for meeting minutes focusing on outcomes and next steps.

### Strategies for Protecting Meeting-Free Time
- **Scheduled Retreats**: Organize regular team retreats, ideally 1-2 times per year, to foster team relationships and prevent relational drift.
  - Allocate funding for facilitators to guide these retreats.
  - **First Step**: Plan the next retreat by setting a date and securing a venue.

## Tools/Resources

- **Agenda and Minutes Templates**: Develop and distribute templates for agendas and minutes to ensure consistency.
- **Time Management Tools**: Use tools like Google Calendar or Microsoft Outlook to schedule and block meeting-free times.
- **Facilitation Resources**: Hire professional facilitators or provide training for internal facilitators for retreats and crucial meetings.
- **Communication Platforms**: Utilize platforms like Slack or Microsoft Teams for distributing agendas and minutes efficiently.

## Review Schedule

- **Quarterly Reviews**: Conduct quarterly reviews of the meeting hygiene system to assess its effectiveness and identify areas for improvement.
  - Collect feedback from participants on the clarity and productivity of meetings.
  - Adjust protocols based on feedback and evolving needs.
  - **First Step**: Schedule the first review session three months from implementation.

By following these steps, you can create a meeting hygiene system that not only streamlines the meeting process but also enhances productivity and preserves essential time for strategic work.
