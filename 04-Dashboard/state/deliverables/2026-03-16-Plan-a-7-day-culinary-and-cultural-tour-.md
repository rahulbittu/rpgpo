## Algarve Culinary and Cultural Events

### Feira dos Enchidos (Monchique)

**First weekend of March** – This traditional fair in the mountain town of Monchique celebrates regional culinary heritage with artisan sausages, homemade cakes, local honey, and traditional liqueurs such as *aguardente de medronho*[5]. The fair also features artisan crafts, live music, and authentic Algarve cultural performances[1].

### Algarve Culinary Traditions

The region is known for *cataplana*, a seafood stew cooked in a distinctive clam-shaped copper pot featuring clams, prawns, fish, tomatoes, herbs, and white wine[1]. Local festivals highlight fresh seafood, regional pastries, and artisanal products[1].

**Themed Cultural and Gastronomic Tours** – Secret Portugal offers tours combining history, culture, and gastronomy, including exploration of Moorish influences in Algarve cuisine[9].
