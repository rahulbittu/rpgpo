## Finding 1: Vancouver Olympic Loop Road Trip (Portland to Vancouver Alternative via Parks and Islands)
This 5-10 day itinerary starts in Vancouver, BC, but can be reversed from Portland via I-5 north to Bellingham/Anacortes ferry (2-3 hours drive), then ferries to islands and Olympic National Park before looping back. Key scenic stops: North Cascades National Park (Hwy 20 drive, Rainy Pass Trailhead Pacific Crest Trail hike), San Juan Island, Deception Pass State Park (2.4-mile Rosario Beach Trail loop, $10 vehicle fee), Whidbey Island, Port Townsend, Sequim, Port Angeles/Hurricane Ridge (Olympic NP, $30/vehicle), Victoria BC ferry, end Vancouver. Outdoor activities: Seawall bike/walk in Vancouver (18 miles), kayaking at Ross Lake Resort ($50-80/hour rental), seal/eagle spotting at Deception Pass. No exact costs listed; ferries ~$50-100 roundtrip/person + vehicle from Anacortes.  
Source: https://www.myolympicpark.com/road-trips/road-trip-itineraries/vancouver-olympic-loop-road-trip/

## Finding 2: Highway 101 West Coast Route from Portland Northward
Highway 101 from Portland north via Cannon Beach (Haystack Rock), Oregon Dunes, Cape Perpetua offers coastal scenery to Washington; continue to Olympic Peninsula for Vancouver access via ferry from Port Angeles to Victoria (~$100-150 roundtrip/person + vehicle, 90 minutes). Outdoor activities: Beach walks at Cannon Beach, dune hikes/buggy rentals at Oregon Dunes ($25-50/hour ATV). Gas costs ~$150-250 roundtrip Portland-Vancouver (500 miles, $4/gallon avg 2026). Lodging: Portland hostels $40-80/night.  
Source: https://www.thebrokebackpacker.com/west-coast-road-trip-usa/

## Finding 3: Columbia River Gorge and Mt. Hood Detour en Route to Vancouver
From Portland, detour east on Historic Columbia River Highway to Hood River/Columbia Gorge (30 minutes, free viewpoints), then north via I-5/Vancouver WA bridge to BC (total 6-hour drive). Stops: Multnomah Falls (free, early AM to avoid crowds), Horsetail/Ponytail Falls (short hikes), Wahclella Falls (1.8-mile hike), Vista House/Women’s Forum viewpoints. Outdoor activities: Waterfall hikes (2-4 miles, free), windsurfing in Hood River ($60-100/lesson). Fits 10-14 day PNW trip including Mt. Rainier/Olympics.  
Source: https://wheatlesswanderlust.com/pacific-northwest-road-trip/

## Itinerary

### Day 1: Portland to Cannon Beach
- **Morning**: Depart Portland and drive to Cannon Beach (~1.5 hours, 80 miles).
- **Activity**: Visit Haystack Rock for a beach walk and tide pooling.
- **Evening**: Stay overnight in Cannon Beach. Consider Cannon Beach Hotel ($150-200/night).

### Day 2: Cannon Beach to Oregon Dunes
- **Morning**: Drive to Oregon Dunes (~3 hours, 150 miles).
- **Activity**: Rent an ATV for dune exploration ($25-50/hour).
- **Evening**: Camp at Oregon Dunes National Recreation Area or stay in a nearby hotel.

### Day 3: Oregon Dunes to Cape Perpetua
- **Morning**: Drive to Cape Perpetua (~1 hour, 50 miles).
- **Activity**: Hike the Cape Perpetua Scenic Area trails.
- **Evening**: Stay in Yachats or camp at Cape Perpetua.

### Day 4: Cape Perpetua to Deception Pass State Park
- **Morning**: Drive to Deception Pass State Park (~5 hours, 250 miles).
- **Activity**: Hike the Rosario Beach Trail loop ($10 vehicle fee).
- **Evening**: Stay in Anacortes or camp at the state park.

### Day 5: Anacortes to San Juan Island
- **Morning**: Take the ferry to San Juan Island (~$50-100 roundtrip/person + vehicle).
- **Activity**: Explore Friday Harbor and kayak around the island.
- **Evening**: Stay overnight on San Juan Island.

### Day 6: San Juan Island to Whidbey Island
- **Morning**: Ferry back to Anacortes, then drive to Whidbey Island (~1 hour, 30 miles).
- **Activity**: Visit local wineries or beaches.
- **Evening**: Stay overnight on Whidbey Island.

### Day 7: Whidbey Island to Port Townsend
- **Morning**: Drive to Port Townsend (~1 hour, 35 miles).
- **Activity**: Explore Victorian architecture and local shops.
- **Evening**: Stay in Port Townsend.

### Day 8: Port Townsend to Olympic National Park
- **Morning**: Drive to Olympic National Park, specifically Hurricane Ridge (~2 hours, 80 miles).
- **Activity**: Hike and explore ($30/vehicle entry fee).
- **Evening**: Camp or stay in Port Angeles.

### Day 9: Port Angeles to Victoria, BC
- **Morning**: Ferry to Victoria, BC (~$100-150 roundtrip/person + vehicle, 90 minutes).
- **Activity**: Explore Butchart Gardens and downtown Victoria.
- **Evening**: Stay in Victoria.

### Day 10: Victoria to Vancouver
- **Morning**: Drive to Vancouver (~3 hours, including ferry).
- **Activity**: Bike or walk the Seawall (18 miles).
- **Evening**: Conclude trip in Vancouver.
