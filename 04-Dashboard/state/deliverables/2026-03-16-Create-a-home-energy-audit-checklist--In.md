## Research Home Energy Audit Standards
## Insulation Assessment Standards
- **R-value targets**: Attics should reach **R-30+** (10-15 inches of fiberglass or foam insulation); basement/crawl space floors need at least **R-25**. Check by inspecting attic joists—if visible, insulation is insufficient.[5]
- **Blower door test**: Gold standard using a fan in the door frame to depressurize the home, paired with infrared cameras to detect air leaks (shown as purple/blue streaks) and missing insulation.[5]
- **DOE Home Energy Score**: On-site evaluation measures insulation levels as part of a 1-10 score assessing permanent features like walls and roofs.[4]

## HVAC Efficiency Best Practices
- **Filter replacement**: Inspect furnace filters; replace if gray/fuzzy to avoid overworking the system.[6]
- **Efficiency ratings for upgrades**: Require **SEER, HSPF, AFUE** ratings; central AC, furnaces, boilers, and water heaters must meet **Energy Star** standards. Heat pumps need **ENERGY STAR Most Efficient** certification (2025).[1][3]
- **Water heater settings**: Set thermostat to **120°F** (not default 140°F) to reduce heat loss; natural gas/propane/oil models qualify under prior tax rules with Energy Star.[1][6]
- **Duct sealing**: Use mastic on ducts during audits.[5]

## Appliance Energy Ratings
- **Energy Star certification**: Mandatory for qualifying upgrades like water heaters (50% solar-derived for solar models), furnaces, and boilers. Check labels for **ENERGY STAR Most Efficient** on heat pumps.[1][3]
- **Phantom loads**: Audit appliances for standby power; use power strips or smart plugs to cut waste.[5]
- **Battery storage**: Minimum **3 kWh** capacity for tax-eligible systems (2025).[3]

## Smart Home Automation for Energy Savings
- **Real-time integration**: DOE Home Energy Score future updates may link with smart devices for dynamic consumption monitoring and behavior-based recommendations.[4]
- **Smart plugs**: Recommended for appliance phantom loads during DIY audits.[5]

## Synthesize Home Energy Audit Checklist
## Plan

### Insulation Assessment
- **Objective**: Ensure adequate insulation to minimize energy loss.
- **Key Metrics**: 
  - Attic insulation should achieve an R-value of at least **R-30**.
  - Basement and crawl space floors should have an R-value of at least **R-25**.
- **Methods**: 
  - Conduct a **Blower Door Test** to identify air leaks.
  - Use infrared cameras to visualize insulation gaps.
  - Evaluate insulation levels using the **DOE Home Energy Score**.

### HVAC Efficiency
- **Objective**: Optimize HVAC system performance to reduce energy consumption.
- **Key Actions**:
  - Replace furnace filters regularly.
  - Upgrade systems to meet or exceed **SEER, HSPF, AFUE** ratings.
  - Set water heater thermostats to **120°F**.
  - Seal ductwork with mastic.

### Appliance Energy Ratings
- **Objective**: Ensure appliances are energy-efficient to lower utility bills.
- **Key Actions**:
  - Verify **Energy Star certification** for all major appliances.
  - Prioritize upgrades to **ENERGY STAR Most Efficient** models for heat pumps.

### Smart Home Automation
- **Objective**: Implement smart technologies to enhance energy savings.
- **Strategies**:
  - Install smart thermostats to optimize heating/cooling schedules.
  - Use smart plugs to manage and reduce phantom loads.
  - Integrate smart lighting systems to automate and reduce electricity use.

## Implementation Steps

1. **Insulation Assessment**:
   - Schedule a professional to conduct a blower door test and infrared scan.
   - Inspect attic and basement insulation; add more if joists are visible.

2. **HVAC Efficiency**:
   - Check and replace HVAC filters monthly.
   - Consult with an HVAC professional to evaluate the system's SEER, HSPF, and AFUE ratings.
   - Adjust water heater settings to 120°F.

3. **Appliance Energy Ratings**:
   - Audit current appliances for Energy Star labels.
   - Plan for phased upgrades to more efficient models.

4. **Smart Home Automation**:
   - Research and purchase smart thermostats, plugs, and lighting systems.
   - Set up automation routines to align with daily schedules.

## Tools/Resources

- **Blower Door Test Kit**: For professional use in detecting air leaks.
- **Infrared Camera**: To visualize insulation deficiencies.
- **Smart Thermostat**: Models like Nest or Ecobee.
- **Smart Plugs and Lighting**: Brands like TP-Link or Philips Hue.

## Review Schedule

- **Quarterly**: Review and replace HVAC filters; check smart device settings.
- **Annually**: Conduct a full home energy audit including insulation and appliance checks.
- **As Needed**: Upgrade appliances and insulation based on audit findings.

By following this checklist, you can systematically improve your home's energy efficiency, reduce utility costs, and contribute to a more sustainable living environment.
