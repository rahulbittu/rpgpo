## Core 1-on-1 Structure and Agenda

**Effective 1-on-1s are weekly 30-45 minute conversations between a manager and direct report[3]**. The key is creating a **meaningful, collaborative agenda** where both participants add items throughout the week[3].

### Essential Agenda Components

Your 1-on-1 agenda should include[2]:
- **Old business and information items** (updates to share)
- **New discussion items** (feedback requests from the team)
- **Action items** (tasks to complete during or after the meeting)

A practical template structure includes[4]:
- Manager name and date
- Three-point agenda: account/opportunity/project updates, special projects involved in, training/PTO/video work
- Meeting notes and next steps section
- Important links reference area

### Best Practices for Agenda Planning

Before each meeting, clarify[2]:
- Meeting objectives and goals
- Which actions are highest priority
- Time estimates for each topic (assign time limits to ensure precision[1])
- Expectations and responsibilities by sharing the agenda prior to the meeting

**Real-world example**: One engineering leader shares agendas with their manager in a shared document ahead of time, which helps them stay organized and ensures their manager can prepare[4].

## Collaboration and Documentation

Both participants should[3]:
- Add talking points and action items in real-time
- Take notes during the meeting
- Assign action items so you leave with a clear plan
- Use a shared document (Google Doc or Word) that both can access and edit

The agenda functions as a **two-way collaboration**, not a manager-driven meeting[3]. Participants can make items private, add hyperlinks, attach documents (up to 50 MB), and use formatting to highlight priorities[3].

## Implementation

Set up recurring 1-on-1s by[3]:
- Creating a recurring Google Calendar event
- Including a static 1-on-1 agenda link in the notes
- Maintaining accurate information to ensure meetings stay organized and tracked

## Recommendations

### Agenda Templates
- **Use a Structured Template**: Implement a clear agenda template that includes updates, new discussion items, and action items. This ensures all critical points are covered and time is managed effectively.

### Career Conversation Frameworks
- **Development Focus**: Incorporate career development discussions regularly. Use frameworks like the GROW model (Goal, Reality, Options, Will) to guide conversations about career aspirations and development paths.

### Strategies for Delivering Difficult Feedback
- **Use the SBI Model**: Situation-Behavior-Impact (SBI) is effective for delivering feedback. Clearly describe the situation, the observed behavior, and the impact of that behavior.
- **Prepare and Practice**: Before delivering difficult feedback, prepare your points and practice to ensure clarity and empathy.

### Adaptations for Remote Meetings
- **Use Video Conferencing Tools**: Ensure all remote meetings are conducted via video to maintain personal connection.
- **Digital Collaboration Tools**: Use tools like Google Docs or Notion for real-time agenda collaboration and note-taking.

### Methods for Tracking Development Goals
- **SMART Goals**: Set Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound goals for development tracking.
- **Regular Check-ins**: Use part of the one-on-one to review progress on these goals and adjust as necessary.
