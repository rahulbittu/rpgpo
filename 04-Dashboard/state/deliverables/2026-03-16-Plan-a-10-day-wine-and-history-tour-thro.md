## Wine Tastings

**Châteauneuf-du-Pape Spring Festival**

Les Printemps de Châteauneuf-du-Pape takes place **April 10-12, 2026**, featuring **100 wineries** showcasing their latest wines alongside workshops, masterclasses, and a gastronomic market.[2] Entry costs approximately **10 euros** for wine tastings.[2]

**Guided Wine Tours from Avignon**

Half-day tours to Châteauneuf-du-Pape depart from Avignon starting at **$110 per person**, visiting 3 different wineries and featuring full-bodied reds made from Grenache, Syrah, Cinsault, Mourvèdre, Carignan, and Cabernet-Sauvignon varieties.[5] Private intensive experiences covering Châteauneuf-du-Pape and Rhône Valley vineyards range from **$652-$1,066 per group** for 6-9 hour tours.[4]

**Maison Brotte Tastings**

Wine tours and tastings at Maison Brotte in Châteauneuf-du-Pape are available as part of the **Explore Southern Rhône Valley and Provence** tour in October 2026, including picnic lunch and vineyard visits.[3]

**Lyon-Based Wine Tours**

Kanpai Tourisme, based in Lyon, offers guided wine tours to Beaujolais, Côtes du Rhône, Burgundy, and Mâconnais regions, featuring visits to vineyards like Côte-Rôtie, Condrieu, Hermitage, Morgon, and Pouilly-Fuissé.[7]

## Historical Sites and River Experiences

**Avignon Papal Palace and River Cruises**

The Palais des Papes in Avignon is included on **8-day Rhône River cruises** departing from Lyon to Arles, with stops in historic towns including Vienne, Tournon, Viviers, and Avignon.[8] The **MS Amakristina** and **Emerald Liberté** offer week-long cruises featuring wine tastings, ancient Roman ruins, and medieval village visits.[8]

**Roman Ruins in Arles**

Arles features Roman monuments accessible via guided walking tours on Rhône River cruises, with UNESCO-listed sites included in 8-day itineraries from Lyon to Nice.[8]

## Synthesize Itinerary
### 10-Day Itinerary for Wine and History Tour through Rhône Valley and Provence

#### Day 1: Arrival in Lyon
- **Activity**: Arrive in Lyon, the gateway to the Rhône Valley.
- **Logistics**: Fly into Lyon-Saint Exupéry Airport. Check into a centrally located hotel.
- **Evening**: Explore Vieux Lyon, a UNESCO World Heritage site, and enjoy a traditional Lyonnaise dinner.

#### Day 2: Lyon Wine Tour
- **Activity**: Join a Kanpai Tourisme guided wine tour.
- **Locations**: Visit vineyards in Beaujolais and Côte-Rôtie.
- **Logistics**: Full-day tour departing from Lyon, includes transportation and tastings.

#### Day 3: Travel to Avignon
- **Activity**: Travel from Lyon to Avignon by train (approx. 2 hours).
- **Logistics**: Check into a hotel in Avignon.
- **Afternoon**: Visit the Palais des Papes, a UNESCO site.
- **Evening**: Dine at a local bistro in Avignon.

#### Day 4: Châteauneuf-du-Pape Wine Tasting
- **Activity**: Half-day guided wine tour from Avignon.
- **Locations**: Visit 3 wineries in Châteauneuf-du-Pape.
- **Logistics**: Book a tour starting at $110 per person. Includes transportation and tastings.

#### Day 5: Les Printemps de Châteauneuf-du-Pape Festival
- **Activity**: Attend the wine festival (April 10-12, 2026).
- **Locations**: Châteauneuf-du-Pape village.
- **Logistics**: Entry fee of 10 euros for tastings. Participate in workshops and enjoy the gastronomic market.

#### Day 6: Travel to Arles
- **Activity**: Travel to Arles by train (approx. 1 hour from Avignon).
- **Logistics**: Check into a hotel in Arles.
- **Afternoon**: Visit the Roman ruins, including the Arles Amphitheatre.
- **Evening**: Enjoy dinner at a local restaurant.

#### Day 7: Explore Provence Lavender Fields
- **Activity**: Day trip to the lavender fields (best visited in late June to early August).
- **Locations**: Plateau de Valensole or Luberon.
- **Logistics**: Rent a car or book a guided tour from Arles.

#### Day 8: Travel to Marseille
- **Activity**: Travel to Marseille by train (approx. 1 hour from Arles).
- **Logistics**: Check into a hotel in Marseille.
- **Afternoon**: Explore the Old Port and Le Panier district.
- **Evening**: Enjoy traditional bouillabaisse at a renowned Marseille restaurant.

#### Day 9: Marseille Cultural Exploration
- **Activity**: Visit the Mucem (Museum of European and Mediterranean Civilisations).
- **Afternoon**: Take a boat tour to the Calanques National Park.
- **Evening**: Relax and enjoy a seafood dinner by the port.

#### Day 10: Departure
- **Activity**: Return to Lyon or depart from Marseille Provence Airport.
- **Logistics**: Arrange transportation to the airport for your flight home.

### Logistics and Tips:
- **Transportation**: Utilize France's efficient train system for intercity travel. Consider renting a car for more remote vineyard and lavender field visits.
- **Accommodation**: Book hotels in advance, especially during festival times.
- **Booking**: Secure wine tours and festival tickets early to ensure availability.
- **Timing**: Adjust the itinerary based on seasonal availability of lavender fields and festival dates.

This itinerary offers a balanced blend of wine, history, and cultural experiences, ensuring a memorable journey through the Rhône Valley and Provence.
