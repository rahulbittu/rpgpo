## Key Findings

1. **Federal Estate Tax Exemption**: By 2026, the federal estate and gift tax exemption will be $15 million per person, allowing significant tax-free wealth transfer. However, the Generation-Skipping Transfer (GST) exemption requires separate planning as it cannot be transferred to a spouse.
   
2. **Trust Structures**: Trusts are essential for asset protection, tax flexibility, and multigenerational wealth transfer. They are particularly beneficial for tech professionals with stock options and intellectual property, providing continuity and protection.

## Detailed Analysis

### Federal Estate Tax Exemption and GST Planning
- **Action**: Utilize the $15 million exemption to gift assets during your lifetime, reducing future estate tax burdens. Establish separate GST plans for transfers to grandchildren.
- **Why**: Maximizing the use of exemptions reduces taxable estate size, preserving wealth.
- **Outcome**: Lower estate taxes and preserved wealth for future generations.
- **First Step**: Consult with an estate planner to draft gifting strategies and GST plans.

### Trust Structures
- **Action**: Establish a revocable living trust for stock options and intellectual property. Consider irrevocable trusts for multigenerational wealth transfer.
- **Why**: Trusts provide asset protection, manage distributions, and ensure continuity without probate.
- **Outcome**: Protected assets, reduced tax liabilities, and seamless wealth transfer.
- **First Step**: Identify assets to place in trusts and consult with a trust attorney to set up the appropriate structures.

### Charitable Giving Strategies
- **Action**: Create a charitable remainder trust or donor-advised fund to manage philanthropic contributions.
- **Why**: These structures offer tax deductions and allow for strategic philanthropy.
- **Outcome**: Tax benefits and fulfillment of charitable objectives.
- **First Step**: Determine charitable goals and consult with a financial advisor to establish the appropriate giving vehicle.

### Digital Asset Succession
- **Action**: Develop a digital asset succession plan, including access to digital accounts and intellectual property.
- **Why**: Ensures digital assets are transferred according to your wishes and remain accessible.
- **Outcome**: Secure and planned transfer of digital assets.
- **First Step**: Inventory digital assets and work with an estate planner to integrate them into your estate plan.
