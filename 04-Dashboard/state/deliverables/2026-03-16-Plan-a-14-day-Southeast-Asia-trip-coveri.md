## Itinerary

### Day 1-4: Bangkok
- **Day 1: Arrival and Initial Exploration**
  - **Activity**: Settle into your accommodation and explore the local area.
  - **Why**: Acclimate to the city and get a feel for your surroundings.
  - **First Step**: Book a hotel in a central location like Sukhumvit or Silom.

- **Day 2: Cultural Immersion**
  - **Activity**: Visit the Grand Palace and Wat Pho.
  - **Why**: These are iconic landmarks that provide insight into Thai culture and history.
  - **First Step**: Purchase tickets online to avoid queues.

- **Day 3: Street Food Tour**
  - **Activity**: Bangkok Backstreets Food Tour with 15+ Tastings.
  - **Why**: Experience the best of Bangkok's street food, including Michelin-listed stalls.
  - **First Step**: Book the tour at [TripAdvisor](https://www.tripadvisor.com/AttractionProductReview-g293916-d14105162-Bangkok_Backstreets_Food_Tour_with_15_Tastings-Bangkok.html).

- **Day 4: Relaxation and Shopping**
  - **Activity**: Visit Chatuchak Weekend Market and Lumpini Park.
  - **Why**: Enjoy shopping and relaxation in a green space.
  - **First Step**: Check market days and plan your visit.

### Day 5-8: Chiang Mai
- **Day 5: Travel to Chiang Mai**
  - **Activity**: Fly to Chiang Mai.
  - **Why**: Maximize time by flying instead of taking a long bus or train ride.
  - **First Step**: Book a flight from Bangkok to Chiang Mai.

- **Day 6: Cultural Exploration**
  - **Activity**: Visit Wat Phra That Doi Suthep and Wat Chedi Luang.
  - **Why**: These temples offer stunning architecture and historical significance.
  - **First Step**: Arrange transportation to Doi Suthep.

- **Day 7: Cooking Class**
  - **Activity**: Join a local cooking class.
  - **Why**: Learn to cook traditional Thai dishes.
  - **First Step**: Book a class through a reputable provider like Thai Farm Cooking School.

- **Day 8: Trekking Adventure**
  - **Activity**: Trekking in Doi Inthanon National Park.
  - **Why**: Experience Thailand's natural beauty and diverse ecosystems.
  - **First Step**: Book a guided trek through a local tour operator.

### Day 9-14: Siem Reap
- **Day 9: Travel to Siem Reap**
  - **Activity**: Fly to Siem Reap.
  - **Why**: Quick and efficient travel to Cambodia.
  - **First Step**: Book a flight from Chiang Mai to Siem Reap.

- **Day 10: Angkor Wat Exploration**
  - **Activity**: Visit Angkor Wat and surrounding temples.
  - **Why**: Explore one of the world's most significant archaeological sites.
  - **First Step**: Purchase a multi-day Angkor Pass.

- **Day 11: Temple Etiquette and Exploration**
  - **Activity**: Guided tour focusing on temple etiquette and history.
  - **Why**: Gain deeper insights into the cultural significance of the temples.
  - **First Step**: Book a guided tour with a local expert.

- **Day 12: Local Market and Cultural Show**
  - **Activity**: Visit Old Market and attend a traditional Apsara dance show.
  - **Why**: Experience local culture and cuisine.
  - **First Step**: Reserve tickets for the dance show in advance.

- **Day 13: Relaxation Day**
  - **Activity**: Enjoy a spa day or relax by the pool.
  - **Why**: Recharge before returning home.
  - **First Step**: Book a spa appointment at your hotel or a local spa.

- **Day 14: Departure**
  - **Activity**: Prepare for departure.
  - **Why**: Ensure a smooth journey home.
  - **First Step**: Arrange airport transfer.

## Costs & Budget
- **Bangkok Street Food Tour**: $47.40 - $69 per person.
- **Cooking Class in Chiang Mai**: Approximately $30-$50.
- **Flights**: Bangkok to Chiang Mai (~$30-$70), Chiang Mai to Siem Reap (~$100-$150).
- **Angkor Pass**: $37 for a one-day pass, $62 for a three-day pass.
- **Accommodation**: Budget $30-$100 per night depending on preferences.

## Logistics
- **Transportation**: Use Grab for local transport in Bangkok and Chiang Mai.
- **Currency**: Thai Baht (THB) in Thailand, Cambodian Riel (KHR) and USD in Cambodia.
- **Visas**: Check visa requirements for Thailand and Cambodia.

## Tips
- **Temple Etiquette**: Dress modestly, covering shoulders and knees. Remove shoes when entering temples.
- **Street Food Safety**: Choose stalls with high turnover and locals eating there.
- **Bargaining**: Common in markets; start with 50-70% of the asking price.
