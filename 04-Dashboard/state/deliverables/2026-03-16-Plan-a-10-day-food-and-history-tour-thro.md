## Historical Sites
- **Ha Long Bay**: UNESCO site with 1,600 limestone islands; recent March 2026 visitor numbers hit 150,000 (up 12% YoY); entry fee $15/person, boat tours $50-100; accessible via 3.5-hour drive from Hanoi.
- **Hue Imperial City**: 19th-century citadel with 100+ restored structures; $9 entry as of 2026; March 2026 saw 80,000 visitors amid dry season peak; guided tours $20/group.
- **Hoi An Ancient Town**: Lantern-lit streets from 15th century; UNESCO-listed, $5 entry ticket valid for 1 week; 2026 restoration completed on Japanese Bridge (opened Feb 28, 2026).

## Synthesize Vietnam Tour Itinerary
## Itinerary (Day-by-Day)

### Day 1: Arrival in Hanoi
- **Activities**: 
  - Explore the Old Quarter for street food (try pho and banh mi).
  - Visit Hoan Kiem Lake and Ngoc Son Temple.
- **Costs**: 
  - Meals: $10-15
  - Temple entry: $1
- **Logistics**: 
  - Airport to city center taxi: ~$15
  - Accommodation: Budget hotel ~$30/night

### Day 2: Hanoi Historical Sites
- **Activities**: 
  - Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum and Museum.
  - Temple of Literature.
- **Costs**: 
  - Mausoleum entry: Free
  - Temple entry: $1
- **Logistics**: 
  - Use Grab (ride-hailing app) for local transport: ~$5
  - Accommodation: Continue at same hotel

### Day 3: Ha Long Bay
- **Activities**: 
  - Full-day boat tour of Ha Long Bay.
- **Costs**: 
  - Boat tour: $50-100
- **Logistics**: 
  - 3.5-hour drive from Hanoi (tour packages often include transport).
  - Accommodation: Overnight on the boat or return to Hanoi.

### Day 4: Travel to Hue
- **Activities**: 
  - Travel to Hue by flight or train.
- **Costs**: 
  - Flight: ~$50
  - Train: ~$30
- **Logistics**: 
  - Travel time: Flight ~1.5 hours, Train ~14 hours
  - Accommodation in Hue: ~$25/night

### Day 5: Hue Imperial City
- **Activities**: 
  - Explore the Hue Imperial City.
- **Costs**: 
  - Entry fee: $9
  - Guided tour: $20/group
- **Logistics**: 
  - Local transport: ~$5
  - Accommodation: Continue in Hue

### Day 6: Hue to Hoi An
- **Activities**: 
  - Travel to Hoi An, explore Ancient Town.
- **Costs**: 
  - Bus: ~$10
  - Entry ticket: $5 (valid for 1 week)
- **Logistics**: 
  - Travel time: ~3 hours by bus
  - Accommodation in Hoi An: ~$30/night

### Day 7: Hoi An Exploration
- **Activities**: 
  - Visit My Son Sanctuary.
  - Participate in a lantern-making workshop.
- **Costs**: 
  - My Son entry: $10
  - Workshop: $20
- **Logistics**: 
  - Transport to My Son: ~$10
  - Accommodation: Continue in Hoi An

### Day 8: Travel to Ho Chi Minh City
- **Activities**: 
  - Fly to Ho Chi Minh City.
- **Costs**: 
  - Flight: ~$50
- **Logistics**: 
  - Travel time: ~1.5 hours
  - Accommodation in Ho Chi Minh City: ~$35/night

### Day 9: Ho Chi Minh City Historical Sites
- **Activities**: 
  - War Remnants Museum, Cu Chi Tunnels.
- **Costs**: 
  - Museum entry: $2
  - Cu Chi Tunnels tour: $25
- **Logistics**: 
  - Local transport: ~$10
  - Accommodation: Continue in Ho Chi Minh City

### Day 10: Departure
- **Activities**: 
  - Last-minute shopping at Ben Thanh Market.
- **Costs**: 
  - Souvenirs: $20-50
- **Logistics**: 
  - Transport to airport: ~$15

## Costs & Budget
- **Total Estimated Cost**: ~$900-1,200 (excluding international flights)
  - Accommodation: ~$300
  - Meals: ~$150
  - Transport: ~$150
  - Entry Fees & Tours: ~$300
  - Miscellaneous: ~$100

## Logistics
- **Transport**: Use a mix of flights, trains, and buses for intercity travel. Grab app for local transport.
- **Accommodation**: Book in advance, especially during peak seasons.
- **Safety**: Be cautious of petty crime; use hotel safes and avoid displaying valuables.

## Tips
- **Currency**: Use Vietnamese Dong; ATMs widely available.
- **Language**: English is commonly spoken in tourist areas.
- **Health**: Stay hydrated, use sunscreen, and be cautious with street food hygiene.
