## Recommended Actions

1. **Plan Outdoor Activities**:
   - **What to Do**: Organize a schedule of outdoor activities similar to those offered at Westgate River Ranch Resort.
   - **Why**: These activities are proven to engage participants and reduce screen time effectively.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Increased relaxation and social interaction, reduced stress, and enhanced focus on the present moment.
   - **First Step**: Research local venues or parks that offer similar activities and create a detailed itinerary for the weekend.

2. **Implement Simple Meal Prep**:
   - **What to Do**: Prepare meals based on the Hocking Hills model, focusing on bulk-prep items like overnight oats and veggie sticks.
   - **Why**: This strategy minimizes the need for digital devices in the kitchen and allows more time for detox activities.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Efficient meal preparation with nutritious options that support energy levels throughout the detox.
   - **First Step**: Purchase ingredients and prepare meals on the Friday before the detox weekend begins.

3. **Incorporate Nature-Based Activities**:
   - **What to Do**: Schedule hikes and contemplative activities in natural settings.
   - **Why**: Exposure to nature has been shown to improve mental well-being and reduce the desire for digital engagement.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Enhanced mindfulness and a deeper connection with the natural environment.
   - **First Step**: Identify local hiking trails and plan a route that includes time for reflection and relaxation.

4. **Address FOMO (Fear of Missing Out)**:
   - **What to Do**: Use journal prompts to reflect on personal goals and the benefits of disconnecting.
   - **Why**: Journaling can help participants process feelings of FOMO and reinforce the purpose of the detox.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Reduced anxiety about missing out on digital interactions and a stronger focus on personal growth.
   - **First Step**: Prepare a set of journal prompts, such as "What do I hope to gain from this detox?" and "How do I feel when I'm disconnected from technology?"
