# Level 2 EV Home Charger Comparison: ChargePoint Home Flex, Wallbox Pulsar Plus, and Grizzl-E Classic

## Finding 1: ChargePoint Home Flex Specifications and Features

The **ChargePoint Home Flex** delivers up to **50A (12 kW)** maximum output when hardwired, though the plug-in version provides **40A (9.6 kW)**[1]. The charger adds approximately **30 miles of range per hour** to non-Tesla vehicles and features a **23-foot SAE J1772 connector**[1].

**Smart Features:**
- App control, scheduling, and energy tracking[2]
- Voice assistant compatibility with Alexa and Google Assistant[2]
- ENERGY STAR certified[3]
- Works both indoors and outdoors[3]

**Installation Options:**
The Home Flex can be hardwired (compatible with circuit breaker ratings from 20A to 80A) or plugged into a 240V outlet with either a **NEMA 6-50 or NEMA 14-50 receptacle**[1]. Hardwiring enables the fastest charge at up to 50A[1].

**Price:** $699[2]

**Warranty:** 3-year limited warranty[3]

Source: https://nuwattenergy.com/en/ev-chargers/compare/chargepoint-home-flex-vs-wallbox-pulsar-plus

## Finding 2: Wallbox Pulsar Plus Specifications and Features

The **Wallbox Pulsar Plus** delivers up to **48A (11.5 kW)** maximum output[2] with a **25-foot cord length** (2 feet longer than ChargePoint)[2]. It adds approximately **38 miles of range per hour** at maximum capacity[2].

**Smart Features:**
- App control, scheduling, power sharing, and Power Boost technology[2]
- Voice assistant compatibility with Alexa and Google Assistant[2]
- Plug options include NEMA 14-50 or hardwired installation[2]

**Price:** $649[2]

The Wallbox is $50 cheaper than the ChargePoint Home Flex while delivering comparable real-world charging speeds across multiple vehicle models[2].

Source: https://nuwattenergy.com/en/ev-chargers/compare/chargepoint-home-flex-vs-wallbox-pulsar-plus

## Finding 3: Grizzl-E Classic Specifications and Features

The **Grizzl-E Classic** delivers **40A (9.6 kW)** maximum output[3] with a **24-foot cord length**[3]. It adds approximately **32 miles of range per hour** at maximum capacity[3].

**Smart Features:**
- No smart features (no app control, scheduling, or voice assistant support)[3]
- Outdoor-rated only (unlike ChargePoint which works indoors and outdoors)[3]

**Installation Options:**
Supports both **NEMA 14-50 plug** and **hardwired installation**[3].

**Price:** $399[3]

**Warranty:** 3-year limited warranty[3]

The Grizzl-E Classic is the most affordable option at $300 less than ChargePoint, but lacks smart features and indoor capability[3].

Source: https://nuwattenergy.com/en/ev-chargers/compare/chargepoint-home-flex-vs-grizzl-e-classic

## Finding 4: Real-World Charging Speed Comparison

Actual charging times vary by vehicle onboard charger capacity. For a **Ford Mustang Mach-E (10.5 kW onboard)**:
- ChargePoint Home Flex: ~36 mi/hr, 5 hours to 80%[3]
- Grizzl-E Classic: ~33 mi/hr, 5.5 hours to 80%[3]

For a **Tesla Model 3 Long Range (11.5 kW onboard)**:
- ChargePoint Home Flex: ~55 mi/hr, 3.8 hours to 80%[2]
- Wallbox Pulsar Plus: ~55 mi/hr, 3.8 hours to 80%[2]

Source: https://nuwattenergy.com/en/ev-chargers/compare/chargepoint-home-flex-vs-grizzl-e-classic

## Finding 5: Installation Through Treehouse Partnership

Toyota and Lexus owners can install a hardwired **ChargePoint Home Flex through Treehouse**, which can reduce charging time by up to **30%** depending on vehicle and setup[4]. The charger is available with either **SAE J3400 NACS** (for 2026+ BEVs) or **SAE J1772** (for 2023-2025 BEVs and PHEVs)[4].

Treehouse provides a **two-year installation warranty** in addition to ChargePoint's **three-year hardware warranty**[4]. A virtual assessment tool generates installation quotes within 48 hours using customer-submitted images[6].

Source: https://www.carpro.com/blog/toyota-lexus-simplify-getting-a-level-2-home-charger
