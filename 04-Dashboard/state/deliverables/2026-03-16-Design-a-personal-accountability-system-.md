## Research best practices for personal accountability systems
## Weekly Check-Ins
Fixed weekly check-in days create routine and predictability. For example, My PT Hub recommends Saturday by noon submissions for client updates, including training feedback, habit summaries, and progress photos, reducing back-and-forth communication.[1] Unicorn Labs uses a "bookend" system: Monday planning where team members publicly commit to weekly tasks, metrics, and due dates; Friday check-ins review completions, slips, and barriers without blame, focusing on problem-solving.[2]  
**Next step:** Schedule your first Monday 15-minute planning session this week, listing 3-5 tasks with due dates in a shared doc like Google Sheets.

## Progress Metrics
Track 3-5 specific habits or metrics weekly, tailored to client or team needs. My PT Hub's medium-accountability clients log 3-5 habits (e.g., workouts, sleep quality, energy levels, soreness, pain), with physical progress questions like "How did workouts feel?" and monthly photos.[1] Unicorn Labs requires outlining key metrics during Monday sessions, revisited Friday to surface issues like unclear priorities.[2]  
**Real example:** High-accountability clients get daily habit tracking and biweekly photos; established clients (months 2-6) track 1-2x weekly.[1]  
**Next step:** Pick 3 metrics (e.g., hours coded, habits hit, revenue tracked) and log them in a simple app like Notion or Excel starting tomorrow.

## Reward Structures
Search results lack explicit reward structures like incentives or bonuses; focus instead on intrinsic motivators from routine and feedback. My PT Hub builds momentum through predictable rhythms (e.g., Monday reviews, Wednesday encouragements, Friday previews), fostering self-motivation without external rewards.[1] Unicorn Labs emphasizes public commitments and issue-spotting as natural accountability drivers, avoiding micromanagement.[2]  
**Note:** No quantified rewards (e.g., $ amounts, points) found in top results from Feb-Mar 2026.  
**Next step:** Test intrinsic feedback by sending yourself a weekly "win" summary email Fridays for 2 weeks.

## Adjustment Protocols
Adjust based on phase, slips, or feedback without policing. My PT Hub scales frequency: new clients 3-4x/week, established 1-2x/week, long-term weekly, with more during challenges; Monday reviews trigger program tweaks.[1] Unicorn Labs' Friday sessions ask "What got in the way?" to realign priorities, documenting changes for the next Monday.[2]  
**Real example:** Medium clients shift to monthly photos after initial phases; teams fix systemic gaps like undocumented actions.[1][2]  
**Next step:** After your first Friday check-in, document 1-2 barriers and adjust next week's tasks explicitly.
