# Effective Breathing Exercises for Improving Lung Capacity

**Five evidence-based breathing exercises can improve lung capacity when practiced 5-10 minutes daily.**[1][2] These techniques strengthen your diaphragm, expand your rib cage, and improve oxygen-carbon dioxide exchange without requiring special equipment.

## Core Breathing Exercises

**Diaphragmatic (Belly) Breathing**

This foundational exercise retrains your body to use your diaphragm rather than shallow chest breathing.[2][3] It strengthens your primary breathing muscle and improves oxygen intake efficiency.[1]

How to practice:[3]
1. Lie on your back with knees bent
2. Place one hand on your chest, the other on your belly
3. Breathe in slowly through your nose for two seconds
4. Feel your belly rise while your chest stays relatively still
5. Exhale through your mouth for two seconds
6. Repeat for 5-10 minutes daily

**Pursed-Lip Breathing**

This technique keeps your airways open longer, making it particularly useful for people with COPD and those experiencing shortness of breath.[2] It slows your breathing rate and improves oxygen-carbon dioxide exchange.[1]

How to practice:[2]
1. Sit or stand in a relaxed position with your back straight
2. Inhale slowly through your nose for two counts
3. Purse your lips as if you were about to whistle or blow out a candle
4. Exhale slowly and gently through your pursed lips for a count of four
5. Repeat this cycle for several breaths

**Box Breathing**

This structured technique trains your body's tolerance to carbon dioxide, which can delay the sensation of breathlessness.[3]

How to practice:[3]
1. Inhale through your nose for four seconds
2. Hold for four seconds
3. Exhale through your mouth for four seconds
4. Hold at the bottom for four seconds
5. Repeat for 2-3 minutes

**Rib Stretch**

This exercise expands your rib cage to allow maximum air intake.[2]

How to practice:[2]
1. Stand upright with your back straight
2. Exhale completely, emptying your lungs
3. Inhale slowly, filling your lungs to maximum capacity
4. Hold your breath for 10-20 seconds, or as long as comfortable
5. While holding, place your hands on your hips with thumbs facing front and pinkies in back
6. Exhale slowly
7. Repeat a few times

**Intercostal Breathing**

This exercise uses the muscles between your ribs to expand your chest cavity and improve gas exchange.[1]

How to practice:[1]
- Place hands on your ribs
- Breathe in deeply, feeling your ribs expand outward

## Clinical Evidence

A structured month-long yoga-based breathing training program produced **clinically and statistically meaningful improvements in lung volumes, expiratory flow, and estimated lung age.**[6]

## Practical Guidelines

- **Frequency:** Aim for 5-10 minutes daily to see results[2]
- **Best practices:** Start with diaphragmatic breathing as a foundational technique, then add other exercises[2]
- **Equipment:** No special equipment is required; exercises can be done anywhere, anytime[2]
- **Progression:** For advanced training, breath-hold (apnea) training can amplify oxygen volume and CO₂ tolerance, particularly useful for swimming and free-diving[4]

- **Day 3-7:** Implement regular mask usage.
  - **Action:** Wear masks during high pollution days, especially when commuting or spending extended periods outdoors.
  - **Expected Outcome:** Decreased inhalation of pollutants, leading to better respiratory health.
