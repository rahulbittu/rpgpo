## LLC vs S-Corp: Detailed Comparison (2026 US Tax Rules)

No 2026-specific comparisons in results (focus was salaries); using latest general US data. **LLCs offer flexibility with pass-through taxation by default (no double tax), ideal for solo consultants avoiding payroll**. **S-Corps allow pass-through but require payroll/salary (subject to FICA), saving 15.3% self-employment tax on distributions over salary** for profits >$50k.

| Aspect | LLC | S-Corp | Key Tax Implications |
|--------|-----|--------|----------------------|
| **Taxation** | Pass-through to personal return (Schedule C); self-employment tax (15.3%) on all net income. | Pass-through; salary subject to payroll tax (FICA 15.3%), distributions tax-free. Saves ~$5k-$20k/year on $100k+ profits. | S-Corp better for high earners; IRS reasonable salary rule (~60% profits as salary). |
| **Formation** | Simple: File Articles of Organization ($50-$500 state fees); no shareholder limits. | File Articles + IRS Form 2553 (elect S status within 75 days); ≤100 US shareholders. | LLC faster/cheaper; S-Corp adds compliance ($800-$2k setup). |
| **Liability** | Personal asset protection. | Same. | Equal. |
| **Benefits** | Flexible management; easy multi-member. | Stock issuance; attracts investors. | S-Corp for growth; LLC for simplicity. |
| **Costs/Ongoing** | Minimal; annual reports $50-$300. | Payroll setup ($1k+/yr), accounting complexity. | S-Corp nets 10-15% tax savings post-$40k salary[no direct 2026 source]. |

- **Benefits for data engineer consultant**: S-Corp saves self-employment tax (e.g., $100k profit: pay $50k salary, distribute $50k tax-free; save ~$7.6k FICA). LLC simpler for <2 members, no payroll[no URL; general knowledge, verify IRS.gov Pub 334].
- **Drawbacks**: S-Corp audit risk on "reasonable salary"; LLC full SE tax.
