## Finding 1: 12-Week Periodization Model for First-Time Competitors

A structured 12-week periodization approach breaks into three distinct phases[1]:

**Phase 1 (Weeks 1–4): Foundation**
- Focus on technique and movement quality for the three lifts (squat, bench press, deadlift)
- Low intensity with emphasis on consistency
- Practice singles each session

**Phase 2 (Weeks 5–8): Strength Building**
- Progressive overload: add 2.5 kg (5.5 lbs) per week to each lift[1]
- Training frequency: 3–4 sessions per week
- Moderate intensity with accessory work introduction
- Increased exposure to heavier loads

**Phase 3 (Weeks 9–12): Meet Preparation**
- Practice competition commands
- Include pause bench variations
- Mock meet in Week 11
- Taper in Week 12 before competition[1]

## Detailed Analysis

### Hypertrophy Phase
- **Objective**: Increase muscle size to support future strength gains.
- **Duration**: Typically 4–6 weeks.
- **Volume/Intensity**: High volume (3–5 sets of 8–12 reps) with moderate intensity (60–75% of 1RM).
- **Exercises**: Compound movements (squats, bench press, deadlifts) with accessory exercises (rows, lunges, tricep extensions).
- **Frequency**: 4–5 sessions per week.

### Strength Phase
- **Objective**: Build maximal strength.
- **Duration**: 4–6 weeks.
- **Volume/Intensity**: Moderate volume (3–5 sets of 3–6 reps) with high intensity (75–90% of 1RM).
- **Exercises**: Focus on the three main lifts with some accessory work.
- **Frequency**: 3–4 sessions per week.

### Peaking Phase
- **Objective**: Maximize strength for competition.
- **Duration**: 2–4 weeks.
- **Volume/Intensity**: Low volume (2–3 sets of 1–3 reps) with very high intensity (85–95% of 1RM).
- **Exercises**: Primarily the three main lifts with minimal accessory work.
- **Frequency**: 3 sessions per week.

### Deload Scheduling
- **Objective**: Allow recovery and prevent overtraining.
- **Frequency**: Every 4–6 weeks or as needed based on fatigue levels.
- **Structure**: Reduce volume and intensity by 40–50% for one week.

### Competition Preparation
- **Objective**: Fine-tune technique and prepare mentally for competition.
- **Tapering**: Gradual reduction in volume and intensity over the final 1–2 weeks before competition.
- **Mock Meet**: Conduct a practice competition to simulate meet conditions.

### Injury Prevention
- **Strategies**: Incorporate mobility work, proper warm-ups, and ensure adequate rest.
- **Monitoring**: Track fatigue and adjust training loads accordingly.
