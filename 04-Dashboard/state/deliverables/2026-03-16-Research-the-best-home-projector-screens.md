## Detailed Analysis
To effectively compare these projector screens, focus on the following key specifications:

- **Gain Values**: This measures the screen's reflectivity. A gain of 1.0 means the screen reflects light uniformly. Higher gain values can enhance brightness in controlled lighting environments but may reduce viewing angles.

- **Viewing Angles**: This indicates how far off-center you can sit before the image quality degrades. Wider viewing angles are better for larger audiences.

- **Ambient Light Rejection (ALR)**: Screens with ALR technology are designed to maintain image quality in environments with ambient light. This is crucial for rooms that cannot be completely darkened.

- **Mounting Options**: Consider whether the screen can be wall-mounted, ceiling-mounted, or if it offers motorized/manual retraction. This affects installation and usability.
