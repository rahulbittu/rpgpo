## Character Bible for "AI & I: A Workplace Comedy"

### 1. **Ethan "The Visionary" Patel**
- **Role**: CEO and Co-Founder
- **Backstory**: Ethan was a coding prodigy turned entrepreneur who dropped out of MIT to start the AI company, NeuralNook. He is driven by a desire to revolutionize AI and make it accessible to everyone. Despite his genius, he often gets lost in grand ideas and forgets the practicalities.
- **Personality**: Charismatic, idealistic, and sometimes oblivious to the chaos around him.
- **Relationship Dynamics**: 
  - Sees potential in everyone, especially interns, often overwhelming them with ambitious projects.
  - Has a mentor-mentee relationship with Maya, the CTO, but frequently clashes with her over practicality versus innovation.
- **Running Gag**: Ethan's tendency to use buzzwords like "synergy" and "disruptive innovation" in every meeting, often out of context.

### 2. **Maya "The Realist" Chen**
- **Role**: Chief Technology Officer (CTO)
- **Backstory**: A former DARPA engineer, Maya joined NeuralNook to bring Ethan's visions to life, though she often finds herself grounding his flights of fancy. She is highly respected for her technical expertise and no-nonsense attitude.
- **Personality**: Pragmatic, sharp-witted, and slightly sarcastic.
- **Relationship Dynamics**:
  - Acts as the voice of reason to Ethan's visionary ideas.
  - Shares a friendly rivalry with Alex, the head of marketing, often debating AI ethics and public perception.
- **Running Gag**: Her constant use of whiteboards to map out solutions, only for Ethan to accidentally erase them during his brainstorming sessions.

### 3. **Alex "The Spin Doctor" Rivera**
- **Role**: Head of Marketing
- **Backstory**: With a background in PR and a knack for storytelling, Alex is responsible for making NeuralNook's complex technology relatable to the public. He thrives on the challenge of turning technical jargon into engaging narratives.
- **Personality**: Charming, persuasive, and a bit of a show-off.
- **Relationship Dynamics**:
  - Friendly banter with Maya over the portrayal of AI in media.
  - Often collaborates with Zoe, the social media manager, to create viral content.
- **Running Gag**: His over-the-top presentations, complete with sound effects and dramatic pauses, which always end with an unexpected technical glitch.

### 4. **Zoe "The Millennial Whisperer" Martinez**
- **Role**: Social Media Manager
- **Backstory**: A digital native, Zoe joined NeuralNook straight out of college. Her ability to navigate social media trends is unmatched, and she keeps the company relevant in the ever-changing digital landscape.
- **Personality**: Energetic, trend-savvy, and slightly rebellious.
- **Relationship Dynamics**:
  - Acts as a bridge between the older and younger employees, often explaining meme culture to the leadership team.
  - Has a playful sibling-like relationship with Sam, the tech support guy.
- **Running Gag**: Her habit of using slang and internet memes in all company communications, leaving the older staff baffled.

### 5. **Sam "The Tech Wizard" Gupta**
- **Role**: IT Support Specialist
- **Backstory**: Known for his quick fixes and encyclopedic knowledge of tech, Sam is the go-to person for any technical issues. He enjoys the challenge of solving problems that stump others.
- **Personality**: Laid-back, resourceful, and slightly eccentric.
- **Relationship Dynamics**:
  - Shares a close friendship with Zoe, often teaming up to prank the rest of the office.
  - Has a love-hate relationship with Ethan's AI assistant, often competing with it to solve problems faster.
- **Running Gag**: His tendency to name all the office devices after sci-fi characters, confusing everyone when they need help.

### 6. **Linda "The Analog Holdout" Thompson**
- **Role**: Office Manager
- **Backstory**: A veteran of the corporate world, Linda is set in her ways and prefers traditional methods over digital solutions. She ensures the office runs smoothly, albeit with a touch of old-school flair.
- **Personality**: Organized, motherly, and a bit stubborn.
- **Relationship Dynamics**:
  - Acts as a mother figure to the younger employees, often offering unsolicited life advice.
  - Frequently butts heads with Zoe over the necessity of digital tools.
- **Running Gag**: Her insistence on printing every email, leading to a mountain of paperwork in an otherwise paperless office.
