## Finding 1: NCLEX 2026 Computer Adaptive Testing (CAT) with NGN-Style Questions
- Uses **Computer Adaptive Testing (CAT)** that adjusts question difficulty based on candidate responses, maintaining variable-length format with unchanged minimum/maximum questions.

- Best practice: Prioritize **case-based practice** over high question volume; use AI-driven adaptive systems with logit-based scoring for realistic progression based on ability, performance consistency, reasoning accuracy, and time efficiency.

- **Module 1** fixed difficulty for all; performance routes to **Module 2**: 65-75% correct on Module 1 unlocks **Hard Module 2** (score ceiling 6.0), below gets **Easy Module 2** (caps at 4.5).
- Best practice: Maximize Module 1 accuracy over speed to access higher score paths; practice task-specific strategies for 4 task types with realistic conversational English exposure.
