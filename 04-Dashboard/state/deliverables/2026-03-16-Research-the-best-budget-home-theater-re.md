## Detailed Analysis

### Feature Comparison
- **Dolby Atmos**: All three models support 5.2.2 configurations, making them suitable for entry-level Atmos setups.
- **HDMI 2.1 Support**: Denon and Yamaha offer partial HDMI 2.1 features, which are crucial for future-proofing against new video standards. Sony lacks these features.
- **Room Correction**: Denon's Audyssey is generally regarded as superior, offering better bass management. Yamaha's YPAO is reliable but less effective, while Sony's DCAC is basic.

### Performance Evaluations
- **Denon AVR-S660H**: Praised for its value and ease of setup, with strong room correction capabilities.
- **Yamaha RX-V4A**: Offers more HDMI inputs but falls short in room correction compared to Denon.
- **Sony STR-DH790**: Simplicity and sound quality are highlights, but it lacks advanced features.

### Pricing
- **Denon AVR-S660H**: $499
- **Yamaha RX-V4A**: $550
- **Sony STR-DH790**: $499

## Recommendations

### Best Receiver Under $500: Denon AVR-S660H
- **Why**: Offers the best combination of features, room correction, and future-proofing with partial HDMI 2.1 support.
- **Expected Outcome**: Superior audio performance in a budget setup, with good value retention due to its features.
- **First Step**: Purchase from Amazon or Crutchfield to take advantage of the current $499 pricing.

### Consideration for Yamaha RX-V4A
- **Why**: If additional HDMI inputs are a priority, and you can stretch your budget slightly.
- **Expected Outcome**: Slightly better connectivity options, but at a higher cost.
- **First Step**: Evaluate if the additional HDMI inputs justify the extra $50.

### Avoid Sony STR-DH790
- **Why**: Lacks HDMI 2.1 support and advanced room correction, which limits future compatibility and performance optimization.
- **Expected Outcome**: May require upgrading sooner as new standards emerge.
- **First Step**: Consider only if simplicity and current 4K HDR support suffice.
