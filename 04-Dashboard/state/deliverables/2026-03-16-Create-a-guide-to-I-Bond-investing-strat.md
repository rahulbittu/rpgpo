## I-Bond Rate Composition (Fixed + Variable)
Series I savings bonds (I Bonds) earn a composite rate combining a **fixed rate** (set at purchase and unchanged for 30 years) and a **semiannual inflation rate** (variable, adjusted every 6 months based on CPI-U). For bonds issued November 1, 2025, through April 30, 2026, the composite rate is **4.03%**, including a fixed rate of **0.90%**. [3][5]

- Interest compounds automatically every 6 months by adding to principal, earning on the growing balance for up to 30 years. [5]
- If inflation falls (e.g., to 2% as in 2010-2020), total yield drops toward the fixed component alone after initial 6 months. [5]

## Purchase Limits and Workarounds
Annual non-competitive purchase limit is **$10,000** per person via TreasuryDirect (electronic); additional $5,000 in paper bonds via tax refund. - Bonds unavailable in IRAs/401(k)s; taxable accounts only. [5]
- Potential tax break: Buy using child's SSN for interest taxed at child's low rate (often 0% if low income). [5]

## Tax Deferral Benefits
Interest is federally taxable but exempt from state/local taxes; deferral allowed until redemption or maturity (30 years). No specific new 2026 changes found (queried "I Bond tax deferral 2026"). [3] (implied via standard rules; see Related Content in source).

## Optimal Holding Periods
Minimum hold: **1 year** (no cash-out before). Early redemption (before **5 years**): forfeit last **3 months' interest**. Hold 30 years for full term; fixed rate provides baseline if inflation drops. [3][5]

- Attractive for long-term inflation hedge/stability vs. ~4% high-yield savings/CDs, especially with 0.90% fixed floor. [3]

## Detailed Reviews

### Rate Composition
- **Details**: The composite rate is a blend of a fixed rate and a variable rate that adjusts every six months based on the Consumer Price Index for All Urban Consumers (CPI-U). This ensures that the bond's yield adjusts with inflation, protecting purchasing power.
- **Action**: Monitor CPI-U updates biannually to anticipate changes in the variable rate component.

### Purchase Limits and Workarounds
- **Details**: The annual limit is $10,000 per individual, with an additional $5,000 available through tax refunds. No effective workarounds for exceeding these limits were identified.
- **Action**: Consider using the gift box strategy to purchase I-Bonds for family members, effectively increasing the household's total investment.

### Tax Deferral Benefits
- **Details**: Interest is deferred for federal taxes until the bond is redeemed or matures, providing a tax-efficient growth method. Interest is exempt from state and local taxes.
- **Action**: Plan to use I-Bonds as part of a tax-efficient investment strategy, especially if you live in a high state tax area.

### Optimal Holding Period
- **Details**: I-Bonds must be held for at least one year. If redeemed before five years, the last three months of interest are forfeited.
- **Action**: Aim to hold I-Bonds for at least five years to maximize returns and avoid penalties. Consider aligning redemption with financial goals or tax planning needs.
