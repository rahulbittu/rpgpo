## Finding 1: Expanded GFCI Protection Requirements for Residential HVAC Equipment

The 2026 NEC now mandates **GFCI protection for outdoor HVAC equipment** in single and multi-family dwellings. Specifically, all 208V or 240V single-phase HVAC equipment rated **50 amps or less** that serves a dwelling unit and is located outdoors requires ground-fault protection[1][2]. This requirement addresses electrical leakage risks in outdoor units exposed to moisture and temperature fluctuations, particularly in regions like the Pacific Northwest[2].

Additionally, **GFCI protection now extends to docking facilities and piers**, where ground-fault protection must be rated **100mA or less** for both branch circuits and feeders[1].

## Finding 2: Receptacle Replacement Requirements and GFCI Compliance

The 2026 NEC significantly tightened receptacle replacement rules under **Article 406.12D**. When replacing receptacles at locations that now require GFCI protection, **the replacement receptacle must be GFCI-protected**, even if the original was not[6]. This eliminates previous allowances for replacing receptacles without upgrading to GFCI protection. All AFCI or GFCI-type receptacles must be installed in **readily accessible locations**[6].
