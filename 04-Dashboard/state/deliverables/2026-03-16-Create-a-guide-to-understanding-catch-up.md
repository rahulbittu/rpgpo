## 401(k) Catch-Up Contributions (2026 Limits)
- **Standard employee deferral**: $24,500 (up from $23,500 in 2025).[1][2][3]
- **Age 50+ catch-up**: Additional $8,000 (total $32,500); up from $7,500 in 2025.[1][2][3][4][5]
- **Ages 60-63 super catch-up**: Additional $11,250 (total $35,750); unchanged from 2025.[1][2][3][4][5][7]
- **Total combined limit (employee + employer, excluding catch-up)**: $72,000 (or 100% compensation); rises to $80,000 (age 50+) or $83,250 (ages 60-63).[2][3][4]
- **Roth-only rule (effective Jan 1, 2026)**: Catch-ups must be Roth if prior-year wages >$150,000 (2025 wages for 2026 contributions); applies to 401(k)/Solo 401(k).[4][5][8]

## Detailed Analysis

### 401(k) Catch-Up Contributions
- The introduction of the super catch-up for ages 60-63 provides a significant opportunity to boost retirement savings. This is particularly beneficial for those nearing retirement who need to maximize their contributions.
- The Roth-only rule for catch-ups for high earners (>$150,000) requires strategic planning, as it affects tax treatment. Roth contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free, which can be advantageous in retirement.

### IRA Catch-Up Contributions
- The IRA catch-up contribution is modest compared to 401(k) options, but it still offers a valuable opportunity to increase retirement savings.
- The lack of a super catch-up option for IRAs means individuals must rely on standard contributions and the regular catch-up to maximize these accounts.

## Recommended Actions

### 401(k) Contributions
1. **Maximize Standard and Catch-Up Contributions**
   - **What to do**: Ensure you contribute the maximum allowable amount, including the age 50+ catch-up and, if applicable, the super catch-up.
   - **Why**: This maximizes tax-advantaged savings potential and leverages employer matching contributions.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Enhanced retirement savings with significant tax benefits.
   - **First Step**: Review current 401(k) contributions and adjust payroll settings to meet the new limits.

2. **Plan for Roth-Only Catch-Ups**
   - **What to do**: If your wages exceed $150,000, plan for Roth contributions for catch-up amounts.
   - **Why**: Roth contributions can provide tax-free income in retirement, which is beneficial for tax planning.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Diversified tax treatment of retirement income.
   - **First Step**: Consult with a financial advisor to understand the implications of Roth contributions on your retirement strategy.

### IRA Contributions
1. **Maximize IRA Contributions**
   - **What to do**: Contribute the maximum to your IRA, including the catch-up amount.
   - **Why**: Every additional dollar contributes to long-term growth potential.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Increased retirement savings with tax-deferred growth.
   - **First Step**: Set up automatic contributions to your IRA to ensure you meet the limits.

### Optimal Contribution Sequencing
1. **Prioritize 401(k) Contributions**
   - **What to do**: Prioritize maximizing 401(k) contributions first due to higher limits and employer matching.
   - **Why**: Employer matching is essentially "free money" and should be fully utilized.
   - **Expected Outcome**: Maximized employer contributions and higher overall savings.
   - **First Step**: Ensure contributions are set to at least meet the employer match threshold.

2. **Supplement with IRA Contributions**
   - **What to do**: Use IRA contributions to supplement 401(k) savings, especially if you have additional funds to invest.
   - **Why**: Diversifies retirement savings and takes advantage of different tax treatments.
   - **Expected Outcome**: More balanced and flexible retirement savings strategy.
   - **First Step**: Assess your budget to allocate funds towards IRA contributions after maximizing 401(k).

By following these specific steps, you can effectively leverage catch-up contributions to enhance your retirement savings strategy.
