### Personal Cybersecurity Audit Checklist

#### 1. Password Manager Migration
- **What to Do**: Migrate to a reputable password manager like 1Password, LastPass, or Bitwarden.
- **Why**: These tools securely store and auto-generate strong passwords, reducing the risk of password reuse and breaches.
- **Expected Outcome**: Enhanced password security and ease of access across devices.
- **First Step**: Export your current passwords from your existing manager (if applicable) and import them into the new tool following the provider's guide.

#### 2. 2FA Enforcement
- **What to Do**: Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all accounts that support it, using apps like Google Authenticator or Authy.
- **Why**: Adds an extra layer of security beyond just passwords, protecting against unauthorized access.
- **Expected Outcome**: Increased account security and reduced risk of unauthorized access.
- **First Step**: Visit the security settings of your key accounts (email, banking, social media) and enable 2FA.

#### 3. Email Alias Strategy
- **What to Do**: Use email alias services like ProtonMail or SimpleLogin to create disposable email addresses.
- **Why**: Protects your primary email from spam and phishing attacks.
- **Expected Outcome**: Reduced spam and phishing attempts, better control over email privacy.
- **First Step**: Set up an account with an email alias provider and start using aliases for new sign-ups.

#### 4. Social Media Privacy Hardening
- **What to Do**: Review and update privacy settings on all social media platforms.
- **Why**: Limits the amount of personal information exposed to the public and potential attackers.
- **Expected Outcome**: Enhanced privacy and reduced risk of personal data exposure.
- **First Step**: Go to the privacy settings of each social media account and adjust them to limit data sharing.

#### 5. Device Encryption
- **What to Do**: Enable full-disk encryption on all devices using tools like BitLocker (Windows) or FileVault (Mac).
- **Why**: Protects data on your devices from unauthorized access in case of loss or theft.
- **Expected Outcome**: Secured data even if the physical device is compromised.
- **First Step**: Access your device's security settings and enable the encryption feature.

#### 6. Breach Monitoring Setup
- **What to Do**: Use breach monitoring services like Have I Been Pwned or Breach Alert to track if your data is compromised.
- **Why**: Early detection of data breaches allows for timely action to secure accounts.
- **Expected Outcome**: Quick response to potential data breaches, minimizing damage.
- **First Step**: Sign up for notifications from a breach monitoring service and regularly check for alerts.
