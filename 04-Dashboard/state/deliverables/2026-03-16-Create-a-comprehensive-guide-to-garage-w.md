## Detailed Analysis

### Tool Prioritization

- **Chisels**: Essential for detailed work and joinery. Recommended sizes: 1/4", 1/2", 3/4", 1".
- **Hand Planes**: Jack and jointer planes are crucial for flattening and smoothing surfaces.
- **Hand Saws**: Crosscut and rip saws for cutting across and along the grain, respectively.
- **Measuring Tools**: Tape measure, combination square, and marking gauge for precision.
- **Clamps**: Bar clamps and C-clamps for securing workpieces.
- **Mallets**: Used for driving chisels and assembling joints without marring surfaces.

### Dust Collection Systems

- **Importance**: Prevents respiratory issues and maintains a clean workspace.
- **Recommended System**: A portable dust collector with a minimum of 1 HP motor and 650 CFM capacity is suitable for a hobbyist setup.
- **First Step**: Install a basic dust collection system that can be expanded as needed.

### Workbench Design

- **Size and Stability**: A sturdy workbench measuring at least 6' x 2.5' with a solid top (preferably hardwood) and a vise for holding workpieces.
- **Customization**: Include storage options like drawers or shelves for tools and materials.
- **First Step**: Build or purchase a workbench that fits your space and needs, ensuring it is level and stable.

### Electrical Requirements

- **Power Supply**: Ensure access to multiple 120V outlets around the workshop for tools and lighting.
- **Lighting**: Install bright, overhead LED lighting for clear visibility, especially over the workbench area.
- **First Step**: Consult with an electrician to assess and potentially upgrade your garage's electrical system to handle the load of power tools.

### Safety Equipment

- **Essential Gear**: Safety glasses, ear protection, dust masks, and a first aid kit.
- **Fire Safety**: Install a fire extinguisher rated for electrical and wood fires.
- **First Step**: Purchase and organize safety equipment in a designated area for easy access.
