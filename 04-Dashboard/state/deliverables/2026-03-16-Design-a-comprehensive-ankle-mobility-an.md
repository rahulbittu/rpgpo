## Ankle Mobility and Stability Program for Trail Runners Recovering from Sprains

### Week-by-Week Plan

#### Week 1-2: Initial Recovery and Stability Building
- **Banded Posterior Tibialis Strengthening**
  - **Why**: Strengthens the arch and improves overall ankle stability.
  - **How**: Sit in a figure-four position, loop a light resistance band around the ball of your foot (anchor under the planted foot), perform 15-20 slow inversion reps per side.
  - **Progression**: Increase band resistance as strength builds.
  - **First Step**: Acquire a light resistance band.

- **Proprioceptive Retraining**
  - **Why**: Enhances balance and coordination, crucial for uneven trail surfaces.
  - **How**: Balance on one foot for 30 seconds, progress to unstable surfaces like a balance pad.
  - **First Step**: Practice balancing on a flat surface, then introduce a balance pad.

#### Week 3-4: Strengthening and Plyometric Introduction
- **Calf Raises**
  - **Why**: Strengthens calves, which support ankle stability.
  - **How**: Stand on a step with heels hanging off, raise up onto toes, hold for 2 seconds, lower slowly. Perform 3 sets of 10-15 reps.
  - **First Step**: Begin with body weight, progress to single-leg raises.

- **Plyometric Progressions**
  - **Why**: Prepares the ankle for dynamic movements and shock absorption.
  - **How**: Start with two-legged hops in place, progress to single-leg hops and lateral jumps.
  - **First Step**: Begin with 3 sets of 10 two-legged hops.

#### Week 5-6: Advanced Strengthening and Sport-Specific Drills
- **Sport-Specific Agility Drills**
  - **Why**: Mimics trail running conditions, improving functional stability.
  - **How**: Incorporate cone drills and ladder drills focusing on quick direction changes.
  - **First Step**: Set up a simple cone drill in an open space.

- **Manual Therapy for Joint Stiffness**
  - **Why**: Reduces stiffness and improves range of motion.
  - **How**: Consult a physical therapist for joint mobilization techniques.
  - **First Step**: Schedule an appointment with a licensed therapist.

### Evidence Basis
- **Therapeutic Associates**: Emphasizes exercises targeting key muscles for stability post-sprain, active restraints over bracing (source: Therapeutic Associates, 2023).
- **Runner's World**: Highlights the importance of strength/mobility exercises and proper mechanics to prevent re-injury (source: Runner's World, 2024).

### Progression
- **Gradual Loading**: Increase intensity and complexity of exercises as comfort and strength improve.
- **Monitoring**: Track progress weekly, adjust exercises based on pain levels and stability improvements.

### Safety Notes
- **Contraindications**: Avoid exercises that cause pain or discomfort; consult a healthcare provider if unsure.
- **Proper Warm-Up**: Always begin sessions with a warm-up to prevent further injury.
- **Rest and Recovery**: Allow adequate rest between sessions to facilitate healing.
