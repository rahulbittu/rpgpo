## Finding 1: Hip Internal/External Rotation Improvements

A documented case study showed significant gains in hip mobility through structured training. One golfer achieved a **36% increase in left hip internal/external rotation and 38% increase in right hip internal/external rotation** after a 30-day winter golf rebuild program[4]. These improvements directly address swing faults like swaying and loss of posture. The exercises that contributed to these gains included:

- Dumbbell Romanian Deadlifts (RDL)
- Dumbbell Split Squats
- Double Leg Seated Box Jumps
- Medicine Ball Slams
- Three-Point Kneeling Bent Over Dumbbell Rows

## Finding 2: Thoracic Rotation & Shoulder Turn Exercises

Thoracic mobility is essential for an effective golf swing. Recommended exercises to improve shoulder turn while maintaining core stability include[3]:

- Reachbacks
- Half-Kneeling Rotation with Side Bend
- Half-Kneeling Resisted Rotation

These exercises specifically target the ability to achieve a good shoulder turn while keeping your lower body stable—a critical component of swing sequence.

Source: https://www.therapeuticassociates.com/strength-for-golfers/

## Finding 3: Dynamic Warm-Up & Flexibility Protocol

Pre-round mobility work should focus on the largest muscle groups[1]:

- **Hip openers** and **torso twists** to improve rotational mobility
- **Shoulder stretches** for upper body range of motion
- **Lower back stretches** for spinal mobility

The recommended frequency is **3-4 workouts per week** combining both strength and flexibility exercises[1]. A specialized daily mobility routine can be completed in **15 minutes**[2].

Source: https://dungenessgolf.com/golf-fitness-exercises-for-flexibility-and-power/

## Finding 4: Hip Mobility Progressions

Advanced hip mobility work includes **Hip 90/90 stretches with controlled rotations** to open internal and external rotation bilaterally[6]. This addresses the foundational mobility needed for weight transfer and rotational power in the golf swing.

Source: https://drvngolf.com/blog/how-to-improve-your-golf-swing

## Finding 5: Core Stability for Post-Round Recovery

Core exercises that support recovery and injury prevention include[1]:

- Russian twists
- Bridges
- Bird dogs

These maintain spinal stability during long rounds and protect your back during powerful swings.

## Synthesize Mobility Routine
## Comprehensive Mobility Routine for Golfers

### Plan

#### Day 1: Focus on Thoracic Rotation
- **Exercises**: 
  - **Reachbacks**: 3 sets of 10 reps per side
  - **Half-Kneeling Rotation with Side Bend**: 3 sets of 8 reps per side
  - **Half-Kneeling Resisted Rotation**: 3 sets of 10 reps per side
  
  **Why**: These exercises enhance thoracic mobility, crucial for achieving a full shoulder turn without compromising lower body stability.
  
  **First Step**: Begin with a light warm-up to increase blood flow to the upper body.

#### Day 2: Hip Internal Rotation
- **Exercises**:
  - **Dumbbell Romanian Deadlifts (RDL)**: 3 sets of 12 reps
  - **Dumbbell Split Squats**: 3 sets of 10 reps per leg
  - **Double Leg Seated Box Jumps**: 3 sets of 8 reps
  - **Medicine Ball Slams**: 3 sets of 12 reps
  
  **Why**: These exercises target hip mobility, crucial for maintaining posture and preventing swing faults like swaying.
  
  **First Step**: Start with dynamic stretches focusing on the lower body.

#### Day 3: Wrist Mobility
- **Exercises**:
  - **Wrist Circles**: 3 sets of 15 reps in each direction
  - **Wrist Flexor Stretch**: 3 sets of 30-second holds
  - **Wrist Extensor Stretch**: 3 sets of 30-second holds
  
  **Why**: Improved wrist mobility aids in better club control and reduces the risk of injury.
  
  **First Step**: Warm up wrists with light resistance bands.

#### Day 4: Dynamic Warm-Up Sequence
- **Routine**:
  - **Arm Circles**: 2 sets of 15 reps
  - **Leg Swings**: 2 sets of 15 reps per leg
  - **Torso Twists**: 2 sets of 15 reps
  - **High Knees**: 2 sets of 30 seconds
  
  **Why**: Prepares the body for physical activity by increasing heart rate and blood flow to muscles.
  
  **First Step**: Perform these exercises in sequence before practice or play.

#### Day 5: Post-Round Recovery
- **Routine**:
  - **Foam Rolling**: 5 minutes focusing on back, hips, and legs
  - **Static Stretching**: 30 seconds per stretch focusing on major muscle groups
  - **Hydration and Nutrition**: Replenish with water and a protein-rich snack
  
  **Why**: Facilitates muscle recovery and reduces soreness after a round of golf.
  
  **First Step**: Begin with foam rolling immediately after your round.

### Evidence Basis
- **Thoracic Rotation**: Exercises like reachbacks and kneeling rotations are supported by Therapeutic Associates for enhancing shoulder turn and core stability[3].
- **Hip Internal Rotation**: Documented improvements in hip mobility from structured exercises such as RDLs and split squats[4].
- **Wrist Mobility**: Essential for club control and injury prevention, though specific sources were not detailed in prior results.
- **Dynamic Warm-Up**: Recommended to increase flexibility and reduce injury risk before play.

### Progression
- **Weekly Review**: Track improvements in mobility and adjust exercises or reps as needed.
- **Monthly Assessment**: Evaluate overall swing performance and adjust the routine based on specific needs or weaknesses.

### Safety Notes
- **Proper Form**: Ensure correct form to prevent injury, especially during weighted exercises.
- **Warm-Up**: Always begin with a warm-up to prepare muscles and joints for exercise.
- **Listen to Your Body**: If any exercise causes pain, stop immediately and consult a professional.
