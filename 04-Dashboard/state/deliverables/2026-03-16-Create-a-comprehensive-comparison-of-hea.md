## Finding 3: HSA Eligibility Expansion and DPC Integration

**Starting January 1, 2026, all Bronze and Catastrophic plans offered through ACA exchanges are automatically considered HSA-compatible**, even if they don't meet traditional HDHP requirements[4]. This represents a significant expansion for self-employed individuals and small business owners.

**2026 HSA contribution limits** are[4]:
- $4,400 for individuals
- $8,750 for families
- Plus $1,000 catch-up contribution if age 55+

A major 2026 change allows HSA funds to pay for **Direct Primary Care (DPC) membership fees up to $150 per month for individuals or $300 per month for families**[4]. This enables a new strategy combining high-deductible health plans, HSAs, and DPC memberships for comprehensive coverage with pre-tax dollars.

Source: https://condley.cpa/healthcare-costs-for-the-self-employed-in-2026-strategies-for-rising-premiums/

## Finding 4: Medicare Changes Affecting Texas Enrollees

For Medicare beneficiaries in Texas, **Part B monthly premiums increased by nearly $18 (approximately 10%) from $185 to $202.90 in 2026**[2]. Additionally, **Part D prescription plan out-of-pocket maximums increased from $2,000 in 2025 to $2,100 in 2026**[2].

A new pilot program beginning January 1, 2026 implements **prior authorization for certain Medicare Part B services in six states including Texas, Arizona, New Jersey, Ohio, Oklahoma, and Washington**[2].
