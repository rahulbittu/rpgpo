## Finding 1: 100 Minus Your Age Rule for Asset Allocation
Kiplinger outlines the **100 Minus Your Age** rule as a simple strategy: subtract your age from 100 to determine stock allocation, with the remainder in bonds or fixed income. For individuals in their 20s (e.g., age 20-29): **80% stocks / 20% bonds**; 30s (age 30): **70% stocks / 30% bonds**; 40s (age 40): **60% stocks / 40% bonds**. This rule adjusts over time and isn't a one-time set-it-and-forget-it; rebalance periodically based on risk tolerance and goals, per John Bergquist of Elysium Financial (article undated but references ongoing use as of publication).[1]
