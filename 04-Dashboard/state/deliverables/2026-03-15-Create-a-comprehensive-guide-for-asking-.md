## Research best practices for asking for a raise
## Timing for Asking for a Raise
Request a raise after demonstrating clear value, such as completing high-impact projects or during performance reviews, but avoid anchoring early by sharing numbers first—let the employer speak to set the range (e.g., internal band of $142K-$162K).[2][3] Negotiate post-offer or after benchmarking data is gathered, as rushing leads to low anchors that cap outcomes below company bands.[3]

## Data to Gather
- Use Levels.fyi, Payscale, and Dice for primary benchmarking to build an "impenetrable anchor" against market rates, revealing gaps like $40K in DFW tech roles.[2]
- Calculate **total compensation** including RSUs, equity (watch "cliff" vesting), sign-on bonuses, and tuition reimbursement, not just base—target $250K–$300K+ for underpaid senior roles.[1][2][3]
- Frame technical skills (e.g., AI Governance, MLOps) as ROI multipliers; MS-IS degree unlocks $171K+ Director pay.[2]
- Research company constraints on base vs. equity flexibility and leveling impacts on future raises.[3]

## Effective Conversation Scripts
- **Translator Pitch for ROI:** "Are you paying for my time, or the risks I reduce? Here's the business case: My MLOps work cuts deployment risks by X%, saving $Y in downtime—equivalent to Z ROI."[2]
- **Respond to 'Hands Tied' (e.g., "Can't go over $130K base"):** "I understand the budget constraint, but in exchange, I want [equity/RSUs/sign-on/tuition reimbursement] to bridge to market total comp of $250K+."[1][2]
- **Delay Anchoring:** When asked for expectations, say: "Based on Levels.fyi/Payscale data for this level in [location], market range is $A-$B total comp—what's the structure here?"[2][3]
- **Post-Call Follow-Up:** "Thank you for the details. I'll review the full package (base, equity cliff, leveling) and respond by [date]."[3]

## Fallback Strategies
- If base is rigid, pivot to flexible components: equity refreshers, sign-on bonuses, or tuition reimbursement (e.g., MS-IS programs like Tarleton State).[2][3]
- Don't decide on the call—pause, ask about comp structure/flexibility, then negotiate via email for better terms.[3]
- If no movement, use data to anchor future internal growth or prepare Texas-specific leverage (no salary history ban).[2]
- Worst case: Walk with market data for external job search targeting $250K–$300K TC.[1]
