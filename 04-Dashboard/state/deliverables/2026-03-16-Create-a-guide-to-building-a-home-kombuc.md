## Finding 2: SCOBY Care & Storage (SCOBY Hotel)

**Maintenance protocol:**
- Store SCOBYs in a glass jar with some kombucha liquid (the "mother" SCOBY plus any "baby" layers that form)
- **Top up evaporation:** Add water or sweet tea periodically to keep SCOBYs submerged[1]
- Keep at room temperature (68–85°F is optimal)
- SCOBYs can be stored dormant for several months if needed
- Discard any moldy or pink/orange fuzzy growth immediately

## Finding 3: Food Safety & Fermentation Standards

**Critical safety protocols:**
- **pH target:** Kombucha should reach pH 2.5–3.5 after fermentation (acidic enough to prevent pathogenic growth)
- **Fermentation duration:** 7–30 days depending on temperature and desired tartness
- **Temperature range:** 68–85°F; below 60°F fermentation slows significantly
- **Contamination prevention:** Use clean (not necessarily sterile) equipment; avoid metal containers
- **Testing:** Use pH strips to verify acidity before consumption

**FDA guidance:** Kombucha is not federally regulated as a food product, but home brewers should follow basic food safety principles (clean equipment, proper fermentation, no cross-contamination).

## Finding 4: Flavor Experimentation & Carbonation Control

**Secondary fermentation (carbonation phase):**
- Duration: 3–7 days in sealed bottles at room temperature
- Add fruit juice, herbs, or spices during bottling for flavor
- Longer secondary fermentation = more carbonation (monitor pressure to avoid over-carbonation)
- "Burp" bottles daily to release excess pressure and prevent explosions

**Popular flavor combinations:**
- Ginger + lemon
- Berry + hibiscus
- Mint + cucumber
- Turmeric + black pepper

**Carbonation control:** Shorter secondary fermentation (3 days) = lighter fizz; longer (5–7 days) = stronger carbonation. Temperature affects speed—warmer = faster carbonation.
