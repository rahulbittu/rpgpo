## Research Tax Implications
## Key Tax Implications of Moving from Texas to Washington

Both Texas and Washington currently have **no state income tax on wages**, but Washington imposes a **7%-9.9% capital gains tax** (enacted 2021, effective 2022) and is advancing a **9.9% "Millionaires Tax"** on personal income over $1 million (SB 6346, passed Senate/House as of March 11, 2026, awaiting Gov. Bob Ferguson signature; effective Jan 1, 2028, payments due 2029).[1][2][3][4]

Texas offers true zero state income tax (no personal, capital gains, or similar levies), while Washington's changes target high earners, potentially creating the nation's highest combined state/local top rate of **18.037%** when paired with local taxes (per Tax Foundation analysis).[2]

## Washington's Capital Gains Tax (Current, Affects Move Now)
- **Rate**: 7% on long-term capital gains exceeding $278,000 exemption (2025 amount, indexed annually); 9.9% on gains above $1 million over exemption.[4]
- **Revenue**: Generated hundreds of millions annually for schools/childcare; upheld after court challenge.[2][4]
- **Impact on Texans**: Applies to WA-sourced gains post-move; no reciprocity needed since TX has no income tax.[5]

Source: https://www.kiplinger.com/taxes/washington-state-millionaire-tax[4]

## Proposed Millionaires Tax (SB 6346, High Likelihood of Enactment)
- **Rate**: 9.9% on adjusted gross household income >$1 million (inflation-indexed every 2 years); standard $1M deduction (nonresidents prorate by WA income ratio).[1][3]
- **Affected**: ~20,000-21,000 households (0.5% of taxpayers), including pass-through business income and RSUs (common for tech workers).[1][2]
- **Revenue**: $3.5B-$4B/year for Working Families Tax Credit expansion (to 810,000 households from 350,000, credits $50-$1,330).[3]
- **Timeline Risks**: Possible delay to 2027 session if revenue allocation unresolved; legal challenges expected (1933 precedent bans non-uniform income tax).[3][7]
- **Business Exodus**: 44% of WA business owners plan personal relocation, 17% business moves (Association of Washington Businesses survey).[1]
