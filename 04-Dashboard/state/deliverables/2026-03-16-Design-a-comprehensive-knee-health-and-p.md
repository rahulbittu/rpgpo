## Key Prehabilitation Strategies for Runners Over 35

**VMO (Vastus Medialis Obliquus) Strengthening**
- Targeted exercises include: short-arc quads, step-ups (6-8 inch height), lateral band walks, and terminal knee extensions
- Typical protocol: 3 sets of 12-15 reps, 3-4x weekly
- Critical for runners over 35 due to natural quadriceps strength decline (~3-8% per decade after age 30)

**IT Band Management**
- Foam rolling: 1-2 minutes per leg, 3-4x weekly
- Strength focus: hip abduction and external rotation exercises (clamshells, side-lying leg raises)
- Stretching: piriformis and hip flexor work, held 30 seconds, 2-3x daily

**Patellofemoral Syndrome (PFS) Prevention**
- Root causes in older runners: weak hip stabilizers, poor running form, rapid mileage increases
- Intervention: hip strengthening (glute medius/maximus work) shows strongest evidence for PFS prevention

**Running Form Corrections**
- Key metrics: cadence (aim for 170-180 steps/minute), knee alignment over ankle, reduced overstriding
- Video gait analysis recommended for runners with history of knee issues

**Cross-Training Integration**
- Swimming, cycling, elliptical: maintain aerobic fitness while reducing impact
- Typical weekly structure: 2-3 impact sessions + 1-2 cross-training sessions for runners over 35
