## Purchase Limits
**$10,000 electronic per calendar year per Social Security Number + $5,000 paper per year via tax refund.**  
Individuals can purchase up to $10,000 in electronic Series I savings bonds annually through TreasuryDirect. An additional $5,000 in paper I Bonds can be bought with federal tax refunds using IRS Form 8888. Minors can have separate limits under a parent's SSN. Total limit resets January 1 each year.  
**Next Steps:** Log into TreasuryDirect.gov; verify SSN limit via account dashboard before purchasing.  
Source: https://www.treasurydirect.gov/savings-bonds/buy-a-bond/

## Tax Advantages
**Federally taxable interest; state/local tax-exempt; defer up to 30 years or until redemption.**  
Interest accrues monthly, compounds semiannually, and is exempt from state and local taxes. Federal tax can be deferred until redemption or maturity (30 years), or reported annually. Qualified education expenses allow penalty-free use (up to $10,000/year penalty-free for higher ed via 529 plans, but interest remains taxable). No tax on principal.  
**Next Steps:** Use IRS Form 1040 to report redeemed interest; consult Publication 550 for deferral rules.  
Source: https://www.treasurydirect.gov/savings-bonds/tax-information-ee-i-bonds/

## Optimal Redemption Timing
**Hold at least 12 months; redeem after 12 months but before 5 years to avoid 3-month interest penalty.**  
I Bonds must be held 12 months minimum. Early redemption between 12-59 months forfeits the prior 3 months' interest. Optimal: Hold 5+ years for full interest; rates adjust semiannually (May and November). Current low rates (1.60%) suggest holding existing higher-rate bonds (e.g., 2024 peak 9.62%) until penalty-free.  
**Next Steps:** Use TreasuryDirect's I Bond Value Calculator at https://www.treasurydirect.gov/BC/SBIDC/ to model redemption dates for your bonds.  
Source: https://www.treasurydirect.gov/savings-bonds/i-bonds/i-bonds-technical-info/
